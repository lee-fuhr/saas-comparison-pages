Best OneDrive alternatives

If you rely on Microsoft 365 for your daily work, OneDrive is the natural choice for file storage and sharing. It offers deep integration with Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook. But you might be exploring other options for better collaboration features, tighter third-party app support, or simply a change in workflow. This article covers three popular OneDrive alternatives and how they work with Office apps, so you can decide which fits your team best.

alternative

Google Drive

. Google Drive works well with Microsoft 365 files through its own suite of editors, but it does not offer native editing inside Office apps. You can use the Drive for Desktop app to sync files locally and open them in Word or Excel, but real-time co-authoring with Office Online requires extra steps. Google Drive excels at team collaboration and search, though you lose some of the seamless, single-click experience you get with OneDrive.

Try Google Drive →
alternative

Dropbox

. Dropbox provides solid file syncing and sharing, and its desktop app integrates with Office by letting you save directly to Dropbox from within Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. It also supports file locking for Office documents to prevent editing conflicts. However, Dropbox lacks a built-in office suite, so you will still need a separate Microsoft 365 subscription to edit files online or collaborate in real time.

Try Dropbox →
alternative

Box

. Box focuses on enterprise security and content management, and it offers an Office Online integration that lets you create and edit documents from the Box web interface. Its desktop app syncs files locally, and you can open them in your local Office apps. Box’s strength is granular permission controls and advanced workflows, but its Office integration is not as deeply embedded as OneDrive’s, and real-time co-authoring relies on Office for the web.

Try Box →

How they compare

OneDrive gives you the deepest Office integration, with instant file access, version history, and real-time co-authoring from inside the apps. Google Drive offers strong collaboration but requires workarounds for Office editing. Dropbox provides reliable sync and local Office file support. Box delivers enterprise-grade security and decent Office online editing. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize native Office features over collaboration tools, security controls, or third-party app ecosystems.
