You love MailerLite because it is simple, affordable, and packed with features like landing pages and automation. But maybe you have hit a limit with its templates, or you want more advanced segmentation, or you just want to see what else is out there. This guide compares five popular MailerLite alternatives so you can find the right fit for your small business or creator workflow.

alternative

Mailchimp

is a well known platform that offers a free plan and a huge library of templates. It has strong reporting and built in CRM tools, but pricing can climb quickly once your list grows past a few thousand contacts.

alternative

ConvertKit

focuses on creators and bloggers with simple forms, landing pages, and visual automation sequences. It excels at tagging and subscriber segmentation but lacks the drag and drop email builder that MailerLite users often enjoy.

Try ConvertKit →
alternative

Brevo

(formerly Sendinblue) combines email marketing with SMS campaigns and a simple CRM at competitive prices. Its automation builder is easy to use, but some users find its deliverability less consistent than MailerLite’s.

Try Brevo →
alternative

ActiveCampaign

is a powerhouse for advanced automation and deep customer journey mapping. It offers conditional content and predictive sending, yet its learning curve is steeper and the monthly cost is higher than MailerLite’s.

alternative

Flodesk

provides beautifully designed email templates and a flat rate pricing model. It is great for visual creators and online shops, but it lacks advanced automation rules and has fewer integrations than MailerLite.

Frequently asked questions

Is MailerLite still a good choice, or should I switch to an alternative today?
MailerLite remains an excellent option for small businesses and creators who want a straightforward tool at a low price. Only switch if you need more advanced automation, better template design, or a different pricing structure that fits your growing list.
Which alternative is best for someone who sends only a few emails per month?
Brevo and Mailchimp both have free or low cost plans that work well for low volume senders. Brevo charges based on emails sent rather than contacts, so it can be cheaper if you email infrequently.
