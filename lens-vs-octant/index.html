Lens vs Octant: which should you pick?

Kubernetes operators managing clusters need a clear view into their workloads. Two popular desktop dashboards, Lens and Octant, both aim to simplify this task. Lens is a full-featured IDE with built-in cluster monitoring, terminal access, and Helm support, while Octant is an open-source tool focused on visual exploration and resource relationships. This article compares Lens versus Octant to help you decide which fits your day-to-day operations better.

Where Lens wins

Lens gives you a richer out-of-the-box experience. Its interactive cluster overview shows CPU, memory, and network usage without extra plugins. You can open a terminal directly into any pod or container, which speeds up debugging. Lens also integrates Helm charts, so you can deploy and manage releases from the same interface. For operators who work across many clusters, Lens stores connection details and lets you switch instantly. The built-in Prometheus metrics (if Prometheus is running) give you real-time dashboards. Lens also offers a paid team edition with RBAC synchronization and shared configurations, which matters in larger organizations.

Where Octant wins

Octant is lighter and more modular. It is completely open-source and free, with no licensing tiers. Its strength lies in visualizing resource relationships: you can click on a pod and see its owner, services that target it, and related ConfigMaps in a graph view. Octant’s plugin system lets you write custom views or integrate external tools, so you can tailor the dashboard without waiting for upstream features. It also has a smart filter that hides resources not relevant to your current context. For operators who prefer a minimal interface and want to avoid vendor lock-in, Octant versus Lens is an easy choice. Octant also runs well on less powerful machines because it does not bundle a heavy metrics backend.

Verdict

Choose Lens if you want a comprehensive all-in-one dashboard that handles metrics, terminals, and Helm deployments without extra configuration. It is ideal for operators who manage many clusters and need quick access to logs and resource status. Choose Octant if you value openness, customization, and a lightweight tool that focuses on resource relationships and can be extended with plugins. Octant versus Lens comes down to preference: Lens gives you more power immediately, Octant gives you more control over your workflow. Both are excellent, so pick the one that matches how you already operate your clusters.
