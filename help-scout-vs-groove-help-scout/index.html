If you are a customer support manager evaluating a shared inbox tool, you have likely come across Help Scout and Groove. Both platforms are designed to help teams manage email based support without a traditional help desk. This article focuses on Help Scout as the primary choice and compares it against Groove, so you can decide which fits your team’s workflow and budget.

alternative

Groove

. Groove is a simpler, more affordable alternative that also provides a shared inbox and knowledge base. It focuses on ease of use and quick setup, making it popular with smaller teams that want to move away from generic email. Groove’s feature set is leaner than Help Scout’s, lacking native live chat and some advanced automation options.

How they compare

Help Scout and Groove both offer shared inboxes and knowledge bases, but they differ in depth and pricing. Help Scout provides more advanced features such as built in live chat, customizable workflows, and detailed reporting. Groove is more budget friendly and simpler to set up, but it lacks live chat and has fewer integration options. For teams that need a scalable solution with omnichannel support, Help Scout is the stronger choice. For very small teams on a tight budget that only need email and a knowledge base, Groove can be sufficient.

Frequently asked questions

Does Help Scout offer a free trial?
Yes, Help Scout offers a 15 day free trial with no credit card required. You can test all features including the shared inbox, knowledge base, and live chat during that period.
Which tool is better for a team with fewer than ten agents?
Both tools work well for small teams. Groove is more affordable and easier to learn, while Help Scout offers more growth potential with live chat and automation. If your team plans to expand, Help Scout is the better long term investment.
