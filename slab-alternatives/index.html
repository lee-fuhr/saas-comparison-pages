Best Slab alternatives

Slab is a popular knowledge base that helps support teams document processes, product info, and internal guides. But as your enterprise grows, you might face limits in Slab’s scalability, search speed, or integration depth. This article covers three strong alternatives – Confluence, Notion, and Guru – so you can find a tool that fits your support team’s needs for a centralized, easy-to-maintain knowledge base.

alternative

Confluence

. Confluence is a mature, enterprise-grade platform with powerful permission controls and deep integration with Jira and other Atlassian tools. It excels at creating structured documentation, especially for teams that already use Jira for ticket management, and it offers advanced search and analytics for support content.

alternative

Notion

. Notion combines knowledge base, wikis, and project management in a flexible workspace. It is ideal for teams that want to write documents, create databases, and collaborate in one place, though its permission model and performance can be less robust for very large enterprises with hundreds of support agents.

alternative

Guru

. Guru is built specifically for knowledge management and integrates directly with support tools like Zendesk, Intercom, and Salesforce. It uses AI-assisted verification to keep content fresh, and it surfaces knowledge inline in the tools your agents already use, reducing the need to switch between platforms.

How they compare

All three alternatives offer a central knowledge base for support teams, but they differ in focus. Confluence is best for organizations that need strong structure, version history, and tight integration with Jira. Notion offers more design freedom and flexibility, making it great for smaller to medium teams that want an all-in-one workspace. Guru prioritizes workflow integration and content freshness, making it the most natural fit for support teams that rely on tools like Zendesk and want to reduce context-switching. Your choice depends on your team size, existing tool stack, and how much control you need over permissions and content lifecycle.

Frequently asked questions

How do these alternatives handle permissions and access control for support teams?
Confluence offers granular permissions at the space, page, and group level, suitable for large enterprises with strict security needs. Notion has permission controls but can be more complex to manage at scale. Guru uses role-based access and integrates with single sign-on, making it straightforward for support teams to grant read-only access to agents and editor access to knowledge managers.
Can these tools integrate with our existing help desk software?
Yes. Confluence integrates with Jira Service Management and has plugins for Zendesk, Freshdesk, and others. Notion has native integrations with Zendesk, Intercom, and Slack via its API and available connectors. Guru offers deep, out-of-the-box integrations with Zendesk, Intercom, Salesforce, and many other support platforms, allowing agents to see knowledge panels directly inside their ticket view.
