Best Power BI alternatives

As a business analyst working in the Microsoft ecosystem, you may have hit the limits of the Power BI free tier – it restricts sharing, collaboration, and data refresh beyond a certain point. Fortunately, several open source and free tools can fill those gaps while still playing nicely with your existing workflows. This article covers four strong **power bi free tier alternatives** – Metabase, Redash, Apache Superset, and Tableau Public – to help you choose the right fit for your team.

alternative

Metabase

. Metabase is an easy-to-use open source analytics tool that lets you build dashboards and ask questions in plain English. It connects to SQL databases, Excel files, and cloud sources, making it a straightforward replacement for basic Power BI tasks. Its simple setup and built-in query builder work well for analysts who want a low-code experience.

alternative

Redash

. Redash specializes in query-based dashboards and visualizations, especially for teams that already write SQL. It supports a wide range of data sources and allows scheduled refreshes, which the Power BI free tier limits. You can share dashboards publicly or with a small group, though it lacks the deep AI features of Power BI.

alternative

Apache Superset

. Apache Superset is a feature-rich, cloud-native BI tool with a modern interface and strong support for complex visualizations. It handles large datasets and offers an SQL Lab for ad-hoc queries, plus role-based permissions. It is more powerful than the free tier of Power BI for self-hosted analytics, but requires some technical setup.

alternative

Tableau Public

. Tableau Public is a completely free version of Tableau that is great for creating and sharing interactive visualizations online. However, all dashboards are public by default, which limits its use for internal business data. It is ideal for portfolio building or public-facing reports, but not for confidential company metrics.
