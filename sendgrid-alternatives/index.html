Best SendGrid alternatives

If you are using SendGrid for transactional email and are exploring other options, you are not alone. Many developers and SaaS teams look for alternatives due to pricing changes, deliverability issues, or specific features like dedicated IPs or advanced analytics. This article covers five reliable alternatives to SendGrid: Postmark, Mailgun, Amazon SES, Brevo, and Resend. Each has different strengths, so the right choice depends on your volume, budget, and technical requirements.

alternative

Postmark

is built specifically for transactional email and focuses on speed and deliverability. It offers a simple API, detailed bounce handling, and a generous free tier for low volumes. Postmark does not support marketing or bulk email, which makes it a clean choice if you only need confirmation emails, password resets, and receipts.

Try Postmark →
alternative

Mailgun

provides a flexible API with tools for both transactional and bulk email, plus advanced analytics and email validation. It works well for teams that need to send high volumes and want granular control over delivery, including custom SMTP settings and IP pools. Mailgun’s pricing can become expensive at higher volumes, so check your expected send rate.

Try Mailgun →
alternative

Amazon SES

is a cost effective option that scales to any volume and integrates natively with other AWS services. It has a steep learning curve for configuration, but once set up, it offers excellent deliverability and low per email pricing. SES requires manual handling of bounces and complaints, so you may need to build additional logic or use a wrapper library.

alternative

Brevo

(formerly Sendinblue) combines transactional email with marketing features like SMS, chat, and CRM. It has a straightforward API and a free plan that includes up to 300 emails per day. Brevo is a good fit for SaaS teams that want a single platform for both triggered messages and campaigns, but its transactional deliverability may not match dedicated providers like Postmark.

alternative

Resend

is a newer provider that focuses on a developer friendly API, SDKs for popular languages, and built in tracking for opens and clicks. It offers a generous free tier and simple pricing per email sent. Resend is ideal for startups and small teams that want modern tooling and quick setup, but it has a smaller support community than more established options.

Frequently asked questions

How do I migrate from SendGrid to another provider without losing emails?
Start by signing up for the new service and generating API keys. Update your application code to use the new provider’s SDK or SMTP settings, and run a test in a staging environment. Then gradually shift traffic by updating environment variables or feature flags. Most providers offer import tools for suppression lists and templates.
Which SendGrid alternative is best for high volume transactional email on a budget?
Amazon SES is the most cost effective option for high volumes, often costing less than $0.10 per 1,000 emails. However, you need to invest time in setup and monitoring. If you prefer a managed solution with simpler pricing, Mailgun and Brevo offer competitive rates for medium volumes.
