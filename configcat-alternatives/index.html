ConfigCat is a solid choice for feature flags, but small teams often need something simpler, cheaper, or more open. If you find ConfigCat’s pricing or feature set doesn’t quite fit your workflow, several alternatives can give you the same control without extra complexity. This article covers four options built for teams that want straightforward flag management, not a full enterprise platform.

alternative

LaunchDarkly

. This is the most mature feature flag tool, but it comes with enterprise pricing and a steep learning curve. For a small team, the free tier supports up to two users, and paid plans start at around $80 per month, which may be overkill if you just need basic toggles.

alternative

Split.io

. Split focuses on experimentation and A/B testing, not just flag management. Its free tier includes five users and unlimited flags, but the platform can feel heavy if your only need is to roll features on and off for a small codebase.

alternative

GrowthBook

. This open source option gives you self-hosted control and a generous free cloud tier with three users and unlimited flags. It is designed for technical teams that want to run their own experiments without paying per seat or per flag.

alternative

Unleash

. Another open source tool, Unleash offers a simple API and a free cloud tier with up to five users and 10k monthly requests. It is well suited for small teams that need a lightweight toggle system and prefer to self-host for privacy.

How they compare

All four alternatives offer free tiers or open source versions, which is ideal for small teams. LaunchDarkly and Split.io are more expensive and targeted at larger organizations with experimentation needs. GrowthBook and Unleash are leaner, self-hostable, and focus on core flag toggling with minimal overhead. Choose GrowthBook if you want built-in A/B testing on a budget, or Unleash if you prefer a pure flag system with simple role management.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is easiest to migrate from ConfigCat?
Unleash and GrowthBook both provide import tools or API-driven workflows that make switching straightforward. Check each tool’s documentation for a migration guide, as most handle flag keys and targeting rules in a similar way.
Can I use these tools for free with a small team?
Yes. GrowthBook offers a free cloud plan for up to three users. Unleash’s free cloud plan covers up to five users and 10k monthly requests. LaunchDarkly and Split.io also have free tiers but with tighter user or flag limits, so read their pricing pages carefully based on your team size.
