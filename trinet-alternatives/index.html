If you are evaluating a TriNet alternative for your company’s PEO and employee benefits needs, you likely want a partner that can handle payroll, compliance, and benefits administration without the complexity or cost that sometimes comes with TriNet. Many businesses find that other providers offer better pricing, more intuitive platforms, or services tailored to smaller teams. Below we compare four popular alternatives: Gusto, Justworks, Paylocity, and ADP. Each has strengths depending on your company size, industry, and benefit priorities.

alternative

Gusto

. Gusto is an all-in-one HR platform designed mainly for small to midsize businesses. It offers payroll, benefits administration (including health insurance and 401(k)), and basic HR compliance tools. Gusto keeps onboarding simple and provides self-service employee portals but may lack the deep advisory support some companies expect from a full-service PEO.

alternative

Justworks

. Justworks is a true PEO that provides co-employment, meaning it helps you manage payroll, benefits, and HR compliance while sharing legal liability. It is known for transparent pricing and strong benefits packages, including access to insurance carriers typically reserved for larger companies. Justworks works best for startups and scaling businesses that need a streamlined PEO experience.

alternative

Paylocity

. Paylocity is a cloud-based payroll and HR software that also offers a broader HCM suite with talent management, time tracking, and analytics. While it is not a PEO in the traditional sense, it provides robust benefits administration and compliance features. Paylocity is a strong choice for companies that want more control and customization rather than a co-employment model.

alternative

ADP

. ADP offers multiple service tiers, from basic payroll (ADP Run) to full PEO services (ADP TotalSource). It has decades of experience, extensive benefits networks, and dedicated support for compliance and risk management. ADP can handle companies of nearly any size, but its pricing and contract terms can be more complex than newer providers.

How they compare

Choosing between these options depends on whether you need a full PEO (co-employment) or just HR software with benefits administration. Gusto and Justworks are simpler for small teams, while Paylocity and ADP offer more flexibility for growing or larger organizations. Consider your budget, desired level of HR support, and the specific benefits you want to offer employees. Gusto and Justworks are more user-friendly with transparent pricing, whereas ADP and Paylocity provide deeper customization but may require more setup.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between a PEO like Justworks and an HR platform like Gusto?
A PEO like Justworks enters a co-employment arrangement with your company, meaning it shares legal responsibility for payroll taxes, worker’s comp, and compliance. This can help you access better benefits rates and reduce HR liability. An HR platform like Gusto does not take on that co-employment role; it provides software tools for payroll and benefits but does not assume shared employer liability.
Can a company switch from TriNet to any of these alternatives midyear?
Yes, you can switch midyear, but you should plan carefully. Benefits plans and payroll calendars often require a few weeks of advance notice. You will need to terminate your contract with TriNet, transfer employee data, and set up new benefits enrollment. Most providers offer onboarding support to make the transition smoother. Be sure to compare cancellation fees and any timeline restrictions from TriNet before moving.
