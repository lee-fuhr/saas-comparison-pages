If you manage payroll for a large enterprise, you know ADP is a giant in the space, but its user experience can feel clunky and outdated. Many payroll managers find the interface overloaded, requiring extra clicks and training for tasks that should be straightforward. Fortunately, there are modern alternatives that prioritize cleaner design, faster workflows, and better employee self-service. This article highlights three strong contenders: Rippling, Gusto, and Paychex, each offering a different take on improving your day-to-day experience.

alternative

Rippling

. Built for companies that want a unified platform, Rippling connects payroll with IT, HR, and device management in one sleek interface. Its automation features reduce manual data entry, and the user dashboard is designed to let you run payroll in just a few clicks. Enterprise managers appreciate the granular permission controls and real-time syncing with other business systems.

alternative

Gusto

. While Gusto has traditionally served small to medium businesses, its newer enterprise tier brings a much cleaner, more intuitive payroll flow than many older platforms. The team management and benefits administration sections are particularly well organized, making it easy for managers to oversee changes without digging through menus. Gusto also offers excellent employee onboarding and PTO tracking built right into the payroll process.

alternative

Paychex

. Paychex has modernized its interface in recent years, offering a robust but streamlined dashboard that rivals ADP’s depth of features. Its payroll processing wizards guide you step by step, reducing errors for complex compensation structures like commissions and bonuses. For large organizations with multi-state compliance needs, Paychex keeps reporting clear and accessible without the clutter.

How they compare

Rippling leads for automation and system integration, Gusto excels in simplicity and employee experience, and Paychex balances feature depth with a cleaner interface. All three outperform ADP on user experience in key areas like payroll setup, employee self-service, and reporting clarity. Your choice depends on whether you value all-in-one integration (Rippling), ease of use (Gusto), or proven enterprise compliance (Paychex). Each offers a free trial or demo so you can test the UX firsthand.
