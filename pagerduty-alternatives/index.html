Best PagerDuty alternatives

PagerDuty is a popular incident management platform, but it may not fit every team’s budget, workflow, or feature needs. On-call engineering teams often look for alternatives that offer better pricing, simpler setup, or tighter integrations with their existing stack. Below we compare five strong contenders: OpsGenie, Better Stack, Spike.sh, Grafana OnCall, and Rootly. Each tool has its own strengths, so the best choice depends on your team size, alert volume, and preferred incident response process.

alternative

OpsGenie

is a mature alternative that focuses on alert routing, on-call scheduling, and escalation policies. It integrates with hundreds of monitoring tools and offers a robust mobile app for acknowledging and resolving alerts. Teams that need fine-grained control over notification rules and seniority-based schedules often choose OpsGenie over PagerDuty for its simpler pricing by active user rather than by action.

alternative

Better Stack

(formerly Better Uptime) combines uptime monitoring and incident management in one product. It automatically captures heartbeat checks, SSL expiry alerts, and status pages, then routes incidents to on-call teams via phone, SMS, or push notifications. Its clean interface and transparent pricing make it a good fit for small to medium engineering teams that want a single pane of glass for monitoring and on-call.

Try Better Stack →
alternative

Spike.sh

is a lightweight incident management tool designed for lean teams. It offers automated escalation, live call routing, and a shared incident timeline without requiring a dedicated DevOps engineer to configure it. Spike.sh is especially popular with startups because it has a generous free tier and charges only per incident response, not per user.

Try Spike.sh →
alternative

Grafana OnCall

is an open-source inspired incident management solution that integrates natively with the Grafana observability stack. It provides flexible on-call scheduling, alert grouping, and escalation chains, and it can be self-hosted or used as a cloud service. Teams already using Grafana dashboards and alerts find OnCall a natural extension, and it avoids vendor lock-in with its open source core.

alternative

Rootly

is a modern incident management platform built around Slack-first workflows. It automates incident creation from alerts, runs postmortems, and generates timeline reports without leaving Slack. Rootly targets teams that want to streamline their incident response process and reduce context switching, especially in fast-paced DevOps environments.

How they compare

- **Pricing models**: OpsGenie and Better Stack charge per user or per team, while Spike.sh charges per incident. Grafana OnCall follows a per-host or per-user model, and Rootly is per user with a focus on Slack usage. PagerDuty traditionally charges by action and user limits. - **On-call scheduling**: OpsGenie and Grafana OnCall offer the most advanced calendars and escalation policies. Spike.sh and Better Stack keep scheduling simple, and Rootly relies on integrations with calendar tools. - **Integration depth**: OpsGenie and Grafana OnCall integrate with the widest range of monitoring tools. Better Stack includes built-in monitoring, while Spike.sh and Rootly work best with major alert sources like Prometheus and Datadog. - **Best for**: OpsGenie for large teams with complex routing, Better Stack for combined monitoring and on-call, Spike.sh for small budget-conscious teams, Grafana OnCall for Grafana users, and Rootly for Slack-centric workflows.
