G2 Buyer Intent helps companies identify prospects who are actively researching software on the G2 platform. However, you may need alternatives if you want data from sources beyond review sites, better coverage for specific industries, or different pricing models. Bombora, TechTarget Priority Engine, and Demandbase ABM each offer unique approaches to tracking purchase intent. This article compares them so you can decide which fits your goals.

alternative

Bombora

focuses on content consumption across a large network of B2B publishers. It uses a “surge score” to show which topics a company is researching, making it useful for identifying accounts that are actively looking into tools like yours.

alternative

TechTarget Priority Engine

specializes in intent data from its network of technology-focused media sites and its “IT Deal Alert” signals. It provides granular insights into specific products and buying stages, which helps sales teams prioritize accounts that are evaluating similar technologies.

alternative

Demandbase ABM

combines intent data with account-based marketing capabilities. It gathers signals from multiple sources, including its own ad network and third party partners, and then layers firmographic and technographic data to create a unified view of prospect behavior.

How they compare

Bombora is strong for topic-level intent from a broad publisher network, but it does not tie signals to specific products as tightly as TechTarget does. TechTarget Priority Engine excels for deep technology purchase intent, especially when your target audience reads tech media. Demandbase ABM offers a more complete ABM platform, integrating intent with advertising and personalization tools. The choice depends on whether you need review-based intent (G2), content consumption intent (Bombora), tech-specific intent (TechTarget), or a full ABM stack (Demandbase). None of these alternatives replace G2’s direct review platform data, so consider combining them for a fuller picture.

Frequently asked questions

How does Bombora’s intent data differ from G2 Buyer Intent?
Bombora measures intent by tracking content consumption across many business websites, not just review platforms. G2 Buyer Intent captures behavior specifically on the G2 review site, such as product comparisons and pricing page visits. Bombora gives a broader view of topic interest, while G2 focuses on active software evaluation within its community.
Can I use TechTarget Priority Engine alone without G2 Buyer Intent?
Yes, but you may miss signals from users who research on review sites rather than tech media. TechTarget Priority Engine works best for accounts that read specialized technology content. Combining both data sources can improve coverage of the full buyer journey, especially for early stage research versus late stage validation.
