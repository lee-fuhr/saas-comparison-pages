Best Todoist alternatives

If you have been using Todoist for personal task management but feel it is missing something, you are not alone. Many productivity enthusiasts look for alternatives that better match their workflow, platform preferences, or motivation style. The good news is that several strong options exist, each with a different take on getting things done. Below we compare five popular Todoist alternatives: Things, OmniFocus, TickTick, Microsoft To Do, and Habitica.

alternative

Things

is a polished macOS and iOS app that uses a structured GTD approach. It excels at breaking projects into actionable steps and offers a clean, distraction-free interface. However, it lacks native Windows or Android support, which limits its appeal to users fully inside the Apple ecosystem.

alternative

OmniFocus

is a powerful GTD tool built for deep organization and custom perspectives. It gives you full control over contexts, forecasts, and reviews, making it ideal for complex projects. The tradeoff is a steeper learning curve and a higher price tag, plus it is available only on Apple devices.

alternative

TickTick

balances simplicity with features like built-in Pomodoro timers, habit tracking, and a calendar view. It supports natural language input similar to Todoist and works across all major platforms. Its free tier is generous, but some users find the interface busier than Todoist.

Try TickTick →
alternative

Microsoft To Do

is a lightweight, free app that integrates seamlessly with Outlook and Microsoft 365. It focuses on daily task lists with reminders and simple list sharing. It lacks advanced project features like nested subtasks or labels, so it suits users who want a no-fuss tool.

alternative

Habitica

turns task management into an RPG where you earn points and level up by completing real tasks. It adds gamification layers like quests and social guilds to keep you motivated. It is less suited for complex project planning and more for habit building and daily checklists.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is closest to Todoist in terms of features and cross-platform support?
TickTick is the most similar. It offers natural language input, tags, filters, a calendar view, and works on every major platform. Its free tier is also very capable.
I want to build better habits, not just manage projects. Which tool should I choose?
Habitica is designed specifically for habit building and motivation through gamification. If you prefer a more traditional approach, TickTick also includes a habit tracker and a Pomodoro timer within its task management system.
