Best Conga CLM alternatives

If your procurement team relies on Conga CLM but finds its configuration options too rigid or its costs too high, you are not alone. Many teams need a contract lifecycle management platform that adapts to their specific workflows, approval chains, and clause libraries without heavy customization fees. This article compares five configurable alternatives: Ironclad, DocuSign CLM, Icertis, Agiloft, and Concord. Each tool offers different strengths for procurement, from out-of-the-box templates to deep enterprise integration.

alternative

Ironclad

. Ironclad focuses on workflow automation and ease of use, allowing procurement teams to build approval sequences and contract templates without coding. Its click-based editor makes it a strong choice for midmarket teams that want fast deployment and a modern user interface. However, its configuration depth may fall short for very complex enterprise agreements.

alternative

DocuSign CLM

. Powered by the DocuSign eSignature ecosystem, this platform excels at streamlining signature workflows and document generation. Procurement teams can create custom fields and rule-based approvals, and it integrates tightly with Salesforce and other CRM systems. Its configuration options are robust but often require professional services for advanced setups.

alternative

Icertis

. Icertis is built for large enterprises with highly complex contract structures and global compliance needs. Its object-based configuration lets procurement teams define custom contract types, clauses, and obligations with granular control. This power comes with a steeper learning curve and higher cost, making it best suited for organizations with dedicated CLM administrators.

alternative

Agiloft

. Agiloft is a low-code platform that gives procurement teams full control over contract data fields, approval routing, and clause libraries through a point-and-click interface. It offers strong flexibility for businesses that want to tailor the system to their exact procurement processes without relying on external developers. The trade-off is a less polished user experience compared to consumer-grade tools.

alternative

Concord

. Concord provides a balance between simplicity and configurability, with drag-and-drop contract templates and customizable approval chains. It is particularly strong for small to midsize procurement teams that need quick setup and transparent pricing. Its configuration options are limited compared to Agiloft or Icertis, but it covers most standard procurement use cases.
