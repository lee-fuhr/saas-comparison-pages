Plausible vs Fathom: which should you pick?

If you run a privacy-focused website, you already know that traditional analytics tools like Google Analytics are out of the question. You need a lightweight, cookieless tracker that respects visitor privacy while still giving you the data you need. Two of the most popular options in this space are Plausible and Fathom. Both are simple, fast, and GDPR-compliant by default, but they take slightly different approaches to design, pricing, and features. This article breaks down the Plausible versus Fathom debate so you can decide which one fits your site best.

Where Plausible wins

**Open-source transparency.** Plausible’s entire codebase is open source. You can inspect every line, self-host the software if you have the technical skills, or trust that the hosted version is what the code says it is. For privacy-conscious owners who want full control, this is a strong advantage.

**More detailed visitor data.** Plausible gives you a richer set of metrics out of the box. You can see entry pages, exit pages, referral source details, and goal funnels. It also breaks down custom events and properties more granularly than Fathom. If you need to track specific user actions (like form submissions or button clicks) beyond simple pageviews, Plausible handles that with less friction.

**Free tier for small sites.** Plausible offers a free plan (currently up to a certain number of monthly pageviews). For tiny personal blogs or experimental projects, this can be the deciding factor versus Fathom, which has no free option.

**Community and plugin ecosystem.** Because Plausible is open source, there are community-built integrations for WordPress, Ghost, and other platforms. You can also use third-party tools to tweak dashboards or export data in more flexible ways.

Where Fathom wins

**Speed and simplicity.** Fathom is famously lean. Its dashboard loads instantly, and the tracking script is smaller than Plausible’s. On slow connections or for very high-traffic sites, every kilobyte counts. Fathom also omits optional features (like custom events or detailed referrals) by default, keeping the interface clean and one-click accessible for owners who just want daily pageviews and top sources.

**Better out-of-the-box privacy posture.** Fathom was designed from the ground up with no session recording, no cookie banners, and no personal data of any kind. While Plausible also claims these traits, Fathom’s hard line means you literally cannot accidentally collect an IP address or fingerprint. For owners who want zero legal ambiguity, Fathom is the safer bet.

**Easier billing and flat pricing.** Fathom uses a simple per-site price that scales predictably. There are no per-pageview caps that surprise you mid-month. If your traffic spikes, you do not risk overage fees or throttling. Plausible’s pricing is usage-based, which can be cheaper for small sites but more unpredictable for volatile ones.

**Migration tooling.** Fathom provides a one-click import from Plausible (and vice versa, less seamlessly). But Fathom’s own migration guides are often praised for being straightforward, especially if you are coming from Google Analytics or Matomo.

Verdict

Choosing between Plausible versus Fathom comes down to your priorities. If you want open-source control, deeper event tracking, and a free starting point, go with Plausible. If you need the lightest possible tracking, flat-rate pricing, and a no-fuss privacy guarantee that is impossible to accidentally compromise, Fathom is the better match. Both tools are excellent for privacy-conscious website owners. Either one will keep your visitors safe and your analytics simple. Evaluate your own needs around data depth versus simplicity, and you will know which one to pick.
