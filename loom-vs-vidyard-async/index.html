Loom and Vidyard are two of the most popular tools for asynchronous screen recordings. Teams use them to share quick updates, walk through demos, or explain complex ideas without scheduling a live meeting. Both platforms let you record your screen, camera, or both and share the video with a link. But their free plans, features, and pricing differ in ways that matter for team workflows. If neither tool fits your needs, there are several alternatives worth exploring.

alternative

Tella

. Tella is a lightweight screen recorder that keeps things simple. It does not require an account to record, and you can trim videos right inside the browser. Tella works best for individuals who want a fast, no-frills way to capture and share a clip.

alternative

Claap

. Claap combines async video with a built-in note taking and task management layer. It allows viewers to comment and react at specific timestamps, making it good for team feedback loops. The tool also integrates with project management apps like Notion and Asana.

alternative

Jumpshare

. Jumpshare offers both screen recording and file sharing in one platform. Videos can be trimmed, annotated, and set to expire after a chosen time. It also supports links, images, and documents, so it doubles as a general file host.

alternative

CloudApp

. CloudApp focuses on quick visual communication with screen captures and webcam overlays. It includes analytics that show who watched your video and for how long. The free plan is limited to 25 recordings and 5 minutes per video.

alternative

Soapbox

. Soapbox by Wistia is built for marketing and sales teams that need polished, branded videos. It lets you edit out mistakes, add calls to action, and track viewer engagement in detail. The free tier includes unlimited recordings but adds a watermark.

How they compare

Loom and Vidyard both offer browser and desktop recording with instant sharing links. Here is how they compare for async team communication.

- **Free plan limits**: Loom caps your library at 25 videos on the free plan, each up to 5 minutes long. Vidyard’s free plan gives you unlimited recordings up to 30 minutes each but adds a Vidyard watermark and limits viewer analytics. - **Editing**: Loom lets you trim the start and end of a video after recording. Vidyard allows basic trimming and also lets you remove the beginning or end in the web editor. - **Integrations**: Loom plugs into Slack, Google Workspace, and many project tools. Vidyard integrates with CRM systems like Salesforce, plus Slack and email platforms. - **Analytics**: Vidyard’s free plan shows only basic view counts. Loom’s free plan shows who watched and for how long but limits the number of videos that get that data. Paid plans unlock deeper insights on both platforms. - **Pricing**: Loom’s Business plan costs about $12.50 per creator per month. Vidyard’s Pro plan starts around $15 per user per month, with team plans available.

Choose Loom if you need a simple, well-known tool with strong free tier analytics. Choose Vidyard if you need longer recordings, unlimited storage, or deeper CRM integrations.

Frequently asked questions

Which tool is better for a small team on a tight budget?
Vidyard’s free plan offers unlimited recordings, so you can record as many videos as you need without paying. However, the videos include a watermark. Loom’s free plan only stores 25 videos, so you have to delete old ones to make room. If watermark branding is acceptable, Vidyard is the more budget-friendly choice.
Can I use these tools to record internal training videos asynchronously?
Yes, both Loom and Vidyard work well for internal async communication. You can record a walkthrough or announcement, share the link in Slack or email, and viewers can watch on their own time. For training content, Vidyard’s longer recording limit (30 minutes on free) is helpful, while Loom’s shorter limit (5 minutes) may require splitting content into multiple clips.
