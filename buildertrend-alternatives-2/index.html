Best Buildertrend alternatives

If you are a home-builder looking to improve how you share project updates with clients, Buildertrend is a popular choice. It offers scheduling, financials, and a client portal. But maybe you want a different focus on communication, lower cost, or deeper integration with design tools. This overview compares four alternatives: Procore, PlanGrid, Autodesk BIM 360, and CoConstruct. Each has strengths for client communication, but the right fit depends on your team size, project complexity, and whether you prioritize simple messaging or full construction management.

alternative

Procore

. Procore is a robust construction management platform built for large commercial projects, but home-builders can use its client communication tools too. Its “Project Home” dashboard lets clients see photos, drawings, and daily logs, and the RFI and submittal workflows are thorough. However, Procore’s pricing and complexity may feel heavy for smaller residential builders who just need a straightforward client portal.

alternative

PlanGrid

. PlanGrid started as a blueprint and mark-up tool, and it still excels at sharing up-to-date drawings with clients. You can attach photos, notes, and version history directly on plans, which makes it easy to explain changes. Its communication features are leaner than Buildertrend’s, but if your main need is drawing-based collaboration with clients, PlanGrid is a focused alternative.

alternative

Autodesk BIM 360

. BIM 360 is a cloud platform for model-based collaboration, often used on larger builds with architects and engineers. Its client portal can give stakeholders access to 3D models, issue tracking, and document control. For a home-builder who regularly uses BIM or works with design teams, this can streamline communication, but it may be overkill if you only need simple progress updates.

alternative

CoConstruct

. CoConstruct is purpose-built for custom home-builders and remodelers, so its client portal feels very natural for this audience. It includes a client dashboard with to-do lists, selections tracking, and a message feed. Compared to Buildertrend, CoConstruct often has a simpler interface and lower starting price, which makes it a strong direct alternative for client communication.

How they compare

All four alternatives offer client communication portals, but they differ in scope. Procore and BIM 360 are best for large, document-heavy teams that need complex workflows. PlanGrid shines when drawings are the center of communication. CoConstruct most closely mirrors Buildertrend’s feature set for home-builders, with a client portal that handles selections and daily updates in plain language. If your priority is a straightforward, builder-friendly client portal without commercial project overhead, CoConstruct or PlanGrid are worth a close look.
