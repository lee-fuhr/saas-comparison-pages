CoSchedule is a popular tool for content marketing, but it may not fit every team’s budget or workflow. If you need an editorial calendar that helps you plan, assign, and track content, there are several solid alternatives. This article covers three top options: Trello, Asana, and Monday.com. Each offers unique features for content teams.

alternative

Trello

uses a card-based system that makes it easy to visualize your content pipeline. It is simple to set up and works well for small teams that want a low-cost tool. You can add due dates, checklists, and labels to keep your editorial calendar organized.

alternative

Asana

provides more structure with tasks, subtasks, and timelines. It is a good choice for teams that need to manage complex workflows and multiple content projects. Asana also has a calendar view that shows your content deadlines at a glance.

alternative

Monday.com

offers a highly visual interface with customizable boards, columns, and automations. It works well for content teams that want to track every stage of production from idea to publication. Monday.com also integrates with many other tools like Slack and Google Drive.

How they compare

Trello is the simplest and most affordable option, but it lacks advanced features like time tracking and reporting. Asana offers stronger project management tools and is ideal for larger teams with many moving parts. Monday.com is the most customizable, making it a good fit for teams that want to tailor their workflow . All three tools can serve as editorial calendars, but none include native social media scheduling like CoSchedule does.

Frequently asked questions

Can Trello, Asana, or Monday.com replace the social media scheduling features of CoSchedule?
Not directly. These tools are project management platforms first. They do not let you schedule and publish posts to social networks. However, you can integrate them with social media scheduling tools like Buffer or Hootsuite to fill that gap.
Which alternative is best for a small content team on a tight budget?
Trello is the most budget-friendly option. Its free plan includes many features that cover basic editorial calendar needs. If your team grows or needs more structure, Asana’s free tier also works well for small groups.
