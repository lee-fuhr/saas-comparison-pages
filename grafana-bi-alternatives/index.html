Best Grafana alternatives

Grafana is a powerful tool for monitoring and visualizing real-time metrics, but it is not always the best fit for every scenario. Monitoring teams sometimes need broader business intelligence (BI) capabilities, such as ad hoc querying, interactive dashboards for non-technical stakeholders, or deeper integration with relational databases. This article explores four alternatives – Metabase, Redash, Apache Superset, and Power BI – and compares them against Grafana to help you choose the right tool for your team.

alternative

Metabase

is a user-friendly BI tool that lets anyone on your team ask questions in plain English and get visual answers. It connects to many SQL databases and focuses on simplicity, making it a good choice for teams that want to share metrics without learning a query language. Unlike Grafana, Metabase excels at ad hoc exploratory analysis but lacks native time-series alerting and real-time streaming support.

alternative

Redash

is an open source BI platform designed for collaborative querying and visualization. It supports a wide range of data sources, including SQL databases, NoSQL, and cloud services, and allows you to schedule refreshes and create alert-based dashboards. Redash is more flexible than Grafana for SQL-heavy workflows but does not offer Grafana’s robust alerting rules, annotation support, or multi-cloud data source aggregation for metrics.

alternative

Apache Superset

is a modern, enterprise-grade BI tool with a rich set of visualizations and a powerful SQL editor. It handles large datasets well and supports both ad hoc queries and curated dashboards, making it a strong candidate for teams that need a single platform for metrics and business reporting. Compared to Grafana, Superset has a steeper learning curve and fewer out-of-the-box integrations with time-series databases like Prometheus or InfluxDB.

alternative

Power BI

is a commercial BI solution from Microsoft that offers deep integration with Azure, Excel, and other Microsoft services. It provides advanced analytics, natural language queries, and sharing capabilities across an organization. For monitoring teams already invested in the Microsoft ecosystem, Power BI can complement Grafana for reporting and executive dashboards, but it is less suited for real-time monitoring and does not natively support many observability data sources.
