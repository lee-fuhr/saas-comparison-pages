If your organization relies on EdCast for learning experience and content curation, you may be evaluating other platforms that better fit your evolving needs. As a VP of L&D at a technology company, you need a solution that aggregates both internal and external content, curates personalized learning paths, and scales across a fast-moving workforce. This article compares three strong alternatives: Percipio, Degreed, and 360Learning.

alternative

Percipio

offers a content-first approach with a deep library of curated courses, books, and videos from Skillsoft. It is ideal if your priority is ready-to-use, high-quality external content that requires minimal curation effort. Percipio’s AI-driven recommendations help surface relevant learning quickly, though its ability to integrate proprietary internal content is more limited than some competitors.

How they compare

When comparing EdCast alternatives, consider your primary need: Percipio is best for turnkey external content at scale, Degreed excels at aggregating diverse sources with skill alignment, and 360Learning is ideal for driving internal content co-creation and collaboration. None offer a perfect one-to-one replacement for EdCast’s combination of curation and AI, but each brings distinct strengths for technology L&D teams.

Frequently asked questions

Which platform is best for integrating with our existing tech stack and HR systems?
Degreed typically offers the strongest integration options with common HRIS and talent management systems, making it a good choice if seamless data flow is critical. Percipio and 360Learning also support standard integrations, but Degreed’s APIs and pre-built connectors are more extensive for enterprise environments.
How do these platforms handle content curation without overwhelming learners?
All three use AI and user behavior to recommend relevant content, but they differ in control. Degreed allows administrators to set curated channels and learning paths, while Percipio relies heavily on its own algorithms. 360Learning gives more power to teams and managers to curate content collaboratively, which can reduce noise in smaller or more specialized groups.
