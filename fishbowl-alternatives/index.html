If your e-commerce business has outgrown Fishbowl or you are finding its interface and complexity a bottleneck, you have strong alternatives. Fishbowl is a capable inventory and manufacturing system, but it can feel heavy for fast moving online stores, especially when integrating with channels like Shopify or Amazon. The right alternative should simplify order management, reduce manual data entry, and scale with your sales without requiring a dedicated IT team.

alternative

Cin7

is a cloud based inventory and order management platform built for multichannel sellers. It connects directly with major e-commerce platforms, marketplaces, and accounting software, giving you real time stock visibility across all sales channels. Cin7 is a strong choice if you need barcode scanning, kitting, and multi warehouse support in one system.

alternative

inFlow

focuses on small to medium e-commerce businesses that want an affordable, easy to set up inventory system. It offers barcode scanning, purchase orders, and basic manufacturing features, and it integrates with Amazon, eBay, and Shopify. inFlow is a good fit if you need a straightforward upgrade from spreadsheets without a steep learning curve.

alternative

Sortly

takes a visual, mobile first approach to inventory management, ideal for teams that do not need heavy ERP features. You can photograph items, organize them in folders, and track quantities with a simple drag and drop interface. Sortly works best for smaller e-commerce operations or businesses that manage physical inventory across multiple locations with minimal order processing needs.

alternative

Linnworks

is a dedicated multichannel order management and fulfillment platform. It centralizes orders from dozens of sales channels and automates tasks like inventory sync, shipping label generation, and repricing. Linnworks excels for high volume e-commerce operations where speed and automation are more important than manufacturing or accounting modules.

alternative

SkuVault

offers inventory and warehouse management features tailored for e-commerce and 3PL fulfillment. It includes cycle counting, pick pack ship workflows, and direct integrations with major carts and marketplaces. SkuVault is a solid alternative if you need strong warehouse process control along with multichannel selling capabilities.

How they compare

Cin7 and Linnworks are the most powerful for high volume multichannel sellers, but they come with higher price points and setup complexity. inFlow offers the best balance of features and affordability for growing businesses, while Sortly is the simplest option for very small teams that prioritize visual inventory over order processing. SkuVault sits between inFlow and Cin7, giving you warehouse focused tools without the full ERP weight. All five alternatives provide cloud access and barcode scanning, but their integrations, automation depth, and pricing vary significantly.

Frequently asked questions

Which Fishbowl alternative is easiest to migrate to from Fishbowl?
inFlow and SkuVault both offer import templates and support for transferring item lists, stock counts, and order history. Cin7 also provides a dedicated migration service, but the transition can be more complex due to its broader feature set. Plan for a test run with a sample of your data before switching.
Do these alternatives integrate with my existing accounting software?
Yes, most do. Cin7 has native connectors for QuickBooks and Xero. inFlow integrates with QuickBooks, Xero, and others. SkuVault and Linnworks link with QuickBooks through third party middleware. Sortly does not offer direct accounting integrations, so you would need to export data manually. Always verify the specific version of your accounting software with the tool’s integration page.
