If you run a small business and need cloud-based accounting with easy bank reconciliation, Xero is a popular choice. But it may not fit every budget or workflow. Whether you find Xero too expensive, too complex, or just want to compare options, several strong alternatives offer automatic bank feeds, simple reconciliation, and similar cloud convenience. Below we cover three top contenders: QuickBooks, FreshBooks, and Wave.

alternative

QuickBooks

. QuickBooks Online is a direct competitor to Xero with robust bank reconciliation features. It connects to thousands of financial institutions, imports transactions automatically, and lets you match them with a few clicks. Many businesses choose QuickBooks for its advanced reporting and strong inventory tracking, though it can become pricey as you add users or features.

alternative

FreshBooks

. FreshBooks is built for service-based businesses and freelancers who need straightforward bank reconciliation. It automatically pulls in bank transactions and matches them to invoices or expenses, saving time on data entry. Its clean interface and built-in time tracking make it a great choice if you bill by the hour, but it offers less depth for product-based businesses.

Try FreshBooks →
alternative

Wave

. Wave is a completely free accounting platform that still provides automatic bank feeds and easy reconciliation. You can connect your bank accounts, have transactions flow in daily, and categorize them with simple rules. While Wave lacks advanced features like payroll (available for a fee) or robust inventory, it is perfect for very small businesses or startups that want to keep costs low.

Frequently asked questions

Which Xero alternative is best for a freelancer who only needs basic accounting?
FreshBooks is a strong choice because its reconciliation is designed around invoices and time tracking, and its interface is simple. Wave is also great if you want a free option, but it lacks time tracking built in.
Can I import my Xero data into QuickBooks or Wave?
Yes, you can export your data from Xero as CSV files and then import them into QuickBooks Online or Wave. Both platforms support CSV imports for chart of accounts, customers, vendors, and transactions. However, you may need to clean up the data to match the new system’s format.
