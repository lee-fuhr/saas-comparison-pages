Best 360Learning alternatives

360Learning is well known for its collaborative, cohort based approach to training. It lets teams build courses together and learn in groups with discussion and peer feedback. But if you are evaluating other options that also support cohort based learning, several platforms offer similar strengths. Here we look at five alternatives: Docebo, LinkedIn Learning, EdCast, Degreed, and Skillsoft. Each can help you run structured group programs while giving you different trade offs in content, customization, and learner experience.

alternative

Docebo

offers a learning management system with strong social and collaborative features, including live virtual classrooms and discussion boards that work well for cohort based programs. Its AI driven recommendations and content marketplace give you flexibility to blend self paced and group learning. Docebo also lets you create custom learning paths that keep cohorts on the same schedule.

alternative

LinkedIn Learning

provides a huge library of expert led video courses that can be assigned to cohorts with due dates and group discussions. Its integration with LinkedIn profiles makes it easy for learners to share achievements, but the platform is less focused on user generated content. For cohort based training, you can use its learning paths and analytics to track group progress.

alternative

EdCast

is a knowledge cloud platform that combines formal cohort courses with informal learning from internal experts. It supports cohort based programs through structured learning journeys, live sessions, and collaborative spaces. EdCast’s strength is surfacing relevant content from multiple sources, which helps keep group learning dynamic and up to date.

alternative

Degreed

focuses on skill development and learning experience, letting you curate content from many providers for cohort programs. It offers tools for creating learning cohorts, assigning group projects, and tracking shared goals. Degreed also integrates with your existing systems, so you can blend internal and external content in one place.

alternative

Skillsoft

provides a broad catalog of business and technology courses plus leadership development programs that are often delivered in cohort formats. Its Percipio platform includes social learning features like discussions and learning groups. Skillsoft also offers live instructor led sessions that fit cohort schedules.

How they compare

All five alternatives support some form of collaborative, cohort based training, but they differ in how much they emphasize user generated content and peer interaction. 360Learning is built around co creation and peer feedback, making it a natural fit for teams that want to author courses together. Docebo and EdCast also offer strong collaboration tools, while LinkedIn Learning and Skillsoft rely more on curated content with added discussion features. Degreed excels at aggregating content from multiple sources for cohort paths. Your choice depends on whether you want to prioritize internal content creation or use existing libraries, and how much control you need over cohort schedules and reporting.
