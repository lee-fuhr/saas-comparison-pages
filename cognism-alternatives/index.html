If your sales team targets European prospects, finding a data provider that respects GDPR is non-negotiable. Cognism has been a popular choice for compliant B2B contact and company data, but it may not fit every budget, workflow, or data need. Whether you are looking for better enrichment coverage, deeper firmographic signals, or a tool that integrates more seamlessly with your CRM, exploring Cognism alternatives can help you stay compliant while improving pipeline quality. Below we compare four tools that European sales teams often evaluate: ZoomInfo Enrich, Clearbit Enrich, BuiltWith, and Leadspace.

alternative

ZoomInfo Enrich

. This tool offers broad company and contact data with a focus on intent signals and technographic filters. It pulls data from multiple public and proprietary sources, but European teams should verify the provider’s GDPR compliance posture, as ZoomInfo has faced regulatory scrutiny in the past. Its enrichment API works well for outbound sales workflows, yet pricing may be higher than some alternatives.

alternative

Clearbit Enrich

. Clearbit is known for real-time data enrichment from an email address or domain, returning company demographics, technology stack, and verified contacts. It maintains strong data privacy policies for EU users and supports consent-based enrichment. However, Clearbit’s coverage can be thinner for smaller European companies, and its strict verification process may limit the volume of enriched records.

alternative

BuiltWith

. BuiltWith focuses on technology profiling rather than direct contact data. It identifies the software and infrastructure a company uses, which is ideal for technical account-based selling. Because it does not store personal data, it sidesteps many GDPR concerns, but it requires you to combine its outputs with a separate contact data provider to get names and email addresses.

alternative

Leadspace

. Leadspace offers an AI-powered platform that enriches and segments B2B audiences while emphasising privacy compliance for European markets. It uses modelled data rather than scraped contact lists, which can reduce the risk of processing non-consented personal data. Its strength lies in account scoring and targeting, but it may require more setup time and has a smaller database than legacy providers.

How they compare

All four tools can supplement or replace Cognism for European sales teams. ZoomInfo Enrich and Clearbit Enrich are direct alternatives for contact and company enrichment, but require careful review of their GDPR documentation. BuiltWith is a niche choice for technographic data without personal data risk. Leadspace focuses on compliant, modelled audiences rather than raw contact lists. Your decision should balance data freshness, compliance overhead, integration ease, and total cost per enriched record.

Frequently asked questions

Are these alternatives fully GDPR compliant for European sales prospecting?
Compliance depends on how you use the data, not just the tool. Each provider publishes a data processing agreement and privacy policy. You should check if they rely on legitimate interest or explicit consent, and confirm that their data sources are transparent. For highest safety, pair any enrichment tool with your own consent management processes.
Can I use these tools without changing my current CRM workflow?
Yes, most offer native integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and other major CRMs. ZoomInfo Enrich and Clearbit Enrich provide REST APIs and browser extensions for real-time enrichment. BuiltWith and Leadspace typically require export or API-based sync. Always test the integration on a small dataset before full rollout.
