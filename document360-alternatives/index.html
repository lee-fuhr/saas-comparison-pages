Best Document360 alternatives

Document360 is a popular knowledge base platform for SaaS product documentation, but it may not suit every team’s budget, workflow, or scaling needs. Many teams look for alternatives that offer simpler editing, lower pricing, or tighter integration with their existing tools. Below are five strong alternatives to consider, each with distinct strengths for building and maintaining product docs.

alternative

Confluence

. Confluence is a powerful collaboration and documentation tool from Atlassian, ideal for teams that already use Jira or other Atlassian products. It offers flexible page trees, rich permissions, and templates, but can feel heavy for pure knowledge base use without extra plugins.

alternative

Helpjuice

. Helpjuice is a dedicated knowledge base platform designed for creating self-service documentation with advanced search and analytics. It focuses heavily on customization and allows you to build branded help centers quickly, though it lacks the project management features found in some alternatives.

alternative

Zendesk Guide

. Zendesk Guide is the knowledge base component of the Zendesk customer support suite, making it a natural choice if you already use Zendesk for ticketing. It offers AI-powered article suggestions and seamless integration with support workflows, but its content authoring tools are less flexible than standalone doc platforms.

alternative

Freshdesk Knowledge Base

. Freshdesk Knowledge Base is part of the Freshdesk customer support system, providing a straightforward way to add help articles alongside ticket management. It is easy to set up and affordable for small teams, though advanced documentation features like version control and nested content are limited.

alternative

Slab

. Slab is a modern knowledge management tool that blends documentation with a wiki-style interface, using a tidy card system and powerful search. It is well suited for internal and external product docs alike, and its integrations with Slack, GitHub, and other tools make it a strong choice for developer-focused teams.

How they compare

When choosing an alternative, consider your team size, budget, and existing tool stack. Confluence is best for teams that need deep project integration, while Helpjuice and Slab offer cleaner authoring experiences for dedicated knowledge bases. Zendesk Guide and Freshdesk Knowledge Base are ideal if you already use those support platforms, but they sacrifice advanced doc features for simplicity. Evaluate each on content permissions, search capabilities, and ease of onboarding to find the right fit.

Frequently asked questions

What should I look for in a Document360 alternative?
Focus on your team’s needs: ease of content creation, search quality, collaboration features, and integrations with tools like Slack or ticketing systems. Also consider pricing per user and whether the platform supports both internal and external documentation.
Can I migrate my existing content from Document360 to another tool?
Yes, most alternatives offer import options for HTML, Markdown, or CSV files. Check each tool’s documentation on migration paths before committing, as the level of automation varies.
