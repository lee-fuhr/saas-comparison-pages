Best Nutanix Xi Leap alternatives

If your enterprise runs critical workloads on a hyper-converged infrastructure (HCI) like Nutanix, you need a disaster recovery solution that fits your architecture without adding complexity. Nutanix Xi Leap is a native DR-as-a-Service (DRaaS) offering built directly into the Nutanix ecosystem, designed to simplify failover and failback while keeping your data on a familiar platform. Zerto is a strong, platform-agnostic alternative that uses continuous replication, but it introduces an extra software layer. This article compares Nutanix Xi Leap against Zerto and other DR tools (Veeam, Druva, Acronis) to help you choose the right DR approach for your hyper-converged environment.

alternative

Zerto

is a continuous data protection (CDP) platform that provides near-zero recovery point objectives (RPOs) by replicating every write operation in real time. It works across any hypervisor, including Nutanix AHV, but requires deploying Zerto Virtual Managers and a separate journal disk, which adds overhead. For enterprises that need multi-cloud or multi-hypervisor flexibility, Zerto is a reliable choice, though it is not native to the Nutanix stack.

alternative

Veeam

offers a mature backup and replication solution for Nutanix AHV, supporting both image-level and file-level recovery. It is best known for its ease of use, orchestration, and cost-effectiveness for backup-driven DR, but its RPOs are typically measured in minutes rather than seconds. Veeam works well if you already use it for backup and want to extend into DR without a separate tool.

alternative

Druva

is a fully cloud-native DRaaS solution that runs on AWS and does not require on-premises hardware or software. It provides integrated backup and DR for Nutanix workloads with a simple consumption model, but it relies on a third-party cloud provider and may have higher egress costs. Druva is a good fit for organizations that prefer a SaaS-based approach with no infrastructure management.

alternative

Acronis

offers a cyber protection platform that includes backup and DR with built-in anti-ransomware capabilities. It supports Nutanix AHV but is generally more suited for small to mid-sized enterprises rather than large-scale, complex DR scenarios. Acronis can be a cost-effective option if you also need endpoint and file protection, though its advanced DR features are limited compared to Xi Leap or Zerto.

How they compare

Nutanix Xi Leap is purpose-built for the Nutanix HCI platform. It runs natively on AHV, uses the same Prism management interface, and requires no additional software or agents for most workflows. This tight integration makes deployment, testing, and failover straightforward for existing Nutanix customers. Zerto, by contrast, is a standalone solution that can protect Nutanix and non-Nutanix environments alike. It offers finer RPO granularity (seconds) through continuous replication, but introduces a separate management console, non-disruptive drifts, and additional licensing costs. For enterprises committed to a single Nutanix stack, Xi Leap’s simplicity and native control often outweigh Zerto’s extra flexibility. Veeam, Druva, and Acronis each bring different trade-offs in cost, cloud model, and feature depth, but none match Xi Leap’s seamless integration with Nutanix AHV.
