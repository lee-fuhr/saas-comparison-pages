Best Paylocity alternatives

Mid-size firms need HR and payroll software that handles complex rules, employee self-service, and compliance without sacrificing ease of use. Paylocity is a popular choice because it offers a broad set of features for these tasks, but it may not be the best fit for every company. Budget, support quality, or specific module gaps sometimes lead businesses to explore alternatives that still provide robust modules for payroll, benefits, time tracking, and talent management. Below are four strong options to consider when evaluating a Paylocity alternative.

alternative

Gusto

. Gusto is a cloud based platform that excels at straightforward payroll and benefits administration for smaller organizations, but it now supports mid-size firms with its “Gusto Pro” tier. It offers automated payroll, compliance help, and a clean employee experience, though its HR modules are less deep than Paylocity’s for larger teams. Mid-size companies that value simplicity over extensive customization may find Gusto a good fit.

alternative

Justworks

. Justworks operates as a professional employer organization (PEO), meaning it co-employs your staff to provide access to larger group health plans and compliance support. It combines payroll, benefits, and HR tools in one package, making it easier for mid-size firms to offload administrative tasks. The tradeoff is that you share some control over HR policies, and the cost is typically higher than a standalone software solution.

alternative

TriNet

. TriNet is another PEO that targets growing and mid-size businesses needing a full HR outsourcing partner. It offers a wide range of benefits options, payroll processing, risk management, and a dedicated HR support team. Because TriNet handles many legal and compliance responsibilities, it suits firms that want to reduce their own HR workload, but the pricing is more complex and often not transparent without a quote.

alternative

ADP

. ADP provides the most comprehensive payroll and HR suite among the alternatives, with tools like ADP Workforce Now designed for mid-size companies. It offers deep features for payroll, time and attendance, benefits administration, and talent management, plus strong integrations. The downside is that ADP can be more expensive and its interface may feel less modern than Paylocity, especially for smaller teams.
