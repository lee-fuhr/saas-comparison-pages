Best Datadog alternatives

Datadog offers powerful observability, but many engineering teams find its pricing scales quickly beyond budget, especially as data volume grows. If you are looking for robust monitoring, logging, and tracing without the hefty monthly bill, there are capable alternatives that fit different architectures and team sizes. This article covers five popular options that can replace or complement Datadog, focusing on controlling costs while maintaining visibility.

alternative

New Relic

focuses on full-stack observability with a modern user interface and a generous free tier that includes 100 GB of data ingestion per month. Its pricing is usage based, similar to Datadog, but the free allowance and predictable overage rates can make it more affordable for smaller workloads. Teams already invested in New Relic’s ecosystem often cite its APM and browser monitoring as strong alternatives to Datadog’s offerings.

alternative

Grafana Cloud

builds on the open source Grafana stack, providing hosted metrics, logs, and traces with a free plan that includes 10,000 series for metrics and 50 GB of logs. Its strength lies in flexibility: you can self-host Prometheus and Loki while using Grafana Cloud as a central dashboard, keeping control of data costs. The per-series and per-GB pricing model can be significantly cheaper than Datadog for high-volume but low-cardinality workloads.

Try Grafana Cloud →
alternative

Dynatrace

uses a patented Davis AI engine for automatic root cause analysis, which reduces manual troubleshooting time for complex environments. Its pricing is based on host units rather than data volume, which can be more predictable if you manage a fixed number of hosts. While Dynatrace is often considered enterprise grade and not necessarily cheaper than Datadog, its AI driven insights and all-in-one license may justify the cost for teams that value automation over granular data ingest controls.

alternative

Prometheus

is the open source foundation of cloud native monitoring, adopted by the CNCF and used widely in Kubernetes environments. It offers a pull based metrics collection model and a powerful query language (PromQL), with no license cost beyond your own infrastructure. The tradeoff is that you must manage storage, alerting, and high availability yourself, making it best suited for teams with DevOps maturity who want maximum control and minimal ongoing spend.

alternative

Better Stack

(formerly Better Uptime) provides modern uptime monitoring, incident management, and status pages with a clear, flat pricing model that includes unlimited team members. It focuses on simplicity and reliability for web services, offering heartbeat monitoring, SSL checks, and log tailing without the complexity of full APM. For teams that primarily need synthetic monitoring and on-call workflows, Better Stack can replace Datadog’s synthetic monitoring at a fraction of the cost.

Try Better Stack →

How they compare

Choosing the right Datadog alternative depends on your team’s priorities around cost, complexity, and required features. New Relic and Dynatrace are full featured competitors with their own pricing quirks; New Relic’s free tier helps small teams, while Dynatrace’s host based model suits fixed infrastructure. Grafana Cloud and Prometheus appeal to teams comfortable with open source tooling and fine grained cost control, with Grafana Cloud offering a managed option and Prometheus providing self hosted freedom. Better Stack is the simplest option for uptime monitoring and incident response, trading deep observability for lower overhead and transparent pricing. No single tool matches Datadog’s breadth, but each excels in specific areas where you can save substantially on costs.
