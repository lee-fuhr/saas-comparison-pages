Best Miro alternatives

If your product team uses Miro for customer journey mapping but wants to explore other options, you are not alone. While Miro is a popular collaborative whiteboard, it may not fit every budget, workflow, or team preference. This article compares four strong alternatives: Lucidchart, Mural, Microsoft Whiteboard, and FigJam. Each tool brings unique strengths for visualizing customer journeys, from structured diagramming to lightweight sketching.

alternative

Lucidchart

is best for teams that need more structured diagramming features alongside journey mapping. It offers prebuilt templates for customer journey maps and integrates tightly with tools like Jira and Confluence. You can add swimlanes, conditionals, and data layers to show pain points and opportunities without leaving the canvas.

alternative

Mural

is the closest alternative to Miro, with a focus on facilitation and workshop-style collaboration. It includes dedicated journey mapping templates, sticky notes, timers, and voting features to run live sessions. Mural also lets you export maps to PDF or embed them in other tools for documentation.

alternative

Microsoft Whiteboard

is a free option for teams already in the Microsoft 365 ecosystem. It supports real time coauthoring, inking, and integration with Teams and OneDrive. While it lacks advanced journey mapping templates, you can quickly sketch a customer journey using freeform shapes and sticky notes.

alternative

FigJam

works well for product teams that already use Figma for design. It offers a simple, reactive whiteboard with journey mapping stencils and component libraries. FigJam’s real time cursor visibility and audio chat help remote teams map journeys quickly, and you can export directly to Figma for detailed prototyping.

How they compare

Miro is the most feature rich for general journey mapping, but Mural gives you stronger facilitation tools. Lucidchart is better if you need a logical diagram structure and data integration. Microsoft Whiteboard is a lightweight, no cost option for ad hoc mapping inside Microsoft shops. FigJam fits best when your product team already uses Figma, as it bridges whiteboarding and design work without leaving the ecosystem.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate my existing Miro journey maps to another tool?
Yes, most alternatives accept common file formats like PDF, PNG, or CSV. Lukidchart and Mural allow importing via direct file upload or copy paste. Microsoft Whiteboard and FigJam have limited import options, so you may need to recreate maps manually.
Which alternative is easiest for non designers to learn?
Microsoft Whiteboard is the most intuitive for beginners because it mimics a physical whiteboard with minimal menus. FigJam also has a low learning curve, but its interface may feel unfamiliar to people who have not used Figma. Mural and Miro are similar in complexity.
