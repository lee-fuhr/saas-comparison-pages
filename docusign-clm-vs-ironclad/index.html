Best DocuSign CLM alternatives

Choosing the right contract lifecycle management (CLM) platform is a critical decision for legal departments. DocuSign CLM, formerly SpringCM, is a market leader that offers deep integration with the DocuSign eSignature ecosystem and strong document generation capabilities. It competes directly with Ironclad, a popular platform built for modern legal teams. This article compares DocuSign CLM with Ironclad and other key alternatives to help you evaluate which platform fits your department’s workflow, compliance needs, and budget.

alternative

Ironclad

. Ironclad is a cloud native CLM designed for fast implementation and ease of use. It excels in contract lifecycle management with a modern interface, automated workflows, and a strong focus on legal operations. Unlike DocuSign CLM, Ironclad emphasizes a click not code approach, making it easier for non technical legal team members to manage templates and approvals.

alternative

Conga CLM

. Conga (now part of Apttus) offers robust document generation and contract analytics, often integrating tightly with Salesforce. It is a good fit for sales driven organizations that need to automate proposal and contract creation. Conga’s strength is in bulk document production and clause libraries, though its user interface can feel less intuitive than DocuSign CLM’s.

alternative

Icertis

. Icertis is an enterprise grade CLM known for its strong AI capabilities and compliance management. It handles complex, global contract portfolios and provides deep insights into contractual obligations. Icertis is best suited for large organizations with sophisticated compliance needs, but it typically requires more implementation time and investment than DocuSign CLM.

alternative

Agiloft

. Agiloft is a flexible, low code CLM platform that can be customized extensively without heavy IT involvement. It is often chosen by organizations that want to tailor workflows, fields, and integrations precisely to their processes. Agiloft’s flexibility is a plus, but it may require more upfront configuration compared to DocuSign CLM’s out of the box features.

alternative

Concord

. Concord is a simpler, more affordable CLM that focuses on collaboration and basic contract management. It is well suited for small to midsize legal teams that need a lightweight solution with strong version control and commenting features. Concord lacks the advanced AI and integration depth of DocuSign CLM, but it offers a lower barrier to entry.

How they compare

DocuSign CLM and Ironclad are the two most commonly compared platforms for legal departments. DocuSign CLM offers deeper integration with DocuSign’s eSignature and broader document generation capabilities, making it ideal for organizations already using DocuSign. Ironclad provides a more modern, user friendly interface and faster deployment, especially for teams that want to automate workflows without heavy coding. For example, Ironclad’s contract repository and approval routing are highly intuitive, while DocuSign CLM’s strength lies in its configurable templates and extensive salesforce integration. Conga, Icertis, Agiloft, and Concord fill different niches: Conga for sales and document generation, Icertis for enterprise compliance, Agiloft for total customization, and Concord for budget conscious teams. The right choice depends on your department’s size, existing tech stack, and the balance between ease of use and advanced functionality.

Frequently asked questions

How does DocuSign CLM compare to Ironclad in terms of integration with existing tools?
DocuSign CLM integrates natively with the DocuSign eSignature platform and has strong connectors to Salesforce, SAP, and Workday. Ironclad offers integrations with Slack, Salesforce, and many other tools through APIs, but its native eSignature is built in. If your team already uses DocuSign for signatures, DocuSign CLM provides a seamless experience.
Which platform is easier for a small legal team to implement without outside help?
Ironclad is generally easier to set up and configure with its intuitive drag and drop workflow builder and self service template management. DocuSign CLM has a steeper learning curve and often benefits from professional services during onboarding, especially for complex clause libraries and conditional logic.
