Best Slack alternatives

Slack is a popular team messaging tool, but its per-seat cost can add up quickly, especially for large teams or organizations on a tight budget. Many teams are now looking for Slack alternatives that offer similar features at a lower price or with a different approach to communication. This article covers five strong options: Microsoft Teams, Discord, Lark, Twist, and Pumble. Each has its own strengths, so you can choose the one that best fits your team’s workflow and budget.

alternative

Microsoft Teams

is part of the Microsoft 365 suite, making it a natural choice if your team already uses Word, Excel, or Outlook. It offers chat, video calls, file sharing, and deep integration with Office apps, but its interface can feel busy compared to Slack.

alternative

Discord

started as a gaming chat app but has grown into a capable team messaging tool with voice channels, screen sharing, and a free tier that supports unlimited message history. Its server-based structure works well for communities or teams that want less formal communication, though it lacks some advanced enterprise features.

alternative

Lark

is an all-in-one collaboration platform that combines messaging, video conferencing, docs, and calendars in one app. It offers a generous free plan and strong automation tools, making it a good fit for teams that want to replace multiple tools with a single, low-cost solution.

Try Lark →
alternative

Twist

takes a threaded, topic-based approach to messaging to reduce noise and keep conversations organized. Instead of endless chat channels, you create threads for specific discussions, which helps teams that feel overwhelmed by real-time chat. It is ideal for asynchronous work but may not suit teams that need instant messaging.

alternative

Pumble

is a free team chat app that focuses on simplicity and unlimited message history. It includes channels, direct messages, file sharing, and integrations, with no limits on users or storage on its free plan. It is a straightforward choice for teams that want Slack-like functionality without the cost.

How they compare

When comparing these Slack alternatives, consider what your team values most. Microsoft Teams is best for organizations already using Microsoft 365, while Discord offers a free and informal option with strong voice chat. Lark provides an all-in-one suite with a low cost, and Twist reduces chat overload through threaded conversations. Pumble is a direct, free alternative to Slack with similar features. Each tool has different pricing models: Microsoft Teams has a free tier with limits, Discord and Pumble offer generous free plans, Lark has a free plan with some restrictions, and Twist has a paid plan starting at a low per-user rate. No tool is perfect for everyone, so choose based on your team’s size, budget, and communication style.

Frequently asked questions

Which Slack alternative is completely free for unlimited users?
Pumble offers a free plan with unlimited users, unlimited message history, and no storage caps on files. Discord also has a free plan with unlimited messages and users, though it includes some limitations on upload size and features.
Can I keep using Slack while testing an alternative?
Yes. Most alternatives offer free trials or free tiers, so you can set up a small test group alongside Slack. This lets your team compare features, ease of use, and cost without disrupting your current workflow.
