Loom and Vidyard both let you record and share async video messages, so your team can communicate without scheduling live calls. But they serve slightly different needs. Loom is built for speed and simplicity, making it a favorite for quick updates and casual team communication. Vidyard is designed for sales and marketing use cases, with deep integrations and detailed viewer analytics. If your team is choosing between Loom versus Vidyard for async video messaging, this comparison will help you pick the right fit.

Where Loom wins

Loom wins on ease of use. You can record your screen, your webcam, or both with just a few clicks, and the sharing link is generated instantly. There is almost no learning curve, which means your whole team can start using it in minutes. Loom also offers a generous free tier that covers most basic needs for small teams. Its editing tools, like trimming and call-to-action buttons, are simple but effective. For internal updates, feedback, or quick how-to videos, Loom is the faster, more intuitive option. The experience is lightweight, and your recipients do not need an account to watch.

Where Vidyard wins

Vidyard wins on analytics and integrations. It tracks who watched your video, for how long, and which parts they rewatched, making it a strong choice for sales teams who want to follow up with prospects. Vidyard connects deeply with CRM tools like Salesforce, HubSpot, and Outreach, so you can log video views directly to a contact record. It also supports more advanced customization, such as branded video players, custom thumbnails, and call-to-action buttons that can embed forms. For teams that need video as part of a structured sales or marketing workflow, Vidyard provides the control and data that Loom lacks. Its free plan is more limited, but the paid tiers offer features that justify the cost for enterprise use.

Verdict

Choose Loom if your main goal is fast, frictionless async communication within your team. It is ideal for project updates, bug reports, or casual collaboration where analytics are not critical. Choose Vidyard if you need to track viewer engagement, integrate with your sales stack, or present a polished, branded experience to external clients or leads. Both tools work well for async video messaging, but the right choice depends on whether you prioritize simplicity versus data and customization. For most internal teams, Loom versus Vidyard comes down to how much you value analytics and CRM integration.
