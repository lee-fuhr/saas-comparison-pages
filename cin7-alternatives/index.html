Cin7 is a popular inventory management platform for e-commerce businesses, but it may not suit everyone. Some managers find it too expensive, too complex for their operations, or lacking in specific features. This article covers five alternatives: Fishbowl, inFlow, Sortly, Linnworks, and SkuVault. Each tool offers different strengths, so you can choose based on your business size, sales channels, and budget.

alternative

Fishbowl

is a robust inventory and manufacturing solution that integrates well with QuickBooks and e-commerce platforms. It is ideal for businesses that need advanced work order and bill of materials tracking. Fishbowl can be a good fit if you handle both sales and production.

alternative

inFlow

targets small to medium sized e-commerce businesses with a focus on order management and barcode scanning. It offers a clean interface and supports serial number tracking. inFlow is a more affordable option than Cin7 for teams that do not need global multi warehouse features.

alternative

Sortly

uses a visual inventory system that lets you take photos of items and organize them with tags. It is simple to use and works well for small teams that need basic stock tracking without complex workflows. Sortly is not built for high volume or multi channel selling.

alternative

Linnworks

is a multichannel e-commerce management platform that centralizes orders, listings, and inventory across marketplaces like Amazon, eBay, and Shopify. It excels at automating shipping and listing tasks. Linnworks is a strong choice if your main challenge is managing multiple sales channels.

alternative

SkuVault

focuses on warehouse efficiency with features like lot tracking, bin locations, and pick pack verification. It integrates with major e-commerce platforms and shipping carriers. SkuVault is designed for medium to large businesses that need precise inventory control and fast fulfillment.

How they compare

These five alternatives differ in scope and complexity. Fishbowl is best for manufacturing heavy operations. inFlow suits smaller e-commerce businesses with straightforward needs. Sortly works for visual stock tracking in low volume settings. Linnworks emphasizes multichannel sales automation. SkuVault prioritizes warehouse accuracy. When choosing, consider your order volume, number of sales channels, and whether you need manufacturing or just inventory management. None of these tools are direct clones of Cin7, so evaluate a free trial to see which fits your workflow.

Frequently asked questions

I sell on multiple marketplaces like Amazon and eBay. Which Cin7 alternative is best?
Linnworks is designed specifically for multichannel selling. It can sync orders, manage listings, and update inventory across many sites at once. SkuVault also integrates with marketplaces, but its strength is warehouse processes, not listings management.
Is there a cheaper alternative to Cin7 for a small e-commerce business?
inFlow and Sortly are generally more affordable than Cin7. inFlow offers barcode scanning and order management at a lower price point. Sortly has a free tier for very small inventories, though it lacks advanced e-commerce integration.
