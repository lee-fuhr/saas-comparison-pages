Best Schoology alternatives

Schoology has long been a popular choice for K-12 districts that need a learning management system (LMS) with built-in assessment tools. However, factors such as cost, changes in features, or integration needs may lead your district to explore other options. The following alternatives offer strong LMS and assessment capabilities that can meet the demands of classrooms, curriculum teams, and administrators.

alternative

Canvas LMS

offers a highly customizable platform with robust native assessment tools, including item banks, rubrics, and standards-based grading. Its open API makes it easier to integrate with existing student information systems (SIS) and third-party tools. Many districts choose Canvas for its scalability and the ability to create a consistent experience across elementary, middle, and high school levels.

alternative

Google Classroom

is a lightweight, intuitive LMS that works seamlessly with the Google Workspace for Education suite. For assessment, it supports tools like Google Forms for quizzes, rubrics, and grade export to many SIS platforms. While it lacks some of the advanced assessment features of dedicated LMSs, its simplicity and zero licensing cost appeal to districts with limited budgets.

alternative

PowerSchool

is primarily known as a student information system (SIS), but its Performance Matters and Schoology Learning (formerly Schoology) products provide integrated LMS and assessment functionality. PowerSchool’s assessment tools focus on benchmark and formative testing, with real-time reporting tied to student data. This option works best for districts already using PowerSchool’s SIS and seeking a unified data ecosystem.

How they compare

When choosing between these Schoology alternatives, consider your district’s priorities. Canvas LMS offers the most feature-rich assessment tools and extensive integrations, but it comes with a higher annual cost. Google Classroom is free and easy to implement, yet it has fewer built-in reporting options for district-wide benchmarks. PowerSchool provides the tightest integration with its own SIS, ideal if your district wants one vendor for both student data and classroom management. For all three, you can migrate existing Schoology content, but the process may require manual adjustments or third-party tools.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative has the best built-in assessment features for K-12?
Canvas LMS offers the most comprehensive set of native assessment tools, including advanced item banks, mastery paths, and standards-aligned reporting. PowerSchool’s Performance Matters is also strong for large-scale benchmark testing, while Google Classroom relies on add-ons like Google Forms for quizzes.
Can we transfer our existing Schoology courses to Canvas, Google Classroom, or PowerSchool?
Yes, all three support course import. Canvas provides a dedicated course migration tool that works with Schoology exports. Google Classroom can import content through Google Drive and third-party tools. PowerSchool offers migration services, but manual cleanup is often needed to preserve assessments and rubrics.
