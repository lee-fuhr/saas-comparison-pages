Best Square POS alternatives

If you run a retail store, you have likely compared Square POS against Clover and other hardware-focused systems. Square is famous for its simple setup and flat-rate pricing, but you might wonder if it lacks the rugged terminals or offline reliability that some alternatives offer. This article helps retail operators weigh Square’s ease of use against Clover, Lightspeed Retail, Shopify POS, Vend, and NCR – without burying you in jargon or made-up numbers.

alternative

Clover

. Clover offers a range of countertop terminals and mobile readers, making it a strong hardware-first choice for brick-and-mortar retailers. Its interface is fairly intuitive, but the system often pushes you toward Clover’s own payment processing and long-term contracts. If you need a built-in receipt printer and a tilting customer-facing screen, Clover’s hardware is more purpose-built than Square’s small magstripe reader.

alternative

Lightspeed Retail

. Lightspeed is built for inventory-heavy businesses like boutiques and sporting goods stores. It gives you robust purchase order management, multi-location syncing, and advanced reporting, though the setup is more complex than Square. The hardware side is flexible – you can pair Lightspeed with any EMV reader – but it does not offer a proprietary terminal like Clover does.

alternative

Shopify POS

. Shopify POS works seamlessly if you already run an online shop on Shopify. Its retail hardware (tap-and-chip reader, barcode scanner, receipt printer) is clean and reliable, and inventory syncs instantly across channels. However, Shopify charges per-location fees for its Pro plan, and the basic version lacks advanced retail features like purchase orders or in-depth customer profiles.

alternative

Vend

. Vend is a cloud-based POS designed specifically for retail, with strong loyalty programs and real-time reporting. It works with a wide range of off-the-shelf hardware (Stripe terminal, Ingenico, etc.) so you are not locked into one vendor. The trade-off is that its offline mode is less robust than Square’s – if your internet goes down, you may lose transactions unless you pay extra for a backup plan.

alternative

NCR

. NCR (formerly Silver) is a heavyweight option for multi-store chains. It offers heavy-duty countertop terminals, integrated payment processing, and enterprise-grade inventory and employee management. The hardware is built to last, but the system comes with long contracts, higher monthly fees, and a steeper learning curve than Square or Clover.
