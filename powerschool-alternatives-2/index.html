Best PowerSchool alternatives

Many school districts rely on PowerSchool for student information system (SIS) functions like attendance, gradebook, and scheduling. However, when your district also needs strong learning management system (LMS) capabilities for delivering online courses, managing assignments, and tracking student progress, PowerSchool can feel limited or clunky. This review explores three alternatives: Canvas LMS, Schoology, and Google Classroom. Each offers a different balance of SIS-like features and full LMS tools, so you can choose the platform that best fits your district’s size, budget, and workflow.

alternative

Canvas LMS

is a full-featured learning management system built for K-12 and higher education. It provides robust course creation, assessment tools, and a mobile app, plus it integrates with many SIS platforms through standard data protocols. Districts that already have a separate SIS can use Canvas as the primary LMS while syncing student rosters and grades, making it a strong alternative if you need advanced LMS functionality but do not require a built-in SIS.

alternative

Schoology

blends LMS capabilities with basic SIS features, such as attendance and gradebooks, in a single interface. This makes it a good choice for districts that want a more unified experience without juggling two separate systems. Schoology also supports parent communication, analytics, and a content marketplace, though its SIS functions may not be as deep as PowerSchool’s for complex state reporting or custom scheduling.

alternative

Google Classroom

is a lightweight, free LMS that works best for districts already using Google Workspace for Education. It simplifies assignment distribution, grading, and feedback, but it lacks the advanced SIS features like enrollment management, attendance tracking, and official gradebook calculations that large districts often need. Google Classroom can be a viable alternative for smaller districts or those willing to supplement it with a separate SIS tool.

How they compare

Canvas LMS offers the most powerful and customizable LMS experience but requires a separate SIS. Schoology provides a middle ground with both LMS and basic SIS capabilities in one platform. Google Classroom is the simplest and most cost-effective choice but lacks the SIS features that larger districts depend on. When deciding, consider your district’s need for reporting, state compliance, and staff training. PowerSchool excels as a dedicated SIS, but these alternatives may serve you better if LMS functionality is your top priority.
