Best TouchBistro alternatives

TouchBistro is a well known iPad POS for full service restaurants, but quick-service venues often need an even faster setup and simpler day-to-day operation. If you are looking to switch from TouchBistro or want a system that is easier to deploy on your own, this guide covers four top alternatives with fast iPad deployment.

alternative

Toast POS

. Toast offers a purpose built restaurant platform that works well for quick-service operations. Its iPad system is quick to deploy and includes integrated online ordering, payments, and loyalty tools. Toast can be more expensive per month than some competitors, but the all-in-one approach saves time.

alternative

Square Restaurants

. Square provides a free iPad POS app with low flat rate transaction fees, making it one of the fastest and most affordable options for quick-service venues. Setup is straightforward, and you can start taking orders with minimal hardware. Square works best for small to medium operations that want to get up and running in hours, not days.

alternative

Lightspeed Restaurant

. Lightspeed offers a robust iPad POS with strong inventory management and reporting features. It is easy to set up for a single location and scales well as you grow. For quick-service venues, Lightspeed may require a bit more menu customization upfront, but the flexibility is worth it for those who need detailed controls.

alternative

Revel Systems

. Revel is a powerful iPad POS designed for larger or multi-location quick-service chains that need advanced features like self-service kiosks and kitchen display screens. Its deployment can be faster when you work with a partner, but the system has a steeper learning curve for very small venues. Revel is best if you plan to add complex functionality later.

How they compare

Toast POS and Square Restaurants offer the fastest deployment for quick-service iPad setups, with Square being the most affordable. Lightspeed and Revel provide more advanced inventory and multi-location features at a higher cost or longer initial setup. For a simple cafe or fast casual spot, Square or Lightspeed are easy picks. For a growing chain with kiosks and online ordering, Toast or Revel are better suited.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use my existing card reader with these alternatives?
Most of these systems require you to use their own payment processing hardware or an integrated card reader. Check compatibility before you switch, as your old reader may not work.
How quickly can I move from TouchBistro to another system?
For a straightforward quick-service venue, setup and migration usually take a few days to a week. The time depends on menu complexity, hardware needs, and whether you need to import data from TouchBistro. Many providers offer white-glove migration services to speed things up.
