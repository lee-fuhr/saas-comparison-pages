As a freelancer, choosing the right time tracking and invoicing tool can save you hours each week. Toggl is a popular choice for its simple, fast time tracking and flexible reporting. Harvest is a strong alternative that combines time tracking with built in invoicing and expense management. This article helps you compare the two so you can pick the best fit for your workflow.

alternative

Harvest

. Harvest offers time tracking, expense logging, and invoicing in one tool, which means you do not need a separate billing app. It sends professional invoices directly from tracked time and expenses, and its reporting shows project profitability. If you want a single platform for tracking and billing without extra integrations, Harvest is a solid choice.

How they compare

Toggl excels at quick time tracking with one click start and stop, plus a powerful timeline view for logging past hours. Its free plan is generous for solo freelancers, but it lacks built in invoicing, so you need to connect it to a separate tool like FreshBooks or use Toggl Track’s limited invoice export. Harvest has a 30 day free trial with no free forever plan, but its invoicing is native and includes payment reminders and online payments via Stripe. For reporting, Toggl gives you colorful dashboards and weekly PDF reports, while Harvest provides detailed project budgets and cost tracking. Toggl is better if you want a lightweight tracker and do not mind integrating a separate invoicing app. Harvest is the better choice if you want an all in one system from tracking to getting paid.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to pay for both Toggl and an invoicing app?
Yes, if you use Toggl, you will likely need a separate invoicing tool like Harvest, FreshBooks, or Wave. Toggl’s free plan is useful for tracking, but its invoicing features are limited to exporting CSV files. Harvest includes invoicing in one subscription, so you avoid paying for two apps.
Which tool is easier for a solo freelancer just starting out?
Toggl is easier to start with because its free plan lets you track unlimited time with no commitment. You can experiment with its simple interface right away. Harvest requires a paid subscription after 30 days, which may feel like a bigger step for a new freelancer who is still building their client base.
