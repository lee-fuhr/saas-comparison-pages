Best Shortcut alternatives

Shortcut is a solid ticketing system for agile teams, but it may not fit every workflow or budget. If your team needs a lighter tool with fewer features or a different pricing model, you have several strong alternatives. This article compares three options: Linear, Jira, and Asana. Each can help you manage user stories, tasks, and sprints with less overhead, depending on your team’s size and focus.

alternative

Linear

. Linear is a fast, modern tool built for software teams. It keeps the interface clean and minimizes clutter, making it ideal for developers who want to move quickly. Linear supports sprint planning, issue tracking, and integrations with Git providers.

alternative

Jira

. Jira is a powerful, highly customizable platform that can scale from small teams to large enterprises. Its flexibility means you can trim down workflows and fields to create a lightweight experience, but the initial setup and ongoing configuration can take more effort. Jira is best for teams that need advanced reporting and deep project management features.

alternative

Asana

. Asana is a project management tool that doubles as a lightweight ticketing system. It offers clear task lists, timelines, and rules for automating repetitive steps. Asana works well for cross-functional teams that want to track work beyond software development, like marketing or product tasks.

How they compare

Linear excels in speed and developer experience, Jira offers the most customization and enterprise features, and Asana provides a balanced project management approach with easy automation. For lightweight needs, Linear and Asana require less setup time, while Jira can be simplified but demands more upfront planning. Pricing varies: Linear has a free tier for small teams, Asana offers free basics, and Jira’s free plan is limited. All three integrate with common tools like Slack, GitHub, and GitLab.

Frequently asked questions

Is Jira too complex for a small agile team?
Jira can be complex out of the box, but you can strip away unused fields and workflows to make it lightweight. However, if you prefer a tool that feels simple from day one, Linear or Asana may be a better fit.
Can Linear replace Shortcut for teams that include non-developers?
Linear is designed primarily for developers and tech leads. Non-developer roles like product managers and designers can still use it, but they may find Asana easier for task management across different departments.
