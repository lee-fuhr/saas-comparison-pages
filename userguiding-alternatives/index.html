If you are a SaaS company focused on self-service onboarding, you have likely evaluated UserGuiding for product walkthroughs and user guidance. But you may be exploring other options due to pricing, feature gaps, or scalability needs. This article compares UserGuiding against four popular alternatives: Appcues, WalkMe, Chameleon, and Gainsight PX. Each tool has its own strengths for delivering in-app onboarding, so understanding the differences can help you choose the right fit for your product and team.

alternative

Appcues

offers a no-code builder for product tours, checklists, and tooltips, making it easy for nontechnical teams to create onboarding flows. It integrates well with analytics platforms like Amplitude and Segment, which helps teams measure activation and retention. However, its self-service tier may become costly as your user base grows, and advanced targeting options are limited compared to enterprise-level tools.

alternative

WalkMe

provides a robust digital adoption platform with in-app guidance, automation, and analytics. It is well suited for complex enterprise products that require contextual help and process automation across multiple steps. The setup can be resource intensive, and the pricing often requires a sales conversation, so it may not be ideal for early-stage SaaS companies wanting a quick, low-cost start.

alternative

Chameleon

focuses on user onboarding and engagement with tooltips, modals, surveys, and checklists that are highly customizable. It offers a flexible CSS/HTML editor for design control and uses segmentation based on user behavior or attributes. Its pricing scales with monthly active users, and some users note that building complex flows can require more technical know-how than other visual builders.

alternative

Gainsight PX

(formerly known as Inline Manual) is a product experience platform that combines onboarding with in-app surveys and digital adoption analytics. It is strong for companies that need to tie onboarding directly to customer health scores and product usage data. The interface has a steeper learning curve, and it is often a better match for B2B SaaS organizations with existing Gainsight investment or a dedicated customer success team.

How they compare

UserGuiding is a mid-market alternative that balances ease of use with features like NPS surveys and resource centers. Appcues excels in simplicity and analytics integrations but can get expensive. WalkMe is the most powerful for complex enterprise workflows but requires more time and budget. Chameleon offers deep customization for design-conscious teams. Gainsight PX is best for those already using Gainsight for customer success insights. Your choice will depend on your team size, technical skill, budget, and how closely you need onboarding to connect with product analytics and customer health.
