Best 6sense alternatives

Enterprises that rely on AI-driven intent signals to focus sales and marketing efforts on high-value accounts often turn to 6sense for its broad data coverage and orchestration features. However, 6sense may not suit every budget, tech stack, or use case. If you need a different balance of cost, data sources, or integration simplicity, several proven alternatives can deliver the predictive intent insights your team needs. Below we explore five tools that compete with 6sense in the enterprise intent signal space.

alternative

MadKudu

focuses on predictive lead scoring using a combination of first-party behavioral data and firmographics. It integrates directly with your CRM and marketing automation to automatically rank accounts based on purchase likelihood. MadKudu is a strong option for enterprises that already have rich first-party data and want a lightweight, scoring-only engine without a full suite of campaign tools.

alternative

Lattice Engines

(now part of Dun & Bradstreet) provides predictive analytics that blends firmographic, technographic, and third-party intent data. It helps sales teams identify accounts showing early buying signals and prioritize outreach. Lattice Engines is particularly suited for organizations that need deep data enrichment alongside intent scoring, especially in complex B2B sales cycles.

alternative

Mintigo

uses artificial intelligence to analyze hundreds of data points from public and private sources to uncover hidden buying intent. It offers account-based marketing features such as audience segmentation and look-alike modeling. Mintigo works well for companies that want a full account intelligence platform rather than a standalone intent data feed.

alternative

Infer

(acquired by Microsoft) was originally built to predict which prospects are most likely to convert using internal CRM data and external signals. While it is no longer sold as a standalone product, its underlying technology lives on in Microsoft Dynamics 365 for Sales insights. Enterprises already invested in the Microsoft ecosystem can access similar predictive intent capabilities without a separate vendor.

alternative

Bombora

provides intent data by analyzing the content consumption of a company’s employees across a large network of business websites. It surfaces “surge” topics and gives enterprises raw intent scores they can integrate into their own CRM or ABM platform. Bombora is ideal for teams that want to buy intent data as a service rather than a full predictive scoring platform.

How they compare

MadKudu and Lattice Engines excel at predictive scoring that blends internal and external data, with MadKudu being more lightweight and Lattice offering richer firmographic enrichment. Mintigo and Bombora lean farther into intent signal detection, but Mintigo adds modeling and segmentation while Bombora provides raw data that you can plug into any system. Infer is no longer a standalone option, though its legacy lives on in Microsoft’s sales tools. Pricing varies widely: Bombora charges per contact or per account, MadKudu typically uses a subscription based on data volume, and Lattice and Mintigo require annual contracts. Choose based on whether you need a full platform (Mintigo), pure intent data (Bombora), or a scoring layer on top of your existing CRM (MadKudu).

Frequently asked questions

Are these alternatives less expensive than 6sense?
Generally yes, especially if you only need intent signals or predictive scoring rather than 6sense’s full marketing orchestration suite. MadKudu and Bombora often offer lower entry points, while Lattice and Mintigo may have comparable pricing to 6sense for enterprise accounts.
Which tool integrates best with Salesforce or HubSpot?
MadKudu and Bombora have native integrations with both Salesforce and HubSpot. Lattice Engines also connects well with Salesforce, while Mintigo supports major CRMs and marketing automation platforms. Check each vendor’s latest integration list, as availability can change.
