If you use Loom to record and share async video messages but your team’s subscription costs are climbing, you’re not alone. As your usage grows, Loom’s paid plans can become expensive, especially for teams that need longer recordings, unlimited storage, or team management features. Fortunately, several solid alternatives offer similar functionality at a lower price point, or even a free tier with fewer limits. Below we compare five Loom alternatives: Vidyard, Berrycast, Tella, Claap, and Arcade. Each has unique strengths, so you can pick the one that best fits your workflow and budget.

alternative

Vidyard

. Vidyard is a video messaging and hosting platform built for sales and marketing teams. It offers unlimited, free individual recordings up to 30 minutes and includes viewer analytics, custom CTAs, and integration with CRMs like Salesforce. The free plan limits you to 25 videos, but paid plans start at a modest monthly fee and remove that cap while adding team features.

alternative

Berrycast

. Berrycast focuses on simplicity and speed, with a lightweight desktop app that lets you record, edit, and share videos in seconds. It offers an unlimited free plan for individuals with recordings up to one hour, plus features like call-to-action buttons and emoji reactions. The paid team plan is affordable and adds team workspaces, custom branding, and advanced privacy controls.

alternative

Tella

. Tella is a newer, browser-based screen recorder that emphasizes professional-looking videos without the clutter. It records your screen, camera, and audio simultaneously, then lets you trim and polish the clip before sharing. The free tier caps recordings at 10 minutes and 25 videos per month, but the paid plan is competitively priced and offers longer recordings, team accounts, and unlimited storage.

Try Tella →
alternative

Claap

. Claap is designed for async collaboration, particularly for product teams, with features like time-stamped comments and tasks. It records both screen and camera, and viewers can reply with their own video without leaving the platform. The free plan includes unlimited recordings up to 5 minutes each, and paid plans unlock longer durations, team workspaces, and advanced analytics.

alternative

Arcade

. Arcade is a product demo and walkthrough tool that uses automated, interactive screencapture rather than traditional video recording. It’s ideal for showing software features step by step, with clickable elements and annotations. The free plan allows unlimited demos but includes an Arcade branding watermark, while paid plans remove the watermark and add team collaboration and analytics.

How they compare

When choosing a Loom alternative, consider your core needs: budget, maximum recording length, team size, and whether you need analytics or interactivity. Vidyard and Berrycast both offer generous free tiers for individuals, with Vidyard leaning toward sales teams and Berrycast toward general quick messaging. Tella and Claap are more affordable paid options for teams that want slick video editing (Tella) or async feedback loops (Claap). Arcade is unique: it’s not a direct one-to-one video recorder but excels at creating software demos. For most teams looking to cut Loom costs, Berrycast or Tella provide the closest drop-in replacement with the lowest barrier to entry.

Frequently asked questions

Do any of these alternatives offer a completely free plan with no recording limits?
No single tool gives you truly unlimited free recordings. Berrycast offers unlimited free recordings up to one hour each, making it the most generous. Others, like Vidyard and Tella, limit the number of videos per month or cap recording length on their free tiers. If you need unlimited everything, you will need a paid plan, but most alternatives are cheaper than Loom’s equivalent plans.
Can I easily migrate my existing Loom videos to one of these tools?
Not directly. These tools do not import videos from Loom. You will need to download your Loom videos from your library and then upload them to your new platform. Some tools, like Vidyard, allow bulk uploads via their API or web interface. For a smooth transition, plan to export your most important videos before canceling your Loom subscription.
