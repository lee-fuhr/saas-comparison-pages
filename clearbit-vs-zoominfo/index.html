Clearbit vs ZoomInfo: which should you pick?

Marketing teams need accurate, up-to-date data to personalize campaigns, score leads, and target the right accounts. Two of the most popular data enrichment providers are Clearbit and ZoomInfo. Both help you fill in missing fields like job titles, company size, and industry, but they approach the problem from different angles. This article compares Clearbit versus ZoomInfo so you can decide which fits your team’s workflow, budget, and compliance needs.

Where Clearbit wins

Clearbit excels at real-time enrichment and web visitor identification. If your marketing team relies on forms, lead capture, or website traffic, Clearbit can append data the moment a prospect enters an email address. It also shows you which companies are visiting your site, even before they fill out a form. That makes Clearbit a strong choice for account-based marketing programs that need immediate signals.

Clearbit is generally easier to integrate with modern marketing stacks. It offers native connections to HubSpot, Marketo, Salesforce, and dozens of other tools. The API is lightweight and developer-friendly, so you can enrich data on the fly without heavy batch processing.

For teams that prioritize data privacy, Clearbit tends to be safer. It pulls data from public sources and signals, which means less risk of scraping private contact information. This can simplify compliance with regulations like GDPR and CCPA, especially if you are operating in Europe or highly regulated industries.

Clearbit also wins on pricing transparency for smaller teams. It offers a free tier and flat-rate plans that scale with usage, so you can start small and grow without large upfront contracts.

Where ZoomInfo wins

ZoomInfo is the heavyweight when it comes to volume and depth of contact data. If your marketing team needs direct dial phone numbers, verified work emails, or detailed technographic and firmographic profiles, ZoomInfo’s database is much larger and more complete than Clearbit’s. This makes ZoomInfo versus Clearbit an easy choice for sales-driven teams that want to reach out to cold prospects with as much context as possible.

ZoomInfo also offers strong intent data. It tracks topics a company is researching, keywords they are searching for, and changes in their technology stack. For marketing teams running ABM campaigns, these intent signals help prioritize which accounts deserve more attention.

Another area where ZoomInfo wins is in data refresh frequency. ZoomInfo claims to update its database continuously using a mix of web crawling, publisher partnerships, and crowd-sourced updates. Clearbit’s data freshness depends more on the signals it receives from your own website and third-party APIs.

For enterprise marketing teams with large target account lists, ZoomInfo’s bulk export and list-building tools are more powerful. You can build a list of 10,000 contacts based on job role, company revenue, and industry, then export it to your CRM in minutes.

Verdict

Choose Clearbit if your marketing team values speed, simplicity, and privacy. It works best for real-time enrichment, identifying anonymous website visitors, and integrating with a modern stack without heavy data management overhead. Choose ZoomInfo if you need deep, verified contact data at scale, especially phone numbers, and plan to run cold outbound campaigns alongside marketing automation. In the Clearbit versus ZoomInfo debate, there is no universal winner. Your decision should hinge on whether your primary need is enrichment on the fly (Clearbit) or a massive, pre-built contact database (ZoomInfo). Many larger organizations use both: Clearbit for web signals and forms, ZoomInfo for list building and sales prospecting.
