Best Heroku alternatives

Heroku ended its free tier in late 2022, leaving many developers searching for a new hosting platform. You likely need something affordable that still offers easy deployment, a generous free plan, or low cost for small projects. This article covers five strong alternatives that can replace Heroku for personal projects, prototypes, and even production apps.

alternative

Railway

focuses on simplicity with Git-based deployments and automatic SSL. It offers a free tier with limited compute and storage, and you pay only for what you use beyond that. Railway works well for full stack apps, databases, and background workers.

Try Railway →
alternative

Render

provides a generous free tier including static sites, web services, and cron jobs. Deployments are automatic from Git, and it supports many runtimes like Node, Python, and Go. Render’s free tier includes 512 MB of RAM and 1 GB of disk per service.

Try Render →
alternative

Fly.io

deploys your apps on global edge servers using a mix of Firecracker microVMs and Cloudflare infrastructure. It offers a free allowance for small apps, and you pay for additional resources like CPU and RAM. Fly.io is ideal for apps that need low latency worldwide.

Try Fly.io →
alternative

Vercel

is optimized for frontend frameworks and static sites, but also supports serverless functions. Its free tier includes generous capacity and builds, making it a top choice for Jamstack projects. Vercel is less suited for backend APIs or stateful services.

alternative

DigitalOcean App Platform

simplifies app hosting with one click deploys from Git, and it includes a free static site tier. Paid plans start at $5 per month for a basic web service with 512 MB RAM. It integrates well with DigitalOcean’s managed databases and storage.

How they compare

All five platforms offer free tiers, but the limits vary. Railway and Render are most similar to Heroku’s old free tier with support for backends and databases. Vercel excels for frontend apps, while Fly.io is best for global edge deployment. DigitalOcean App Platform has a higher starting cost but gives you access to a full cloud ecosystem. For small personal projects, Render or Railway are the easiest drop in replacements. For production apps, consider Fly.io or DigitalOcean for their scalability.

Frequently asked questions

Which Heroku alternative has the most generous free tier?
Render is often considered the most generous, offering 512 MB RAM, 1 GB disk, and free SSL for web services, plus free static sites and cron jobs. Railway also has a free tier, but with lower compute limits.
Can I migrate my existing Heroku app to an alternative with minimal changes?
For most apps, yes. If your app uses a PostgreSQL database, Railway and Render both support PostgreSQL with straightforward migration. You may need to adjust environment variables and buildpacks, but the process is usually smooth with Git based deployments.
