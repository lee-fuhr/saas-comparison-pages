As an ops leader, you know that activity-based revenue analytics can transform how your team forecasts, coaches, and measures performance. Two of the most discussed platforms in this space are People.ai and Clari. People.ai focuses on capturing every sales activity automatically from email, calendar, and CRM, then using that data to drive forecasting, pipeline inspection, and rep coaching. Clari is known for its revenue intelligence and forecasting capabilities, but its approach to activity data is more dependent on manual entry or CRM integration. Choosing the right tool depends on whether you need deep, automated activity capture (People.ai) or a broader revenue platform with strong forecasting (Clari). This article compares these two and highlights three other alternatives for activity-based analytics.

alternative

Clari

is a revenue intelligence platform that excels at forecasting and pipeline management. It ingests data from CRM, email, and calendar, but its activity capture is less granular than People.ai’s, often requiring integration with other tools for full event tracking. Clari’s strength is in predicting deal outcomes and providing executive-level dashboards, making it a strong choice if your priority is top-down forecasting rather than rep-level activity analysis.

alternative

Gong

focuses on recording and analyzing revenue conversations across calls, video, and email. While it provides deep insights into talk patterns, objection handling, and competitive positioning, it is not a comprehensive activity tracker for all sales motions. Gong is best suited for teams that want to improve messaging and coaching through conversation intelligence, complementing rather than replacing activity-based analytics.

alternative

Chorus.ai

(now part of ZoomInfo) is similar to Gong in that it records and transcribes sales calls, then uses AI to surface key moments and talk track effectiveness. It offers good activity analytics for calls but does not capture other activities like emails or CRM updates natively. Ops leaders will find it valuable for call coaching and deal inspection, but it lacks the broad activity-based forecasting that People.ai provides.

alternative

Outreach Signal

is part of the Outreach sales engagement platform. It tracks email opens, clicks, call outcomes, and meeting activity from your outreach sequences. Signal is excellent for understanding which touchpoints move deals forward, but it is tied directly to Outreach’s sequence-based workflows. If your team uses Outreach for prospecting, Signal gives you clear activity analytics; otherwise, it may feel too narrow for full pipeline analytics.

How they compare

People.ai and Clari both help forecast revenue and analyze pipeline health, but they differ in how they collect and use activity data. People.ai automatically captures every rep activity from native integrations (email, calendar, phone, CRM) and builds a complete activity history with no manual logging. This makes it ideal for ops leaders who want to see which activities correlate with closed deals, run coaching programs based on real behavior, and forecast with high accuracy using actual activity trends.

Clari relies more heavily on CRM data and manual logging, though it can integrate with enrichment and conversation tools. Its forecasting models are sophisticated for predicting deal stage progression and overall revenue, but you get less visibility into the day-to-day activities that drive those numbers. For teams that already have strong CRM hygiene and want a revenue intelligence layer on top, Clari works well. For teams that need to fix activity data quality and move from gut-feel to activity-driven insights, People.ai is the stronger choice.

Both tools support revenue operations, but People.ai leans into activity analytics for coaching and rep productivity, while Clari leans into executive forecasting and deal risk scoring. Your decision should hinge on whether your biggest need is “show me what my reps do to win” or “show me a reliable forecast for my board.”
