If you are a solo practitioner, you need a practice management tool that keeps billing simple and document storage organized without a steep learning curve. MyCase is a popular choice, but it isn’t the only option. This guide covers four strong MyCase alternatives: Clio, PracticePanther, Smokeball, and Filevine. Each offers different strengths for solo lawyers who want straightforward billing and dependable document management.

alternative

Clio

. Clio is the industry standard with a full suite of practice management features. Its billing module includes trust accounting and online payments, while document storage integrates with cloud services like Google Drive and Dropbox. Solo practitioners appreciate Clio’s clean interface and strong mobile app, though it can feel more feature-rich than some need.

alternative

PracticePanther

. PracticePanther focuses on usability and automation. Its billing includes recurring invoices and automatic payment reminders, and document storage allows drag-and-drop uploads with version control. Solo lawyers often choose it for its modern look and built-in client portal, which makes sharing documents easy.

alternative

Smokeball

. Smokeball sets itself apart with automatic time capture and deep document automation. The billing system connects directly to your email and calendar to log time, and document storage auto-saves and organizes files from your work. It is especially useful for solo practitioners who do high-volume transactional work and want every billable minute tracked.

alternative

Filevine

. Filevine is built with project management at its core, making it ideal for case-heavy practices like personal injury. Its billing handles time tracking and flat fees, and document storage is folder-based with powerful search. Solo practitioners who manage many active cases will like its task automation and deadline tracking, though it may be overkill for simpler billing needs.

How they compare

For a solo practitioner who wants simple billing and document storage, the best choice depends on your workflow. MyCase offers an all-in-one solution with integrated billing and storage in a user-friendly package. Clio provides more advanced features and integrations, but with a higher price point. PracticePanther emphasizes automation and client communication at a mid-range cost. Smokeball excels at automatic time capture for those who bill by the hour. Filevine is strongest for project-heavy practices but can be complex for straightforward needs. Every option has a free trial, so test the one that matches your daily tasks.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is the easiest to set up for a solo practitioner?
PracticePanther and MyCase are generally considered the easiest because they have guided onboarding and intuitive interfaces. Both allow you to start billing and uploading documents within minutes without needing to configure many settings.
Can I import my existing documents and billing data from MyCase into another tool?
Yes, most alternatives offer data import services or CSV templates. Clio, PracticePanther, and Smokeball all have dedicated migration teams or self-service tools. Check each provider’s support page for specific instructions, and always back up your files before starting the transfer.
