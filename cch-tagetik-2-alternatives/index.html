For enterprises that need to consolidate financial data across dozens or hundreds of legal entities, CCH Tagetik reporting provides a unified platform that handles complex multi-currency, intercompany, and ownership scenarios. Built on a single data model, CCH Tagetik reduces manual spreadsheet work and gives finance teams a single source of truth for actuals, budgets, forecasts, and statutory reporting. Its built-in intelligence and workflow automation help large organizations close faster while staying compliant with local and international accounting standards.

alternative

BlackLine

. BlackLine focuses on automating the financial close process, including account reconciliations, task management, and consolidation. Its reporting capabilities are strong for transactional-level detail and variance analysis, but enterprises with very complex multi-entity ownership structures may find BlackLine’s consolidation module less flexible than a dedicated platform.

alternative

FloQast

. FloQast is designed to streamline the month-end close with checklist workflows, tie-out reports, and integration with major ERP systems. It excels at improving close efficiency and audit readiness, though its reporting tends to be more operational and less suited for multi-entity statutory consolidation with shifting ownership percentages.

alternative

Trintech

. Trintech provides a comprehensive suite for financial close, reconciliation, and intercompany management. Its Cadence platform offers powerful reporting dashboards and compliance controls, making it a strong option for large enterprises. However, its consolidation features may require more manual configuration for complex equity eliminations and partial ownership rules.

alternative

Adra

. Adra by Trintech is a focused solution for account reconciliations and transaction matching, with some reporting for close status. While it improves data accuracy and reduces close time, it does not offer native multi-entity consolidation reporting. Enterprises needing full consolidation are better served by a dedicated consolidation tool.
