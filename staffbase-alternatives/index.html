If your organization is evaluating Staffbase for a mobile-first employee intranet, you likely value a tool that connects frontline and desk workers in a single, easy-to-use app. Staffbase is a strong choice, but it is not the only option. As an HR leader, you need a platform that prioritizes mobile experience, employee engagement, and simple administration. Below are four viable Staffbase alternatives, each with its own strengths for different company sizes, industries, and communication styles.

alternative

Beekeeper

focuses heavily on the frontline workforce, offering a mobile-first intranet with integrated workflow tools like checklists, shift scheduling, and messaging. It works well for industries such as manufacturing, hospitality, and retail where employees may not have a company email or computer.

alternative

Simpplr

excels at creating a modern, consumer-grade intranet that combines content management with employee communication. Its mobile app is highly customizable, and it integrates deeply with Microsoft 365 and other enterprise tools, making it a good fit for knowledge workers and larger organizations.

alternative

Workvivo

puts employee engagement and culture at the center, with a mobile app that feels like a social feed. It offers features like employee recognition, digital celebrations, and user-generated content, which help build community across remote and on-site teams.

alternative

Firstup

(formerly known as SocialChorus) is built for multi-channel communication, allowing HR leaders to reach employees via mobile app, email, SMS, and digital signage. It offers strong audience segmentation and analytics, making it ideal for large, distributed organizations with diverse communication needs.

How they compare

All four alternatives provide mobile-first employee intranet capabilities, but they differ in focus. Beekeeper is best for frontline-heavy industries. Simpplr is ideal for content-rich, knowledge-oriented organizations. Workvivo prioritizes culture and community. Firstup excels at multi-channel reach and targeting. Your choice should depend on your workforce type, existing tech stack, and primary communication challenges.

Frequently asked questions

What should I look for in a mobile-first employee intranet for a non-desk workforce?
Prioritize offline access, simple navigation, push notifications, and integration with operational tools like scheduling or task management. The app should work on personal devices without requiring a corporate email login.
Can I replace Staffbase with one of these alternatives without losing existing integrations?
Yes, but you must check each tool’s native integrations with your current systems, such as Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, or HRIS platforms. Most alternatives offer APIs and pre-built connectors, but migration effort will vary.
