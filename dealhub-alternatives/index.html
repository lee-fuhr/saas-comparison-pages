Best DealHub alternatives

DealHub is a popular configure price quote (CPQ) platform that helps SaaS and technology vendors deliver guided selling experiences. However, some teams look for alternatives because of pricing, integration requirements, or feature gaps in their specific sales process. This article covers five strong alternatives: Salesforce CPQ, Apttus, Conga CPQ, Oracle CPQ, and Zuora CPQ. Each tool has its own strengths for guided selling, so the right choice depends on your tech stack, deal complexity, and revenue model.

alternative

Salesforce CPQ

. If your sales team already lives inside Salesforce, this alternative offers deep native integration and a familiar interface. It handles complex pricing, product rules, and approval workflows well, but it can become costly as you add users and advanced features.

alternative

Apttus

. Apttus (historically a standalone CPQ provider) focuses on complex quoting and contract lifecycle management for enterprise deals. Its guided selling logic works well for multi-tier products and services, though the platform may require more upfront configuration and training compared to lighter tools.

alternative

Conga CPQ

. Formerly part of the same product lineage as Apttus, Conga CPQ combines robust document generation with guided selling flows. It integrates tightly with Salesforce and also offers a standalone option, making it a flexible choice for vendors that need strong proposal automation alongside quoting.

alternative

Oracle CPQ

. Oracle’s solution is built for large, global organizations that need to manage massive product catalogs, sophisticated pricing rules, and multi-language support. It delivers powerful guided selling, but the cost and implementation complexity often make it better suited for enterprises with dedicated IT resources.

alternative

Zuora CPQ

. Designed for subscription and usage based revenue models, Zuora CPQ excels at guiding sellers through recurring revenue deals. It integrates closely with Zuora Billing and RevPro, which is ideal for SaaS vendors, but it may feel overkill if your business deals mostly in one time transactions.

How they compare

Salesforce CPQ is best if you already use Salesforce and want a seamless add on. Apttus and Conga CPQ are strong for enterprises that need deep contract automation, with Conga leaning more on document generation. Oracle CPQ handles the largest scale and most complex product configurations, but at a higher cost and setup time. Zuora CPQ is the clear winner for subscription businesses that need guided selling tied to billing. All five can replace DealHub, but your final choice should match your CRM, deal size, and revenue type.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a free or low cost alternative to DealHub for small SaaS vendors?
None of the alternatives listed are free. Salesforce CPQ, Apttus, Conga CPQ, Oracle CPQ, and Zuora CPQ all charge per user or per subscription, often with significant onboarding fees. For small vendors, consider starting with a platform like Salesforce CPQ if you already use Salesforce, or explore lighter CPQ add ons that may have lower entry points.
Which alternative works best if I want strong guided selling without a big upfront investment?
Conga CPQ often strikes a good balance because it can run on top of Salesforce (which many vendors already have) and offers tiered pricing. Its guided selling features are solid for medium sized product catalogs, and the implementation time is typically shorter than Oracle or Apttus.
