Best Teachable alternatives

If you are a course creator or coach selling online courses, you might be looking for Teachable alternatives. Teachable is a solid platform, but it may not fit every budget, niche, or feature wish list. Some creators want lower transaction fees, better built-in marketing, or more advanced course customization. Others need an all-in-one solution that includes coaching tools or website hosting. Below we walk through five common alternatives so you can compare what each one offers.

alternative

Thinkific

is a direct competitor to Teachable that focuses on course creation and student management. It offers a free plan with unlimited courses and students, which makes it a good starting point for new creators. Thinkific also includes built-in quizzes, surveys, and a drag-and-drop builder, but its email marketing and affiliate features are more basic than some other platforms.

Try Thinkific →
alternative

Kajabi

is an all-in-one platform that combines course hosting, email marketing, sales funnels, and website building. It is more expensive than Teachable but saves you from paying for separate tools like Mailchimp or ClickFunnels. This makes it ideal for coaches who want to manage their entire business from one dashboard without technical hassle.

Try Kajabi →
alternative

Podia

is a simple and affordable alternative that focuses on ease of use and no transaction fees on its higher plans. It supports courses, digital downloads, memberships, and coaching sessions in one platform. Podia lacks advanced quizzes and assessments, but its straightforward interface and flat monthly pricing appeal to creators who want minimal complexity.

Try Podia →
alternative

LearnWorlds

is designed for creators who want highly interactive and branded courses. It includes a built-in video editor with interactive elements, such as notes and transcripts, plus advanced assessments and certificates. LearnWorlds also supports a white-label mobile app, which is rare among Teachable alternatives, but its pricing is higher and the interface can feel more complex.

alternative

Udemy

is a marketplace where you can upload your courses for sale, not a software platform for hosting your own school. It is free to enroll and publish but takes a large revenue share, especially if students come from Udemy’s organic traffic. Udemy is best for reaching a broad audience quickly, but you have little control over pricing, branding, and student relationships.

How they compare

All five alternatives serve different needs. Thinkific and Podia are the closest to Teachable in terms of simplicity and pricing, with Podia offering no transaction fees on its paid plans and Thinkific offering a generous free plan. Kajabi is the most expensive but provides a full business ecosystem. LearnWorlds excels at interactivity and mobile apps. Udemy is a marketplace rather than a platform, so it is only an alternative if you are willing to give up control and accept lower margins. Choose based on your budget, desired features (email marketing, coaching, interactivity), and how much control you want over branding and pricing.

Frequently asked questions

Which Teachable alternative has the lowest fees?
Podia’s paid plans have no transaction fees, and Thinkific’s free plan also avoids transaction fees (though it has limited features). Kajabi and LearnWorlds have no transaction fees on their standard plans but carry higher monthly costs. Udemy takes a significant revenue share, up to 63% on organic sales.
Can I migrate my existing Teachable courses to another platform easily?
Most alternatives offer import tools or support for common course file formats like SCORM, CSV, or direct content migration. You will typically need to re-upload videos, quizzes, and text lessons manually or use a migration service. Check each platform’s help center for specific instructions before switching.
