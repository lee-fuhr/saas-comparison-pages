Best Teamflow alternatives

If your distributed team relies on Teamflow for low-latency video and avatar based collaboration, you might be looking for other options that offer similar real time presence. Teamflow creates a virtual office where you move your avatar between rooms and video activates automatically. Below we cover five alternatives that also emphasize avatar movement and smooth video feeds, helping you compare features like latency, customization, and ease of use.

alternative

SpatialChat

. This platform uses spatial audio and video that fades as you move your avatar away from others. It works well for casual standups or social events because you can freely walk between rooms without loading delays. The interface is simple, but you cannot change your avatar appearance beyond a static image.

alternative

VirBELA

. VirBELA focuses on large virtual environments, such as entire office floors or conference halls, with full 3D avatars. It supports low latency video calls when you approach someone, and you can customize your avatar’s look. The tradeoff is a heavier download and steeper learning curve for new users.

alternative

Hubs

. Built by Mozilla, Hubs runs entirely in a web browser and uses 3D scenes with avatar based video chat. You can create custom rooms with uploaded models, and latency is generally low for browser based tech. However, the video quality can drop if your internet connection is unstable, and the avatar options are basic.

alternative

Meetion

. Meetion combines a 2D map interface with video icons that open live feeds when you click on a colleague. It keeps video latency low because you only stream when you interact, and the layout resembles a floor plan. The downside is that there are no animated avatars – you only see profile pictures.

alternative

Remo

. Remo shows a floor plan with tables that you can drag your avatar onto, automatically connecting your video and audio with others at the same table. It is designed for networking events or all hands meetings, and the latency for video is similar to standard web conferencing. Avatar movement is limited to clicking preset seats, not free walking.

How they compare

All five alternatives provide low latency video and avatar interaction, but they differ in how much control you have over the environment and avatars. SpatialChat and Remo are the easiest to pick up, while VirBELA and Hubs offer more immersive 3D worlds. Meetion is the most lightweight option for teams that just want a map based presence. If free movement and avatar appearance matter most, VirBELA leads; for simplicity and low capacity, choose Meetion or SpatialChat.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative has the lowest video latency for large teams?
SpatialChat and Remo typically maintain low latency because they use standard peer to peer video connections in small groups. For larger events, VirBELA may introduce a slight delay due to its 3D rendering, but it still performs well on dedicated servers.
Can I import my own 3D models into any of these tools?
Only Hubs and VirBELA allow you to upload custom 3D models for rooms or objects. The other tools have fixed layouts and avatar options, so you cannot add your own assets.
