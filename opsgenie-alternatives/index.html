If you are evaluating OpsGenie alternatives, you are likely looking for a reliable incident management platform that fits your team’s workflows and budget. OpsGenie is a strong tool, but factors like pricing changes, feature gaps, or integration preferences may lead you to explore other options. The following alternatives are popular among DevOps engineers for on-call scheduling, alert routing, and incident response.

alternative

PagerDuty

. PagerDuty is the most widely adopted incident management platform, known for its robust automation, machine learning based noise reduction, and deep integrations with monitoring tools like Prometheus and Datadog. It offers flexible on-call schedules, escalation policies, and a mature API that many DevOps teams rely on for custom workflows.

alternative

VictorOps

. VictorOps (now Splunk On-Call) focuses on team collaboration with built-in chatops features and a timeline view that shows the full incident lifecycle. It is a good fit for teams that want real time visibility into who is on call and what actions have been taken during an incident.

alternative

xMatters

. xMatters (now part of Derdack) excels in advanced on-call scheduling with support for complex rotation patterns and location based escalation. It also offers prebuilt integrations with IT service management (ITSM) tools like ServiceNow, making it suitable for enterprises that need to agree incident response with change management processes.

alternative

Splunk On-Call

. Splunk On-Call (formerly VictorOps) is a solid choice if your team already uses the Splunk ecosystem for observability. It provides tight integration with Splunk’s search and alerting capabilities, along with mobile friendly interfaces and customizable notification rules.

How they compare

All four alternatives offer on-call scheduling, escalations, and integrations with common monitoring tools. PagerDuty leads in market share and automation features but can be more expensive at scale. xMatters is best for complex scheduling and ITSM alignment. Splunk On-Call and VictorOps (now the same product) shine in collaboration and Splunk ecosystem integration. Pricing varies by team size and feature tier, so test a trial with your typical alert volume before committing.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a small DevOps team with a tight budget?
xMatters and Splunk On-Call often have more flexible pricing for smaller teams, and both offer free tiers or trial periods. Evaluate your specific alert volume and integration needs to find the most cost effective option.
Can I migrate my on-call schedules and escalation policies from OpsGenie to these tools easily?
Most tools provide REST APIs and import features for schedules and user data. Expect some manual mapping of rotations and rules, especially if you use complex patterns. Check each vendor’s migration documentation for specific guidance.
