Best Magento alternatives

Magento has long been a go to choice for enterprise merchants who need full control over their online store. But its high cost of ownership, steep learning curve, and demanding server requirements lead many businesses to look for a simpler or more affordable solution. This article compares three popular Magento alternatives – Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce – with a focus on enterprise merchants who want a highly customizable, self‑hosted platform. Note that only WooCommerce is truly self‑hosted; Shopify and BigCommerce are cloud‑based, but they still offer strong customization options for many enterprise needs.

alternative

Shopify

is a fully hosted, all‑in‑one platform that handles hosting, security, and updates for you. It offers a large app marketplace and a range of themes, but deep customization beyond what apps provide can be limited. Enterprise merchants on Shopify often use its “Shopify Plus” tier for higher performance and dedicated support, though you will not have direct server access.

alternative

BigCommerce

is another cloud‑based solution that comes with built‑in features like multi‑storefront management and strong SEO tools. It supports headless commerce, which gives you more flexibility in designing the front end. However, like Shopify, you cannot modify the server environment or host the platform on your own infrastructure.

Try BigCommerce →
alternative

WooCommerce

is the only self‑hosted alternative on this list, running as a plugin on top of WordPress. It gives you full ownership of your data and server, along with near‑unlimited customization through code and thousands of extensions. Because you control the hosting, you can scale resources as needed, but you are also responsible for maintenance, security, and performance optimization.

Try WooCommerce →

How they compare

- **Hosting model**: WooCommerce is self‑hosted; Shopify and BigCommerce are cloud‑based. - **Customization depth**: WooCommerce offers the most control via code and a large plugin ecosystem. Shopify and BigCommerce limit server‑level changes but provide robust APIs and headless options. - **Total cost**: WooCommerce has lower starting costs (only hosting and plugin fees) but can become expensive with advanced extensions. Shopify and BigCommerce have monthly subscription fees that include hosting and support. - **Ease of use**: Shopify and BigCommerce are easier to set up and maintain. WooCommerce requires more technical knowledge or a dedicated development team. - **Scalability**: All three can handle enterprise traffic, but WooCommerce depends on your hosting provider’s capabilities. Shopify Plus and BigCommerce Enterprise offer managed scaling.
