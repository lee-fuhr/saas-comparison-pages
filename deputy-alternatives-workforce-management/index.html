As an operations manager overseeing multiple hospitality locations, you need scheduling software that saves time and reduces errors across sites. Deputy is a popular choice for automated scheduling, but your specific workflows might benefit from other options. This article covers three notable alternatives: When I Work, 7shifts, and Shiftboard.

How they compare

When I Work is best for teams wanting a balance of simplicity and affordability without industry specific bells and whistles. 7shifts excels in restaurant focused features such as tip management, POS integrations, and compliance alerts. Shiftboard suits larger operations with complex scheduling rules, shift bidding, and high employee volumes. All three offer mobile access and multi location support, but their strengths differ. Deputy provides solid general scheduling, but these alternatives may better address particular hospitality pain points, so consider which workflow gaps matter most to your team.

Frequently asked questions

How do I decide between Deputy and When I Work for my hotel group?
Consider your need for restaurant specific features. Deputy offers strong general scheduling and time tracking, but When I Work has simpler pricing and built in shift swapping that may reduce admin work. If you run restaurants or bars, 7shifts might be a better fit. Test free trials to see which interface your managers and staff prefer.
Can these alternatives handle multiple locations with different labor laws?
Yes, all three support location based compliance settings. 7shifts and Shiftboard include alerts for overtime and breaks based on local laws. When I Work also allows you to set location specific rules. For large groups with varying state or city regulations, Shiftboard offers the most granular control over scheduling constraints.
