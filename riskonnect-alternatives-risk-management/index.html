As a VP of Compliance at a healthcare organization, you know that integrated risk management (IRM) is critical for navigating HIPAA, GDPR, payer requirements, and emerging cyber threats. Riskonnect has been a go-to platform for many, but it may not be the perfect fit for every organization’s culture, budget, or specific compliance focus. This article explores three strong alternatives (OneTrust, LogicManager, MetricStream) and how they stack up against Riskonnect for healthcare IRM needs.

alternative

OneTrust

. OneTrust emphasizes privacy, ethics, and governance, making it a natural fit if your organization’s primary compliance driver is data protection and its related frameworks. Its pre-built content for HIPAA and global privacy regulations reduces setup time, and the platform’s workflow engine helps automate vendor risk assessments and breach notification processes.

alternative

LogicManager

. LogicManager focuses on turning risk into opportunity through a structured, user-friendly interface that encourages cross-departmental engagement. Healthcare compliance teams appreciate its customizable risk registers and built-in controls for HIPAA safety standards, as well as its low-code approach that lets you adapt the tool without heavy IT involvement.

alternative

MetricStream

. MetricStream offers an enterprise-grade GRC platform with deep modules for audit management, third-party risk, and regulatory change management. For large health systems with multiple facilities and complex reporting requirements, MetricStream provides the scalability and integration power needed to unify risk data across clinical, operational, and compliance silos.

How they compare

Riskonnect remains a strong all-around IRM choice, especially for organizations that value configurable dashboards and a broad risk library. OneTrust leads in privacy-focused use cases and regulatory content, making it ideal for compliance teams that prioritize data protection over other risk domains. LogicManager wins on ease of adoption and speed to value for smaller to mid-sized healthcare providers, while MetricStream is best for large enterprises that need to manage high volumes of risks across many departments. Your decision should balance implementation complexity, total cost of ownership, and the specific compliance gaps you need to close.
