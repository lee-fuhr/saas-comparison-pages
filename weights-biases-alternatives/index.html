Best Weights & Biases alternatives

Weights & Biases is a popular experiment tracking platform, but it may not suit every team. Some need a self hosted solution, lower cost, or different integration patterns. Here we compare four common alternatives: MLflow, Comet ML, Neptune.ai, and DVC.

alternative

MLflow

: MLflow is an open source platform with three core components: tracking, projects, and models. It works well for teams that want full control over their infrastructure and prefer not to rely on a third party service. Its tracking server can be self hosted, and it integrates with many popular frameworks.

alternative

Comet ML

: Comet ML offers a cloud based experiment tracking service with a generous free tier for individual researchers. It provides automated logging, artifact storage, and model registry features. Teams that want a hosted solution similar to Weights & Biases but with different pricing may consider Comet.

alternative

Neptune.ai

: Neptune.ai is a metadata store designed for MLOps, supporting experiment tracking, model registry, and monitoring. It has a strong focus on collaboration and organizes runs into projects with rich search capabilities. It offers both cloud and self hosted options.

alternative

DVC

: DVC (Data Version Control) is not a traditional experiment tracker but focuses on versioning data and machine learning pipelines alongside code. It works best for teams already using Git and wanting to track data lineage and model reproducibility. DVC does not provide a web dashboard by default, but you can use DVC Studio for a visual interface.

How they compare

Weights & Biases is the most full featured but comes with usage limits on the free tier and costs can grow. MLflow is the most open and customizable, especially for self hosting. Comet ML offers a similar hosted experience with a free tier for small teams. Neptune.ai is robust for larger collaborative projects with strong metadata management. DVC is best for teams that prioritize data versioning and pipeline tracking over a rich UI.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is the best free option for a small team?
MLflow is free and open source, so you can run it on your own hardware with no per user cost. Comet ML also has a free tier that includes unlimited public projects and some private projects.
I need to track hyperparameters and metrics during training. Can I use DVC for that?
DVC does not directly track hyperparameters and metrics in a dashboard. You would need to combine DVC with a tool like MLflow or manually log metrics using its metrics feature. For a seamless experiment tracking dashboard, consider MLflow or Neptune.ai instead.
