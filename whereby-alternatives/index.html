Best Whereby alternatives

If you use Whereby for simple browser-based video calls but are looking for other options maybe due to cost, features, or reliability, you are in the right place. Whereby is known for being easy to use with no downloads required, but other tools can offer similar convenience with different trade-offs. This article covers five Whereby alternatives that work well for small teams and creators who want a straightforward video call experience without heavy setup.

alternative

Zoom

. Zoom is a widely used video conferencing tool that offers a free tier for up to 40 minute group calls and up to 100 participants. It requires a download for the best experience, but you can join from a browser with limited features. Zoom includes recording, screen sharing, and breakout rooms, making it a strong option if you need more than simple calls.

Try Zoom →
alternative

Google Meet

. Google Meet is built into Google Workspace and works entirely in a browser without any software install. It supports up to 100 participants on the free plan with a 60 minute limit for group calls. Meet integrates well with Google Calendar and Gmail, which is handy if your team already uses those tools.

Try Google Meet →
alternative

Daily.co

. Daily.co is designed for developers and teams who want to embed video calls into their own products, but it also offers a simple browser-based room for direct use. The free plan allows one room with up to 50 participants and no time limit on calls. Daily.co focuses on reliability and low latency, which helps for real time conversations.

alternative

Jitsi

. Jitsi is an open source video conferencing platform that runs entirely in the browser with no account required to start a call. It supports unlimited participants and offers features like screen sharing and live streaming. Because it is self hosted or available on meet.jit.si, you have more control over privacy and data but may need technical help to set up your own server.

alternative

Dyte

. Dyte is a newer option that provides a browser-based video calling experience with an emphasis on customization and developer APIs. The free tier gives you up to 10,000 minutes per month and supports up to 50 participants per room. Dyte works well for creators who want to add video chat to their own website or app without building from scratch.

How they compare

Whereby is best for quick browser calls with a simple link, but its free plan limits participants to 50 and calls to 60 minutes. Zoom offers more features and larger groups but requires a download for full use. Google Meet is excellent for teams already using Google apps and needs no install. Daily.co and Dyte are great for developers who want to embed video calls, while Jitsi is ideal for privacy minded users who want an open source, no account solution. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize ease, features, or control.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use any of these alternatives without creating an account?
Yes, Jitsi and Daily.co allow you to join a call in your browser without signing up. Google Meet and Zoom also let you join meetings without an account if the host sends you a link, though Zoom may require a free account to start a meeting.
Which alternative offers the best free plan for small teams?
Google Meet gives 60 minute group calls for up to 100 participants with no download, and Jitsi offers unlimited participants and call time with no account needed. Daily.co provides one room with no time limit for up to 50 people. Each free plan has different limits, so pick based on your team size and call length needs.
