If your team needs to combine vendor management with a centralized knowledge base, you might be comparing different approaches. Guru Enterprise is not a vendor management system (VMS) itself, but a knowledge management platform that lets you capture, verify, and share expert knowledge across your organization. Workday VMS is a dedicated VMS for managing contingent workers, services procurement, and supplier relationships. The key decision is whether to use a standalone VMS like Workday VMS and then layer on a knowledge base like Guru, or to rely on a VMS that includes some knowledge features. This article focuses on how Guru Enterprise can work alongside a VMS to fill the knowledge gap, and it compares the main VMS alternatives for teams that prioritize knowledge base integration.

alternative

Coupa VMS

. Coupa VMS offers strong procurement and spend management features, including contingent workforce and services procurement. It provides some prebuilt templates and workflows, but its native knowledge base capabilities are limited. Integrating Guru with Coupa VMS can give your team a dedicated place to store onboarding guides, compliance policies, and vendor best practices that are separate from procurement processes.

alternative

Beeline

. Beeline is a leading VMS for managing external talent and services, known for its robust sourcing and compliance tools. It includes a document management module, but not a full knowledge base with real-time verification and suggestions. Pairing Beeline with Guru lets you push curated knowledge about preferred suppliers, rate cards, and engagement processes directly to your workforce while keeping Beeline as the system of record for vendor transactions.

alternative

SAP Fieldglass

. SAP Fieldglass is a large enterprise VMS focused on managing external workers and services, with deep integrations into SAP ecosystem. It has some content management features, but they are designed for contract and rate card storage rather than living knowledge articles. Using Guru alongside Fieldglass helps your procurement, HR, and hiring teams access up-to-date how-to guides and policy answers without leaving their workflow.

alternative

Workday VMS

. Workday VMS is part of the Workday platform and tightly integrated with Workday HCM and Financials. It offers strong analytics and self-service capabilities, but its knowledge base is limited to what you can store in Workday’s standard document or notes fields. To get a true knowledge base with context‑sensitive suggestions and expert verification, you would need to add Guru on top of Workday VMS.

How they compare

Guru Enterprise provides a knowledge layer that any VMS can integrate with, while Workday VMS offers a standalone VMS with limited built‑in knowledge capabilities. If your team values a single platform for both vendor management and knowledge, Workday VMS alone may fall short because it does not include a dynamic, verified knowledge base. The better approach is to use a VMS that fits your procurement needs, such as Coupa, Beeline, SAP Fieldglass, or Workday VMS, and integrate Guru Enterprise to capture and deliver institutional knowledge about vendors, processes, and compliance directly where your team works. This combination gives you the best of both worlds: robust vendor management plus a living knowledge base that reduces ramp‑up time and errors.

Frequently asked questions

Can Guru Enterprise replace a VMS like Workday VMS?
No. Guru is a knowledge management platform, not a vendor management system. It does not handle worker onboarding, purchase orders, time sheets, or invoicing. Instead, Guru complements a VMS by storing and surfacing the knowledge about how to use the VMS, who your preferred vendors are, and what policies apply. You need a dedicated VMS for the operational side of vendor management.
How does Guru integrate with a VMS like Workday VMS?
Guru offers integrations via browser extensions, Slack, Teams, and APIs. You can embed Guru cards directly into Workday forms or use the Guru Slack bot to answer VMS‑related questions. For deeper integration, you can sync vendor data from Workday into Guru using webhooks or custom workflows, so that knowledge articles automatically update when vendor details change.
