If your hiring team relies on data to make decisions, you may have found that Ashby’s analytics fall short of what you need. While Ashby offers a solid ATS with basic reporting, many teams require deeper insights, customizable dashboards, and more granular pipeline metrics. This article explores three strong alternatives for data-driven teams: Greenhouse, Lever, and Workable. Each tool provides a different approach to analytics, so you can choose the one that best fits your hiring process.

alternative

Greenhouse

. Greenhouse offers advanced reporting with customizable scorecards, source effectiveness analysis, and real-time pipeline dashboards. Its built-in diversity and equity reports help teams track hiring funnel health at every stage. You can also export raw data for your own analysis in tools like Tableau or Power BI.

alternative

Lever

. Lever uses a “Nurture” model that tracks candidate engagement over time, giving you metrics on time-to-hire and source-to-hire by role. Its analytics module includes trend graphs, conversion rates, and the ability to filter by recruiter, department, or job posting. Lever also integrates with BI tools for deeper custom reporting.

alternative

Workable

. Workable provides a straightforward analytics dashboard with key hiring metrics such as cost-per-hire, time-to-fill, and source breakdown. You can build custom reports using its drag-and-drop builder and schedule automatic email reports. For teams that prefer simplicity, Workable’s analytics are easy to set up without extra training.

How they compare

Greenhouse leads in advanced reporting and data export flexibility, making it ideal for teams that need to drill into granular pipeline data. Lever excels at tracking candidate journey and engagement over time, which helps optimize sourcing and nurturing strategies. Workable offers the easiest setup for basic hiring metrics and is best for smaller teams that want quick insights without complexity. If you prioritize fully customizable dashboards and raw data access, choose Greenhouse. If long-term candidate behavior analytics matter most, choose Lever. If you need simple, out-of-the-box reports, choose Workable.

Frequently asked questions

Will switching from Ashby to one of these alternatives require a long setup time?
It depends on the tool. Greenhouse and Lever typically take a few weeks to fully configure, especially if you are migrating historical data. Workable offers a faster onboarding process, often within a few days. All three provide onboarding support and API access for data transfer.
Which alternative offers the best analytics for measuring recruiter performance?
Greenhouse gives you the most detailed activity reports, including individual recruiter metrics like time spent per stage and interview feedback scores. Lever also tracks recruiter-specific conversion rates. Workable includes general activity logs but lacks advanced individual performance analytics.
