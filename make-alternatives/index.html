Best Make alternatives

If you are a no-code team using Make (formerly Integromat), you already know how powerful it is for automating complex workflows. But maybe you are hitting limits with pricing, scalability, or the need for self hosting. Or you simply want to compare other platforms to see if one fits your specific use case better. This article covers the main alternatives to Make: Zapier, n8n, Activepieces, Tray.io, and Workato. Each tool has strengths and trade offs, so we break them down in plain language to help you decide.

alternative

Zapier

. Zapier is the most popular automation platform, known for its huge app directory and ease of use. It works best for simple, single step automations, but its multi step workflows (called Zaps) can get expensive fast. For teams already invested in Make’s visual builder, Zapier may feel less flexible for complex logic.

alternative

n8n

. n8n is an open source, self hosted alternative that gives you full control over data and infrastructure. Its node based workflow editor is similar to Make, but you pay for hosting rather than per task. This makes n8n a strong choice for teams that need custom integrations or have high volume automation needs without recurring subscription costs.

Try n8n →
alternative

Activepieces

. Activepieces is a newer, open source platform focused on simplicity and developer friendly features. It offers a visual builder with a clean interface and supports both self hosting and a cloud version. For no-code teams coming from Make, Activepieces may feel more lightweight, though its app library is still smaller than Zapier or Make.

alternative

Tray.io

. Tray.io targets enterprise teams with complex workflow needs and advanced data transformation. Its visual builder is powerful but has a steeper learning curve than Make. Tray.io also uses a connector based pricing model that can be more predictable for high volume users, but it may be overkill for smaller teams.

alternative

Workato

. Workato is an enterprise integration platform (iPaaS) that excels at connecting large business applications like Salesforce, NetSuite, and Workday. It offers robust governance, version control, and team collaboration features. For Make teams scaling up to enterprise needs, Workato is a serious candidate, though its pricing and complexity are higher.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate my existing Make workflows to another platform?
Yes, but it is not automatic. You will need to manually recreate each scenario in the new tool. Some platforms like n8n or Activepieces have similar node based editors, making the translation easier. Exporting JSON definitions from Make and importing is only possible in limited cases, so plan for some rework.
Which alternative is best for a small no-code team on a tight budget?
For a small team, n8n or Activepieces (self hosted) are the most cost effective because they have no per task fees. You only pay for your own server. Zapier is also an option if your automation volume is low, but costs escalate quickly. Make itself remains a strong choice if your current workflow fits within its free tier or low cost plans.
