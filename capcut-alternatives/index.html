Best CapCut alternatives

If you have been using CapCut for mobile video editing but need a change, you are not alone. Maybe the recent restrictions or changes in the app have you looking elsewhere. Whether you want more creative control, a simpler interface, or different pricing options, several capable alternatives can handle your editing needs without a steep learning curve. Here are three solid picks to replace CapCut on your phone.

alternative

InShot

is a straightforward all in one editor that works well for social media clips and short videos. It offers trimming, cutting, speed control, and a wide range of filters and stickers without overwhelming you with advanced tools. You can also adjust video aspect ratios easily, which makes it a favorite for TikTok and Instagram Reels.

alternative

VN Video Editor

gives you a professional timeline layout similar to desktop software, but it stays completely free with no watermark. It supports multiple layers of video, audio, and text, plus keyframe animation for precise movements. Many users appreciate that VN does not push subscription upgrades or limit exports.

alternative

Canva Video

is ideal if you already use Canva for graphics and want to create polished videos with templates. You can drag and drop clips, add transitions, and overlay text or animations directly inside the Canva app. While it is more design focused than traditional video editors, its library of stock elements and easy collaboration features make it a strong CapCut alternative for quick, branded content.

How they compare

InShot is the easiest to learn and great for simple edits on social media. VN Video Editor offers the most powerful free editing tools with a timeline and keyframes. Canva Video is best for template driven, branded projects where you also need graphics or text overlays. All three work on mobile and can replace CapCut depending on which features matter most to you.

Frequently asked questions

Will I lose my CapCut projects if I switch to one of these alternatives?
CapCut projects are stored in its own app format, so you cannot open them directly in InShot, VN, or Canva. You will need to export each finished video from CapCut and then reimport the final clip if you want to edit it further in the new app. Starting fresh with the alternative is usually easier.
Which alternative is best for removing watermarks without paying?
VN Video Editor is fully free and adds no watermark to your exports. InShot offers a free version with a watermark, but you can remove it by watching an ad or upgrading to the paid version. Canva Video requires a paid subscription to remove watermarks on certain stock elements, but basic editing exports are watermark free with a free account.
