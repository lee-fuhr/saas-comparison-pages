As a lifelong learner, you have many online course platforms to choose from. Two of the most popular are Udemy and Coursera. In this article, we focus on Udemy as a primary option, with Coursera as a strong alternative. We will compare them to help you decide which fits your learning style and goals.

alternative

Coursera

partners with top universities and companies to offer courses, specializations, and degrees. Coursera courses typically have a structured schedule and include graded assignments. Many Coursera courses are free to audit, but you pay for a certificate or full access.

How they compare

Udemy offers a huge marketplace of individual courses on nearly any topic, often on sale for very low prices. Courses are self-paced with lifetime access. Coursera offers more structured learning paths from accredited institutions, with quizzes and peer-graded projects. Coursera certificates tend to carry more academic weight, while Udemy certificates are for personal achievement. Udemy is better for exploring a wide range of skills quickly and cheaply. Coursera is better for formal learning or career advancement where university credentials matter.

Frequently asked questions

Can I get a university degree on Udemy?
No, Udemy does not offer degrees or accredited programs. It focuses on individual skill-based courses. For a degree or specialization, consider Coursera.
Do Udemy courses expire?
No, once you purchase a Udemy course, you have lifetime access to the materials, including any updates the instructor makes. However, you may lose access if you close your account or if the course is removed for policy violations.
