You already know Fathom is a solid, privacy-first analytics tool. But maybe you are looking for something cheaper, self-hosted, or with a different feature set. Whether you need a simpler dashboard, longer data retention, or a fully open source option, there are several worthy alternatives built on the same privacy-respecting principles. This article covers five tools that share Fathom’s core philosophy of no cookies, no personal data collection, and lightweight tracking.

alternative

Plausible

. Plausible is the most well-known Fathom alternative with a similar one-page dashboard and hosted service model. It focuses on simplicity and transparency by making its code open source, though the paid cloud plan starts at a lower monthly price than Fathom. Plausible also offers a self-hosted option for those who want full control over their data.

Try Plausible →
alternative

Simple Analytics

. Simple Analytics strips away all clutter to show only the metrics that matter: page views, referrers, countries, and devices. It is designed for bloggers and small businesses who want zero distractions and a privacy promise that goes beyond just not using cookies. The interface is deliberately minimal, and it blocks known privacy-invasive scripts automatically.

Try Simple Analytics →
alternative

Umami

. Umami is a self-hosted, open source alternative that gives you complete ownership of your analytics data. You install it on your own server and customize the dashboard with your own widgets and themes. It is free and lightweight, but requires some technical know-how to set up and maintain, making it ideal for developers or site owners comfortable with server management.

alternative

GoatCounter

. GoatCounter is a privacy-friendly, lightweight analytics tool that can be used as a hosted service or self-hosted. Its free tier offers unlimited sites and pageviews, making it a great choice for budget-conscious site owners. The dashboard is simple but includes features like visitor sessions and detection of bots, and it does not require any JavaScript if you prefer a pixel-based approach.

alternative

Cabin

. Cabin is a newer, fully open source analytics tool that runs on your own infrastructure. It focuses on raw data ownership and offers a clean interface with exportable datasets. Cabin is built for users who want to dig deeper into their traffic without sacrificing privacy, but like Umami and GoatCounter, it requires some setup effort.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate my Fathom data to one of these alternatives?
Most tools do not offer direct import from Fathom, but you can export your Fathom data as a CSV file. Then you can either manually compare metrics or start fresh with the new tool, since historical data is not critical for ongoing analytics.
Which alternative is closest to Fathom in ease of use?
Plausible is the closest match. It has a similar one-page dashboard, a hosted cloud option with a monthly fee, and requires no technical setup. If you want to stay on a hosted service without managing a server, Plausible is the most straightforward switch.
