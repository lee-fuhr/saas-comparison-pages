Best Resolver alternatives

Companies that need both incident management and governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) capabilities often face a choice between specialized platforms. Resolver and LogicGate are two strong options, but they serve different priorities. Resolver focuses on unifying security operations, incident response, and risk management in a single interface. LogicGate offers a flexible GRC platform that lets you build custom workflows and applications. This article compares Resolver with LogicGate and looks at other alternatives to help you decide which tool fits your needs.

alternative

ServiceNow GRC

. ServiceNow GRC is part of the larger ServiceNow platform, which many companies already use for IT service management. It provides risk management, audit, and compliance modules that integrate with incident and case management. The tradeoff is that it works best inside the ServiceNow ecosystem and can become expensive as you add modules.

alternative

MetricStream

. MetricStream is an enterprise-grade GRC platform with deep capabilities for risk, compliance, audit, and third-party management. It is well suited for large, heavily regulated industries like finance and healthcare. However, its complexity and cost can be a barrier for midsize companies or those that need a lighter incident management tool.

alternative

RSA Archer

. RSA Archer is a mature GRC suite that helps organizations manage risk, compliance, and audit processes. It offers a large library of prebuilt content and integrations. The downside is that implementation and ongoing administration often require dedicated resources, and the user interface can feel dated.

alternative

LogicGate

. LogicGate is a low-code GRC platform that lets you build custom applications for risk, compliance, and incident management. It is flexible and supports a wide range of workflows without heavy customization. The main limitation is that its incident management features are not as prebuilt or deep as those in Resolver, especially for security operations.

How they compare

Resolver is built specifically for security operations and incident management, with strong GRC capabilities layered on top. It excels at handling real-time security incidents, automating response workflows, and managing risk in a single console. LogicGate is more of a general-purpose GRC builder, giving you the freedom to design your own processes, but it lacks the out-of-the-box incident response features that security teams need. If your priority is fast, integrated incident management with GRC, Resolver is the stronger choice. If you need a highly customizable GRC tool and incident management is secondary, LogicGate may work better.

Frequently asked questions

Can Resolver handle compliance workflows like reporting and audit management?
Yes, Resolver includes GRC modules for compliance, risk assessments, and audit management, so you can manage incidents and compliance in one platform.
Is LogicGate easier to customize than Resolver?
Yes, LogicGate uses a low-code visual builder that lets you create custom workflows and dashboards without programming, while Resolver offers more preconfigured templates for incident and risk processes.
