UKG is a popular choice for integrated HR and payroll, but some companies find the platform too complex, expensive, or misaligned with their specific workflows. If you are evaluating other options, it helps to look at tools that offer similar all-in-one capabilities. The following alternatives each bring strengths in workforce management, payroll processing, or employee experience that may better fit your organization’s size, industry, or budget.

alternative

Kronos

. Kronos is now part of UKG, so it is not a separate alternative. However, companies that already use legacy Kronos workforce management tools may choose to stay on that platform rather than migrate to the full UKG suite. Evaluate whether your current needs match the older product’s scheduling and time-tracking features.

alternative

ADP Workforce

. ADP Workforce (often called ADP Workforce Now) is a cloud-based HR and payroll platform designed for mid-sized to large businesses. It offers strong payroll compliance, benefits administration, and a range of integrated modules. Its extensive partner network can help with customization, but some users find the interface less intuitive than newer competitors.

alternative

Verint

. Verint focuses on workforce engagement and optimization, especially for contact centers and frontline employees. Its scheduling, forecasting, and quality management tools can integrate with separate payroll systems. Verint is a good fit if your main challenge is managing a large hourly workforce rather than handling full HR administration.

alternative

Workday

. Workday is an enterprise-grade cloud HCM that covers HR, payroll, and financials. It is known for its modern user experience and strong analytics. Workday works best for organizations with over 1,000 employees and a budget for a higher-cost subscription, but it can be overkill for smaller companies seeking a simpler payroll solution.

How they compare

All four alternatives cover core HR and payroll, but they differ in target audience and feature depth. ADP Workforce is the most direct all-in-one competitor to UKG, with similar breadth for mid-market companies. Verint is narrower, excelling in workforce management but requiring separate payroll. Workday offers a premium, unified platform for large enterprises. Kronos (legacy) is not a true alternative unless you are already using it. Pricing varies widely, with ADP and Workday generally costing more than Verint, and UKG often sitting in the middle. Consider your company size, primary pain points (scheduling vs. payroll compliance), and integration needs when choosing.
