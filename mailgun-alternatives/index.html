Best Mailgun alternatives

Mailgun is a popular choice for sending transactional emails, but you may be looking for alternatives due to pricing changes or reliability concerns. Whether you need higher deliverability, simpler pricing, or better support for high volumes, there are several strong options. This article covers five Mailgun alternatives: SendGrid, Postmark, Amazon SES, Brevo, and SparkPost. Each tool has its own strengths for developers who need to send password resets, order confirmations, and other automated emails reliably.

alternative

SendGrid

offers a broad feature set including email APIs, SMTP, and marketing tools. It scales well from startups to enterprises, but its pricing can become complex with add-ons for dedicated IPs and engagement data. For developers who need both transactional and promotional sending in one platform, SendGrid is a common choice.

Try SendGrid →
alternative

Postmark

focuses exclusively on transactional email with high deliverability and fast sending. It has a simple per-email pricing model and no marketing features, which keeps the service lean and reliable. Postmark is ideal if you want predictable costs and a developer-first API with clear documentation.

Try Postmark →
alternative

Amazon SES

is a low-cost option that works well for high-volume senders already using AWS. Pricing is based on usage, and you can send millions of emails per month for a fraction of the cost of dedicated services. However, you need to manage your own deliverability and reputation, and the setup requires more effort.

alternative

Brevo

(formerly Sendinblue) combines transactional email with marketing automation and CRM features. Its pricing is based on the number of emails sent per month, not on contacts. Brevo can be a good fit if you want an all-in-one solution, but its deliverability may vary for high-volume transactional-only use cases.

Try Brevo →
alternative

SparkPost

provides a high-performance email infrastructure designed for large senders. It offers real-time analytics, webhooks, and flexible pricing tiers. SparkPost is a solid choice when you need advanced deliverability tools and can commit to a volume-based contract.

Frequently asked questions

Which Mailgun alternative is cheapest for sending 100,000 transactional emails per month?
Amazon SES is typically the cheapest option at around $10 per month for that volume. Postmark and Brevo have higher per-email costs but simpler pricing. Your total cost may also include fees for data transfer, dedicated IPs, or additional features.
Do any of these alternatives offer free plans or trials for developers?
Yes. SendGrid offers a free plan with 100 emails per day forever. Brevo has a free tier with 300 emails per day. Postmark provides a 30-day trial with 100 emails. Amazon SES is pay-as-you-go with a free tier of 62,000 emails per month if you send from an EC2 instance. SparkPost does not have a free plan but offers a test sending limit.
