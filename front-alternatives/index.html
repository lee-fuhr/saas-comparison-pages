If your support team relies on a shared email inbox, you have likely used or considered Front. Front is great for merging email with internal collaboration, but it is not the only option. Depending on your team size, budget, and workflow needs, other tools might be a better fit. This article covers five popular Front alternatives so you can compare them and decide which one works best for your team.

alternative

Help Scout

. Help Scout focuses on simple, email-based customer support with a clean interface. It offers shared mailboxes, a knowledge base, and helpful reporting without the complexity of a full help desk. Teams that want an easy transition from a basic inbox will appreciate its straightforward setup.

Try Help Scout →
alternative

Zendesk

. Zendesk is a full featured customer service platform that scales from small teams to large enterprises. It provides robust ticket management, automation, multichannel support (email, chat, phone, social), and advanced analytics. Teams expecting high volume or needing deep customization often choose Zendesk over Front.

Try Zendesk →
alternative

Freshdesk

. Freshdesk offers a user friendly interface with strong automation, ticket collision detection, and a built-in knowledge base. It supports email, chat, and phone at competitive price points. Small to medium sized teams looking for an affordable all-in-one help desk often find Freshdesk a solid alternative.

alternative

Intercom

. Intercom is built for conversational support across email, in-app messaging, and live chat. It includes a shared inbox, automated workflows, and customer data tools. Teams that prioritize real time conversations and proactive engagement will like Intercom, though its email features are less traditional than Front’s.

alternative

Missive

. Missive combines a shared email inbox with team chat and task management in one app. It lets you assign conversations, add comments, and track progress without leaving the inbox. Teams that want collaboration tools tightly integrated with email often prefer Missive over Front.

How they compare

All five alternatives share the goal of helping support teams collaborate on email, but they differ in emphasis. Help Scout and Freshdesk are more traditional help desks with strong email management and reporting. Zendesk is the most powerful and customizable, suitable for large operations. Intercom shines for real time chat and messaging, while Missive blends email with chat and task features. Pricing varies widely: Help Scout and Missive are mid range, Freshdesk starts low, Zendesk and Intercom grow with features. Your choice depends on whether you need simple email collaboration (Help Scout, Missive) or a full multichannel platform (Zendesk, Freshdesk, Intercom).

Frequently asked questions

Can I import my data from Front to one of these alternatives?
Yes, most tools offer import options. Help Scout, Zendesk, Freshdesk, Intercom, and Missive all support importing emails and conversations from Front, typically via CSV or direct API. Check each tool’s documentation for the exact steps and any limitations.
Which alternative is easiest to set up for a small team new to help desk software?
Help Scout and Missive are often the easiest because they feel similar to a shared Gmail or Outlook inbox. Both have simple onboarding and require little configuration. Freshdesk also has a user friendly setup, while Zendesk and Intercom may need more time to customize for your workflows.
