Campaign Monitor is a popular choice for designers, offering beautifully coded templates and a drag-and-drop editor. But you might need a different platform if your budget is tight, you want more templates, or you need specific integrations. Here are three strong Campaign Monitor alternatives that keep template quality and ease of editing front and center.

alternative

Mailchimp

. Mailchimp provides a large library of customizable templates with a smooth drag-and-drop builder. Its editor lets you tweak HTML code directly, making it a good fit for designers who want both visual control and the ability to hand-code.

AWeber

. AWeber offers well-coded drag-and-drop templates that are responsive out of the box. The platform includes a template library with modern designs, and its editor is beginner-friendly while still allowing HTML access for deeper customization.

GetResponse

. GetResponse combines a drag-and-drop email builder with a collection of professionally coded templates tailored for marketing campaigns. Its editor is intuitive for visual design, and it supports custom HTML blocks so you can import your own coded designs.

How they compare

All three alternatives give designers drag-and-drop editing and beautifully coded templates. Mailchimp stands out for its template variety and strong integration ecosystem. AWeber focuses on simplicity and reliable deliverability, which is useful for small to medium lists. GetResponse offers a more all-in-one platform with landing pages and automation, plus a template editor that respects custom code. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize template quantity, ease of use, or extra marketing tools.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for designers who need drag-and-drop editing with custom HTML support?
Mailchimp and GetResponse both allow you to switch between visual editing and raw HTML. If you want the largest template library and robust code editing, start with Mailchimp. If you also need automation and landing pages, GetResponse is a strong option.
Can I import my Campaign Monitor templates into these tools?
Yes, you can copy and paste HTML code from Campaign Monitor into Mailchimp, AWeber, or GetResponse. However, you may need to adjust CSS and structure to match each platform’s container settings. AWeber is the most forgiving for simple pastes, while Mailchimp may require you to use its “code” content block.
