Best Prometheus alternatives

Prometheus is a popular open source monitoring and alerting toolkit, especially for cloud native environments. However, as your infrastructure grows or your requirements change, you may encounter limitations in storage scalability, high availability, or built-in visualization. Site reliability engineers often evaluate alternatives that better fit long term retention, out of the box dashboards, or managed service convenience. This article covers four common Prometheus alternatives: Grafana, InfluxDB, Datadog, and New Relic.

alternative

Grafana

. While best known as a visualization layer for Prometheus, Grafana also offers its own monitoring backend through Grafana Mimir and Grafana Cloud. Mimir provides long term storage for Prometheus metrics with horizontal scaling and native PromQL support, making it a seamless upgrade path for teams that want to keep their existing Prometheus configuration.

alternative

InfluxDB

. This time series database focuses on high write throughput and efficient storage for metrics and events. It uses its own query language (Flux, moving toward SQL) and is often paired with Telegraf for data collection. InfluxDB can be a good fit if you need a unified store for both Prometheus style metrics and application performance data.

alternative

Datadog

. Datadog is a fully managed monitoring and observability platform that integrates metrics, traces, and logs in one interface. It offers an extensive library of integrations, built in alerting, and automated dashboards. For teams that want to reduce operational overhead and consolidate monitoring tools, Datadog provides a turnkey alternative to self hosted Prometheus.

alternative

New Relic

. New Relic is another SaaS based observability platform with strong support for application performance monitoring, infrastructure monitoring, and distributed tracing. It can ingest Prometheus metrics via its Prometheus remote write endpoint, allowing you to keep your existing exporters while moving to a managed backend. New Relic’s AI driven anomaly detection and easy to use dashboards appeal to SREs seeking faster incident response.

How they compare

All four alternatives address Prometheus limitations around long term storage, high availability, and native visualization. Grafana Mimir keeps PromQL and community alignment while adding scalability. InfluxDB offers a different query model and strong write performance but requires separate data collection. Datadog and New Relic are managed services that reduce maintenance effort at the cost of vendor lock in and ongoing fees. Your choice depends on whether you value open source flexibility, cost control, or a fully integrated observability experience.

Frequently asked questions

Can I still use my existing Prometheus exporters with these alternatives?
Yes. Grafana Mimir and InfluxDB accept Prometheus remote write, and both Datadog and New Relic have dedicated endpoints that ingest metrics from Prometheus exporters. You do not need to replace your agent or exporters when switching backends.
Which alternative is easiest to migrate to from Prometheus?
Grafana Mimir typically requires the least effort because it uses the same PromQL query language and remote write protocol. You can point your existing Prometheus server to Mimir as a remote storage backend or replace it entirely with a Mimir cluster while keeping your dashboards and alerting rules mostly unchanged.
