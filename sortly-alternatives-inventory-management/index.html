If you run a retail store and currently use Sortly for visual inventory management, you might be looking for an alternative that offers more advanced features as your business grows. Sortly is known for its photo based inventory tracking and simple interface, but it can lack robust reporting, lot tracking, or integration with accounting tools. This article compares three popular alternatives: Fishbowl, inFlow Inventory, and Zoho Inventory. Each tool has different strengths, so understanding how they stack up against Sortly’s visual focus will help you choose the right fit for your retail operation.

alternative

Fishbowl

is a strong alternative for retail stores that need manufacturing and advanced warehouse management alongside inventory control. It offers visual inventory features through barcode scanning and image attachments, but its main appeal is deeper functionality like work orders, lot tracking, and QuickBooks integration. Fishbowl requires a steeper learning curve and higher cost than Sortly, so it is best for growing retailers who already plan to scale beyond basic visual inventory.

Zoho Inventory

offers a cloud based solution with visual inventory capabilities including item images, barcode labels, and order management. It connects seamlessly with other Zoho apps like Zoho Books and Zoho CRM, which is useful if you already use their ecosystem. Zoho Inventory is more affordable than Fishbowl and scales well for small to midsize retailers, but its visual features are not as photo centric as Sortly’s interface.

How they compare

Sortly excels at visual inventory for small teams that want a simple photo based system, but it lacks advanced tools for reporting, multichannel selling, and warehouse management. Fishbowl is the most powerful option for complex retail operations, though it comes with higher cost and complexity. inFlow offers a middle ground with solid visual support and essential retail features like reorder points and sales history. Zoho Inventory is the most budget friendly alternative, especially if you already use Zoho apps, but its visual interface is less photo driven than Sortly’s.
