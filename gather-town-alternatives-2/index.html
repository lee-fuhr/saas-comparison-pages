If your company wants a virtual campus with networking lounges, you have likely tried Gather.town. It offers pixel art style spaces where you can move your avatar around and bump into people. But you might be looking for something with a different look, better audio features, or a more professional vibe. Here are five solid alternatives to consider.

alternative

SpatialChat

. This platform uses a proximity based audio system where you can walk into a room and hear people nearby. It has a modern, clean interface and supports video feeds inside customizable backgrounds. Many teams use it for informal hangouts and networking events.

alternative

VirBELA

. VirBELA is a 3D virtual world designed for professional events and persistent campuses. It includes features like stage presentations, breakout lounges, and interactive objects. It works well for larger companies that want a more immersive and branded environment.

alternative

Hubs

An alternative to Gather.town.

alternative

Meetion

. Meetion focuses on lightweight, browser based virtual spaces with a friendly, cartoon like design. It offers activity tables and conversation circles that mimic real world networking. The platform is easy to set up and does not require any downloads.

alternative

Remo

. Remo uses a floor plan layout with tables that you can click to join. Each table is a separate video chat room, and the spatial audio helps you overhear nearby conversations. It is popular for virtual conferences and structured networking sessions.

How they compare

All five alternatives let you create a virtual campus with areas for networking, but they differ in style and complexity. SpatialChat and Remo are best for quick, table based networking. VirBELA and Hubs offer more immersive 3D worlds, while Meetion keeps it simple with a browser friendly interface. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize ease of use, visual polish, or control over the environment.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is most like Gather.town for casual interactions?
SpatialChat is closest because it uses proximity audio and allows free movement around a 2D or 3D space. It has a modern look and is easy to set up for informal hangouts and networking lounges.
Do any of these alternatives work without requiring users to download software?
Yes, Hubs by Mozilla and Meetion both run entirely in a web browser. Users just need a link and a compatible browser to join, which makes it convenient for guests and external participants.
