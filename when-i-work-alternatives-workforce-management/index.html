Managing shift schedules for a food service franchise with over 1,000 employees is a complex task. When I Work is a popular choice, but it may not deliver the compliance depth, restaurant-specific features, or multi-location oversight your operation needs. This article compares three strong alternatives – Deputy, 7shifts, and Homebase – to help you decide which scheduling app fits your large workforce best.

alternative

Deputy

. Deputy excels at time and attendance tracking with built-in labor law compliance, making it ideal for franchises that need to enforce break rules and overtime limits across many sites. Its shift scheduling is straightforward, and the drag-and-drop interface works well for large teams. Deputy also offers robust reporting to link schedules with actual costs. **7shifts**. 7shifts is built specifically for restaurants, so features like tip pooling, shift swaps, and auto-scheduling by sales forecasts are standard. It handles high employee turnover and variable hour demands effectively, and its integrated task management helps streamline opening and closing duties. The platform includes a mobile app for easy employee self-service.

Homebase

. Homebase provides an all-in-one solution with scheduling, time clocks, team communication, and hiring tools in one platform. It is designed to simplify operations for multi-location businesses, with a simple interface that reduces training time. Homebase also offers a free plan for smaller teams, which can be useful for testing before rolling out to all locations.

How they compare

Deputy focuses on strict compliance and advanced time tracking, which is essential for franchises in regulated industries. 7shifts offers the most restaurant-tailored features, such as sales-based forecasting and shift trading. Homebase provides the broadest feature set for the price, including hiring and communication tools, but may lack the deep compliance controls of Deputy or the culinary-specific workflows of 7shifts. For a franchise with over 1,000 employees, consider your priority: compliance (Deputy), restaurant operations (7shifts), or an all-in-one system (Homebase).

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for managing labor costs across multiple locations?
Deputy offers strong cost tracking by linking scheduled shifts to actual clock-ins and generating reports per store. 7shifts uses sales data to predict labor needs, which can help you budget more accurately. Homebase also provides labor cost summaries, but its forecasts are less advanced than 7shifts.
Can these apps integrate with my existing POS system?
Yes, all three support major POS integrations. Deputy connects with systems like Square and Toast. 7shifts has direct integrations with Toast and Aloha. Homebase integrates with Square, Clover, and many others. Check with your POS provider for specific compatibility before committing.
