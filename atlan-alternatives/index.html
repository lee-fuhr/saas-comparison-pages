Modern data teams need governance tools that bring people together, not slow them down. Atlan stands out because it combines a friendly user experience with powerful workflow automation, making it easier for analysts, engineers, and business users to manage data assets together. Instead of locking governance behind rigid policies, Atlan uses a “Wikipedia for data” approach where anyone can contribute, review, and approve metadata. This helps teams move faster while still keeping data trustworthy and compliant.

alternative

Alation

. Alation is a mature data catalog with strong automated metadata harvesting and a robust data marketplace. It excels at driving data literacy through its patented query log analysis, but its interface can feel less modern and its collaboration features are not as fluid as Atlan’s.

alternative

Collibra

. Collibra is an enterprise-grade data governance platform with deep policy management and lineage tracking. It is best for heavily regulated industries that need strict workflow controls, but its configuration can be complex and its cost is often higher than competitors.

alternative

Atlan

. Even though Atlan is our primary focus, it also functions as an alternative to other tools. It emphasizes a user-friendly, search-first experience with real-time collaboration features like embedded Slack and automation across 50+ connectors.

alternative

DataHub

. DataHub is an open source data catalog built by LinkedIn that now powers many modern data stacks. It offers strong real-time metadata ingestion and a flexible graph model, but it requires more engineering effort to set up and maintain compared to a SaaS solution like Atlan.

alternative

Secoda

. Secoda is a lightweight, AI-powered data catalog aimed at small to mid-size teams. It simplifies metadata management with natural language search and automated documentation, but it lacks the advanced governance workflows and enterprise integrations that larger teams may need.
