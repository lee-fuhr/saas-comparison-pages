As a startup investor, you rely on accurate company data to find promising deals and track market trends. Crunchbase is a popular resource, but its coverage of private company funding and executive contacts can be limited. Here are four alternatives that may better serve your needs for prospecting and due diligence.

alternative

ZoomInfo

offers a vast database of business contacts and company profiles, including direct dial and email addresses. It excels at providing real time intent signals and firmographic data. Investors can use it to identify key decision makers at target companies.

alternative

Clearbit

focuses on enriching and appending data through APIs, making it ideal for investors who need to integrate company information into their own CRM or workflow. It provides detailed company attributes like technology stack and funding history. Clearbit’s data is often used for lead scoring and segmentation.

alternative

Lusha

specializes in providing direct contact details for professionals, including mobile phone numbers and email addresses. Its lightweight Chrome extension allows quick lookups without leaving LinkedIn or other platforms. Investors can quickly gather contact info for founders or executives.

alternative

Apollo.io

combines a B2B contact database with engagement tools like email sequences and LinkedIn automation. It offers company data such as funding rounds, employee count, and recent news. Investors can build targeted lists and reach out to prospects directly from the platform.

How they compare

Each tool has distinct strengths. ZoomInfo provides the broadest dataset for outreach, Clearbit excels in data enrichment for existing systems, Lusha is best for quick contact lookups, and Apollo.io combines data with outreach automation. Your choice depends on whether you need raw contact volume, CRM integration, speed, or an all in one sales engagement platform.

Frequently asked questions

Are these alternatives more expensive than Crunchbase?
Pricing varies widely. ZoomInfo and Clearbit often charge premium fees for comprehensive access, while Lusha and Apollo.io offer more affordable tiers or credit based models. Many provide free trials, so test the data quality for your target companies before committing.
How accurate is the data in these tools for private startups?
Accuracy depends on the tool’s data sources and update frequency. ZoomInfo and Clearbit use proprietary data scraping and partnerships, which can miss very early stage startups. Lusha and Apollo.io rely on public profiles and user contributions, so verify key details through direct checks.
