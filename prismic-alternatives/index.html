Best Prismic alternatives

Prismic is a well known headless CMS that uses slices and custom types to build flexible page layouts. Many frontend developers choose it for its editing experience and API support. But over time you might hit limitations like pricing changes, limited backend control, or a need for different content modeling. This article covers five Prismic alternatives that can better fit your project needs as a frontend developer.

alternative

Contentful

: Contentful provides a mature content infrastructure with a strong REST and GraphQL API, a rich text editor, and robust access control. It is a solid choice for enterprise projects that need high scalability and strict content governance. However, its pricing can escalate quickly, and the content modeling lacks the slice based flexibility that Prismic offers.

Try Contentful →
alternative

Sanity

: Sanity gives you a fully customizable content studio with real time collaboration and a portable query language called GROQ. You can build your own editing experience using React components, which gives developers deep control. Sanity is a great fit if you want to tailor the CMS backend to your workflow and need a document oriented content model.

Try Sanity →
alternative

Strapi

: Strapi is an open source headless CMS that you can self host, giving you full ownership of your data and infrastructure. It comes with a plugin system and a straightforward admin panel, making it easy to add custom features. Strapi is ideal for developers who want no vendor lock in and are comfortable managing their own server, though it requires more maintenance than a SaaS solution.

Try Strapi →
alternative

DatoCMS

: DatoCMS combines an intuitive dashboard with a GraphQL API and built in asset optimization for images. It has strong localization support and a tree based content structure that simplifies managing related content. DatoCMS works well for projects that need fast global content delivery and straightforward content modeling without a steep learning curve.

alternative

Storyblok

: Storyblok offers a visual editor with live preview and a component based content system that feels similar to Prismic’s slices. Editors can see changes in real time, and developers can nest and reuse components flexibly. Storyblok is a compelling alternative if you want an easy to use editing interface combined with powerful content composition.

How they compare

When choosing a Prismic alternative, think about your priorities. Contentful and Storyblok excel at providing a polished editing experience for non technical users. Sanity and DatoCMS offer greater backend customization and developer friendly APIs. Strapi stands alone as the only fully open source, self hosted option. Pricing varies: Contentful and DatoCMS can get expensive at scale, while Sanity and Strapi have generous free tiers. If you need full content control and don’t mind server management, go with Strapi. If you want a flexible editing experience with minimal setup, consider Storyblok or Contentful.

Frequently asked questions

What are the main reasons to switch from Prismic to another headless CMS?
Common reasons include wanting more flexible content modeling, lower costs for high content volume, self hosting to avoid vendor lock in, or stronger localization features. Prismic’s slice system is a strength, but alternatives like Sanity or Storyblok offer similar or more customizable approaches.
Which Prismic alternative is best for a small team on a tight budget?
Strapi and Sanity both have generous free tiers and low cost plans for small teams. Strapi is completely free if you self host, while Sanity’s free plan includes generous content limits. DatoCMS and Contentful have free tiers with stricter limits, and Storyblok’s free plan only supports one user. Evaluate your content volume and team size to pick the best fit.
