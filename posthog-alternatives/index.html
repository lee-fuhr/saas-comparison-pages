Engineering-led product teams often choose PostHog for its open-source foundation, self-hosting capability, and developer-friendly approach. But you might look for alternatives if you need a different pricing model, more specialized features, or a tool that scales differently. This article covers five common alternatives – Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap, Pendo, and Hotjar – and helps you compare them against PostHog for product analytics.

Heap

How they compare

PostHog stands out as the only fully open-source option among these tools, with self-hosting and a generous free tier for cloud usage. Mixpanel and Amplitude offer more advanced behavioral analytics and larger data volumes but are proprietary and charge per tracked user or event. Heap simplifies implementation through auto-capture but limits data control. Pendo adds user engagement features on top of analytics, while Hotjar focuses on qualitative insights like session replays. Choose PostHog when you need data sovereignty, open-source code, and a developer-centric workflow. Choose a proprietary tool when you prefer a managed service, richer out-of-the-box reports, or specific non-analytics features such as guides or heatmaps.

Frequently asked questions

Are there any fully open-source alternatives to PostHog that also support self-hosting?
For product analytics specifically, PostHog is the leading open-source choice. Other open-source tools like Matomo or Plausible focus more on website analytics and lack product-specific features such as feature flags, session recordings, and group analytics.
What is the main reason engineering teams switch from PostHog to one of these alternatives?
The most common reason is needing a simpler setup or more advanced behavioral analysis at scale. Some teams find PostHog’s self-hosting maintenance overhead too high, or they prefer a cloud-only solution with dedicated support. Others need specialized features like Heap’s auto-capture or Pendo’s in-app guidance that PostHog does not offer.
