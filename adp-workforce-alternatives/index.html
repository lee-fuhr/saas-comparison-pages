Best ADP Workforce alternatives

ADP Workforce is a well known payroll and HR platform, but many mid-size firms find that its pricing structure, limited customization, or customer support do not match their growth needs. As your company expands, you need a tool that scales with headcount, integrates with existing systems, and offers modern features like mobile self-service and advanced analytics. The following alternatives each bring different strengths, so you can choose the one that fits your operational style and budget.

alternative

Kronos

. Kronos (now part of UKG, but still available as a legacy product) focuses on workforce management with deep time and attendance tracking. It excels for industries with complex scheduling, shift differentials, and labor compliance rules. However, its payroll module may require additional configuration compared to all-in-one platforms.

alternative

UKG

. UKG combines the strengths of Kronos and Ultimate Software into a single cloud HCM suite. It offers payroll, HR, talent management, and workforce management in one system, making it a strong direct competitor to ADP Workforce. Mid-size firms appreciate its user friendly interface and flexible deployment options, though the price can be higher than some standalone payroll tools.

alternative

Verint

. Verint is primarily a workforce engagement management solution built for contact centers and high volume service operations. It provides advanced forecasting, scheduling, and quality management features but lacks a full payroll engine. Firms that already have a payroll vendor often use Verint to optimize labor costs and improve agent productivity.

alternative

Workday

. Workday delivers a modern cloud based HCM platform designed for organizations that prioritize analytics, global compliance, and a unified employee experience. It is highly scalable and includes payroll, HR, finance, and planning modules. For mid-size firms, Workday can be more expensive and complex to implement than ADP Workforce, but it offers deeper insights and automation.

How they compare

All four alternatives address common ADP Workforce pain points such as rigid pricing, limited self-service, and slow support. Kronos and UKG are the closest replacements for payroll and time tracking, with UKG providing a more complete HCM suite. Verint is not a payroll tool itself but works as an add on for workforce optimization. Workday is the most comprehensive and expensive option, ideal for firms that plan to scale rapidly and need real time data across departments. Your choice should depend on whether you prioritize cost, feature depth, or future growth.

Frequently asked questions

Are these alternatives more affordable than ADP Workforce for a mid-size firm?
Pricing varies widely by company size, number of employees, and required modules. Kronos and UKG often offer per employee per month pricing that can be comparable or slightly higher than ADP. Verint is typically priced for contact center seats rather than full employee base. Workday is generally the most expensive option. Request quotes from each vendor with your specific headcount and needs.
How long does it take to switch from ADP Workforce to one of these systems?
Implementation timelines depend on the complexity of your payroll and HR processes. A simple data migration to Kronos or UKG can take 3 to 6 months. Workday often requires 6 to 12 months due to its broader scope. Verint can be implemented in a few weeks if you already have a payroll system. Plan for data cleanup, testing, and employee training to ensure a smooth transition.
