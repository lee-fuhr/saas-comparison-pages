If your team relies on Google Workspace for email, calendars, and meetings, Google Drive is the natural home for your files. But sometimes you need a different cloud storage solution, maybe for tighter security rules, larger storage quotas, or better integration with other tools you already use. Dropbox, OneDrive, and Box are three popular Google Drive alternatives that each bring something different to a team that still wants to keep working with Google Workspace apps.

alternative

Dropbox

offers straightforward file sync and sharing with a clean user interface. It connects to Google Workspace through its app center, letting you create and edit Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides directly from Dropbox folders. However, it does not natively link to Google Calendar or Gmail, and its collaboration features are best for file sharing rather than real time editing.

alternative

OneDrive

is built for Microsoft 365 but also works with Google Workspace through third party connectors and browser extensions. It provides generous storage plans, strong file versioning, and deep integration with Microsoft Teams and Outlook. Teams that use both Google and Microsoft tools may find OneDrive helpful, but its native Google app support is limited compared to Google Drive itself.

Box

is designed for enterprise security, compliance, and workflow automation. It integrates directly with Google Workspace, allowing users to create and edit Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides from Box, and it supports custom metadata and advanced sharing controls. Box is a strong choice for teams in regulated industries that need detailed audit trails and content governance.

How they compare

All three alternatives offer file sync, sharing, and some level of Google Workspace integration, but they differ in focus. Dropbox excels at simplicity and cross platform sync. OneDrive is best for teams that also use Microsoft 365. Box leads in security and workflow features. For pure Google Workspace integration, none match the native experience of Google Drive, but Box and Dropbox provide the most direct editing of Google Docs from their platforms. Pricing varies, with OneDrive often being the most affordable for large storage, while Box charges more for advanced security features.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Dropbox with Google Workspace apps?
Yes. Dropbox offers an app connector that lets you create, edit, and collaborate on Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides files stored in Dropbox. You can also preview these files, but you will still need a Google Workspace account to edit them. The integration works well for file management, though it does not include calendar or email syncing.
Which alternative is best for a team that uses both Google and Microsoft tools?
OneDrive is the strongest choice for teams that rely on both platforms because it pairs naturally with Microsoft 365 while also offering third party tools to connect with Google Workspace. Dropbox and Box also work across both ecosystems, but OneDrive gives you the deepest integration with Outlook, Teams, and Office desktop apps.
