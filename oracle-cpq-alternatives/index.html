Best Oracle CPQ alternatives

Oracle CPQ is a widely used configure price quote solution, especially among large enterprises with complex product catalogs. However, some organizations find it expensive, rigid, or difficult to integrate with existing systems. If you are evaluating Oracle CPQ alternatives, the following tools offer strong capabilities for managing intricate pricing rules, product configurations, and multi-tier sales processes. Each has strengths that may better match your specific workflow, billing model, or tech stack.

alternative

Salesforce CPQ

. This tool is built directly on the Salesforce platform, making it a natural choice for companies already using Salesforce for CRM. It handles complex product configurations and pricing rules well, and its native integration reduces data silos. Large enterprises with a heavy Salesforce investment often prefer it for its seamless quoting and approval workflows.

alternative

Apttus

. Known for its quote to cash capabilities, Apttus (now part of Conga) excels at managing sophisticated product hierarchies and multi-option bundles. It offers strong approval routing and contract lifecycle management. Enterprises with complex pricing models and long sales cycles often benefit from its depth of functionality.

alternative

Conga CPQ

. Conga CPQ focuses on document generation and streamlined quoting, with powerful automation for creating proposals and contracts. It works well for companies that need to generate many custom documents along with quotes. Its integration with Conga’s broader revenue management suite helps large teams manage the entire quote to cash process.

alternative

DealHub

. DealHub provides a modern, user friendly CPQ interface paired with subscription billing and revenue recognition features. It supports complex product catalogs with dynamic pricing and guided selling. Enterprises transitioning to subscription or usage based models frequently choose DealHub for its flexibility and real time price adjustments.

alternative

Zuora CPQ

. Part of the Zuora revenue platform, this tool is designed for subscription and recurring revenue businesses. It handles sophisticated product catalogs with multiple pricing dimensions like tiers, usage, and one time fees. Large enterprises that need tight integration with billing, invoicing, and revenue reporting often select Zuora CPQ.

How they compare

Salesforce CPQ integrates deeply with Salesforce but can be costly for standalone use. Apttus and Conga CPQ share a merged lineage, with Apttus offering richer configuration logic and Conga emphasizing document automation. DealHub provides a modern interface and strong subscription capabilities but may lack some advanced configuration features of older platforms. Zuora CPQ is best for subscription businesses but less suited for one time product sales. Oracle CPQ remains strong for on premise deployment and extreme customization, while these alternatives often offer better cloud native integration or lower total cost of ownership.

Frequently asked questions

Can these alternatives handle product catalogs with thousands of SKUs and complex compatibility rules?
Yes. Salesforce CPQ, Apttus, Conga CPQ, DealHub, and Zuora CPQ all support multi level product hierarchies, constraint rules, and conditional logic. You will need to evaluate each tool’s configuration engine for your specific rule complexity, but most large enterprise deployments can be accommodated with proper setup.
How do these tools compare to Oracle CPQ in terms of implementation time and cost?
Implementation time varies widely. Salesforce CPQ often deploys faster for existing Salesforce users, while Apttus and Conga CPQ may require more upfront data modeling. DealHub and Zuora CPQ tend to be quicker for modern subscription based catalogs. Costs are generally lower than Oracle CPQ’s enterprise licensing, but you should factor in integration work and ongoing subscription fees.
