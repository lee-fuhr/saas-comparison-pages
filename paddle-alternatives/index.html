Best Paddle alternatives

Paddle is a popular choice for B2B SaaS and desktop software vendors because it combines payment processing, subscription management, tax compliance (including VAT and sales tax), and licensing into one platform. This all-in-one approach simplifies operations, especially for vendors selling globally. However, Paddle may not fit every business model, budget, or set of requirements. Some vendors need more flexible billing, lower transaction fees, or deeper integration with existing systems. Below we explore seven leading Paddle alternatives that offer different strengths in payment processing and licensing.

alternative

Chargebee

is a subscription management and billing platform that pairs with payment gateways like Stripe, Braintree, and GoCardless. It offers strong automation for recurring billing, invoicing, and revenue recognition, but does not include native payment processing or licensing features. You will need separate tools for those.

alternative

Recurly

specializes in recurring billing and subscription lifecycle management with built-in dunning and smart retry logic. It connects to multiple payment gateways but does not provide its own payment processing or software licensing. Recruity is a good fit for vendors who already have a payment processor.

alternative

Zuora

is a comprehensive subscription management platform designed for large enterprises with complex billing needs, such as usage based pricing and multi currency support. It offers extensive API capabilities but lacks native payment processing and licensing. Zuora requires significant implementation effort and is best for high volume sellers.

alternative

Stripe Billing

is the subscription billing module within the Stripe ecosystem, giving you direct access to Stripe’s payment processing infrastructure. It handles recurring invoices, metered billing, and tax calculation via Stripe Tax, but does not include software licensing features. It is ideal for vendors who already use or plan to use Stripe for payments.

alternative

SaaSOptics

focuses on subscription analytics, revenue recognition, and financial operations for SaaS companies. It integrates with payment gateways like Stripe and Braintree but does not process payments or manage software licenses. SaaSOptics is best for vendors who need robust accounting and reporting more than billing automation.

alternative

Fusebill

offers a subscription billing platform with built-in payment processing through its own gateway or integrations with third party processors. It supports recurring billing, invoicing, and dunning but does not provide software licensing capabilities. Fusebill is a mid market option that combines billing and payments without the licensing layer.

alternative

Aria Systems

provides enterprise grade subscription billing and monetization for complex pricing models, including usage, tiered, and hybrid plans. It integrates with major payment gateways like Stripe and Worldpay but does not process payments natively or handle software licensing. Aria is suited for large vendors with intricate billing workflows.

How they compare

When choosing among these Paddle alternatives, consider three primary factors: payment processing integration, software licensing capabilities, and tax compliance. Chargebee, Recurly, and Stripe Billing require you to bring your own payment processor, while Fusebill and SaaSOptics offer limited built in options. None of the alternatives provide native software licensing or product key management like Paddle does. For tax compliance, Stripe Billing and Chargebee offer tax calculation modules (with extra fees), but integrating tax across multiple jurisdictions often requires a third party service like TaxJar or Avalara. Zuora and Aria Systems are best for large enterprises with complex billing, while SaaSOptics is focused on finance and analytics. Desktop software vendors who need licensing features will likely need to pair one of these billing tools with a separate licensing solution.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a Paddle alternative that handles both payments and licensing like Paddle?
No single alternative offers the exact same combination of built in payment processing, tax compliance, and software licensing. Most alternatives focus on subscription billing and integrate with external payment gateways. For licensing, you would need a separate tool such as Keygen, Gemfury, or to build your own system.
Which Paddle alternative is best for desktop software vendors?
Desktop software vendors often need offline licensing and product key management, which is not a native feature in any of the alternatives listed. Stripe Billing combined with a third party licensing service is a common setup. For simpler needs, Chargebee or Recurly plus a lightweight licensing tool may work. Evaluate each platform’s API flexibility and third party integrations before choosing.
