As a sales or support professional, you know that async video can replace long emails and scheduling hassles. Two of the most popular platforms are Loom and Vidyard. Both let you record your screen and webcam, share a link, and track viewer engagement. But they differ in features, pricing, and integration depth. This article compares Loom and Vidyard head to head, then looks at five alternative tools worth considering.

alternative

CloudApp

. CloudApp focuses on quick screen captures and GIFs alongside video. It offers a lightweight recorder with annotation tools and a simple sharing workflow. Its free tier includes basic tracking, but advanced analytics require a paid plan.

alternative

Soapbox

. Soapbox is built for sales teams that want to create polished, branded videos. It includes a teleprompter, custom thumbnails, and call-to-action buttons. Soapbox integrates with CRMs like Salesforce and HubSpot, but its free tier is limited.

alternative

BombBomb

. BombBomb is tailored for relationship-driven sales and real estate. It records video directly in your email client and tracks opens and clicks. Its strength is its email integration, but it lacks dedicated screen recording options.

alternative

Hippo Video

. Hippo Video offers a full suite for video marketing and sales. It includes a video editor, interactive CTAs, and a video website builder. Hippo Video works well for teams that need video in email campaigns and CRM workflows, though its interface can feel busy.

alternative

Screencast-O-Matic

. Screencast-O-Matic (now ScreenPal) is a simple recording tool for tutorials and quick demos. It has a free version with watermarks and basic trimming. It is less focused on sales analytics and more on affordable screen capture.

How they compare

Both Loom and Vidyard let you record async videos with screen and camera, then share a link. Loom is known for its ease of use and generous free plan (up to 50 videos, 5 minutes each). Vidyard offers a deeper free tier (up to 25 videos, no duration limit) but focuses on enterprise features like CRM integrations and detailed viewer heatmaps. For sales teams, Vidyard’s advanced analytics and seamless Salesforce integration are strong advantages. For support teams that want speed and simplicity, Loom’s one-click recording and automatic link copying win out. Pricing differs: Loom’s paid plans start at $12.50 per month, while Vidyard’s start at $19 per month (billed annually). Neither tool is wrong, but choose based on your need for advanced tracking versus friction-free recording.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Loom or Vidyard for free without a watermark?
Yes, both Loom and Vidyard offer free plans that do not watermark your videos. Loom’s free plan limits video length to 5 minutes and 50 total videos. Vidyard’s free plan caps you at 25 videos total but has no time limit per video. Both allow unlimited viewers on free plans.
Which tool has better CRM integration for sales teams?
Vidyard offers deeper native CRM integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Microsoft Dynamics. It syncs video view data directly into contact records. Loom supports basic webhook and Zapier connections but lacks native CRM deep linking. If your team lives in a CRM, Vidyard is the stronger choice.
