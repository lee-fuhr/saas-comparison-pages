Best Spendesk alternatives

European firms with multi-currency spending needs often start with Spendesk because of its virtual cards, expense reports, and approval workflows in euros and other currencies. But as your company grows, you may want a tool with different features, pricing, or geographic support. We cover three alternatives – Expensify, Ramp, and Brex – that also offer multi-currency spend controls, though each works best in specific situations.

alternative

Expensify

. Expensify is a mature expense management tool that handles multi-currency receipts and reimbursement in over 200 countries. It works well for companies that already have a corporate card provider but need strong receipt scanning, approval routing, and direct reimbursement to employees or contractors in their local currency.

alternative

Ramp

. Ramp provides corporate cards and spend management with real-time controls and accounting integrations, and it now supports multi-currency spending for European firms through its partnership with a card issuer. However, Ramp is still relatively new to the European market, so you may find fewer local payment rails and smaller customer support teams compared to Spendesk.

alternative

Brex

. Brex offers corporate cards and spend management with multi-currency capabilities, including the ability to spend in 150+ currencies and pay foreign vendors via wire transfer. Its platform is designed for fast-growing tech companies, though it is primarily built for US-based firms and may not meet all European compliance or invoicing requirements without additional setup.

How they compare

Spendesk is the most Europe-centric option with strong multi-currency virtual cards, automated expense categorisation, and deep integrations with local accounting software like Sage and Cegid. Expensify is better for companies that need simple expense reporting and reimbursement across many currencies, but it lacks built-in corporate cards. Ramp offers modern spend controls and seamless automation for US and increasingly European teams, but its European features are still maturing. Brex excels in global spending and vendor payments, but its European onboarding can be slower and it may not comply with every local tax or reporting rule.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a European firm that mostly uses euros but also handles a few other currencies like GBP and SEK?
Spendesk or Ramp are both strong choices. Spendesk gives you local virtual cards in each currency and easy reconciliation with European accounting tools. Ramp now supports multi-currency cards too, and its approval workflows are very flexible, but check if your specific currencies are covered in your country.
Can I use Expensify as a full replacement for Spendesk if I already have a corporate card provider?
Yes, but only if you are satisfied with your current card provider’s multi-currency support. Expensify focuses on the expense reporting and reimbursement side. You would not get virtual cards or real-time spending limits from Expensify itself, so you need to combine it with a separate card solution that offers multi-currency controls.
