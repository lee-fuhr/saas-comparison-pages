Best Figma alternatives

Figma has become a central tool for many design teams, but it is not the only option. Rising subscription costs, internet dependence, or a need for offline capabilities might push you to explore other tools. This article covers five strong alternatives – Sketch, Adobe XD, Penpot, Framer, and Lunacy – to help you find the right fit for your workflow and team., ## ALTERNATIVES

**Sketch**. A veteran in interface design, Sketch offers a mature ecosystem with a huge library of plugins and symbols. It runs natively on macOS and relies on cloud sharing for team collaboration, which works best if your whole team uses Apple hardware. Its focus on responsive design and component reuse makes it a solid choice for UI designers who prefer a one time license model.

**Adobe XD**. Integrated tightly with the Adobe Creative Cloud, XD lets designers move assets seamlessly between apps like Photoshop and Illustrator. It provides real time coediting on documents and supports voice prototyping and auto animate for micro interactions. The free starter plan covers unlimited prototypes and one shared link, which is handy for small teams.

**Penpot**. This is the first open source design and prototyping platform that runs entirely in the browser. It uses open standards like SVG and supports real time collaboration without any commercial restrictions. Penpot is ideal for teams that want full control over their design data and need a transparent, self hosted or cloud hosted solution.

**Framer**. Framer combines design and high fidelity prototyping in one tool, with a strong focus on interactive and animated components. It offers a large component library and a simple coding interface for advanced interactions. Teams that prioritize polished, production ready prototypes over pure UI design will find Framer especially powerful.

**Lunacy**. Developed by Icons8, Lunacy is a free Windows and macOS application that natively opens Sketch files. It supports real time collaboration, built in libraries of icons and photos, and works offline. Lunacy is a great bridge for teams who need to collaborate across platforms without paying for a subscription., ## COMPARISON SUMMARY

- **Platform support**: Sketch (macOS only), Adobe XD (Windows and macOS), Penpot (web based), Framer (web and desktop), Lunacy (Windows and macOS). - **Pricing model**: Sketch (annual subscription with perpetual option), Adobe XD (free starter plan, CC subscription), Penpot (free and open source), Framer (free plan, paid for advanced features), Lunacy (free for individuals and teams). - **Collaboration style**: Sketch uses cloud documents and shared libraries, Adobe XD uses links and coediting, Penpot offers real time via browser, Framer uses shared projects, Lunacy uses built in real time and local files. - **Offline capability**: Lunacy works fully offline, Sketch works offline with local files, others require internet for most features. - **Open source**: Only Penpot is fully open source., ## FAQ

Q: Is there a free alternative to Figma that works well for teams? A: Yes, Penpot is completely free and open source with real time collaboration built in. Lunacy is also free for teams and works offline, though it is desktop based. Both let multiple designers work on the same file without a subscription.

Q: Which Figma alternative is best for prototyping? A: Framer excels at high fidelity, interactive prototypes with animations and logic, making it a top choice for motion rich interfaces. Adobe XD also offers strong prototyping features like auto animate and voice interactions, and it integrates with the rest of the Adobe suite.
