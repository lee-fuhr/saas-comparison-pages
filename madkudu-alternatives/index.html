Best MadKudu alternatives

If you are a growth team looking for predictive lead scoring based on firmographic and behavioral data, MadKudu has long been a popular choice. It integrates marketing automation and CRM data to help you prioritize leads. However, you may need a different approach, budget fit, or feature set. This article covers the main MadKudu alternatives: 6sense, Lattice Engines, Mintigo, Infer, and Radius. Each tool has its own strengths, so we will compare them to help you decide.

alternative

6sense

focuses on account-based marketing and sales with predictive intelligence across the full buying journey. It uses AI to identify anonymous website visitors, enrich contacts, and score accounts based on both behavioral and firmographic signals. Growth teams can use 6sense to target accounts that are in market, even before leads fill out a form.

alternative

Lattice Engines

(now part of Dun & Bradstreet) provides predictive lead and account scoring by combining firmographic data with behavioral triggers. It helps B2B teams prioritize leads that are most likely to convert, using models built on your historical sales data. The platform also offers recommendations for next-best actions and personalized content.

alternative

Mintigo

(now part of Microsoft) specializes in predictive lead scoring and account intelligence by analyzing vast amounts of external firmographic and intent data. It identifies companies that show buying signals and scores them based on fit and behavior. Growth teams can use Mintigo to focus on accounts that match their ideal customer profile and are actively researching.

alternative

Infer

(acquired by Salesforce) offered predictive lead and account scoring by combining internal CRM data with external data sources. It was known for its simple integration with Salesforce and automated lead prioritization. While no longer actively sold as a standalone product, some teams still use legacy instances or seek similar functionality through Salesforce’s Einstein features.

alternative

Radius

(also acquired by Dun & Bradstreet) provided predictive lead scoring and account-based marketing by layering firmographic, technographic, and behavioral data. It helped teams identify high-fit accounts and rank them by purchase intent. Like Infer, Radius is now part of a larger platform, but its predictive scoring approach remains relevant for comparison.

How they compare

MadKudu is best for teams that want a lightweight, data-agnostic predictive scoring tool that plugs into existing stacks like Marketo or Salesforce. 6sense offers a broader account-based platform with strong intent and anonymous visitor tracking. Lattice Engines and its successor (Dun & Bradstreet) are ideal for companies needing deep firmographic enrichment and B2B data from a trusted source. Mintigo (Microsoft) excels at combining external intent signals with predictive scoring for large account lists. Infer and Radius are older options that are now embedded in larger platforms, so new buyers should look at their parent products (Salesforce Einstein, Dun & Bradstreet Lattice) for similar capabilities. For pure lead scoring based on firmographic and behavioral data, MadKudu and 6sense are the strongest direct alternatives.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use these alternatives if I don’t have historical CRM data for model training?
Yes, but the accuracy of predictive scoring will depend on the tool’s ability to use third-party data as a starting point. 6sense and Mintigo use external intent and firmographic data to score leads even without your own historical data. Lattice Engines can also use Dun & Bradstreet’s data universe. However, the best results come from training models on your own closed-won and lost records.
Which tool is best for a small growth team without a dedicated data science resource?
MadKudu is designed for easy setup without data science support, using simple rules and machine learning models that run automatically. 6sense also offers a self-service interface with predictive scoring out of the box. Lattice Engines and Mintigo may require more upfront configuration but often include support from their teams. For the least technical overhead, MadKudu and 6sense are the most accessible.
