If your team relies on Grafana for dashboards but needs a more unified observability stack, simpler setup, or advanced analytics, there are strong alternatives. Grafana excels at flexible visualization across multiple data sources, but it does not store data itself and often requires a companion system like Prometheus or Elasticsearch. The five alternatives below each include their own data storage, alerting, and dashboards. This article compares Datadog, New Relic, Prometheus, Elastic, and Dynatrace so you can decide which fits your workflows and budget.

alternative

Datadog

. Datadog is a SaaS platform that combines metrics, logs, and traces in a single interface with pre-built dashboards and alerting. It reduces the operational overhead of managing separate storage and visualization components. Teams that want a fully managed solution with broad integrations often choose Datadog over Grafana’s self-hosted model.

alternative

New Relic

. New Relic is a full-stack observability platform that uses a single agent to collect data from applications, infrastructure, and logs. Its dashboards are tightly coupled with APM and AI-powered anomaly detection. New Relic is a good fit for teams that prefer an all-in-one SaaS tool with built-in intelligent alerting.

alternative

Prometheus

. Prometheus is an open-source monitoring system with its own time-series database and a powerful query language called PromQL. It includes a basic expression browser and alerting through Alertmanager. While Prometheus pairs naturally with Grafana for visualization, some teams use its native tools for simple dashboards and alerting without an extra layer.

alternative

Elastic

. Elastic offers the ELK stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana) which provides dashboards (Kibana) that are deeply integrated with log and metric analytics. Kibana supports visualizations similar to Grafana but also excels at log search and machine learning. This alternative suits teams that prioritize log management and already use Elasticsearch for storage.

alternative

Dynatrace

. Dynatrace is an AI-driven observability platform that automatically discovers and maps application dependencies. Its Davis AI provides root cause analysis and automatic baselining for alerts. Dynatrace’s dashboards are built on top of this automated context, making it a strong choice for teams that want deep, automated insights without manual dashboard configuration.

How they compare

Datadog and New Relic are SaaS offerings with per-host or per-use pricing, which simplifies operations at a recurring cost. Prometheus and Elastic are open-source and self-managed, giving you full control but requiring more engineering time. Dynatrace is also SaaS but typically more expensive, reflecting its AI automation. Grafana itself is open-source and often used as a visualization layer on top of Prometheus or Elastic; the alternatives here each include their own data storage and alerting. For teams that want a single vendor and minimal integration work, Datadog or New Relic work well. For teams that value open source and custom pipelines, Prometheus or Elastic are strong choices. Dynatrace appeals to organizations that need automated root cause analysis and can justify the higher cost.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good alternative to Grafana for teams that want an all-in-one solution without self-hosting?
Datadog or New Relic both provide integrated metrics, logs, and traces with pre-built dashboards and alerting. They eliminate the need to manage separate data stores like Prometheus or Elasticsearch. However, they come with subscription costs, so evaluate per-host pricing against your data volume.
Can Prometheus completely replace Grafana for dashboarding?
Prometheus has a basic expression browser and alerting via Alertmanager, but its visualization capabilities are limited compared to Grafana. Most teams use Prometheus for data collection and alerting while relying on Grafana for advanced dashboards. If you only need simple, pre-built graphs, Prometheus alone may be sufficient, but it is not a direct dashboard replacement.
