Droplr is a simple tool for sharing screenshots and short videos. But as a video creator, you may need more advanced features like editing, longer recordings, or live streaming. If you are searching for Droplr alternatives, here are four tools that video creators often turn to.

alternative

Loom

is a web-based tool that records your screen and camera simultaneously. It is great for quick messages and tutorials, with automatic sharing links. However, it has limited editing and a recording time cap on the free plan.

alternative

Camtasia

is a full featured screen recorder and video editor in one. You can add transitions, annotations, and effects, making it ideal for polished tutorials or presentations. It requires a one time purchase and a steeper learning curve.

alternative

OBS

An alternative to Droplr.

alternative

Screencast-O-Matic

is a user friendly tool with both screen recording and simple video editing. It offers a free version with watermarks and paid plans for longer recordings and more features. It works well for beginners and educators.

How they compare

Loom is the fastest for sharing, Camtasia is the best for editing, OBS is the most flexible for live streaming and custom setups, and Screencast-O-Matic is a balanced option for casual use. Each tool excels in different areas, so choose based on whether you need speed, polish, control, or simplicity.

Frequently asked questions

Which Droplr alternative is best for recording software tutorials?
Camtasia is a strong choice because it combines recording with editing tools like callouts and zoom effects. Loom also works well for simple walkthroughs if you do not need complex edits.
Is there a completely free alternative to Droplr for video creators?
Yes, OBS Studio is free and open source with no watermarks or time limits. However, you will need separate software for editing and hosting your videos.
