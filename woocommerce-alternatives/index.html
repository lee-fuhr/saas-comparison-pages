If you run a WordPress store with WooCommerce, you already know it offers immense flexibility. But sometimes you need a simpler setup, lower maintenance, or different built-in features. This article covers five strong WooCommerce alternatives that can help you move your store without losing functionality.

alternative

Shopify

is a fully hosted platform that handles security, updates, and hosting for you. It offers a huge app marketplace and built-in payment processing, making it easy to launch quickly. However, monthly fees and transaction charges can add up if you use its default payment gateway.

Try Shopify →
alternative

BigCommerce

gives you many native features that Shopify charges extra for, such as multi-currency support and real-time shipping quotes. It scales well for growing businesses and has no transaction fees when using a built-in gateway. The learning curve is a bit steeper than Shopify, but the savings can be worthwhile.

Try BigCommerce →
alternative

Wix eCommerce

provides a drag-and-drop builder that is beginner-friendly and includes 50-plus free store templates. It bundles hosting and domain options, so you do not need separate services. However, advanced customization and SEO control are more limited compared to WooCommerce.

alternative

Squarespace

offers stylish, mobile-responsive templates designed for visual brands like artists or boutiques. Its simple commerce tools cover inventory, orders, and promotions without needing third-party plugins. The tradeoff is less flexibility for complex setups like subscriptions or multi-vendor markets.

alternative

Ecwid

works as a plugin that can add a store to any existing WordPress site (or other platforms) without replacing your whole site. It is great if you only want to sell a few products while keeping your current blog or business website. The free plan is limited, and advanced features require a paid subscription.

How they compare

- **Ease of use:** Wix and Squarespace are simplest; BigCommerce and Shopify are moderate; Ecwid is simple as an add-on. - **Cost:** Ecwid starts free; Shopify and BigCommerce have monthly fees plus transaction costs; Wix and Squarespace include hosting in their plans. - **Scalability:** BigCommerce and Shopify handle large catalogs best; Squarespace and Wix suit smaller stores. - **Customization:** WooCommerce is still the most flexible, but among these, BigCommerce and Shopify offer more developer access. - **Ownership:** With self-hosted WooCommerce you own your data; all alternatives here are hosted tools.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate my product data from WooCommerce to one of these tools easily?
Yes, each platform offers import tools or plugins that can move products, customers, and order history. You may need to adjust tax and shipping settings manually after migration.
Do I need technical skills to switch from WooCommerce to an alternative?
Not necessarily. Platforms like Shopify, Wix, and Squarespace are designed for non-developers. You can use built-in wizards and support guides. For BigCommerce or Ecwid, some data mapping may be needed, but it is still manageable.
