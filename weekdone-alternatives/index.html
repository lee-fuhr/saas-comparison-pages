If your team relies on Weekdone for weekly OKR check-ins and clear progress dashboards, you know the value of simplicity. But maybe you have outgrown it, need stronger integrations, or just want to explore options that fit your culture better. The good news is that several tools offer similar weekly rhythms and straightforward dashboards. Below we compare five popular Weekdone alternatives to help you find the right fit for your team.

alternative

Leapsome

. Leapsome combines OKR tracking, performance reviews, and engagement surveys in one platform. Its weekly check-in feature lets team members update progress on objectives and key results quickly, and the dashboard gives managers a simple view of team health and goal progress. It is a good choice if you want to blend goal management with people development. **15Five**. 15Five is built around a weekly check-in format where employees answer a short set of questions about wins, challenges, and progress on goals. The tool then surfaces a “best-self review” and a dashboard that managers can scan in minutes. It is especially strong for teams that want regular feedback loops without heavy overhead.

15Five

. 15Five is built around a weekly check-in format where employees answer a short set of questions about wins, challenges, and progress on goals. The tool then surfaces a “best-self review” and a dashboard that managers can scan in minutes. It is especially strong for teams that want regular feedback loops without heavy overhead.

alternative

Betterworks

. Betterworks focuses on continuous performance management with OKRs as the core. Its weekly check-in prompts ask for updates on key results, and the dashboard shows a rolling view of progress across teams and individuals. It often appeals to larger organizations that need alignment but still want a simple weekly rhythm.

alternative

Officevibe

. Officevibe is more of an employee engagement and pulse survey tool, but it includes a lightweight OKR check-in module. The weekly check-in questions can include goal progress, and its dashboard highlights morale, satisfaction, and alignment trends. It is best for teams that want to pair goal tracking with frequent culture measurement.

alternative

Reflektive

. Reflektive offers a continuous feedback platform with OKR tracking and weekly check-ins. Managers can see a simple progress dashboard and give real-time recognition or coaching based on updates. It works well for teams that value ongoing conversations alongside periodic goal reviews.

How they compare

All five alternatives support weekly check-ins and simple dashboards, but they differ in focus. Leapsome and Betterworks are built for robust OKR management with added performance review features. 15Five and Reflektive lean into feedback and coaching alongside goal updates. Officevibe prioritizes engagement and culture, with OKR as a secondary component. Weekdone itself stays hyper-focused on weekly OKR check-ins and minimal dashboards. Your choice depends on whether you want a pure goal tool or one that also handles people development, feedback, or engagement.
