As a Google Workspace admin, you already rely on Google Meet for internal and external video calls. But business needs change. You may need advanced security controls, larger meeting capacities, or deeper integration with non-Google productivity suites. Evaluating alternatives like Zoom and Microsoft Teams helps you make an informed decision when Google Meet is not the best fit for every use case.

alternative

Zoom

. Zoom offers a straightforward user interface and strong third-party app integrations. Its webinar and breakout room features are mature, and the admin dashboard provides granular reporting on meeting usage and participant activity.

alternative

Microsoft Teams

. Microsoft Teams tightly integrates with Microsoft 365, making it the natural choice for organizations already using Office and Exchange. It provides compliance boundaries, information barriers, and eDiscovery features that meet enterprise governance requirements.

How they compare

All three platforms offer HD video, screen sharing, and scheduling via calendar tools. Google Meet excels at simplicity and seamless integration with Google Workspace. Zoom stands out for large events and ease of use for external participants. Microsoft Teams delivers the deepest security and compliance controls for Microsoft-centric organizations. Your decision should hinge on your existing tech stack, regulatory obligations, and whether you prioritize ease of management or advanced meeting features.

Frequently asked questions

Can we keep using Google Meet for internal meetings while switching to an alternative for external calls?
Yes, many organizations run multiple video tools. You can route internal calls to Google Meet and external client calls to Zoom or Teams. Just ensure your single sign-on and calendar integrations still work smoothly.
How do admin controls compare between Google Meet, Zoom, and Microsoft Teams?
Google Meet provides basic usage reports and guest access controls. Zoom has detailed user activity reports and meeting lock options. Microsoft Teams offers the most extensive admin controls, including information barriers, retention policies, and compliance recording.
