Best Prezi alternatives

Prezi is famous for its zooming canvas and non-linear flow, which helps speakers tell stories that jump between ideas instead of following slides in a straight line. But Prezi’s subscription cost, limited offline access, and occasional motion sickness from too much zooming can be drawbacks. If you want dynamic, non-linear storytelling without those trade-offs, several alternatives offer similar freedom with their own unique strengths.

alternative

Canva

gives you a drag-and-drop design studio with a huge template library and a presentation mode that lets you add links, animations, and embedded videos. Its “present with a mic” feature and ability to jump to any page out of order make it a solid Prezi alternative for speakers who want visual polish without a steep learning curve.

alternative

Visme

combines presentation design with data visualization and interactive elements like clickable hotspots and hover effects. You can build a non-linear story by linking slides and adding navigation buttons, and it works well for both live talks and self-running presentations sent to audiences.

alternative

Powtoon

focuses on animated video presentations, but its drag-and-drop timeline lets you create a flow that zooms, pans, and transitions between scenes. Speakers who want a more cinematic, motion-driven story can use Powtoon to guide the viewer’s eye without the slide-by-slide feel.

alternative

Slidebean

uses AI to help you organize your content into a clean, minimal deck. While it is more linear by default, you can still add hyperlinks between slides and customize the order for a branching narrative. It is best for speakers who want professional design quickly and are comfortable with a slide-based approach that still allows jumps.

alternative

Crello

(now part of VistaCreate) offers thousands of animated templates and a simple editor for adding text, icons, and video. Its “present” mode works like a single canvas where you can click between elements in any order, making it a lightweight Prezi alternative for quick, visually engaging talks.

Frequently asked questions

Can I truly go back and forth between topics with these tools, like Prezi’s zoom?
Yes, in different ways. Canva and Visme let you set clickable navigation links or buttons to jump to any slide or element in any order. Powtoon and Crello rely more on a single canvas or timeline, but you can still arrange content so the audience sees different parts on demand.
Do these alternatives work offline for live speaking?
Most require an internet connection for the full interactive features. Canva and Slidebean have limited offline export (PDF or static images), while Visme and Powtoon are primarily cloud-based. For fully offline non-linear storytelling, a local program like Microsoft PowerPoint with custom hyperlinks might be a better option.
