If you manage frontline workers, you know the challenge of keeping a deskless team connected and informed. Two platforms often come up: Beekeeper and Staffbase. Beekeeper is built from the ground up for mobile-first communication, targeting hourly and shift workers who rely on their phones. Staffbase started as an employee app for intranet and internal communications, used more by corporate office environments. This article helps frontline workforce managers decide which platform fits their specific needs.

alternative

Staffbase

is an enterprise intranet suite that puts a strong emphasis on internal communications and content management for organizations. It offers a branded employee app, a news editor, and advanced analytics for communication teams. Staffbase works best when your workforce is mostly on desktops or tablets and you need a polished, corporate communications hub.

How they compare

Choose Beekeeper if your primary need is mobile-first access for hourly, shift, or deskless workers. Beekeeper makes onboarding easy for people without email logins, and its shift information and two way chat keep frontline teams aligned in real time. Choose Staffbase if your organization values a central intranet, rich editorial workflows for newsletters, and integration with existing corporate IT systems like SharePoint or Outlook. Staffbase is stronger for broad internal communications campaigns but less suited for hands on shift management and instant frontline collaboration.

Frequently asked questions

Can Beekeeper and Staffbase both replace my existing intranet?
Not . Beekeeper is not a traditional intranet, it is a frontline communication and operations hub. It replaces paper, email, and siloed messaging for deskless workers. Staffbase can act as a full intranet replacement for knowledge management and corporate news, but it lacks the shift scheduling and task management features that frontline teams need daily.
Which platform works better for workers who do not have a company email address?
Beekeeper handles this natively. Workers can sign up with a phone number or personal email, and managers can invite them via SMS or QR code. Staffbase typically requires an email based login and directory sync from Active Directory or a similar system, which can be a barrier for hourly workers without corporate accounts.
