Finance teams evaluating corporate card and spend management platforms often start with Ramp for its automation features and savings insights. But as your company grows or your needs shift, you might find that a different platform fits better. This guide covers the top Ramp alternatives: Brex, Divvy, Airbase, Mercury, and Spendesk. Each has strengths in areas like international payments, procurement controls, or startup banking integration. We will compare them so you can choose the right tool for your finance stack.

alternative

Brex

. Brex offers corporate cards with higher credit limits for startups and established companies, plus integrated expense management. Its rewards program focuses on points for travel and software, and it provides cash management accounts. Brex is a strong choice if you want a single platform for banking, cards, and expense reporting.

Divvy

. Divvy combines corporate cards with budget controls and bill payment in one platform. You can assign spend limits per employee and sync transactions to accounting software in real time. Divvy is especially useful for companies that need flexible budget management and want to reduce manual reconciliation.

alternative

Airbase

. Airbase provides a full procure to pay solution including corporate cards, virtual cards, and invoice management. Its approval workflows and audit trails give finance teams tight control over spending. Airbase is ideal for mid market companies that need robust procurement and accounting integrations.

Mercury

. Mercury offers business banking and corporate cards with no monthly fees, and it integrates directly with accounting software. It focuses on startups and tech companies, providing a simple dashboard for managing spend. Mercury is a good alternative if you prioritize low cost banking combined with basic card management.

alternative

Spendesk

. Spendesk is built for European and international teams, offering virtual and physical cards with real time spend tracking. It includes approval workflows, receipt capture, and integration with local accounting tools. Spendesk is a strong choice if your company operates across multiple currencies or has a remote workforce.

How they compare

Ramp and Brex both offer card based expense management with automation, but Brex provides broader banking features and higher credit limits. Divvy and Airbase emphasize budget controls and procurement workflows, making them better for companies with strict approval processes. Mercury is a lightweight, low cost option for startups already using Mercury banking. Spendesk excels for international teams needing multicurrency support and local compliance. Your decision should depend on whether your priority is credit access, procurement depth, banking integration, or global spend management.

Frequently asked questions

Can Ramp alternatives offer the same card controls and automation as Ramp?
Yes, most alternatives provide real time spend limits, receipt matching, and automated accounting sync. Brex and Divvy have similar automation features, while Airbase adds procurement workflows. The key difference is in how each platform handles approvals, virtual cards, and integrations with your existing tools.
Which alternative is best for a company with international employees or contractors?
Spendesk is built for international teams with multicurrency cards and compliance in many countries. Brex also supports global spend but focuses more on US based companies. Mercury and Airbase are primarily US focused, while Divvy has limited international functionality.
