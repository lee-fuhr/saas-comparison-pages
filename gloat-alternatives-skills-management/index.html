If you are a VP of Workforce Planning at a consulting firm, you may already use Gloat to match talent with project work based on skills. As your firm scales and demands more granular insights into contractor pools, internal mobility, and client staffing, you may want to evaluate other platforms that offer different strengths in AI matching, skill taxonomy, or integration with existing HR systems. This article compares three strong alternatives: Eightfold, Fuel50, and Workday Skills Cloud. We focus on what matters most for consulting firms: accuracy of talent matching, speed of deployment, and the ability to surface hidden skills across a distributed workforce.

alternative

Eightfold

uses a deep learning model that builds talent profiles from any data source, including resumes, project history, and even performance notes. For consulting firms with large contractor networks, this means you can quickly identify people with niche skills that might not be listed on a traditional resume. The platform also offers job and project matching at scale, which is useful when you staff multiple client engagements simultaneously.

alternative

Fuel50

places a strong emphasis on career pathing and internal talent mobility, which can be a differentiator for consulting firms that want to retain top consultants. Its AI recommends skill development paths and projects that agree with individual career goals, helping reduce turnover. The interface is designed to be employee friendly, making it easier to encourage consultants to update their skills and availability.

alternative

Workday Skills Cloud

is a native component of the Workday HCM ecosystem, so it integrates seamlessly if your firm already uses Workday for HR. It provides a skills ontology that is updated continuously from job data, course completions, and performance reviews. The matching engine surfaces internal talent for project staffing and can also suggest skill gaps to address before a new client engagement begins.

How they compare

All three alternatives offer AI driven talent matching, but they differ in where they excel. Eightfold is strongest for breadth of data ingestion and large scale matching, ideal for firms with extensive contractor databases. Fuel50 is better suited for firms that want to combine workforce planning with career development to boost retention. Workday Skills Cloud is the best fit for firms already on Workday, because it avoids data duplication and offers a unified view of skills across the organization. Gloat remains strong in project based gig matching and internal marketplace design, so consider whether your priority is speed of deployment (Eightfold), employee engagement (Fuel50), or system consolidation (Workday).

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative works best for consulting firms that rely heavily on external contractors rather than internal employees?
Eightfold is often the best choice because its AI can ingest and match profiles from many external sources, such as contractor databases, LinkedIn, and previous project records. It does not require contractors to maintain an internal profile. Workday Skills Cloud can also manage external talent if your firm uses Workday Recruiting, but its core strength is internal skills data.
How long does it typically take to implement one of these platforms for a consulting firm with 5,000 plus consultants?
Implementation timelines vary widely by data readiness and integration complexity. Eightfold can often be operational within 8 to 12 weeks if you have structured talent data. Fuel50 may take 12 to 16 weeks because it requires career path customization. Workday Skills Cloud is faster if you already run Workday HCM (4 to 8 weeks), but longer if you need to migrate from a different HR system. These are rough estimates; always request a pilot timeline from the vendor.
