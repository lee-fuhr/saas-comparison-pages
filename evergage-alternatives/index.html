Best Evergage alternatives

Evergage (now part of Salesforce Interaction Studio) is a strong platform for context-aware recommendations and unified customer profiles. But it may not fit every budget, tech stack, or growth stage. If you need similar capabilities with different pricing, easier integration, or a specific focus on personalization and profile unification, several strong alternatives exist. This guide covers seven tools that can replace or compete with Evergage, each with distinct strengths for companies that rely on real-time customer data and tailored experiences.

alternative

Dynamic Yield

excels at omnichannel personalization with machine learning driven recommendations and A/B testing. It builds unified customer profiles by combining behavioral data, purchase history, and real-time signals, then uses those profiles to serve context-aware content across web, mobile, email, and kiosks. Dynamic Yield is owned by Mastercard, which adds a layer of payment data integration potential.

alternative

Monetate

focuses on testing and personalization for ecommerce brands, especially those using legacy or custom tech stacks. It offers context-aware product recommendations and can stitch customer data from multiple sources into a single profile, but its strength is in easy to set up experiments and rapid campaign deployment. Monetate is a good fit for large retailers that need to iterate quickly without heavy engineering work.

alternative

Insider

combines a customer data platform, personalization engine, and multi-channel campaign tools in one suite. It builds unified profiles from web, app, and offline data, then triggers context-aware recommendations based on behavior, segment, or lifecycle stage. Insider is known for its strong segmentation capabilities and predictive modeling that helps brands anticipate customer needs.

alternative

Optimizely

(formerly Episerver) provides a digital experience platform with content management, A/B testing, and personalization features. Its recommendation engine uses customer profiles to deliver relevant content and products, but it is best suited for enterprises that already use Optimizely’s content management system. The platform’s strength lies in combining content and commerce personalization with full stack experimentation.

alternative

Adobe Target

is part of Adobe Experience Cloud and offers advanced personalization and testing with deep integration into Adobe’s ecosystem. It uses machine learning to build unified customer profiles via Adobe Analytics and Adobe Audience Manager, then delivers context-aware recommendations across web, mobile, and apps. Adobe Target is ideal for large organizations already invested in Adobe products, but it requires significant setup and ongoing management.

alternative

Qubit

provides a customer data platform and personalization engine focused on retail and travel. It unifies first party data into a single view and uses AI to serve recommendations and next best actions. Qubit is known for its ability to handle large scale personalization with a strong analytics layer, making it suitable for brands that want to move beyond basic rule based targeting.

alternative

Convert.com

is a simpler, more affordable A/B testing and personalization tool that still offers context-aware recommendations via its visitor segmentation and targeting features. It can pull in data from third party sources to build basic customer profiles, but it does not have as deep a unified profile engine as Evergage or the others. Convert.com is a good choice for medium sized businesses that need a budget friendly option with solid testing capabilities.

How they compare

All seven alternatives offer context-aware recommendations and tools to build customer profiles, but they differ in complexity, price, and integration depth. Dynamic Yield and Insider provide the most comprehensive all in one personalization and profile tools, similar to Evergage. Monetate and Qubit are well suited for ecommerce focused brands with existing data stacks. Adobe Target and Optimizely are best for large enterprises already using their respective platforms. Convert.com is the most straightforward and affordable option, but its profile unification is more limited. When choosing, consider your data sources, team size, and whether you need a standalone personalization engine or a full digital experience platform.
