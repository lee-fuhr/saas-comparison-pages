Best TouchBistro alternatives

TouchBistro is a reliable iPad-based POS, but for cafés and quick-service spots, it can feel built more for full-service restaurants. The monthly fees, hardware costs, and table-focused features may not match the speed and simplicity your counter-service business needs. That is why many operators look for TouchBistro alternatives that are cheaper, easier to set up, or better suited for high-volume, low-ticket orders. Here we compare three popular iPad-based options: Toast POS, Square Restaurants, and Lightspeed Restaurant.

alternative

Toast POS

offers a dedicated quick-service mode with a streamlined order screen and built-in online ordering. Its hardware is iPad compatible, and the flat-rate pricing includes no surprise fees for processing or integrations. Toast also provides strong inventory tracking and customer management, making it a good fit for cafes that want one system for dine-in and takeaway.

alternative

Square Restaurants

is the most affordable alternative, with no monthly subscription for the basic plan and a simple, intuitive interface. It works on any iPad and lets you start taking orders in minutes. Square is ideal for small cafés that need low upfront costs and easy payment processing, though advanced features like multi-location reporting come at an extra cost.

alternative

Lightspeed Restaurant

gives café owners powerful inventory and supplier management without the complexity of a full enterprise system. Its iPad app is fast for counter orders, and it supports tableside service if you ever add that. Lightspeed also offers multi-location reporting and offline mode, useful for cafes with busy weekends or unreliable internet.

How they compare

All three tools run on iPads, but they differ in pricing and focus. Square Restaurants is the cheapest entry point with no monthly fee, perfect for a single café just getting started. Toast POS has a higher monthly cost but includes more restaurant-specific features, like built-in online ordering and loyalty programs. Lightspeed Restaurant falls in the middle, with strong inventory tools that suit cafes that bake or prepare food in batches. For quick-service spots that prioritize speed and low overhead, Square often wins. For cafes that want room to grow into full restaurant features, Toast or Lightspeed are better options.

Frequently asked questions

Is TouchBistro better for table-service restaurants than for cafes?
Yes. TouchBistro was designed mainly for full-service dining, with features like table mapping and server assignments. For quick-service cafes that take orders at a counter, TouchBistro can feel clunky. The alternatives listed here are built to handle counter orders more efficiently.
Can I migrate my menu and customer data from TouchBistro to these alternatives?
Each provider offers import tools, but the process is not always automatic. Square Restaurants lets you upload a CSV of items. Toast and Lightspeed have onboarding teams that help with data transfer. Expect some manual work for custom modifiers or gift card balances.
