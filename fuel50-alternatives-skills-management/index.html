If you are evaluating career pathing platforms for a university or large academic institution, you likely know Fuel50 as a tool that maps employee skills to career opportunities. While Fuel50 offers strong internal talent mobility features, other platforms may better fit the unique needs of academia, such as managing faculty career tracks, integrating with existing HR systems, or supporting large scale skills data. This article compares Fuel50 with three key alternatives: Gloat, Eightfold, and Workday Skills Cloud. Each option brings different strengths for career development directors who need to guide staff and faculty toward clear, data driven career paths.

alternative

Gloat

. Gloat focuses on internal talent marketplaces and project based work, which can help academic staff explore short term assignments or temporary roles across departments. Its intelligent matching engine suggests opportunities based on skills, interests, and career goals, but it may require more customization for tenure and faculty specific pathways.

alternative

Eightfold

. Eightfold uses artificial intelligence to build detailed skills profiles from resumes, learning history, and past job data, making it easier to uncover hidden talent within large academic workforces. Its skills ontology updates automatically, though you may need to supplement with academic specific taxonomies for research tracts and teaching roles.

alternative

Workday Skills Cloud

. Workday Skills Cloud is a skills taxonomy layer that integrates natively with Workday HCM, common in many large universities. It provides a central skills library and enables career pathing directly within existing HR workflows, but it requires your institution to already use Workday for full benefit and can be less intuitive for standalone career development initiatives.

How they compare

Fuel50 is best if you want a dedicated career pathing tool with strong emphasis on coaching and employee engagement, but it may lack the AI driven skills inference of Eightfold or the seamless HR integration of Workday Skills Cloud. Gloat excels at creating internal talent marketplaces for project work, while Eightfold offers deeper skills discovery and automatic profiles. Workday Skills Cloud is ideal for institutions already on Workday, as it avoids data duplication. For academic environments, consider whether you need faculty specific tracks, integration with existing HR systems, or a tool that can scale across both staff and faculty roles.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative works best for universities that do not use Workday?
Eightfold and Gloat are both strong choices. Eightfold provides robust AI skills matching without reliance on a specific HR system. Gloat focuses on internal talent mobility through projects and gigs, which can be implemented alongside any HCM, though it may require more manual setup for faculty career paths.
Can these tools handle faculty tenure tracks and adjunct roles in addition to staff positions?
Yes, but you will need to customize each tool. Eightfold allows you to build custom career pathways and skills definitions. Gloat can define project based roles for adjuncts or temporary assignments. Fuel50 also supports custom career maps. Workday Skills Cloud works best if you already have defined career structures in Workday; you can add academic specific job families and skills.
