Best Carbonite alternatives

If you run a small business, losing data can mean lost customers, missed payroll, or even closing your doors. Cloud backup and recovery tools like Carbonite and Druva help you protect your files and systems without needing an IT team. Carbonite is a popular choice for small businesses because it offers simple, automatic backups with clear pricing. Druva also provides cloud backup but focuses more on larger organizations and includes features like endpoint protection. This article compares Carbonite to Druva and other alternatives to help you choose the right tool for your business.

alternative

Zerto

. Zerto specializes in continuous data protection and disaster recovery, which is ideal if your small business cannot afford any downtime. It replicates data in near real time so you can fail over to a secondary location quickly, but it is more expensive and requires more expertise to set up than Carbonite.

alternative

Veeam

. Veeam offers backup and recovery for virtual, physical, and cloud workloads, with a strong reputation in the mid-market. It provides flexible restore options, including instant VM recovery, but its licensing can be complex and may not fit a very small budget. Many small businesses choose Veeam when they have a mix of on-premises and cloud resources.

alternative

Druva

. Druva is a cloud-native backup platform that works well for companies that need to protect endpoints, servers, and SaaS applications in one place. It can handle large-scale deployments but may feel complex for a very small team, and its pricing is based on the amount of data you back up rather than a flat rate. The service includes features like ransomware detection and global deduplication to save storage.

alternative

Acronis

. Acronis combines backup, anti-malware, and disaster recovery into one product, which can simplify IT management. Its Cyber Protect suite is easy to deploy and offers both file-level and full system backups. However, some users find the interface less intuitive than Carbonite’s, and pricing can increase quickly as you add features.

How they compare

Carbonite and Druva both protect your data in the cloud, but they target different needs. Carbonite is simpler, with flat pricing and less manual configuration, making it ideal for small businesses that want a set and forget solution. Druva offers more advanced features like endpoint backup and ransomware protection, but it can be harder to budget for because costs grow with data volume. If your main goal is straightforward file and server backup without complexity, Carbonite is the better fit. If you need broader coverage for laptops, mobile devices, and strict compliance, consider Druva or an alternative like Acronis.
