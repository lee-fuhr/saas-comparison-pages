Gumroad is a popular platform for selling digital products, including online courses. It is simple to use and charges a flat fee per sale. However, lifelong learners who also create courses sometimes look for alternatives because they want more built in teaching tools, better student management, or different pricing. Below are four strong alternatives that offer more course focused features.

alternative

Podia

is an all in one platform that lets you sell courses, memberships, and digital downloads without transaction fees on higher plans. It includes email marketing and a simple website builder, making it great for creators who want to keep everything under one roof.

alternative

Teachable

is designed specifically for online courses and gives you a clean, branded school site. It offers features like quizzes, certificates, and course completion tracking, which help you build a structured learning experience for your students.

alternative

Kajabi

is a full featured marketing and course platform with tools for email automation, sales funnels, and pipelines. It works well if you want to run your entire online business from one place, but it comes with a higher monthly price.

alternative

Thinkific

focuses on course creation with an easy drag and drop builder and built in community features. It offers a free plan to get started, making it a good option for beginners who want to test course creation without upfront costs.

How they compare

All four alternatives give you more control over your course content and student experience than Gumroad. Podia and Thinkific offer free or low cost entry plans, while Teachable balances features and price for intermediate creators. Kajabi is the most expensive but includes advanced marketing and automation tools. If you need simple checkout with no monthly fee, Gumroad still works well. But for building a course business with quizzes, community, and email, these alternatives provide a better fit.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a complete beginner who has never made a course before?
Thinkific is a good choice because its free plan lets you create and host a course without paying anything upfront. The drag and drop builder is intuitive, and you can add students directly. Podia also has a free trial and simple interface.
Do these alternatives charge transaction fees on top of the monthly subscription?
Yes, but the fees vary. Podia charges no transaction fees on its plus plan and above. Teachable charges a 5 percent fee on the free plan but none on higher tiers. Thinkific has no transaction fees on any paid plan. Kajabi does not charge extra transaction fees on any of its plans.
