Best Wave alternatives

If you use Wave for your small business finances, you already appreciate its free pricing and simple setup. But as your freelance work or micro-business grows, you might want features like project profitability reports, time tracking, payroll, or inventory management. Wave’s free plan covers the basics of invoicing and receipts, but its limited features can hold you back. These five alternatives offer more advanced tools while still being designed for small operations.

alternative

QuickBooks

is a robust option with powerful features like payroll, inventory, and tax preparation. It offers several plans for different business sizes, including a self-employed version for freelancers. The learning curve is steeper than Wave, but the depth of features is unmatched for growing businesses that need detailed reports and multi-user access.

Try QuickBooks →
alternative

FreshBooks

excels at invoicing and time tracking for service-based freelancers. Its clean interface makes it easy to send estimates, log hours from a timer, and accept credit card payments. It is best for professionals who need to track billable time per project and want automated late payment reminders.

Try FreshBooks →
alternative

Zoho Books

works well if you already use other Zoho apps or want a tool that scales affordably. It offers automation for recurring invoices, bank reconciliation, and inventory tracking. Pricing is per business tier, and its free plan covers up to one user and a limited number of transactions.

Try Zoho Books →
alternative

ZipBooks

has a free tier that offers more than Wave, including time tracking and project profitability reports. Its smart features like auto-categorization and color coded reports save you time. Paid plans add advanced reporting, inventory, and the ability to send unlimited invoices.

alternative

Kashoo

is a good choice for solopreneurs who want simplicity without sacrificing core features. It offers straightforward invoicing, auto-categorization of expenses, and integration with payment processors. The interface is clean and requires almost no learning curve, making it a smooth switch from Wave.

How they compare

Each tool has its strengths. QuickBooks is the most feature-rich but costs more and takes time to learn. FreshBooks is best for service freelancers who bill by the hour. Zoho Books is ideal for those in the Zoho ecosystem. ZipBooks offers a competitive free plan with features Wave lacks. Kashoo is the simplest for single users who want a bare bones upgrade. Your choice depends on whether you need inventory, payroll, multi-user access, or detailed project reports.

Frequently asked questions

How do I move my data from Wave to another accounting tool?
Most alternatives offer data import tools for CSV files. Export your transactions, invoices, and client list from Wave, then use the import wizard in your new tool. Check each tool’s support page for specific guides and templates to ensure a clean transfer.
Is there a free accounting alternative that has more features than Wave?
Yes, ZipBooks offers a free tier that includes time tracking and project profit reports, features Wave does not offer on its free plan. Zoho Books also has a free plan for very small businesses with limited transactions. Free versions often limit the number of clients, invoices, or users, so check the details.
