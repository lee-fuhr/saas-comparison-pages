If you are a freelancer looking for a simple way to schedule social media posts while keeping evergreen content running on repeat, you might already know Publer. It does the job, but sometimes you need a different fit for your budget, workflow, or platform mix. Here are three solid Publer alternatives that focus on ease of use and evergreen posting: Buffer, Hootsuite, and Later. Each has its own strengths, and this guide helps you pick the right one.

alternative

Buffer

. Buffer is built for simplicity with a clean interface that makes scheduling a breeze. Its “Requeue” feature lets you recycle your best evergreen posts on a loop, so you can set them once and forget them. This makes Buffer a great choice if you value minimal setup and want to focus on a few key platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn.

Hootsuite

. Hootsuite offers more advanced features like bulk scheduling and team collaboration, which can be useful if your freelance work grows. It also supports evergreen posting through its “Bulk Composer” and auto-scheduling options, but the dashboard can feel crowded. For a freelancer who wants robust analytics and multiple social accounts in one place, Hootsuite is a powerful alternative.

Later

. Later is a visual-first scheduler that excels for Instagram and other image-heavy platforms. Its “Recycler” feature automatically reshare older posts, making it easy to keep your evergreen content active without manual work. Later also provides a simple calendar drag and drop system, ideal if you plan visually and need a tool that feels intuitive.

How they compare

When choosing between these Publer alternatives, consider what matters most to you. Buffer is the simplest option with a straightforward requeue function for evergreen posts, perfect if you want minimal fuss. Later is best for visual platforms like Instagram and offers a dedicated recycling tool for reposting. Hootsuite has the most features and supports larger account management, but its complexity might be overkill for a solo freelancer. All three support evergreen capabilities, but Buffer and Later are more focused on ease of use for small scale scheduling.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for reposting evergreen content automatically?
Both Buffer and Later have dedicated features for this. Buffer’s Requeue and Later’s Recycler let you set a post to repeat on a schedule so you do not have to manually reshare.
Can I use these tools for free as a freelancer?
Yes, all three offer free plans with limits on the number of posts or accounts. Buffer’s free plan allows up to 10 scheduled posts per account, Later’s free plan covers one social set with 30 posts, and Hootsuite’s free plan supports up to 2 accounts with 5 scheduled posts. Check their pricing pages for current details.
