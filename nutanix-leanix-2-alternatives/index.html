Best Nutanix Xi Leap alternatives

Disaster recovery as a service (DRaaS) on hyper-converged infrastructure (HCI) can simplify failover and reduce datacenter complexity. Nutanix Xi Leap is a native DRaaS solution built directly into the Nutanix platform, allowing enterprises to replicate and recover virtual machines without separate hardware or additional software layers. It offers one-click orchestration, automatic application grouping, and non-disruptive testing from the same management interface you already use for your Nutanix cluster. For organizations already running Nutanix, Xi Leap minimizes operational overhead and aligns with HCI principles of simplicity and scalability.

alternative

Zerto

delivers continuous data protection with near-zero recovery point objectives (RPOs) by replicating changes at the hypervisor layer. It works across multiple hypervisors and clouds, making it a strong choice for heterogeneous environments, but it requires a separate virtual appliance and licensing that can add cost compared to a native Nutanix solution.

alternative

Veeam

provides mature backup and recovery capabilities with flexible replication options for on-premises, cloud, and hybrid scenarios. Its DR functionality is part of a broader data protection suite, so enterprises already using Veeam for backups can extend it to DR, though it lacks the tight HCI integration and simple one-click orchestration that Xi Leap offers on Nutanix.

alternative

Druva

is a cloud-only SaaS platform that eliminates on-premises infrastructure for backup and DR. It supports Nutanix AHV clusters through its agentless backup but focuses on data protection rather than full site-to-site failover orchestration, which may not meet enterprises that need rapid, automated recovery within the same HCI ecosystem.

alternative

Acronis

offers cyber protection that includes backup, disaster recovery, and security features in a single platform. It can protect Nutanix workloads via agentless backup, but its DR capabilities are more limited than Xi Leap’s native integration, and it is often used for smaller deployments rather than large enterprise HCI environments.
