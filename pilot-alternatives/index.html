If you are a small business owner using Pilot for bookkeeping, you might be looking for other options. Pilot is a full service bookkeeping solution, but it may not fit every budget or workflow. This article compares six strong alternatives that give you more control, lower costs, or different features. Each tool listed here offers something distinct, whether you want do it yourself software or a hybrid service.

alternative

QuickBooks

. QuickBooks is the most widely used accounting software for small businesses. It offers invoicing, expense tracking, payroll, and reporting in one platform. You can choose between the online version for cloud access or the desktop version for local control.

alternative

FreshBooks

. FreshBooks is designed for service based businesses like freelancers and consultants. It excels at time tracking, project billing, and sending professional invoices. The interface is simple and intuitive, making it easy to learn without accounting experience.

alternative

Wave

. Wave is a free accounting software for basic bookkeeping needs. It includes invoicing, receipt scanning, and bank reconciliation with no monthly fee. You only pay for optional extras like payment processing or payroll.

alternative

Bench

. Bench provides a human bookkeeping service similar to Pilot. A dedicated team categorizes your transactions and prepares monthly financial statements. You get a software dashboard to view your reports, but Bench handles the data entry for you.

alternative

Zoho Books

. Zoho Books is part of the larger Zoho ecosystem, making it a great choice if you already use Zoho apps. It automates bank feeds, tracks expenses, and handles inventory and projects. The pricing is affordable for growing small businesses.

alternative

Xero

. Xero is a cloud based accounting platform known for its strong bank reconciliation and collaboration features. It allows unlimited users on most plans, which is useful if you have a bookkeeper or accountant. Its app marketplace connects to hundreds of third party tools.

How they compare

Pilot and Bench are both full service bookkeeping options where a real person does the work for you. QuickBooks, Xero, and FreshBooks are do it yourself software that give you more hands on control. Wave is the only free option, but it lacks advanced features and support. Zoho Books is a strong middle ground if you want automation without a high price tag. The best choice depends on whether you prefer to delegate bookkeeping or manage it yourself.

Frequently asked questions

When should I switch from Pilot to a do it yourself tool like QuickBooks or Xero?
Switch if you want to reduce monthly costs, have the time and confidence to manage your own books, or need more customization like inventory or payroll features that Pilot may not offer.
Can I use Wave for free and still get reliable bookkeeping?
Yes, Wave is free for core features like invoicing and expense tracking. However, you will need to handle all data entry yourself, and customer support is limited. It works best for very small businesses with simple finances.
