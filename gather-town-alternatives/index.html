Gather.town made remote work feel less lonely by blending video chat with a pixel-art world. But not every team wants that exact retro vibe or needs a platform that works well for large all-hands meetings. If you are looking for a different balance of gamification, spatial audio, or simpler navigation, several alternatives offer fresh ways to connect. Below we compare five tools that remote companies can use to create a fun, interactive office environment without relying on Gather.town.

alternative

Teamflow

focuses on virtual offices that feel like real workplaces. You walk around a 2D floor plan, and your video and audio automatically switch based on proximity. It works especially well for teams that want spontaneous side conversations without the overhead of scheduling separate calls.

alternative

Wonder.me

takes a more minimalist approach. Instead of a map, you see a grid of people and can move yourself into small “wonder” rooms to talk. It is less about exploring a game world and more about creating quick, informal breakout moments during meetings or co-working sessions.

alternative

Sococo

offers a top-down office layout with color-coded rooms (conference room, break room, etc.). You click to enter a room, and your webcam turns on for everyone there. It is straightforward and low on flashy graphics, which makes it a good fit for companies that want structure without distraction.

alternative

SpatialChat

uses a canvas where you drag your avatar near others to hear and see them. The background can be any image (a beach, a spaceship, a cozy cafe), and you can share screens or whiteboards in the same space. It is more visual than Wonder.me but less game-like than Gather.town.

alternative

VirBELA

is the most immersive option on this list. It builds full 3D environments with customizable avatars, virtual campuses, and event spaces. It is designed more for large conferences or onboarding programs than daily stand-ups, but teams that crave a true video-game feel will love it.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a team that wants a game-like experience without overwhelming complexity?
SpatialChat offers a good middle ground. You can customize the background and move your avatar freely, but you do not have to learn quest mechanics or manage inventory. It feels playful without being distracting.
Can I use these tools for company-wide all-hands meetings or just for small team interactions?
Some tools scale well, others do not. VirBELA and Teamflow can handle large groups because they divide participants into zones or rooms. Wonder.me and Sococo work best for groups under 50 people. SpatialChat can host hundreds but might feel crowded if too many users try to talk at once.
