Dscout is a popular tool for diary studies, but it may not fit every budget or workflow. Field researchers often explore alternatives when they need different pricing, participant recruitment, or analysis features. Below we cover four substitutes that can handle diary studies with their own strengths.

alternative

UserTesting

focuses on video feedback and task-based tests, but its diary study capabilities are more limited than dscout. You can set up longitudinal studies with scheduled prompts, but the platform is designed more for quick, unmoderated sessions. It offers a large participant panel but may cost more for extended diary research.

alternative

Lookback

excels at live, moderated sessions and screen recording, which can complement diary studies when you want to observe behavior in real time. It supports asynchronous video diaries through its “Lookback Gaze” feature, though it is less structured for multi-day journaling. The platform is strong for in-depth interviews and usability tests alongside diary tasks.

alternative

PlaybookUX

is built for lightweight, remote research with a simple interface for collecting video responses. It works for diary studies via scheduled prompts and allows participants to record videos on their own time. It is a cost-effective option for small teams, but lacks advanced analytics or panel management.

alternative

UserZoom

is an enterprise platform that includes diary study capabilities through its “Unmoderated” and “Mobile” study types. It offers robust recruitment and analysis tools, making it suitable for large scale diary research. However, the complexity and high price may be overkill for smaller projects.

How they compare

Dscout remains the most dedicated diary study tool with its structured journal entries and rich media capture. UserTesting is better for quick video tasks rather than extended diaries. Lookback suits moderated research with diary elements. PlaybookUX offers a simple, low cost entry point for diary studies. UserZoom provides enterprise grade features but at a higher cost and complexity.

Frequently asked questions

Can UserTesting be used for multi-day diary studies?
Yes, but it requires manual setup of daily tasks and lacks the dedicated journaling interface that dscout offers. It works best for short, task based studies rather than open ended diary entries.
What is the cheapest alternative to dscout for diary studies?
PlaybookUX is generally the most affordable option for small teams. It has a simple pricing model and no long term contracts, but it lacks advanced recruitment and analysis features.
