Mid-market firms often need a risk management platform that adapts to their unique processes without requiring a huge IT team. Onspring is built for this: it offers flexible workflow-based risk management that you can configure yourself. This article compares Onspring against four common alternatives, with a focus on ServiceNow GRC. The goal is to help you decide which tool best fits your need for customizable workflows and manageable costs.

alternative

ServiceNow GRC

is a powerful platform that ties risk management into a larger IT service management ecosystem. It works well if you already use ServiceNow for other functions, but its complexity and licensing costs can be high for mid-market firms. You may need dedicated administrators to maintain workflows and integrations.

alternative

MetricStream

provides enterprise-grade governance, risk, and compliance features with deep reporting. It is often chosen by large organizations because of its breadth, but mid-market teams can find the setup and ongoing configuration overwhelming. Custom workflows tend to require professional services or specialized in-house skills.

alternative

RSA Archer

is a long-standing GRC tool with a vast library of use cases and modules. Its flexibility comes from a highly configurable data model, but that same flexibility can lead to long implementation cycles. Mid-market firms may face higher total cost of ownership due to licensing tiers and support costs.

alternative

LogicGate

focuses on low-code process automation for risk and compliance. It is user-friendly and quick to deploy, making it appealing for smaller teams. However, its library of pre-built GRC content is smaller than some competitors, so you might need to build more from scratch.

How they compare

Onspring and ServiceNow GRC both offer workflow automation, but they target different needs. Onspring is purpose-built for mid-market firms: it provides pre-built risk templates and drag-and-drop workflow builders that non-technical users can manage. ServiceNow GRC is more suited for enterprises that already invest in the ServiceNow platform and need deep integration with ITSM. In terms of cost, Onspring typically has lower annual fees and fewer hidden expenses. For workflow flexibility, Onspring lets you modify processes in minutes with conditional logic, while ServiceNow GRC often requires scripting or certified administrators. If you value fast setup and ongoing agility without a large support team, Onspring is the stronger choice for mid-market risk management.

Frequently asked questions

How easy is it to change a risk workflow after it is live in Onspring versus ServiceNow GRC?
Onspring allows you to modify workflows, forms, and reports through a visual editor without writing code. Changes take effect immediately. In ServiceNow GRC, altering workflows often requires updating scripts or calling the platform team, which can cause delays.
Can Onspring integrate with my existing HR or finance systems without extra licensing costs?
Yes. Onspring includes a REST API and pre-built connectors for common business tools. There are no additional fees for API access or standard integrations. ServiceNow GRC may require purchasing extra integration hub modules or paying per integration transaction.
