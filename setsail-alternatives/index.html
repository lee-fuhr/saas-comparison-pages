SetSail is a popular revenue intelligence platform that uses activity data to improve forecast accuracy. But if your mid-market company values a simple, spreadsheet-style approach to forecasting over complex automation, you might be looking for alternatives. Whether you want less configuration, clearer pipeline views, or a lower price point, several tools offer a more straightforward manual or spreadsheet-like experience. Here we review five notable alternatives to SetSail and compare them for teams that prefer simplicity.

alternative

Clari

. Clari is a leader in revenue operations, but it can feel heavy for mid-market teams wanting a spreadsheet-style workflow. Its strength is AI-driven predictions and data integration, which may be more than needed if you just want to view and edit a clean pipeline table like a spreadsheet.

alternative

Aviso

. Aviso offers revenue forecasting with a focus on sales execution and deal reviews. It can be simpler than Clari but still relies on connecting many data sources. For teams that want a manual, cell-by-cell forecasting method, Aviso may require more setup than you prefer.

alternative

BoostUp

. BoostUp provides revenue intelligence with an emphasis on rep behavior and pipeline health. Its interface is less spreadsheet-like than some alternatives, but it does offer a structured pipeline view. However, the tool is best for teams that want automation, not manual data entry.

alternative

People.ai

. People.ai captures activity data to build forecasts automatically, which reduces manual work. This is the opposite of a spreadsheet-style approach. If your team wants to type in numbers directly rather than rely on activity signals, People.ai may feel too rigid and automated.

alternative

Forecastly

. Forecastly is designed specifically as a spreadsheet-style revenue forecasting tool for mid-market companies. It lives in Google Sheets or Excel, so your team can keep their familiar workflow. Forecastly adds live pipeline data and collaboration features without forcing you to learn a new interface.

Frequently asked questions

What makes a tool “spreadsheet-style” for revenue forecasting?
Spreadsheet-style means you can enter and edit forecast numbers directly in a grid, just like in Excel or Google Sheets. The tool should not require heavy automation or data mapping, and should let you manually override any cell without friction.
Is SetSail itself a good option for companies that prefer manual forecasting?
SetSail is built around activity data and machine learning, so it works best when you want forecasts generated from rep actions. If your team prefers to type numbers into a familiar spreadsheet layout and then share that view with managers, SetSail may feel too automated and less transparent.
