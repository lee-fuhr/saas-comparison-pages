Best PowerSchool alternatives

PowerSchool is a widely used student information system (SIS) and gradebook platform, but many districts find that its complexity, cost, or limited flexibility no longer fits their needs. When your district requires robust SIS and grading integration, switching to an alternative means finding a system that can handle attendance, report cards, and grade calculations as smoothly as possible. This article compares three common alternatives: Canvas LMS, Schoology, and Google Classroom. Each offers different strengths for districts that need strong SIS and grading workflow support.

alternative

Canvas LMS

offers powerful grading features with flexible gradebook settings, standards based grading, and deep integration with many SIS systems through automated data sync. It can replace some SIS gradebook functions if your district uses a separate SIS for core student records. Canvas is especially strong for K-12 districts that want a single platform for instruction and assessment.

alternative

Schoology

comes with built in SIS capabilities such as attendance, report cards, and a master gradebook, which means it can serve as both your learning management system and a partial SIS replacement. Its grading integration is tight and real time, making it a strong choice for districts that want to reduce the number of separate tools. Schoology also includes extensive parent communication features.

alternative

Google Classroom

is a free and simple tool that integrates seamlessly with Google Workspace, but it lacks a native gradebook that connects directly to a district SIS. To get robust SIS and grading integration, districts typically need to add a third party sync tool like Schoolytics or a separate SIS. It works best for smaller districts or as a supplement rather than a full SIS alternative.

How they compare

Canvas LMS gives you the most advanced grading features and the most integration options with existing SIS systems, but it is the most expensive. Schoology includes its own SIS gradebook and attendance, so it can reduce the need for a separate SIS, making it a strong all in one choice for districts that want to simplify. Google Classroom is the cheapest and easiest to start with, but it requires extra tools to handle the deep grading and SIS integration that larger districts need. Your choice depends on whether you prefer a standalone LMS that connects to your SIS or a combined platform that replaces some SIS functions.

Frequently asked questions

Can Canvas LMS completely replace my district’s PowerSchool SIS?
No. Canvas is a learning management system with a powerful gradebook, but it does not manage core SIS records like enrollment, scheduling, or demographic data. You would still need a separate SIS to handle those functions, though Canvas can sync grades and assignments with your chosen SIS.
What is the biggest limitation of Google Classroom for districts that need strong grading integration?
Google Classroom’s basic gradebook does not allow weighted categories, custom grading scales, or direct SIS sync without using a paid third party tool. Districts with complex grading policies or those that need automated report card generation will find Google Classroom insufficient without additional software.
