Best PlanGrid alternatives

If your field crew relies on mobile access to blueprints and the ability to make mark-ups on the go, you likely know PlanGrid well. It has been a go-to tool for keeping drawings current and collaborating from the job site. But maybe you are looking for a change because of cost, integration needs, or specific features that PlanGrid does not offer. The good news is there are strong alternatives that also put drawings and mark-ups right in your hands. Below we look at four popular options: Procore, Buildertrend, Autodesk BIM 360, and CoConstruct.

alternative

Procore

. Procore gives field crews a mobile app where they can view the latest drawings, add mark-ups, and link them directly to issues or punch list items. Its tools for document control and RFIs mean your mark-ups travel with the project history, not just the drawing.

alternative

Buildertrend

. Buildertrend focuses on small to mid-size crews. You can open plans on your phone or tablet, highlight changes, and attach photos or notes to those mark-ups. It also integrates daily logs and field communications so your blueprint comments connect to the rest of your workflow.

alternative

Autodesk BIM 360

. BIM 360 (now part of Autodesk Construction Cloud) offers robust mobile access to 2D drawings and 3D models. Field crews can measure, add mark-ups, and sync changes instantly with the office. It works especially well if your team already uses other Autodesk tools like Revit or AutoCAD.

alternative

CoConstruct

. CoConstruct is built for custom builders and remodelers. Its mobile app lets you view plans, add mark-ups, and share them with subs or the home office. The mark-ups stay tied to the project schedule and budget, making it easy to see how a drawing note affects the overall plan.

How they compare

All four alternatives give your field crew mobile access to blueprints and the ability to mark them up. The main differences come down to project size, integration, and price. Procore is a full-featured platform for larger projects and teams, but it can cost more. Buildertrend is simpler and more affordable for smaller crews, though it may lack some advanced document tools. Autodesk BIM 360 is powerful if you work with BIM or 3D models, but it has a steeper learning curve. CoConstruct suits custom home builders who want tight links between drawings, change orders, and client communication. None of these tools are like PlanGrid, so your choice depends on which extra features (like daily logs, RFIs, or model viewing) matter most to your crew.
