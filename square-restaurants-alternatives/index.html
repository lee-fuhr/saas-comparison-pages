Best Square Restaurants alternatives

Square Restaurants is a popular POS system for eateries, but it ties you to Square’s own payment processing and specific hardware. If you want more freedom to choose your own terminals, tablets, or printers, you need a solution that works with whatever equipment you already own or prefer. This article covers three strong alternatives that offer hardware-agnostic setups, giving you flexibility without locking you into one vendor’s ecosystem.

alternative

Toast POS

is a restaurant-focused platform that works with a wide range of third-party hardware, including standard tablets, receipt printers, and payment terminals. It offers built-in online ordering and loyalty features, plus you can choose your own payment processor if you prefer a different rate structure. This makes it a solid choice if you want a dedicated restaurant system without being forced to use Toast’s specific devices.

alternative

Lightspeed Restaurant

gives you the ability to mix and match hardware from different brands, so you are not stuck with proprietary terminals. The cloud-based system scales from a single food truck to multi-location fine dining, and it integrates with popular payment gateways like Stripe and Worldpay. Lightspeed also provides real-time inventory tracking and menu management, all while letting you use your existing iPad or Android tablet as the main register.

alternative

TouchBistro

is an iPad-native POS that lets you pair your own iPads with a variety of receipt printers, cash drawers, and card readers. It includes table management, floor plan views, and offline mode, so you can keep taking orders even if the internet drops. TouchBistro does not lock you into its own payment processor either, giving you the freedom to negotiate your own rates.

How they compare

All three alternatives share a hardware-agnostic philosophy, meaning you can use your own tablets, printers, and payment terminals. Toast excels in deep restaurant-specific features like kitchen display systems and online ordering. Lightspeed offers broader retail and inventory capabilities for eateries that also sell merchandise. TouchBistro focuses on simplicity and offline reliability, making it ideal for smaller or mobile operations. Your choice depends on how much restaurant specialization you need versus how much hardware flexibility you want.

Frequently asked questions

Can I keep using my existing credit card terminals with these alternatives?
Yes. Toast, Lightspeed, and TouchBistro all support third-party payment processors and standard EMV terminals. You just need to check compatibility with their software, but in most cases you can bring your own hardware.
Do these alternatives work offline if my internet goes down?
TouchBistro has a built-in offline mode that lets you continue taking orders and processing payments. Lightspeed and Toast also offer offline capabilities, though the specific features vary. Always confirm with the provider how orders sync once the connection is restored.
