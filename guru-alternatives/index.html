Best Guru alternatives

Many customer support and sales teams start with Guru because of its AI-powered knowledge capture and verification features. However, as your team grows or your workflows change, you might need a different tool for organizing internal documentation. Common reasons to look for Guru alternatives include pricing that scales with users, more flexible editing and structure, better integration with your existing tech stack, or a simpler interface for non-technical team members. Below we compare five strong contenders to help you find the right fit.

alternative

Confluence

. Confluence by Atlassian is a robust documentation platform built for larger teams that need deep integration with Jira and other project management tools. It offers powerful page hierarchy, templates, and permissions but can feel heavy for small teams or those who only need a simple knowledge base.

Try Confluence →
alternative

Notion

. Notion combines docs, wikis, databases, and project boards in one flexible workspace. It is popular for its easy editing, customizable layouts, and strong search, though it may require more setup time to match Guru’s AI verification and inline capture features.

Try Notion →
alternative

Tettra

. Tettra is designed specifically for internal knowledge bases with a focus on simplicity and team adoption. It uses a question and answer format and integrates well with Slack, making it easy for support and sales teams to ask and answer questions without leaving their chat app.

alternative

Slab

. Slab offers a clean, modern knowledge base with strong search and Markdown editing. It emphasizes organization through nested pages and groups, and it provides integrations with tools like Slack, Google Drive, and GitHub, making it a solid alternative for teams that want a structured but uncluttered wiki.

alternative

Document360

. Document360 is a dedicated knowledge base platform built for customer-facing and internal documentation. It includes advanced analytics, article versioning, a lightweight editor, and AI search, making it a direct competitor to Guru for teams that prioritize self-service support content.

How they compare

- **Best for small teams or Slack lovers**: Tettra (simple Q&A, tight Slack integration). - **Best for all-in-one workspace**: Notion (docs, databases, project management). - **Best for Atlassian ecosystem**: Confluence (Jira integration, enterprise features). - **Best for clean, minimalist wikis**: Slab (Markdown, nested pages, great search). - **Best for customer-facing knowledge bases**: Document360 (advanced analytics, article versioning, AI search).

All five alternatives offer free trials or free tiers, so you can test them before switching.
