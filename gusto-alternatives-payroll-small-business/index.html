Gusto is a popular payroll platform for small businesses, but it is not the only option. You might be looking for Gusto alternatives because of pricing, feature gaps, or specific needs like international hiring. This guide covers four strong competitors: Rippling, ADP, Deel, and OnPay. Each offers a different balance of cost and capability, so you can pick the one that fits your business size and workflow.

alternative

Rippling

is an all in one platform that combines payroll, HR, IT, and device management. It works well for growing companies that want to unify employee data and automate onboarding, but it can be more expensive than Gusto for very small teams.

alternative

ADP

is a giant in the payroll industry with plans that range from basic for small businesses to full enterprise tools. It offers deep compliance support and a wide network of integrations, though the setup process and customer service can feel less personal than Gusto.

alternative

Deel

specializes in global payroll and contractor management, making it a strong choice if you hire remote workers in other countries. It handles local compliance and payments in many currencies, but its U.S. domestic payroll features are less robust than Gusto’s.

alternative

OnPay

is a straightforward, affordable option for small U.S. based businesses with W-2 employees and contractors. It includes HR, benefits administration, and time tracking at a lower monthly price than Gusto, though it lacks the advanced integrations and mobile app polish of Gusto.
