If your team relies on the Elastic Stack for log and metric analysis, you know its strengths in open source flexibility, scalability, and a unified search experience. However, you might be evaluating other options due to rising costs of Elasticsearch clusters, the need for simpler setup, or different observability features. This article covers four popular Elastic Observability alternatives: Datadog, New Relic, Splunk, and Dynatrace. Each offers a distinct approach to ingesting, analyzing, and visualizing logs and metrics, and we will highlight their key differences to help you decide.

alternative

Datadog

. Datadog is a SaaS-based platform that combines logs, metrics, and traces into one interface with strong out-of-the-box integrations and alerting. It excels at real-time dashboards and correlation across infrastructure layers, though its pricing model can become expensive as data volume grows, especially for high-cardinality log data.

alternative

New Relic

. New Relic provides a full-stack observability solution with a focus on application performance monitoring alongside logs and metrics. Its query language (NRQL) is relatively easy to learn for teams moving from Elasticsearch, and it offers a generous free tier, but some users find its log retention limits restrictive compared to Elastic’s self-managed options.

alternative

Splunk

. Splunk is a veteran in log analysis with powerful search processing language (SPL) and robust enterprise features for security and compliance. It handles massive volumes of machine data well but typically carries a higher cost due to its indexing-based pricing, which can be a shock compared to Elastic’s more flexible data-tiering approach.

alternative

Dynatrace

. Dynatrace uses an AI-powered engine called Davis to automatically detect anomalies and perform root-cause analysis across logs, metrics, and traces. Its auto-discovery and real-time topology mapping reduce manual configuration, but it may be overkill for teams that only need basic log and metric aggregation without advanced APM capabilities.

Frequently asked questions

Will I lose my existing Elasticsearch dashboards and alerts if I switch to an alternative?
Not directly, but you will need to recreate them. Most alternatives provide APIs and migration guides for exporting queries and dashboards, but the exact transformation is manual. Plan to invest time in rewriting your most critical visualizations and alerts.
Which alternative is the most cost-effective for a team that already has self-managed Elasticsearch hardware?
That depends on your data volume and retention needs. If you run Elastic on your own servers, moving to a cloud SaaS option like Datadog or New Relic may increase costs because you pay per GB ingested. Splunk’s indexing-based pricing can also be expensive. Consider Dynatrace if you want all-in-one APM plus logs, but its per-host model may be cheaper only if you have many hosts with low log volume. Evaluate using a trial period with your actual data patterns.
