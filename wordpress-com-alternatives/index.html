Best WordPress.com alternatives

If you are a blogger who likes the idea of managed hosting but also wants the freedom to customize with plugins and themes, WordPress.com might be your first choice. However, its pricing tiers can feel restrictive, and some features are locked behind higher plans. Luckily, several alternatives give you that same managed convenience while still letting you tap into the flexibility of the WordPress ecosystem or similar tools.

alternative

Wix

. Wix is a fully managed website builder that handles hosting, security, and updates for you, so you never have to worry about technical maintenance. While it does not use the WordPress ecosystem, its drag and drop interface and large app market let you add features like blogging, SEO, and ecommerce without coding. The trade off is that you cannot export your site easily, and you are limited to Wix’s own tools rather than the vast library of WordPress plugins.

alternative

Squarespace

. Squarespace offers managed hosting with beautiful, designer focused templates and a simple editor that works well for bloggers who want a polished look quickly. Like Wix, it is not part of the WordPress ecosystem, so you miss out on plugins like Yoast SEO or WooCommerce. But if you value an all in one solution with reliable uptime and built in analytics, Squarespace can be a solid alternative to WordPress.com.

Try Squarespace →
alternative

Ghost

. Ghost is a managed open source platform built specifically for creators and publishers who want speed and simplicity. It gives you a clean writing experience with powerful SEO and membership tools, and you can self host or use their paid managed plan. Unlike Wix or Squarespace, Ghost is not a site builder for every use case, but for bloggers focused on content and newsletters, it offers a lightweight alternative to the heavier WordPress ecosystem.

How they compare

WordPress.com gives you the widest plugin and theme library but can feel pricey for full customization. Wix and Squarespace are easier to set up but lock you into their own ecosystems. Ghost is the best fit if you prioritize writing, speed, and membership features, though it lacks the extensive plugin marketplace of WordPress. Choose based on whether you need maximum flexibility (stick with WordPress) or a simpler managed experience (try Wix, Squarespace, or Ghost).

Frequently asked questions

Can I move my site from WordPress.com to one of these alternatives easily?
Moving content from WordPress.com to Wix or Squarespace often requires manual copy and paste or a paid migration service. Moving to Ghost is easier if you export your WordPress content as an XML file, but you may still need to rebuild your design.
Do these alternatives support custom domain names and email?
Yes, all three alternatives let you connect a custom domain name. For professional email, Wix and Squarespace offer paid email plans or integrations with third party providers like Google Workspace. Ghost does not include email hosting but works with email forwarding services.
