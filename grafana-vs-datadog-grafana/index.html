When you are building an observability stack as a site reliability engineer, choosing the right visualization and monitoring platform is critical. Grafana and Datadog are two of the most popular options, but they serve slightly different needs. Grafana is an open source, flexible dashboarding and alerting tool that works with many data sources, making it a strong choice for teams that want full control. Datadog is a commercial, all-in-one observability platform that includes APM, logs, and infrastructure monitoring. This guide focuses on Grafana as the primary recommendation for SREs who value customization and cost control, while Datadog acts as a robust alternative when you want a more integrated, out-of-the-box solution.

How they compare

Grafana wins for SREs who need a flexible, cost-effective dashboarding layer that can sit on top of existing monitoring infrastructure. It gives you full control over data sources, alert rules, and visualization, but you have to manage the backend yourself or use Grafana Cloud for a hosted option. Datadog is better when you want a unified platform that covers metrics, traces, and logs from day one with minimal configuration, but the pricing can become unpredictable at scale. For most SRE teams, starting with Grafana (especially with Prometheus) offers a transparent, vendor-neutral foundation that can later be extended with cloud services if needed.

Frequently asked questions

Is Grafana completely free to use?
The core Grafana software is open source and free to self-host. Grafana Labs also offers a free tier of Grafana Cloud with limited usage. For advanced features like enterprise plugins, synthetic monitoring, or higher alerting limits, you need a paid plan, but many SRE teams run the open source version successfully.
Can I use Datadog as a data source in Grafana?
Yes, there is a community maintained Grafana data source plugin for Datadog. It lets you query Datadog metrics and display them in Grafana dashboards. This can be useful if you want to keep Grafana as your single pane of glass while still leveraging Datadog’s agent and integrations.
