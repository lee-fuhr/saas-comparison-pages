Box is a leading content management platform designed for enterprises that need robust security, compliance certifications, and granular permission controls. However, some organizations may evaluate alternatives due to budget constraints, existing ecosystem alignment, or specific feature requirements. This article compares Dropbox, Google Drive, and OneDrive against Box, focusing on security, compliance, and permission granularity for enterprise use.

alternative

Dropbox

. Dropbox offers strong file sync and sharing with features like file recovery, version history, and shared folder permissions. Its enterprise tier (Dropbox Business) includes admin controls, team folders, and integrations with tools like Slack and Zoom. However, its advanced compliance certifications, such as FedRAMP, are more limited than Box, and granular permission settings are less flexible for complex organizational structures.

alternative

Google Drive

. Google Drive is deeply integrated with Google Workspace, providing real-time collaboration and AI-powered tools. It supports data loss prevention (DLP) and context-aware access controls through Google Workspace’s security center. While Google Drive holds key certifications like SOC 2 and ISO 27001, its permission model is folder-based and can be less granular than Box’s ability to set permissions at the file and folder level with expiration and password options.

alternative

OneDrive

. OneDrive is part of Microsoft 365 and offers seamless integration with Office apps, SharePoint, and Teams. It provides enterprise-grade security features including conditional access, information protection, and retention policies through Microsoft Purview. OneDrive’s permission controls are robust but often rely on SharePoint site permissions, which can be more complex to manage than Box’s intuitive interface for granular sharing at the individual content level.

How they compare

Box stands out for its purpose-built security and compliance features, including FedRAMP authorization, a dedicated trust boundary, and fine-grained permission controls down to the file level. Dropbox is simpler to use but may lack the depth of compliance certifications and permission granularity that heavily regulated enterprises need. Google Drive excels in real-time collaboration and AI integration but its permissions are folder-centric. OneDrive provides strong integration with Microsoft 365 and advanced data governance via Purview, though its permission model can be complex. For enterprises where security, compliance, and granular controls are top priorities, Box remains the most focused solution, but each alternative can serve specific needs when paired with their respective ecosystems.

Frequently asked questions

Does Dropbox have enterprise-level compliance certifications like Box?
Dropbox Business offers SOC 2, ISO 27001, and HIPAA compliance, but it does not hold FedRAMP authorization or some of the broader government certifications that Box provides. Enterprises in highly regulated industries should verify the specific compliance frameworks each vendor supports.
Can Google Drive or OneDrive match Box’s granular permission controls?
Google Drive allows folder-level permissions and some file-level restrictions, but lacks the ability to set fine-grained access like view-only, download, or share permissions per file without custom scripting. OneDrive with SharePoint can achieve similar granularity, but the setup and management are more complex compared to Box’s straightforward interface for setting expiration dates, password protection, and dynamic access policies.
