Building a skill based learning roadmap is a strategic move for any company that wants to close competency gaps and prepare for future roles. Degreed is a popular platform for this purpose, but it is not the only option. Your organization might need different integrations, content libraries, or admin controls. This article reviews five strong Degreed alternatives so you can choose the tool that best fits your skill taxonomy, budget, and learner experience goals.

alternative

Docebo

. Docebo offers a flexible learning management system with strong content marketplace options and AI driven learning paths. It allows you to blend your own content with third party courses, making it easier to map skills to specific roles. Its extended enterprise features also help if you need to train customers or partners alongside employees.

alternative

LinkedIn Learning

. LinkedIn Learning provides a vast video library of business, technology, and creative topics that can be quickly assigned to skill based roadmaps. Because it sits inside the LinkedIn ecosystem, you can automatically surface relevant courses based on a user’s profile and career interests. The platform is especially useful for self directed learners who want to fill specific skill gaps without heavy admin overhead.

alternative

360Learning

. 360Learning emphasizes collaborative learning where employees create and share knowledge with each other. This is a good fit if your skill roadmap relies heavily on internal expertise rather than off the shelf content. Its coauthoring and feedback tools let subject matter experts quickly build and update skill focused courses.

alternative

Skillsoft

. Skillsoft offers a large catalog of business and technology courses plus books and videos aligned to common skill frameworks. It includes prescriptive learning paths and assessments that help you measure proficiency before and after training. For companies that need a comprehensive content library with built in certifications, Skillsoft is a reliable contender.

How they compare

If your priority is a vast, prebuilt library of professional content, LinkedIn Learning and Skillsoft are strong choices. Docebo gives you the most control over content blending and extended user groups. EdCast excels at AI driven personalization across multiple content sources. 360Learning is best when you want to rely on internal experts and peer to peer learning. Degreed itself offers a robust skills ontology and recommendation engine, but these alternatives provide different strengths around content sourcing, user collaboration, or platform customization.

Frequently asked questions

Which tool is best if we already have a large library of custom training content?
Docebo and EdCast are both strong options because they let you integrate your own content alongside external courses. Docebo provides a fully featured LMS, while EdCast acts more as a content aggregation hub that can pull in your materials from multiple systems.
Can any of these alternatives help us map learning to specific job roles and skill levels?
Yes. Skillsoft and Degreed both offer built in skill taxonomies that agree courses to job roles. EdCast’s AI also maps skills to proficiency levels. LinkedIn Learning uses skill tags from your learners’ profiles to recommend relevant content for their current roles or career paths.
