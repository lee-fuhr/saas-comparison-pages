Best Aircall alternatives

If you use Aircall for your customer calls but want a cheaper or better-integrated option, you are not alone. Many teams look for alternatives because of pricing, call quality issues, or limited integrations with their CRM and help desk tools. In this article we review five solid Aircall alternatives: Dialpad, JustCall, CloudTalk, Talkdesk, and OpenPhone. Each one targets different needs, so we will help you pick the right fit for your team.

alternative

Dialpad

. Dialpad uses AI to transcribe calls in real time and surface action items, which is helpful for teams that need fast follow-ups. It integrates tightly with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Slack, and its unlimited domestic calling plan often costs less than Aircall for small to mid-sized teams.

Try Dialpad →
alternative

JustCall

. JustCall offers a simple, affordable per-user plan with unlimited calls in many countries and built-in SMS and voicemail drops. It connects with popular CRMs like Pipedrive and Zoho, but its advanced features like call recording and analytics come at a lower monthly price than Aircall.

Try JustCall →
alternative

CloudTalk

. CloudTalk is built for sales and support teams that need powerful call routing, IVR menus, and real-time analytics. It works with HubSpot, Salesforce, and Zendesk, and its pricing scales well for larger teams, often beating Aircall on cost per user when you add extra features.

alternative

Talkdesk

. Talkdesk is an enterprise-grade solution with robust AI, workforce management, and omnichannel support. It shines for large customer-facing teams that need deep integrations with platforms like Salesforce and ServiceNow, but its higher starting price makes it more suitable for teams that outgrow Aircall’s features.

alternative

OpenPhone

. OpenPhone is a modern, easy-to-use phone system for small teams and startups that want both calling and messaging in one app. It supports shared numbers, local and toll-free numbers, and integrates with Slack and Linear, all while costing significantly less than Aircall per user per month.

Try OpenPhone →
