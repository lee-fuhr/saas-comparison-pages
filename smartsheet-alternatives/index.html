If your operations team relies on Smartsheet for spreadsheet-style project management but is looking for a change, you are not alone. While Smartsheet offers a familiar grid view and strong automation, other platforms can provide better flexibility, easier collaboration, or lower costs. This article compares the top Smartsheet alternatives for operations teams that want to keep a spreadsheet-like interface while adding more project management power.

alternative

Monday.com

. Monday.com combines a visual, customizable board view with spreadsheet-like tables, making it easy for operations teams to track tasks, timelines, and resources. Its automations and integrations (Slack, Jira, Excel) help reduce manual updates, and the platform offers strong reporting dashboards.

Try Monday.com →
alternative

Airtable

. Airtable is the closest alternative to a spreadsheet, blending a relational database with a familiar grid interface. Operations teams can link records across tables, create rich field types (attachments, dropdowns, linked records), and build custom apps without coding.

Try Airtable →
alternative

ClickUp

. ClickUp offers a fully customizable spreadsheet-style table view alongside list, board, and Gantt views. It includes powerful automations, time tracking, and goals features, and its free tier is generous enough for small to midsize operations teams.

Try ClickUp →
alternative

Asana

. Asana provides a clean, spreadsheet-like list view with columns for custom fields, due dates, and assignments. Its rules and approvals streamline workflows, and the timeline view helps operations teams visualize dependencies without losing the grid layout.

alternative

Wrike

. Wrike’s table view mirrors a spreadsheet while adding project management features like Gantt charts, workload management, and request forms. It is built for enterprise-level operations, offering advanced security and custom workflows.
