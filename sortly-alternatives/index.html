Best Sortly alternatives

Sortly is a popular inventory management tool known for its visual, photo-based tracking and user-friendly interface. It works well for small teams and simple stock counts. However, as an e-commerce manager, you may find Sortly lacks the deeper integrations, order management features, or multi-channel support your growing business needs. If you are looking for a system that handles sales channels, warehouse workflows, or barcode scanning at scale, here are five strong alternatives worth evaluating.

alternative

Cin7

is a cloud-based inventory and order management platform built for complex, multi-channel e-commerce operations. It connects with major sales channels like Amazon, Shopify, and eBay, and offers real-time stock syncing across warehouses, making it suitable for businesses with high order volumes and multiple locations.

alternative

Fishbowl

is a robust inventory solution that integrates tightly with QuickBooks and offers advanced manufacturing and warehouse management features. It is a good fit for e-commerce managers who need to track raw materials, assemble products, and manage pick-pack-ship workflows, though it requires more setup time than simpler tools.

alternative

inFlow

provides a straightforward inventory management system with strong barcode scanning and purchase order capabilities. It is often chosen by small to mid-sized e-commerce businesses that want an affordable, on-premise or cloud option with solid reporting and the ability to handle serialized inventory.

alternative

Linnworks

specializes in multichannel order and inventory management for online sellers. It automates order routing, listing, and stock updates across platforms like Amazon, Etsy, and Walmart, which helps e-commerce managers reduce manual work and prevent overselling.

alternative

SkuVault

is a warehouse-focused inventory platform designed for high-growth e-commerce and 3PL operations. It emphasizes speed and accuracy in picking, packing, and shipping, with features like lot tracking and cycle counting that are useful for businesses managing thousands of SKUs.

How they compare

Sortly is visual and simple but lacks the multichannel integration, order management, and advanced warehouse features that e-commerce managers often need. Cin7 and Linnworks excel at connecting sales channels and automating order flows. Fishbowl and inFlow offer stronger manufacturing and barcode scanning respectively, while SkuVault focuses on warehouse speed and accuracy. Your choice depends on your scale, channel mix, and whether you need manufacturing or warehousing depth.

Frequently asked questions

Can any of these alternatives replace Sortly’s photo-based inventory tracking?
Most alternatives do not emphasize photo-based inventory like Sortly does. However, you can attach images to products in inFlow and SkuVault, and Cin7 allows media uploads. If visual tracking is essential, you may need to supplement your new system with a simple image library or keep Sortly for that one use case.
Which alternative is best for a small e-commerce team just growing past Sortly?
For a small team with a few sales channels, inFlow is a budget-friendly upgrade that adds barcode scanning and purchase orders. If you need multichannel order automation, Linnworks is a strong next step. Both offer simpler onboarding than Cin7 or Fishbowl, which are better suited for larger operations.
