Best Dropbox alternatives

Dropbox has long been a go to for professionals who need reliable cloud sync and file sharing across devices. However, changing business needs, pricing shifts, or integration requirements may lead you to explore other options. This article covers three strong Dropbox alternatives: Google Drive, OneDrive, and Box. Each offers solid sync, sharing, and cross device access, but they differ in features, pricing, and ecosystem fit. Use this guide to decide which one best supports your daily workflow.

alternative

Google Drive

. Google Drive integrates tightly with Google Workspace apps like Docs, Sheets, and Slides, making it ideal for teams already using Gmail and Google Calendar. Its sharing controls are straightforward, and you can sync files across Windows, Mac, Android, and iOS devices. Storage starts at 15 GB free, with paid plans offering up to 2 TB or more through Google Workspace.

Try Google Drive →
alternative

OneDrive

. OneDrive is built into Microsoft 365, so it works seamlessly with Word, Excel, and Outlook. It offers robust file versioning, real time collaboration, and deep Windows integration including Files On Demand. Personal plans start with 5 GB free, while Microsoft 365 subscriptions include 1 TB per user along with desktop Office apps.

Try OneDrive →
alternative

Box

. Box focuses on enterprise grade security and compliance, with features like granular permissions, workflow automation, and advanced audit logs. It supports sync across devices and integrates with over 1,400 business apps including Salesforce and Slack. Plans start with 10 GB free for individuals, with business tiers offering unlimited storage and custom retention policies.

Try Box →

How they compare

All three alternatives provide reliable cloud sync and sharing across devices. Google Drive excels at real time co authoring inside its own suite, while OneDrive is best for Microsoft Office heavy workflows and Windows users. Box is the strongest choice when compliance, security, and detailed access controls are top priorities. Pricing and storage allocations vary, so evaluate which ecosystem you already use and what level of control you need before switching.
