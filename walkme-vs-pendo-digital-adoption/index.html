As a product manager, choosing the right digital adoption platform (DAP) can shape how your users experience your product and how your team measures success. WalkMe and Pendo are two leading tools, but they serve different primary functions. WalkMe focuses on guiding users through workflows with in-app overlays, task automation, and step by step onboarding. Pendo combines product analytics, in-app messaging, and feedback to help you understand user behavior. This article focuses on WalkMe as a DAP for hands on user guidance, while also covering Pendo as the main alternative.

Frequently asked questions

Which tool is better for reducing support tickets from confused users?
WalkMe is generally more effective for reducing support tickets because it provides proactive, step by step guidance that addresses common user questions before they contact support. Pendo can also offer in-app help, but its primary strength is analytics rather than task automation.
Do both tools work with single page applications or mobile apps?
Yes, both WalkMe and Pendo support web applications, including single page apps, and offer mobile SDKs for native mobile apps. WalkMe has a dedicated mobile solution with gesture based triggers, while Pendo provides mobile analytics and guide capabilities through its SDK.
