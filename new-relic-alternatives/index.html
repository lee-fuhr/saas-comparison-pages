Best New Relic alternatives

If your platform or SRE team is evaluating new relic alternatives, you are likely looking for better cost control, simpler data ingestion, or a more open approach to observability. New Relic offers a broad APM platform, but its pricing model and proprietary agent can become expensive as data volume grows. Many teams now consider Datadog, Dynatrace, Grafana Cloud, OpenTelemetry, or Sentry to agree with their specific monitoring needs, whether that means full stack observability, lightweight error tracking, or vendor neutral instrumentation.

alternative

Datadog

provides a unified platform for infrastructure, application performance, and log monitoring with strong dashboards and alerting. Its pricing is usage based and can escalate quickly, so teams should model their expected data volumes before committing. Datadog also offers pre built integrations for hundreds of services, making it a direct competitor to New Relic for full stack observability.

Try Datadog →
alternative

Dynatrace

uses automatic discovery and AI driven root cause analysis to reduce manual effort for platform teams. Its Davis AI engine correlates metrics, traces, and logs without requiring manual configuration. However, Dynatrace tends to be more expensive than other options at scale, making it best suited for organizations that value automated insights over custom instrumentation.

alternative

Grafana Cloud

combines managed Prometheus and Loki with Grafana dashboards, giving teams a composable observability stack. It is a strong choice if you already use open source tools and want to avoid vendor lock in. Grafana Cloud scales predictably with usage, and its pricing is generally lower than New Relic for similar data volumes, especially for metrics and logs.

Try Grafana Cloud →
alternative

OpenTelemetry

is not a product but a vendor neutral standard for collecting traces, metrics, and logs. By adopting OpenTelemetry, your team can instrument applications once and send data to any backend, including New Relic, Datadog, or open source options. It reduces switching costs and future proofs your observability strategy, though it requires more upfront setup than a pre built agent.

alternative

Sentry

focuses on error and performance monitoring for front end and backend applications, with a strong emphasis on debugging and release tracking. It is more lightweight than full APM suites and works well for developer centric teams. Sentry’s pricing is based on events, so it can be cost effective if your primary need is exception and transaction monitoring rather than infrastructure wide observability.

Try Sentry →

How they compare

New Relic offers a mature all in one APM platform but can be expensive at scale. Datadog provides a similar breadth with more integrations, while Dynatrace excels at automation but at a higher price point. Grafana Cloud delivers an open and modular alternative that integrates well with existing Prometheus and Loki setups. OpenTelemetry is the strategic choice for vendor neutrality, and Sentry is best for focused error and performance tracking. Your decision should weigh cost, required features, and the team’s tolerance for configuration versus managed services.
