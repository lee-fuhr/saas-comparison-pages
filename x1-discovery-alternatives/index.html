Law firms handling large data sets need search tools that deliver fast, forensic-grade results. Everlaw is a popular cloud based eDiscovery platform, but its search speed and indexing depth can fall short when dealing with massive or unprocessed data. X1 Discovery offers a powerful alternative by using patented forensic indexing technology that works on both cloud and on-premise environments. It scans data at the raw filesystem level, allowing lawyers to find relevant evidence quickly without waiting for full processing. This article introduces X1 Discovery and compares it to other leading tools in the market.

alternative

Relativity

– Relativity is a comprehensive eDiscovery platform built for large teams and complex cases. It provides extensive review and analytics features, but its search speed can be slower on large datasets compared to forensic indexing tools. Relativity often requires significant setup time and training, which may not suit firms that need immediate forensic search capabilities.

alternative

Everlaw

– Everlaw is a cloud based eDiscovery tool with an intuitive interface and strong collaborative features. It excels at predictive coding and document review, but its search does not index at the forensic level that X1 Discovery offers. Everlaw is a good fit for firms that prioritize ease of use over raw speed on unprocessed data.

alternative

Logikcull

– Logikcull focuses on automation and simplicity, with flat pricing and fast processing for smaller to medium sized cases. It works well for straightforward discovery needs, but its search depth is limited when handling very large or complex datasets that require forensic grade indexing. Logikcull is less suitable for firms that must search across terabytes of raw data.

alternative

Disco

– Disco uses artificial intelligence to accelerate review and reduce costs, making it a modern choice for many law firms. Its search and analytics are strong, but it is purely cloud based and does not provide the same level of forensic granularity as X1 Discovery. Disco is ideal for firms that want AI driven review, but not for those needing direct filesystem level searches.

alternative

Casetext

– Casetext is best known as a legal research tool, though its CARA AI technology supports some eDiscovery document analysis. It offers useful search features for known documents, but it is not a dedicated forensic search platform. Casetext is a poor fit for law firms that require fast, deep searches across large, unprocessed data sets.

How they compare

X1 Discovery stands apart from these alternatives because it uses forensic indexing that works directly on source data without requiring full processing or conversion. This gives it a major speed advantage over Everlaw, Relativity, and others when searching large, raw data sets. While tools like Disco and Logikcull offer automation and ease of use, they lack the deep filesystem level search that X1 provides. For law firms that need to find evidence quickly across millions of documents, X1 Discovery delivers the fastest forensic grade search. The other tools are better suited for firms that prioritize cloud collaboration, AI review, or lower upfront complexity over pure search speed and forensic depth.

Frequently asked questions

How does X1 Discovery search data differently than Everlaw?
X1 Discovery indexes data at the raw filesystem level, allowing it to search unprocessed files and metadata almost instantly. Everlaw requires data to be processed and loaded into its platform before search, which can take hours or days for large datasets. This makes X1 much faster for forensic investigations and early case assessment.
Can X1 Discovery handle data that is not in standard document formats?
Yes, X1 Discovery can search across a wide range of file types including emails, attachments, databases, and even deleted or fragmented data. Its forensic index works on the underlying data structures, so it finds content that many other eDiscovery tools miss. This is especially useful for law firms dealing with complex or nonstandard data sources.
