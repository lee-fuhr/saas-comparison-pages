Best Lokalise alternatives

Lokalise is a strong translation management platform for software and web content, but it may not fit every global marketing team’s budget, workflow, or scale. Whether you need deeper machine translation integration, a more traditional CAT tool, or a platform built for enterprise collaboration, several alternatives can meet your needs without the features you don’t use.

alternative

DeepL

is primarily a neural machine translation engine known for high-quality language output, especially in European languages. It offers a Pro plan with glossary support and team features, but it does not include full translation management capabilities like visual context editing or project dashboards. Marketers who need quick, reliable machine translation with minimal project overhead may find DeepL a useful complement or lightweight alternative to Lokalise.

alternative

Smartling

is an enterprise-grade platform that combines translation management with powerful automation and analytics. It provides a visual context editor, workflow automation, and integration with marketing technology stacks, making it a strong choice for teams that manage high-volume multilingual campaigns. Smartling’s pricing and feature set are best suited for larger organizations that require a full-service solution.

alternative

Phrase

(formerly PhraseApp) specializes in software and website localization with a focus on developer-friendly APIs and version control integration. It offers translation memories, glossaries, and machine translation, plus a flexible pricing model that scales from small teams to enterprise. For marketers collaborating closely with engineering teams on continuous localization, Phrase provides a smooth alternative to Lokalise.

alternative

Wordfast

is a traditional computer-assisted translation (CAT) tool that emphasizes offline work and compatibility with industry-standard translation memory formats. It is available as a desktop application (Wordfast Pro) or a cloud-based platform (Wordfast Anywhere). Marketers who work with freelance translators or need a cost-effective, lightweight solution for translating documents rather than live web content may prefer Wordfast’s simplicity.

How they compare

DeepL is best for teams that prioritize raw translation quality and want a simple tool, not a full project management system. Smartling delivers enterprise features for large marketing departments with complex workflows. Phrase sits between them, offering robust localization features with developer-friendly integrations. Wordfast appeals to budget-conscious teams that need a classic CAT tool with offline capability. Lokalise remains a strong all-around choice, but each alternative excels in a specific area: DeepL for speed, Smartling for scale, Phrase for developer collaboration, and Wordfast for flexibility.

Frequently asked questions

Which Lokalise alternative is best for a small marketing team on a tight budget?
Wordfast offers a low-cost desktop option and a free cloud version, making it a good choice for small teams that don’t need advanced project management features. DeepL’s Pro plan is also affordable for teams focused on machine translation quality rather than workflow automation.
Can I use DeepL as a full replacement for Lokalise?
No, DeepL is a translation engine, not a translation management system. You would need to handle project coordination, version control, and context editing separately. It works best as a complement to Lokalise or as a simple tool for ad-hoc translations, not as a direct replacement for a platform like Lokalise.
