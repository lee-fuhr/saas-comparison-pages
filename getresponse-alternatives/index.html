If you use GetResponse for email marketing but need native webinar hosting inside the same platform, you already have a solid starting point. GetResponse offers built in webinar tools, but its pricing and feature set may not fit every business. This article covers three GetResponse alternatives that also let you run webinars directly from your email platform: ConvertKit, Mailchimp, and Constant Contact. Each has different strengths, so we will compare them clearly.

alternative

ConvertKit

ConvertKit does not include its own webinar tool, but it integrates deeply with popular webinar services like Zoom and Crowdcast. You can trigger email sequences automatically based on who registered, attended, or missed a webinar. This makes it a strong choice if you already use a separate webinar provider and want tight email automation.

alternative

Mailchimp

Mailchimp offers live webinar hosting through its built in “Audience” and “Campaigns” features, but only as part of the higher tier Standard and Premium plans. You can create a webinar landing page, send invitations, and follow up automatically within the same account. However, the webinar functionality is more basic than dedicated webinar platforms.

Try Mailchimp →
alternative

Constant Contact

Constant Contact includes a native webinar tool called “Online Webinars” that is available on all paid plans. You can schedule, host, and record webinars, then send automated email sequences to attendees and no shows. The feature is simpler than specialist platforms but works well for small to mid sized businesses that want an all in one solution.

Frequently asked questions

Can I run live webinars with ConvertKit?
No, ConvertKit does not host live webinars itself. It connects with third party tools like Zoom or Google Meet through integrations, so you can manage registration and follow up emails from within ConvertKit.
Does Constant Contact limit how many attendees I can have in a webinar?
Yes, Constant Contact’s webinar feature has attendee limits that depend on your plan. Basic plans allow up to 100 attendees, while higher plans support more. Check your plan details before scheduling large events.
