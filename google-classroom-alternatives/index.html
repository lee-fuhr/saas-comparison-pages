Best Google Classroom alternatives

Google Classroom is a popular free tool for schools that use Google Workspace for Education, but it may not fit every teacher’s needs. Some educators want more advanced grading, richer assessments, or better parent communication. If your school is looking for free classroom management options that still work with Google Workspace, here are three strong alternatives to consider.

alternative

Canvas LMS

offers a free account for teachers (Canvas Free-for-Teacher) that integrates with Google Workspace, allowing users to attach Google Drive files, use Google Calendar, and sign in with Google. It provides more robust quiz tools, a built-in gradebook, and detailed analytics compared to Google Classroom, though the free version lacks some institutional features like master course templates.

alternative

Schoology

provides a free basic tier (Schoology Basic) that supports Google Drive integration and single sign-on with Google accounts. It includes discussion boards, a parent portal, and advanced assessment options such as rubrics and audio feedback, making it a good choice for schools that need more instructional flexibility without leaving the Google ecosystem.

alternative

PowerSchool

(formerly PowerSchool Learning and Haiku Learning) offers a free version called PowerSchool Learning Free that works with Google Workspace for file storage and login. It focuses on simplicity with a clean interface, built-in gradebook, and easy assignment creation, though its free tier has limited integrations compared to paid plans.

How they compare

All three alternatives are free to use at a basic level and connect with Google Workspace for sign-on and file storage. Canvas LMS shines in assessment depth, Schoology excels in communication features, and PowerSchool keeps things simple for quick setup. Google Classroom remains the most streamlined for pure Google integration, but these tools offer extra features that may better match your school’s workflow.

Frequently asked questions

Can I keep using Google Drive and other Google tools with these alternatives?
Yes. All three – Canvas LMS, Schoology, and PowerSchool – let you attach files from Google Drive, use Google Calendar for due dates, and often allow Google accounts for login. You do not need to abandon your existing Google Workspace setup.
Are these alternatives completely free for a whole school to use?
Each tool has a free tier designed for individual teachers or small groups, but full school-wide deployment usually requires a paid license. Check each provider’s current free plan limits to see if it fits your school’s size and needs.
