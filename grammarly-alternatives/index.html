Best Grammarly alternatives

Grammarly is a popular writing assistant that checks grammar, spelling, and style. But it might not suit every writer – some want a more affordable option, others need features like in‑depth style reports or a distraction‑free editor. The good news is there are several strong alternatives that cater to different writing needs, whether you draft fiction, academic papers, or business emails. This article covers four top contenders: ProWritingAid, Hemingway Editor, QuillBot, and LanguageTool.

alternative

ProWritingAid

. This tool goes beyond basic grammar checks to offer detailed reports on readability, pacing, overused words, and sentence variety. It integrates with popular writing software like Scrivener and Google Docs, making it a favorite for long‑form authors and editors who want to polish their manuscript in depth.

alternative

Hemingway Editor

. Focused on clarity and conciseness, Hemingway highlights complex sentences, passive voice, and adverbs that weaken your writing. It gives you a readability grade and color‑coded suggestions so you can tighten your prose, which is ideal for bloggers, journalists, and anyone trying to write more directly.

alternative

QuillBot

. Best known as a paraphrasing and summarising tool, QuillBot also includes a grammar checker and a built‑in thesaurus. Writers use it to rephrase sentences, avoid redundancy, or quickly adjust the tone of their work – great for non‑native speakers or anyone who wants to experiment with different phrasing.

alternative

LanguageTool

. This open‑source checker supports over 20 languages and detects grammar, punctuation, and style issues. It works in browsers, Microsoft Word, and many other platforms, and its free tier is generous enough for casual use. Writers who work in multiple languages or want a privacy‑focused alternative will appreciate LanguageTool.

How they compare

Grammarly excels at real‑time suggestions and tone detection, but it is more expensive. ProWritingAid offers deeper style analysis and is better for book‑length work. Hemingway Editor is best for short, punchy writing and readability. QuillBot is unique for paraphrasing and synonyms. LanguageTool is the strongest budget‑friendly multi‑language option. Choose based on your primary writing task – long‑form editing, clarity, rewriting, or multilingual support.
