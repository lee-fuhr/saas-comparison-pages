Best Contractbook alternatives

Contractbook is a solid option for contract management, but it may not fit every small business. Some users find its pricing steep for basic storage and signing, or they need features that Contractbook does not emphasize. This article covers three alternatives (Ironclad, Conga, Precisely) that offer simpler contract storage and signing tools geared toward small teams. Each option is evaluated on ease of use, core features, and cost.

alternative

Ironclad

. Ironclad is built for legal teams, which makes it more powerful than many small businesses need, but it still works well for simple storage and e-signing. The interface is modern and guided, so you can set up basic workflows without much training. However, its pricing often starts higher than Contractbook’s, so it may only fit small businesses with a dedicated legal or compliance budget.

alternative

Conga

. Conga (formerly Apttus) focuses on document generation and contract lifecycle management, including storage and signing. The platform is easy to navigate for creating templates and sending contracts for signature. Small businesses on a limited budget may find Conga’s base tier more affordable than Contractbook, though advanced automation features add cost.

alternative

Precisely

. Precisely (previously known as “Contract Manager” within the Precisely suite) offers a straightforward repository for storing contracts with basic search and version control. Its signing capability is integrated but simpler than dedicated e-sign tools. Precisely is a good fit if you need a low-cost, no-frills vault for contracts and do not require complex workflows or integrations.

How they compare

Contractbook provides a balanced mix of storage, signing, and basic automation at a mid-range price. Ironclad is best for businesses that anticipate growth in legal complexity but comes with a higher cost. Conga offers flexible templates and signing at a potentially lower entry point, with upgrade costs for advanced features. Precisely is the simplest and cheapest option, focusing purely on storage and signing without extras like AI or collaboration. Small businesses should choose based on their current need for process complexity versus budget.

Frequently asked questions

Are any of these alternatives free for very small businesses?
None offer a truly free tier for contract storage and signing, but Conga and Precisely often have lower starting prices than Contractbook. You may find a free trial for each to test before committing.
Can I import contracts from Contractbook into these tools easily?
Most alternatives support bulk uploads of PDF and Word files, but they may not preserve custom fields or metadata. You will likely need to re-enter tags or categories after importing. Check each tool’s import guide for details.
