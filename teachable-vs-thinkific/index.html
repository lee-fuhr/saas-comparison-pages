Choosing between Teachable versus Thinkific is one of the first big decisions you will make as a new course creator. Both platforms let you build, host, and sell online courses without any technical skills. They look similar at first glance, but they approach a few key tasks differently. This article breaks down where each platform stands out so you can pick the one that fits your first course best.

Where Teachable wins

Teachable gives you stronger marketing tools right out of the box. If you plan to grow your audience with email campaigns, coupons, or upsells, Teachable makes these features easy to find and use. Their built-in affiliate system is also more straightforward than Thinkific’s, which helps if you want students or partners to promote your course for a commission.

Another area where Teachable wins is course drip and scheduling. You can release lessons on a fixed calendar or set rules like “unlock the next module five days after the student joins.” This feels intuitive for first-time creators who want to control the pace of learning without writing complex logic.

Teachable also offers a more polished mobile app experience. Their native mobile app for students is available on all plans, which keeps your content accessible on phones without extra work from you.

Where Thinkific wins

Thinkific wins on flexibility when it comes to how you build your course. You can drag and drop sections, use different lesson types (videos, PDFs, quizzes, surveys, text), and rearrange everything without restrictions. New creators often find Thinkific’s course builder less frustrating because it does not force you into a rigid layout like Teachable can sometimes feel.

Thinkific also offers a better free plan for testing the waters. With Thinkific’s free plan you can have unlimited students and build up to three courses with no transaction fees. Teachable’s free plan caps you and charges a transaction fee per sale. If you are launching your very first course and want to keep costs low while you learn, Thinkific gives you more breathing room.

Another win is Thinkific’s community feature. You can create a discussion board or a basic community space right inside your course. Teachable requires a separate tool for this. For a new creator who wants students to interact without leaving the platform, Thinkific saves you an extra integration.

Verdict

For a new course creator, your choice between Teachable versus Thinkific comes down to your main goal. If you want strong built-in marketing and are comfortable with a slightly steeper learning curve for advanced sales features like affiliates, go with Teachable. If you want a more flexible course builder, a free plan that does not charge you per sale, and a simple community feature for your students, choose Thinkific.

Both platforms will launch your first course successfully. Thinkific is the better option if you want to save money and design your course how you imagine it. Teachable is the better option if you plan to invest in selling and scaling from day one. Pick the one that matches the way you want to start teaching.
