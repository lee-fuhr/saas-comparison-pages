Small and midsize law firms need software that combines billing and a client portal to streamline operations. MyCase is a well known option, but it may not fit every firm’s workflow or budget. If you are evaluating MyCase alternatives, consider these four tools that offer integrated billing and client portals: Clio, PracticePanther, Smokeball, and Filevine.

alternative

Clio

. Clio is a robust practice management platform with strong billing features and a client portal called Clio Connect. It integrates with many third party apps, making it flexible for firms that need custom workflows. Many firms prefer Clio for its comprehensive reporting and trust accounting capabilities.

alternative

PracticePanther

. PracticePanther offers a clean interface with built in billing, payment processing, and a client portal that allows secure messaging and document sharing. It automates repetitive tasks like invoice generation and payment reminders. Small firms often find PracticePanther simpler to set up than MyCase.

alternative

Smokeball

. Smokeball focuses on automation and document assembly, with integrated time tracking and billing that syncs with a client portal. It is known for capturing billable time automatically from emails and documents. Smokeball is a strong choice for firms that want to minimize manual data entry.

alternative

Filevine

. Filevine combines case management with billing and a client portal, but it is especially designed for high volume firms handling large numbers of cases. Its billing module supports complex fee arrangements and trust accounting. The client portal allows clients to view case updates and pay invoices online.

How they compare

MyCase is strong for firms that want an all in one solution with a straightforward client portal and billing. Clio offers more integrations and scalability for growing firms. PracticePanther provides ease of use and automation for small teams. Smokeball excels at automatic time capture and document generation. Filevine is best for firms with heavy caseloads and complex billing needs. Each alternative has its own strengths, so the best choice depends on your firm’s size, practice area, and specific workflow requirements.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know which MyCase alternative is right for my firm?
Start by listing your must have features, such as integrated billing, client portal, or automation. Then test the top two or three alternatives with a free trial. Focus on how well each tool handles your daily tasks like time entry, invoice creation, and client communication.
Can I migrate my data from MyCase to another tool?
Most practice management tools offer data migration services or import tools to help you move contacts, cases, and billing history. Check with each vendor about the process and any fees. Plan for some downtime during the transition to ensure data accuracy.
