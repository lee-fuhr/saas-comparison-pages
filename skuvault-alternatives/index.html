If you manage an e-commerce operation, you know that inventory management can make or break your fulfillment speed and customer satisfaction. SkuVault is a popular option that integrates with major sales channels and offers barcode scanning and lot tracking. However, it may not be the perfect fit for every business, especially as your product catalog grows, your budget shifts, or your integration needs change. Below we look at five strong alternatives so you can compare features, pricing, and ease of use before making a switch.

alternative

Cin7

is a cloud-based inventory platform built for multi-channel sellers and wholesalers. It connects with over 800 apps including Shopify, Amazon, and QuickBooks, and offers order management, manufacturing, and advanced reporting in one system.

alternative

Fishbowl

is a desktop-first solution that works well for growing manufacturers and mid-sized e-commerce businesses. It integrates directly with QuickBooks and offers work orders, lot tracking, and barcode scanning, but requires a local server setup.

alternative

inFlow

targets small to medium businesses that need straightforward stock control and order management. It syncs with platforms like WooCommerce and Amazon, provides bin locations and reorder alerts, and offers a free tier for very small operations.

alternative

Sortly

takes a visual, photo-based approach to inventory tracking, which works well for teams that manage lots of individual items, spare parts, or retail stock. It lacks deep e-commerce channel integrations but excels at simple, intuitive inventory organization on mobile devices.

alternative

Linnworks

is a multi-channel order and inventory management system designed for high-volume e-commerce sellers. It centralizes sales from dozens of marketplaces and includes advanced automation rules, but its pricing can be steep for smaller operations.

How they compare

Choose based on your business size and primary needs. For all-in-one multi-channel selling with strong integrations, Cin7 is a solid SkuVault alternative. If you manufacture goods and need work order capabilities with QuickBooks, Fishbowl is worth evaluating. For a simple, budget-friendly tool with a free entry point, inFlow fits many small e-commerce teams. Sortly is best for visual inventory tracking, not for heavy channel integration. Linnworks suits large sellers who need to automate complex order workflows across many marketplaces. Each offers a free trial so you can test before committing.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is the closest match to SkuVault?
Cin7 is often seen as the most direct competitor because it provides similar barcode scanning, lot control, and deep e-commerce integrations. Linnworks also parallels SkuVault’s multi-channel focus but with a stronger emphasis on order automation.
Do any of these alternatives offer a free or low-cost plan?
Yes, inFlow has a free plan that supports up to 100 products and one user. Sortly also offers a free tier with limited features. The other tools begin with paid subscriptions, so review each pricing page for current rates.
