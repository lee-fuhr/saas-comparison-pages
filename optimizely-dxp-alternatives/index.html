Best Optimizely DXP alternatives

Optimizely DXP is a strong platform for content management and experimentation, but it may not fit every team’s budget, technical stack, or specific experimentation workflows. Marketing teams that prioritize fast, flexible testing might need a solution with deeper personalization or a more open content infrastructure. Below we compare four popular alternatives, focusing on how each supports experimentation for marketers.

alternative

Sitecore

. Sitecore offers a comprehensive DXP with built-in AI-powered personalization and A/B testing features. Its strength for experimentation lies in its ability to segment audiences based on rich customer profiles and deliver tailored content variants without additional third-party tools. However, its complexity and higher cost may be a barrier for teams that want a lighter, more agile experimentation setup.

alternative

Adobe Experience Manager

. Adobe Experience Manager integrates with Adobe Target for advanced multivariate testing and personalization across web and mobile. Marketing teams already using the Adobe ecosystem benefit from seamless data sharing and campaign orchestration. The platform can feel heavy for experimentation alone, and licensing often requires a broader Adobe commitment.

alternative

Contentful

. Contentful is a headless CMS that lets marketing teams build custom experimentation stacks using their preferred testing tools (e.g., Google Optimize, VWO). Its API-first approach gives full control over content delivery and variant management, which is ideal for teams that want to decouple content from presentation. The trade-off is that experimentation features are not included out of the box, so you need to integrate separate tools.

alternative

Magnolia

. Magnolia offers a hybrid headless CMS with built-in personalization and A/B testing capabilities. It lets marketers create content variations and define visitor segments through a visual interface, without needing developer support for every test. Magnolia is well suited for enterprises that want some experimentation functionality plus strong content management, though its user base is smaller than Optimizely’s.
