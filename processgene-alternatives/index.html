If your organization needs low-code GRC workflow automation, you are likely evaluating platforms that let business users build and adjust workflows without heavy IT involvement. ProcessGene and MetricStream are two well known options, but they take very different approaches. ProcessGene is built from the ground up as a low-code platform, letting you create compliance, risk, and audit processes quickly. MetricStream, while powerful, often requires more technical configuration and custom coding. This article compares ProcessGene with MetricStream and other leading alternatives to help you choose the right fit for flexible, user driven automation.

alternative

ServiceNow GRC

. ServiceNow GRC runs on the same platform as its IT service management modules. It offers good integration with existing ServiceNow environments, but its low-code capabilities are limited to the broader platform and can require developer support for complex GRC workflows.

alternative

MetricStream

. MetricStream is a comprehensive GRC suite with deep functionality for risk, compliance, and audit. However, its configuration often relies on scripting and professional services, making it less accessible for non-technical teams that want to rapidly prototype and change workflows.

alternative

RSA Archer

. RSA Archer provides extensive customization through its “workspace” model. While it is flexible, building and modifying workflows typically requires specialized training or vendor assistance, and the interface can feel heavy for teams looking for true low-code simplicity.

alternative

LogicGate

. LogicGate is a native low-code GRC platform designed for process automation. It offers a visual workflow builder and prebuilt templates, making it easy for business users to launch and iterate on GRC processes without IT bottlenecks.

How they compare

When comparing ProcessGene vs MetricStream for low-code GRC automation, the main differences are in ease of use and speed of change. ProcessGene lets you design, test, and deploy workflows using drag and drop tools and simple logic, so your compliance or risk team can adapt processes as regulations change without waiting for a development cycle. MetricStream, in contrast, typically requires more upfront technical work, often involving custom code or vendor led configuration. For organizations that need business users to own and modify GRC workflows, ProcessGene offers a faster, more accessible path. MetricStream may still be a good choice for large enterprises that require deep, prebuilt risk models and are willing to invest in technical resources.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate existing MetricStream workflows to ProcessGene without losing data?
Yes, you can export data and process definitions from MetricStream and import them into ProcessGene. The migration typically requires some mapping of fields and rules, but ProcessGene’s flexible data model makes it straightforward. Contact ProcessGene support for a step by step guide.
Does ProcessGene require developers to set up integrations with other systems?
No, ProcessGene includes built-in connectors for common systems like Active Directory, databases, and APIs. Most integrations can be configured by GRC analysts using the low-code interface, without writing custom code.
