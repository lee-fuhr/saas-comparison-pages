As an HR manager, you likely want a tool that keeps employees connected and aligned without adding administrative burden. The choice often comes down to two popular platforms: 15Five and Betterworks. 15Five started as a lightweight check-in tool focused on weekly pulse surveys and manager feedback, while Betterworks grew as a robust OKR platform built for strategic goal alignment. This article helps you understand the core differences so you can decide which approach fits your team’s culture and maturity.

alternative

Betterworks

focuses on OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) from the ground up, offering powerful alignment views, cascading goals, and performance calibration tools. It is designed for organizations that want to tie every individual’s work to company-wide strategic objectives. Betterworks works well for larger or more mature teams that already have regular check-in rhythms and need a structured goal framework instead of a simple pulse tool.

How they compare

The main difference comes down to purpose and depth. 15Five is a lightweight check-in and performance enablement tool, great for building manager-employee trust through regular, low-friction conversations. Betterworks is a dedicated OKR and performance management platform, ideal for driving strategic alignment and measuring progress against business outcomes. If your company prioritizes culture, engagement, and frequent touchpoints, 15Five is often the better starting point. If your company needs a top-down goal cascade with rigorous tracking, Betterworks is the stronger choice.
