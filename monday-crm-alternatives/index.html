If you use Monday.com for project management, its built-in CRM might seem like a convenient choice. But as your sales process grows, you may find that Monday CRM lacks the dedicated sales features you need, such as advanced pipeline management, lead scoring, or deep email integration. Evaluating specialized CRM platforms can help you decide whether to stick with Monday CRM or switch to a tool built from the ground up for sales.

alternative

HubSpot

. HubSpot offers a free CRM that covers contact management, deal tracking, and email sync with ease. Its paid plans add powerful marketing automation and sales sequences, making it a strong choice if you want to unify your sales and marketing efforts without leaving Monday.com entirely.

alternative

Pipedrive

. Pipedrive is designed for visual deal pipeline management, with simple drag and drop stages and customizable sales activities. It focuses on helping you move deals forward quickly, which can be a better fit than Monday CRM if your team relies on a clear, repeatable sales process.

Try Pipedrive →
alternative

Salesforce

. Salesforce is the most customizable CRM on the market, with endless fields, workflows, and integrations for complex sales operations. It is best for larger teams that need deep analytics and automation, but it comes with a steeper learning curve and higher cost than Monday CRM.

alternative

Zoho CRM

. Zoho CRM provides a broad set of sales tools including lead management, workflow automation, and AI-powered predictions at a lower price point. It integrates well with other Zoho apps and is a solid option for small to mid-sized businesses that want more dedicated CRM features than Monday offers.

alternative

ClickUp

. ClickUp combines project management and CRM in one platform, similar to Monday.com but with more flexible views and custom fields for sales tracking. If you like Monday’s all-in-one approach but want a different interface or deeper task and document integration, ClickUp can be a direct alternative.

How they compare

Monday CRM works best for teams already deep in the Monday ecosystem who need basic contact and deal tracking alongside projects. HubSpot excels at inbound marketing and sales alignment, Pipedrive at pipeline velocity, Salesforce at enterprise customization, Zoho at affordability and breadth, and ClickUp at blending project tasks with CRM. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize sales specialization (Pipedrive, HubSpot) or prefer to keep project management and CRM in one tool (ClickUp, stay with Monday).

Frequently asked questions

Can I still use Monday.com for project management if I switch to a dedicated CRM?
Yes. Many dedicated CRMs like HubSpot and Pipedrive integrate with Monday.com, so you can keep your project boards in Monday and move your sales pipeline to the CRM. Alternatively, you can switch to ClickUp to replace both tools at once.
Is Monday CRM free or included in my Monday.com plan?
Monday CRM is a paid add-on to your Monday.com subscription. It is not included in the free plan or basic paid plans. This means you may be paying extra for sales features that a dedicated CRM like HubSpot’s free tier or Zoho’s low cost plan already provides.
