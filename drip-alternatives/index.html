Best Drip alternatives

If you are an ecommerce brand currently using Drip for email automation, you may be exploring other platforms that better fit your growing needs. Drip has long been a solid choice for email marketing, but its recent changes in ownership and pricing, or the need for more advanced segmentation and SMS capabilities, might prompt you to look elsewhere. The good news is that several powerful alternatives exist that are built specifically for online stores and offer deeper integrations with platforms like Shopify and WooCommerce.

alternative

Klaviyo

. Klaviyo is the most direct competitor to Drip and is widely used by ecommerce brands that want advanced segmentation, predictive analytics, and native SMS marketing. Its deep integrations with shopping carts let you build flows based on purchase behavior, browse history, and product affinity without needing a third-party connector.

Try Klaviyo →
alternative

ActiveCampaign

. ActiveCampaign offers robust email automation combined with a built-in CRM, making it a strong choice if you want to manage both marketing and sales workflows in one place. Its conditional logic and scoring features give you fine-grained control over who gets which message, though it can feel less ecommerce-focused out of the box than Klaviyo.

Try ActiveCampaign →
alternative

Mailchimp

. Mailchimp is a familiar name that now includes ecommerce features like product recommendations and abandoned cart flows even on its lower tier plans. It works well if you want a simple, all-in-one marketing platform, but its automation builder is less flexible than Drip’s for complex multi-step sequences.

alternative

Omnisend

. Omnisend is built specifically for ecommerce and stands out for its pre-built automation workflows that target cart abandonment, welcome series, and post-purchase follow-ups. It also offers strong SMS and web push notification capabilities, often at a lower entry price than Drip for mid-volume senders.

Try Omnisend →
alternative

Brevo

. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) provides email marketing, transactional emails, and SMS in a single platform with affordable pricing based on credits rather than subscriber count. It is a good option if you want to send high volumes of transactional emails without paying per contact, but its ecommerce automation lacks the native product sync you get with Drip.
