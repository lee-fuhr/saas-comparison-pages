If your customer support team uses Guru but needs a different solution for onboarding new hires, you are not alone. Many teams want a centralized knowledge base that works directly inside the tools their agents use every day. Guru is a strong option, but other platforms may offer features that better fit your budget, workflow, or scale. Below we review four Guru alternatives that also provide browser extensions for in-app knowledge access.

alternative

WalkMe

. WalkMe is a digital adoption platform that overlays step-by-step guidance on top of any web application. It helps support teams create interactive walkthroughs for onboarding without requiring users to leave the system. The browser extension ensures new agents see context-sensitive help right when they need it.

alternative

Whatfix

. Whatfix offers in-app guidance, task lists, and self-help widgets that can be embedded in your support tools. Its browser extension delivers pop-up tips and onboarding flows based on what the user is doing. Teams can build a centralized knowledge base that appears directly in the agent’s workflow.

alternative

Spekit

. Spekit is a just-in-time learning and knowledge platform that integrates natively with Salesforce, Zendesk, and other common tools. Its browser extension surfaces relevant knowledge cards next to fields or buttons as agents work. Spekit’s strength is making onboarding content instantly accessible without switching tabs.

alternative

UserGuiding

. UserGuiding is a user onboarding and product adoption tool that works through a browser extension. It lets you create tooltips, checklists, and hotspots to guide new support agents through your knowledge base. The platform focuses on simple setup and does not require coding.

Frequently asked questions

Can these tools replace my existing knowledge base software completely?
Yes, each alternative includes a centralized knowledge repository that you can manage and update. However, the primary focus varies. WalkMe and Whatfix emphasize guidance and analytics, while Spekit and UserGuiding lean toward lightweight learning support. Evaluate which features matter most for your onboarding process.
Do all of these options work with every browser?
Most support Chrome and Firefox extensions, but coverage differs. WalkMe, Whatfix, and Spekit also work in Safari and Edge. UserGuiding focuses on Chrome at this time. Check each tool’s current browser compatibility before committing.
