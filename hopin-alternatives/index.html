Best Hopin alternatives

If you produce virtual events and rely on Hopin for networking, you may have noticed recent changes that make it less reliable for interactive experiences. Hopin’s acquisition and rebranding have shifted its focus, so many producers are now looking for alternatives that still prioritize attendee-to-attendee connections. The tools below offer strong networking features, from video speed dating to topic-based chat rooms, and can often replace Hopin without losing the engagement your audience expects.

alternative

Eventbrite

. Eventbrite is best known as a ticketing platform, but its virtual event tools now include live video, one-on-one networking, and breakout rooms. While the networking features are not as deep as Hopin’s, Eventbrite works well for hybrid events and offers easy attendee management. It is a solid choice if you already use Eventbrite for registration and want a simple, integrated way to add virtual networking.

alternative

Cvent

. Cvent provides a full event management suite with advanced networking options like video speed networking, 1:1 meetings, and topic-based discussion boards. It is built for large corporate events and trade shows, so the networking features are robust and customizable. However, Cvent can be expensive and complex, making it best for organizers with bigger budgets and dedicated event teams.

alternative

Whova

. Whova focuses heavily on attendee engagement with features such as a community board, live polling, and in-app messaging that encourages organic networking. Its “virtual expo floor” and “attendance tracker” help producers create meaningful matchmaking without needing extra integrations. Whova is more affordable than Cvent and is designed for conferences and multi-day events that rely on spontaneous interactions.

How they compare

Eventbrite is the easiest to adopt for existing users but offers the least networking depth. Whova strikes a balance between cost and engagement features, making it ideal for midsized conferences. Cvent provides the most comprehensive networking tools for large-scale events, but it requires a higher investment and more setup time. All three are valid Hopin alternatives depending on your event size and budget.

Frequently asked questions

Do these alternatives offer a free trial or a flexible pricing model?
Yes, all three offer free trials or tiered pricing. Eventbrite has a free plan with basic features but charges fees per ticket. Cvent offers a demo and custom quote based on your event size. Whova has a standard pricing page with monthly and annual plans, plus a free starter tier for small events.
Can I migrate my existing Hopin event data to these platforms?
Migration depends on the tool. Eventbrite allows you to import attendee lists from Hopin via CSV. Cvent has a dedicated migration team that can help transfer registration data and schedules. Whova provides CSV import tools and offers onboarding support for complex migrations. No tool offers a one-click transfer, but all have guides to help you move your data.
