Vena tools is a popular choice for FP&A teams that want to keep a spreadsheet-like interface while adding collaboration and workflow controls. However, not every organization finds that Vena fits their budget, IT requirements, or specific planning workflows. If your FP&A department relies on spreadsheet-based planning but is exploring other options, here are four strong alternatives that also prioritize a familiar grid interface without sacrificing scalability.

alternative

Planful

. Planful offers a spreadsheet-like user interface with built-in financial consolidation, budgeting, and forecasting. It is designed to be easy for finance teams to adopt without heavy IT support, and it provides flexible driver-based modeling that works well for companies that want to extend beyond basic Excel templates.

alternative

Adaptive Insights

. Adaptive Insights, now part of Workday, combines a familiar grid view with powerful reporting and dashboard capabilities. It is known for its intuitive drag-and-drop modeling and strong integration with existing ERP systems, making it a good fit for mid-market to large FP&A teams that need both simplicity and depth.

alternative

Anaplan

. Anaplan uses a proprietary in-memory engine that feels like a giant spreadsheet but is built for complex, large-scale planning. It excels when you need to model intricate scenarios across sales, operations, and finance, but it may require more training than other spreadsheet-based tools.

alternative

Workiva

. Workiva is primarily known for its connected reporting and compliance platform, but its financial planning module links spreadsheets directly to source data and disclosures. It is ideal for teams that need to keep everything from budgets to board reports in one auditable, spreadsheet-friendly environment.
