SocialBee is a social media scheduling tool that helps small and medium businesses recycle their content by category. But not every SMB finds it the right fit, whether due to budget, features, or workflow. If you run a business that needs category-based content recycling, three strong alternatives are MeetEdgar, Buffer, and Later. Each has its own approach to keeping your evergreen posts active without manual work.

alternative

MeetEdgar

organizes your posts into categories such as “tips,” “promotions,” or “blog posts.” It then automatically pulls from those categories to fill your schedule, so you never run out of fresh posts. The tool also lets you set recycling rules, like how often a post repeats, making it a direct alternative to SocialBee.

alternative

Buffer

offers category-based scheduling through its “Buffer Queue” and “Buffer for Teams” plans. You can create separate content groups and set posting priorities, though recycling is more manual than automatic. It is a simpler, lower cost option if you do not need heavy automation.

alternative

Later

focuses on visual content with a calendar that supports category tags. You can use those tags to filter and recycle posts, especially for Instagram and other visual platforms. Later’s strength is its drag and drop planning, but its auto-recycling features are less robust than SocialBee’s.

How they compare

All three tools allow category-based content recycling, but they differ in automation depth. MeetEdgar is the closest match to SocialBee with full auto-recycling per category. Buffer is simpler and cheaper but requires you to manually queue posts for recycling. Later is best for visual platforms and offers basic category tagging, but its recycling is not as automatic. SocialBee itself remains a strong choice for SMBs that want granular control over categories and recycling frequency.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Buffer to automatically recycle posts by category like SocialBee does?
Buffer does not have a built in auto-recycle feature. You can create separate queues for different content types and manually add posts to them, but the tool will not automatically pull from a category and repost without your input.
Does Later let me set a minimum time between repeats of the same post?
Later does not have a dedicated “minimum gap” setting. You can manually schedule repeats, but you are responsible for spacing them out. For automatic spacing with category controls, MeetEdgar or SocialBee are better options.
