If you are a fleet operator using Samsara, you already know it offers strong telematics and compliance tools. But maybe you want more flexible pricing, different hardware options, or deeper integrations. Exploring Samsara alternatives can help you find a solution that better fits your specific fleet size, budget, and operational needs. This article covers three strong competitors: Verizon Connect, Geotab, and GPS Insight.

alternative

Verizon Connect

provides a comprehensive fleet management platform with robust GPS tracking, driver behavior monitoring, and integrated ELD compliance. It also offers real-time video-based safety features and detailed route optimization, making it a good fit for large or multi-vehicle fleets. Verizon Connect tends to bundle services, which can simplify billing but may limit a la carte choices.

alternative

Geotab

is an open-platform telematics provider that uses a single plug-and-play device for most vehicles. Its strength lies in data accessibility and hundreds of third-party integrations, allowing you to build a custom compliance and analytics stack. Geotab’s marketplace lets you add apps for ELD, asset tracking, or EV fleet management as needed, which appeals to operations that want flexibility.

alternative

GPS Insight

focuses on user-friendly software with strong compliance and maintenance tracking features. It offers flexible hardware choices and a clear pricing structure without long contracts, which can be ideal for smaller or mid-sized fleets. Their customer support is often highlighted as responsive, and the platform includes straightforward DVIR and HOS tools.

How they compare

All three alternatives deliver core telematics and compliance capabilities similar to Samsara. Verizon Connect excels in large-enterprise features and video safety. Geotab leads in open integration and customization. GPS Insight provides a simpler, contract-free approach with strong customer support. The best choice depends on your fleet size, need for third-party apps, and preference for bundled vs. modular pricing.
