Best Asana Premium alternatives

If your team has outgrown Asana’s free plan but you are not ready to pay for Asana Premium, you have strong alternatives that offer advanced project tracking, custom fields, and timeline views. Asana Premium is a solid tool, but some teams need different pricing, more flexible custom fields, or a Gantt chart that works differently. Below we compare four top choices: Monday.com, ClickUp, Smartsheet, and Wrike. Each one handles complex projects in its own way, so you can pick the one that fits your workflow best.

alternative

Monday.com

. Monday.com provides a visual, board-based interface with robust timeline views (Gantt-style) and custom fields that can be added to any column. It works well for teams that want a colorful, drag-and-drop experience with strong automation rules, but the cost can add up quickly when you need advanced features like time tracking or guest access.

alternative

ClickUp

. ClickUp offers an enormous amount of customization, including custom fields, multiple project views (list, board, Gantt, timeline), and a powerful hierarchy of Spaces, Folders, and Lists. It is often cheaper than Asana Premium on a per-seat basis, but the sheer number of options can be overwhelming for new users.

alternative

Smartsheet

. Smartsheet uses a spreadsheet-like interface that makes it easy to manage dependencies, custom fields, and resource-heavy timeline views. It is a strong choice for teams that need formal project management with reporting, Gantt charts, and automated workflows, though it has a steeper learning curve for people used to traditional task boards.

alternative

Wrike

. Wrike combines task boards, custom fields, and a timeline view with enterprise-grade features like request forms and workload management. It excels at handling large, cross-functional projects with multiple dependencies, but its pricing jumps significantly when you move beyond the free or team plans.

How they compare

All four alternatives offer advanced project tracking, custom fields, and timeline views. Monday.com is best for visual, collaborative teams but can get expensive. ClickUp gives the most customization and features for the price. Smartsheet suits data-driven teams who prefer a spreadsheet layout. Wrike is built for enterprise complexity with strong request and reporting tools. None are exact clones of Asana Premium, so test the free trials to see which interface and pricing model your team enjoys using.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative has the most similar timeline view to Asana Premium?
Monday.com and ClickUp both offer Gantt-style timeline views that work similarly to Asana’s timeline. Smartsheet also has a strong timeline feature, but it looks more like a spreadsheet. Wrike’s timeline is robust but may require more setup for simple projects.
Can I import my Asana projects into these tools?
Yes, all four tools offer import options from Asana, though the level of automation varies. ClickUp and Monday.com have direct CSV or native imports. Smartsheet and Wrike also support CSV imports, so you can bring over your tasks, custom fields, and due dates with some manual mapping.
