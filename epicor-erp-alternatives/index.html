Best Epicor ERP alternatives

Mid-market manufacturers often choose Epicor ERP because of its strong roots in discrete and process manufacturing. However, as your business grows, you may need more flexibility and the ability to add modular features without overhauling your entire system. The four alternatives described below – SAP, Oracle ERP, Microsoft Dynamics 365, and Infor CloudSuite – each offer different strengths in modularity, scalability, and industry fit. This article will help you compare them so you can find the right fit for your shop floor and your budget.

alternative

SAP

offers a powerful suite of manufacturing modules, including advanced planning, quality management, and supply chain execution. Its modular structure lets you start with core financials and production, then add capabilities like predictive maintenance or warehouse management as needed. Keep in mind that SAP implementations can be more resource-intensive than Epicor, especially for smaller mid-market teams.

alternative

Oracle ERP

(NetSuite or Fusion Cloud) provides a cloud-native platform with extensive customization options and modular add-ons for manufacturing, such as lot tracking, work orders, and demand planning. It integrates tightly with other Oracle products and supports various production types from make-to-stock to engineer-to-order. The subscription pricing scales with your user count, making it easier to grow without large upfront costs.

alternative

Microsoft Dynamics 365

is a modular cloud solution that lets you purchase only the applications you need, like inventory management or production control, and add more later. It integrates seamlessly with Microsoft Office 365, Teams, and Power BI, which can reduce training time for your existing team. This flexibility makes it a strong option if you want a familiar user interface and gradual adoption.

alternative

Infor CloudSuite

delivers industry-specific editions for manufacturing with a focus on user experience and configurability. Its modular design allows you to implement core functionality first – such as order management and production scheduling – and then expand with add-ons for maintenance, quality, or analytics. Infor is known for deep functionality in verticals like automotive or food and beverage, which can match or exceed Epicor’s industry depth.

How they compare

All four alternatives offer modular add-ons and cloud deployment options, but they differ in complexity and ecosystem fit. SAP and Oracle typically provide the deepest functionality for large enterprises but can be more costly and complex for mid-market manufacturers. Microsoft Dynamics 365 stands out for its familiar interface and flexible per-app pricing, while Infor CloudSuite excels in industry-specific features that may reduce customization needs. When evaluating, consider your preferred deployment model, the modules you truly need now, and how easily you can add new capabilities later without heavy custom code.
