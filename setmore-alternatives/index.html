Setmore is a popular scheduling tool for small businesses, but its free plan limits you to one calendar and basic features. If you need a free plan with more client management options like online booking, reminders, and intake forms, exploring alternatives can help. Below we compare three top options for small businesses that need a free scheduling solution with basic client management.

alternative

Calendly

offers a free plan that includes one calendar connection and unlimited events, but it lacks built-in client management features like notes or intake forms. It is best for solo professionals who need simple scheduling with easy calendar sync, not for tracking client history.

alternative

Acuity Scheduling

provides a free plan with one calendar and basic client management features such as intake forms, client notes, and automated reminders. It is ideal for small businesses that want a more robust free option, though some advanced features require payment.

Try Acuity Scheduling →
alternative

Doodle

focuses on group polling and simple one-on-one scheduling, with a free plan including basic meeting creation and time zone support. However, it does not offer dedicated client management tools like client profiles or reminders, making it less suited for ongoing client relationships.

How they compare

For small businesses needing basic client management on a free plan, Acuity Scheduling stands out because it includes client intake forms and notes. Calendly is simpler but lacks client tracking, while Doodle is best for one-off meetings and polls. Setmore’s free plan has similar limits, but these alternatives offer different strengths depending on your need for client history versus simple scheduling.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Calendly for free if I need to manage client notes?
No, Calendly’s free plan does not include client notes or profile management. You would need to use a separate tool or upgrade to a paid plan for those features.
Does Acuity Scheduling’s free plan allow team members to log in?
No, the free plan only supports one calendar and one user. You would need a paid plan to add team members or multiple calendars.
