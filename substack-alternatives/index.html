Best Substack alternatives

Substack made it easy to start a paid newsletter, but it is not the only option. Many creators want more control over design, better email deliverability, or lower fees. This guide compares five Substack alternatives: Ghost, ConvertKit, Beehiiv, Buttondown, and Revue. Each tool has strengths for different goals, whether you want a full website, advanced automation, or a simple mailing list. Consider your budget, technical comfort, and long-term plans before switching.

alternative

Ghost

Ghost is an open-source platform that gives you a full website, blog, and membership system. It has a built-in editor and supports paid subscriptions with no revenue cut beyond payment processing fees. You can host it yourself or use Ghost Pro, and it works well for writers who want a professional, standalone publication.

alternative

ConvertKit

ConvertKit is an email marketing platform built for creators, not just newsletters. It offers advanced tagging, automation, and landing pages to segment your audience and send targeted sequences. The free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers, and paid plans include commerce features for digital products and paid subscriptions.

Try ConvertKit →
alternative

Beehiiv

Beehiiv is a newsletter-first platform with built-in growth tools like referral programs and a recommendation network. It also includes a web magazine, ad network, and analytics to help you monetize beyond subscriptions. Beehiiv has a generous free tier and is a strong choice if you want to grow quickly without managing a separate website.

Try Beehiiv →
alternative

Buttondown

Buttondown is a minimalist, email-first tool that focuses on simplicity and ease of use. It integrates with your own domain and supports Markdown drafting, with automatic archiving to a public web archive. Buttondown has no hidden fees and charges a flat monthly rate based on subscriber count, making it predictable for independent writers.

alternative

Revue

Revue is owned by Twitter (now X) and integrates tightly with the platform for cross-promotion. It offers a free tier with paid subscription ability, but its features are more limited compared to others. Revue is best for creators who already have a strong Twitter presence and want a no-fuss way to start a newsletter from their social account.

How they compare

All five tools let you send email newsletters and charge for subscriptions, but they differ in scope. Ghost gives you a full website and the most design control. ConvertKit excels at audience segmentation and automation. Beehiiv offers built-in growth features and ad revenue. Buttondown is the simplest, with flat pricing and no bloat. Revue is the most basic and is tied to Twitter. Your choice depends on whether you need a website, advanced email logic, growth tools, or just a clean mailing list.

Frequently asked questions

Can I import my Substack subscriber list to these alternatives?
Yes, all five platforms support importing subscribers from Substack via a CSV file. Make sure to check each tool’s import instructions and confirm that your paid subscribers will keep their access after migration.
Do I lose my email list if I switch from Substack to one of these alternatives?
No, you can export your subscriber list from Substack and import it into a new platform. However, your Substack archive will stay on Substack unless you manually delete it. Some platforms like Ghost and Beehiiv allow you to redirect your Substack URL to your new site.
