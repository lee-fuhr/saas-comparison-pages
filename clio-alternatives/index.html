Best Clio alternatives

If your law firm is evaluating practice management software, you have likely come across Clio as a leading option. However, Clio is not the only choice. Many firms look for alternatives that offer features like better document automation, flat fee billing, or industry-specific workflows. This article explores four popular Clio alternatives: MyCase, PracticePanther, Smokeball, and Filevine. Each tool caters to different firm sizes and practice areas, so understanding their strengths can help you find the best fit for your team.

alternative

MyCase

. MyCase is a cloud-based platform known for its simple client portal and strong billing tools. It includes built-in payment processing and a law practice dashboard that tracks key metrics, making it a solid choice for small to mid-sized firms that want an all-in-one solution without complex setup.

alternative

PracticePanther

. PracticePanther emphasizes automation and customization, with features like workflow triggers, document templates, and a Zapier integration. Its mobile app is highly rated, and the software supports multiple fee structures including flat fee, hourly, and contingency, which appeals to firms with diverse billing needs.

alternative

Smokeball

. Smokeball focuses on small law firms that need deep document management and automatic time capture. It uses AI to suggest billing entries based on email and calendar activity, and it offers a powerful document assembly tool that integrates with Microsoft Word, making it ideal for firms that handle high volumes of paperwork.

alternative

Filevine

. Filevine is designed for litigation and transactional practices that require robust project management and case linking. Its “granular permission” settings allow large firms to control access at the document level, and the platform includes strong reporting dashboards and a legalhold feature for e-discovery.

How they compare

Clio remains a strong generalist choice with broad integrations and a mature ecosystem. MyCase offers a more user-friendly client portal and simpler billing. PracticePanther excels in automation and scalability. Smokeball is best for document-heavy firms that want automatic time capture. Filevine is built for complex case management and large team collaboration. When choosing, consider your firm’s size, practice area, and whether you need flat fee billing, document automation, or advanced project tracking.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main reason firms switch from Clio to an alternative?
Firms often switch because they want better document automation, more affordable pricing for their practice size, or deeper project management features that Clio does not emphasize. Others move to a tool like Smokeball for its automatic time capture or to Filevine for its litigation-focused workflows.
Which alternative is best for a solo practitioner with a limited budget?
MyCase and PracticePanther both offer competitive pricing for solo practitioners. MyCase provides a straightforward all-in-one package, while PracticePanther’s automation can save time. Smokeball may be worth the investment if you handle many documents, but its pricing is typically higher for solos with lower monthly volume.
