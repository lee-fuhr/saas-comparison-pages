As a product manager, choosing the right tool to collect user feedback, run NPS surveys, or validate feature ideas is critical. SurveyMonkey and JotForm are two popular platforms, but they serve slightly different use cases. SurveyMonkey is built primarily for surveys and research, while JotForm leans toward form building and workflow automation. This article helps you compare the two so you can pick the best fit for your product team.

alternative

JotForm

. JotForm excels at building custom forms with a drag-and-drop editor and powerful conditional logic for payments, workflows, and approvals. It is more flexible for creating branded signup forms, order forms, or intake processes, but its survey analytics are less robust than SurveyMonkey’s. JotForm offers a generous free plan and lower starting costs, which can be attractive for early-stage product teams.

How they compare

SurveyMonkey is the stronger choice if your primary goal is to design, distribute, and analyze surveys with statistical rigor. It provides ready-made survey templates, advanced filtering and cross-tabulation, and a longer history in the survey research space. JotForm, on the other hand, is better when you need a general-purpose form builder that handles payments, file uploads, and workflow automation. Its form creation is faster and more visual, but its reporting is simpler. For product managers running market research or customer satisfaction surveys, SurveyMonkey is the safer pick. For operational forms or lightweight feedback collection, JotForm can save time and money.
