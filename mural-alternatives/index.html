Enterprise design and innovation teams often rely on Mural for virtual collaboration, but changing needs around budget, security, or feature depth may prompt you to explore mural alternatives. Whether you need tighter diagramming, more developer friendly integrations, or a lighter tool for quick workshops, several strong options exist that can match or exceed Mural’s capabilities in specific areas.

alternative

Miro

. Miro offers a similar infinite canvas with a broader library of diagramming shapes and developer focused integrations like Jira and Confluence. Its robust voting and timer features make it a strong choice for scaled agile ceremonies, and its enterprise plan includes advanced admin controls and single sign on.

Try Miro →
alternative

FigJam

. FigJam is built by Figma and excels for teams already using the Figma ecosystem, offering real time co creation with a simple, intuitive interface. It includes widgets, sticky notes, and voting but may lack some of the templating depth and export options found in Mural.

Try FigJam →
alternative

Lucidchart

. Lucidchart is purpose built for complex diagramming and process mapping, making it ideal for technical design sessions that require precise shapes, connectors, and data linking. Its enterprise features include strong access controls and integration with tools like Salesforce and AWS, but it is less oriented toward freeform brainstorming.

Try Lucidchart →
alternative

Whimsical

. Whimsical focuses on speed and simplicity with a clean canvas for flowcharts, wireframes, and mind maps. Its live cursors and lightweight collaboration work well for small to medium design teams, though it may not scale to the large, multi workshop use cases that Mural supports.

How they compare

Miro is the closest direct alternative to Mural for large team collaboration and agile workflows. FigJam is best for design led teams already in the Figma ecosystem. Lucidchart is ideal for technical diagramming and data modeling. Whimsical suits fast, lightweight ideation sessions. Consider your team’s primary activities (brainstorming, diagramming, or sprint planning) and your existing tool stack when choosing an alternative.

Frequently asked questions

How do these mural alternatives handle security and compliance for enterprise teams?
Miro, Lucidchart, and FigJam all offer enterprise grade security features including SOC 2 compliance, data encryption, and single sign on. Whimsical provides encryption in transit and at rest but may require a custom agreement for advanced compliance needs. Always review each vendor’s security documentation against your organization’s policy.
Can I migrate existing Mural boards to another tool?
Most tools offer import from common formats like PDF or image exports, but direct board migration is limited. Miro provides a Mural import feature, while FigJam and Lucidchart may require manual recreation or third party scripts. Plan for a gradual transition and test a few boards before full migration.
