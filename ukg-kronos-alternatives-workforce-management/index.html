As a Director of Workforce Management at a retail chain with over 20,000 hourly employees, you already know that time and attendance is the backbone of your operations. UKG (formerly Kronos) has been a longtime player, but many organizations find it complex to maintain, expensive to scale, and slow to adapt to modern retail needs like mobile scheduling and real-time labor compliance. You are likely evaluating alternatives that offer simpler administration, lower total cost, and better employee self-service. The right alternative must handle high-volume data, integrate with your HR and payroll systems, and support union rules, paid time off accruals, and predictive scheduling laws. Below we break down three common alternatives: Deputy, When I Work, and ADP Workforce Now.

alternative

Deputy

is a cloud-based workforce management platform that combines scheduling, time tracking, and communication in one tool. For large retail chains, Deputy offers strong automation for shift swapping and compliance alerts, plus native integrations with major payroll providers. Its mobile-first design works well for hourly employees, but larger organizations should confirm its ability to handle 20,000+ users without performance lag.

alternative

When I Work

focuses on ease of use for scheduling and time clocks, particularly for businesses with a high volume of hourly workers. It includes features like shift trading, labor cost forecasting, and geofenced mobile clock-ins. While it is popular with mid-market retailers, its enterprise support and customization for complex union or pay rule scenarios may not match the depth of UKG.

alternative

ADP Workforce Now

is a full human capital management suite that includes robust time and attendance, payroll, benefits, and HR administration. For a retail chain of your size, ADP’s scale and compliance tools are a strong fit, especially if you already use ADP for payroll. The trade-off is that ADP can be as complex to implement as UKG, and its scheduling module is sometimes less intuitive than standalone tools.

How they compare

All three alternatives are viable for retail chains with 20,000+ hourly employees, but they serve different priorities. Deputy offers the best balance of modern features and integration ease at scale. When I Work is the most user-friendly for both managers and employees but may require extra configuration for complex labor laws. ADP Workforce Now provides the deepest enterprise-level payroll and HR integration, but it comes with a longer implementation timeline and higher cost. Your decision should hinge on whether you want a dedicated workforce management tool (Deputy or When I Work) or a unified suite (ADP). Pilot testing with a subset of stores is strongly recommended before committing to a full rollout.

Frequently asked questions

Can these alternatives handle union rules and multiple pay rates for 20,000+ employees?
Yes, but not equally. Deputy and ADP Workforce Now both support complex pay rule sets, wage attachments, and union agreements when configured properly. When I Work offers these features too, but it may require additional setup or third-party add-ons for very intricate union contracts. Always test with your most complex store location before scaling.
How long does a typical transition from UKG to one of these alternatives take for a chain of this size?
Expect 6 to 12 months for a full rollout, depending on the complexity of your current UKG configuration and the number of integrations. ADP Workforce Now often takes longer due to its broader scope. Deputy and When I Work can move faster, but you still need time for data migration, integration testing, manager training, and phased store launches.
