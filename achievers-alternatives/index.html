Best Achievers alternatives

Achievers is a popular employee recognition and rewards platform that helps large organizations drive engagement through points, badges, and a customizable reward catalog. However, some companies find that Achievers does not perfectly fit their culture, budget, or technical needs – especially when they want deeper gamification or more control over their reward options. This article explores four strong alternatives for large organizations seeking gamified employee engagement and custom reward catalogs: Bonusly, Kazoo, Motivosity, and Fond.

alternative

Bonusly

facilitates peer to peer recognition by letting employees award points that can be redeemed for a wide range of rewards. Its gamified elements like leaderboards and badges encourage friendly competition, but the catalog customization options may be too limited for very large enterprises with unique reward needs.

alternative

Kazoo

blends recognition with performance management, continuous feedback, and goal setting, making it a more comprehensive HR tool for large organizations. It offers a fully customizable reward catalog, though its gamification features – such as points and leaderboards – are less prominent than Achievers’ built-in game mechanics.

alternative

Motivosity

builds a culture of appreciation through public thanks, kudos, and manager nominations, all layered with gamified levels and achievement badges. The reward catalog can be tailored to your organization, but the platform is designed more for cultural engagement than for complex, high-volume reward administration.

alternative

Fond

provides a rewards and recognition platform backed by a large marketplace of gift cards, merchandise, and experiences, plus your own custom rewards. Gamification comes through points, challenges, milestones, and leaderboards, though Fond focuses more on reward delivery than on deep, ongoing engagement campaigns.

How they compare

When choosing among Achievers and its alternatives, large organizations should weigh gamification depth, reward catalog flexibility, and integration ease. Achievers offers strong gamification (levels, badges, challenges) and a highly configurable catalog, but it can be expensive and complex to implement. Bonusly is simpler and cheaper but has less catalog control for large teams. Kazoo provides a full HR suite with good catalog customization but weaker standalone gamification. Motivosity excels at cultural appreciation and simple game mechanics but may lack enterprise scale for high volume programs. Fond offers the largest reward selection and solid gamification, yet its engagement features are more transactional than behavioral. No single tool is perfect – each balances these tradeoffs differently, so map your specific needs for gamification versus reward breadth versus integration before deciding.

Frequently asked questions

Which of these alternatives gives me the most control over the reward catalog?
Fond and Achievers both allow you to add custom rewards (company swag, local experiences, unique items) and block certain options from a large marketplace. Kazoo also offers strong customization, while Bonusly and Motivosity are more limited unless you work with their support teams.
Can these platforms integrate with our existing HR systems like Workday or SAP?
Yes, all four alternatives support standard integrations with major HRIS platforms, but the depth and cost of integration vary. Kazoo and Fond have the most prebuilt connectors for large enterprises, while Bonusly and Motivosity rely more on single-sign-on and manual data exports. Always test integration compatibility during a trial.
