If you run a podcast and want to offer members-only episodes with simple payment processing, Supporting Cast is a solid choice. But you might be exploring other options to see what fits your workflow, budget, and audience size best. This article covers seven popular alternatives, comparing their key features for creators who need straightforward member distribution and payment handling.

alternative

Patreon

. Patreon is the most well-known membership platform, letting you offer tiered rewards including private podcast feeds. It handles payment processing and subscriber management, but it takes a percentage of your revenue and requires listeners to use a separate Patreon app or RSS feed link.

alternative

Supercast

. Supercast is designed specifically for podcasters, giving you a dedicated membership site and private RSS feeds for each podcast. It integrates with your existing hosting and handles billing, but it charges a flat monthly fee plus transaction costs rather than a revenue share.

alternative

Memberful

. Memberful focuses on membership management and works with Stripe for payments. It can connect to your own website or podcast host, offers private podcast feeds, and lets you keep more control over branding, but it requires some technical setup and has a monthly subscription cost.

alternative

Ko-fi

. Ko-fi offers a simple “support me” page with optional monthly memberships and podcast feed access. It is free to use on its basic plan, with a small fee on each transaction, making it a low-risk option for creators who want minimal upfront commitment.

alternative

SubscribeStar

. SubscribeStar is a membership platform similar to Patreon but with fewer restrictions on content types. It processes payments and provides private RSS feeds for supporters, though its user interface is less polished and audience reach is smaller.

Fanhouse

. Fanhouse is a subscription platform that focuses on creators offering exclusive content, including audio. It provides a simple page for paid subscribers and handles billing, but it is newer and may have fewer integration options for podcast distribution than established tools.

How they compare

All seven alternatives let you receive payments for members-only podcast content, but they differ in cost structure, ease of setup, and podcast-specific features. Patreon and SubscribeStar take a percentage of your revenue, while Supercast and Memberful charge monthly fees. Ko-fi and Buy Me a Coffee have low or no monthly costs but simpler features. Fanhouse is a newer option with basic podcast support. Supporting Cast itself offers a direct, podcast-first approach with no per-transaction revenue share and a flat monthly fee. Your choice depends on your budget, how much control you want over branding, and whether you prefer a platform built specifically for podcast membership or a broader fan funding tool.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use these alternatives without changing my podcast host?
Yes, most alternatives work with your existing podcast host by providing a private RSS feed or a custom feed URL that you add to your hosting account. Supercast and Memberful are designed to integrate directly, while Patreon, Ko-fi, and others require you to manually link the feed.
Which alternative has the lowest fees for a new creator?
Ko-fi and Buy Me a Coffee have no monthly subscription fees, only small per-transaction charges (around 5% plus payment processing fees). This makes them the most cost-effective for creators just starting out, though they offer fewer podcast membership features than other tools.
