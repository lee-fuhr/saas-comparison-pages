LastPass recently changed its pricing and limited free tier features, leaving many individuals and small teams looking for a reliable alternative. The good news is that several password managers offer strong security, cross-platform support, and affordable plans. This article compares five top contenders: 1Password, Bitwarden, Keeper, Dashlane, and NordPass. Each tool is evaluated based on usability, security, and value for money.

alternative

1Password

. It offers a polished experience with features like travel mode and built-in two-factor authentication. Its family plan covers up to five people, and teams can start with the Business plan. Pricing is higher than some competitors, but the interface is intuitive and works on every major platform.

Bitwarden

. This open source password manager is free for individuals and costs just a few dollars per month for families or small teams. It includes unlimited devices, sharing, and self-hosting options for advanced users. Bitwarden is trusted by security experts and has undergone independent audits.

Keeper

. Keeper provides strong encryption and a range of plans, including a free tier with limited storage. Its small business plan adds admin controls, breach monitoring, and secure file storage. The user interface is clean, but some advanced features require a paid subscription.

alternative

Dashlane

. Dashlane combines password management with a built-in VPN and dark web monitoring in its premium plans. It has an easy-to-use interface and a smart autofill engine. The free version is limited to one device, so families and small teams will likely need a paid plan.

Try Dashlane →
alternative

NordPass

. Developed by the team behind NordVPN, NordPass uses the same zero-knowledge architecture and has a simple, modern design. It offers a free tier with unlimited devices but limited features, and its paid plans include password health reports and data breach scanning. For small teams, the business plan provides shared folders and user management.

How they compare

All five alternatives are secure and support common features like password generation, autofill, and secure sharing. Bitwarden stands out for its low cost and open source transparency, making it ideal for budget-conscious users. 1Password and Dashlane offer more polished interfaces but at a higher price. Keeper is strong on compliance and admin controls, while NordPass appeals to those already in the Nord ecosystem. For small teams, Bitwarden’s Teams plan and Keeper’s small business plan offer the best balance of features and price. Most allow importing directly from LastPass, so switching is straightforward.

Frequently asked questions

Which LastPass alternative is best for a small team on a tight budget?
Bitwarden’s Teams plan at just a few dollars per user per month is the most affordable. It includes unlimited sharing, two-factor authentication, and audit logs. The free version for individuals can also be useful if your team has less than five people and you use the free tier for personal use.
Can I import my passwords from LastPass to any of these tools?
Yes, all five alternatives support importing from LastPass. You typically export a CSV file from LastPass, then use the import feature in the new tool. Bitwarden and 1Password have direct migration guides on their websites to make the process smoother.
