Best OBS alternatives

As a video creator, you likely know OBS (Open Broadcaster Software) as the go-to free tool for live streaming and recording. It is powerful and highly customizable, but it has a learning curve and lacks built-in editing or sharing features. If you need something simpler, more collaborative, or with integrated cloud storage, exploring OBS alternatives can save you time. Below we cover four popular options for different workflows: Loom, Camtasia, Screencast-O-Matic, and Droplr.

alternative

Loom

is a screen and camera recorder aimed at quick async communication. You record a video, and Loom automatically uploads it to the cloud and provides a shareable link. It is ideal for creators who want to send short tutorials, feedback, or updates without dealing with file management or editing.

alternative

Camtasia

is a full-featured screen recorder and video editor in one package. Unlike OBS, it includes a timeline editor with transitions, annotations, and effects. Camtasia works well for creators who need polished tutorials or presentations but prefer an all-in-one tool rather than combining OBS with separate editing software.

alternative

Screencast-O-Matic

(now called ScreenPal) offers a free basic recorder plus a paid editor with captions, effects, and hosting. It is more beginner-friendly than OBS and includes a video hosting platform for easy sharing. This makes it a good choice for educators or business users who want simple recording and storage in one place.

alternative

Droplr

focuses on quick screen captures and short video clips with instant sharing. It is lighter than OBS and integrates with project management tools. Droplr is best for creators who need to record small snippets for bug reports, quick demos, or team communication rather than full-length productions.

How they compare

OBS is best for advanced, customizable streaming and recording at no cost but requires more setup. Loom excels for fast, shareable videos with no editing. Camtasia is the strongest editor-recorder combo for polished content but costs more. Screencast-O-Matic offers a balanced mix of recording, editing, and hosting for beginners. Droplr targets lightweight, instant sharing for teams. Choose based on whether you need editing, collaboration, or simplicity over OBS’s flexibility.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use OBS alternatives for free?
Yes, some offer free tiers. Loom has a free plan limited to 25 videos at a time. Screencast-O-Matic’s free version records up to 15 minutes with basic tools. Droplr’s free plan includes limited captures. Camtasia only has a paid trial. For full features you may need a subscription or one-time purchase.
Which OBS alternative is best for editing videos?
Camtasia is the best choice for built-in editing with a timeline, transitions, and effects. Screencast-O-Matic also includes an editor on paid plans. Loom and Droplr do not offer video editing. If you need to trim, cut, and polish your recordings, go with Camtasia or Screencast-O-Matic.
