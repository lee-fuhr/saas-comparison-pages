Best Stripe alternatives

Stripe is the go to payment processor for many developers and SaaS founders because of its clean API and developer tools. But no single solution fits every business model. You might need lower transaction fees, built in subscription management, better international coverage, or a simpler checkout experience. This article covers five strong alternatives to Stripe: Square, Braintree, Paddle, Lemon Squeezy, and Adyen. Each has strengths that matter for developers and SaaS companies., ## ALTERNATIVES

**Square** offers a unified commerce platform that works well for both online payments and point of sale. Its developer APIs are straightforward, and it includes built in invoicing and recurring billing. Square is a strong choice if you also sell physical products or want an all in one business toolkit.

**Braintree** is a PayPal owned service that supports a wide range of payment methods including PayPal, Venmo, and credit cards. Its drop in UI and server side SDKs make integration fast for developers. Braintree is especially useful if you want to offer PayPal as a payment option without extra complexity.

**Paddle** handles the full merchant of record role, meaning it takes responsibility for taxes, compliance, and fraud. This can save SaaS founders significant legal and accounting overhead. Paddle is built for software companies and offers subscription management, dunning, and global tax handling out of the box.

**Lemon Squeezy** provides a Paddle like merchant of record service with a modern developer experience. Its API is simple, and it supports license keys, updates, and customer portals. Lemon Squeezy is a good fit if you sell digital products or software and want to offload tax and compliance work.

**Adyen** is an enterprise grade payment platform that processes transactions in over 150 currencies. Its unified commerce approach lets you accept payments online, in app, and in store with a single integration. Adyen is ideal for high volume SaaS businesses or platforms that need deep customization and global reach., ## COMPARISON SUMMARY

Each alternative shines in different areas. Square is best for businesses that blend online and offline sales. Braintree is a solid choice when you need PayPal alongside cards. Paddle and Lemon Squeezy both act as merchants of record, removing tax and compliance burdens. Paddle is more mature with subscription tools, while Lemon Squeezy offers a simpler API and modern feel. Adyen targets larger operations that require multi currency support and enterprise features. Your decision should hinge on transaction volume, geographic reach, regulatory complexity, and whether you want to stay the merchant of record or offload that responsibility., ## FAQ

**Q: Can I migrate from Stripe without rewriting my entire payment integration?** A: Most alternatives provide REST APIs and client libraries similar to Stripe, so you can often keep your backend architecture. But you will need to update your checkout flow, webhook handling, and any subscription logic. Plan for a few weeks of development and testing rather than a simple configuration change.

**Q: Do these alternatives charge lower fees than Stripe for SaaS businesses?** A: Pricing varies by volume and features. Stripe’s standard rate is 2.9% + 30 cents per transaction. Square and Braintree have comparable rates. Paddle and Lemon Squeezy usually charge a higher flat fee (often 5% + 50 cents) because they include tax handling and compliance. Adyen’s fees are negotiable and become cheaper at high volumes. Compare total cost including hidden fees like chargebacks, monthly minimums, and currency conversion.
