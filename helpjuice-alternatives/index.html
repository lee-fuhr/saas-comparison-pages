If you run a small or midsize business and want to give your customers a way to find answers on their own, Helpjuice is a popular knowledge base tool. But maybe its pricing feels too high, its editor is clunky, or you need tighter integration with your support ticketing system. That is why looking at **Helpjuice alternatives** makes sense. Below we cover three other options that work well for building customer self-service portals.

alternative

Document360

. This tool is built specifically for knowledge bases and includes a powerful editor, version control, and a built-in analytics dashboard. It also offers a public or private portal, so you can share docs with customers or keep internal guides separate.

alternative

Zoho Desk

. Zoho Desk is a full help desk platform, but its knowledge base module lets you create a self-service portal that links directly to tickets. If you already use Zoho products or want one system for both support and documentation, this is a natural fit.

alternative

Guru

. Guru focuses on company knowledge, team collaboration, and verified content. It collects information from across your apps and turns it into cards that can be shared in a customer portal. It works well if you need to combine internal and external knowledge in one place.

How they compare

All three alternatives can power a customer self-service portal, but they differ in focus. Document360 is the most purpose built for knowledge bases, with advanced features like content categorization and SEO tools. Zoho Desk is best if you want a unified help desk and knowledge base. Guru excels at capturing and verifying knowledge from your team, then publishing it externally. Pricing and complexity vary, so your choice depends on whether you need a standalone knowledge base or a system that ties into your existing support workflow.
