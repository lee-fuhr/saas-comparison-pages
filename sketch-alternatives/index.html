Best Sketch alternatives

You have used Sketch for years, and it has been a reliable tool for UI and icon design on Mac. But the design industry has shifted toward real time collaboration, cross platform access, and more flexible pricing. Maybe your team now needs to work on Windows too, or you want faster prototyping in the browser. Whatever the reason, several capable sketch alternatives exist. This guide covers five options: Figma, Adobe XD, Penpot, Framer, and Affinity Designer. Each serves a different workflow, so you can decide which one fits your next project.

alternative

Figma

. It runs in the browser and on desktop, so you can access your files from any computer. Real time collaboration is built in, making it easy to design with developers and stakeholders. Figma’s vector tools feel similar to Sketch, but its auto layout and component properties are more powerful for responsive design.

alternative

Adobe XD

. This tool is part of the Adobe ecosystem, which means tight integration with Photoshop and Illustrator. Its repeat grid and responsive resize features speed up layout work, and it offers both Mac and Windows support. Keep in mind that Adobe XD is less actively developed than Figma, and Adobe has shifted focus toward Figma’s acquisition (though that deal fell through).

alternative

Penpot

. Penpot is a free and open source design tool that works entirely in the browser. It supports SVG based vector editing and has built in design systems and prototyping. Because it is open source, you can self host it on your own server if you need full data control.

alternative

Framer

. Framer started as a prototyping tool, but it now includes a full vector editor for screen design. It is especially strong for interactive prototypes and micro animations, and it integrates with your existing codebase. Framer also lets you publish designs as live websites, which is useful for marketing pages or simple apps.

Try Framer →
alternative

Affinity Designer

. This is a one time purchase alternative with no subscription fees. It runs natively on Mac, iPad, and Windows, and it handles both vector and raster graphics in one app. Affinity Designer excels at illustration and print work, but it lacks the collaboration and prototyping features that Sketch users may rely on.

How they compare

If you need seamless team collaboration and cross platform access, Figma is the most direct replacement for Sketch. Adobe XD offers similar vector capabilities with Adobe integration but has an uncertain future. Penpot is best for open source enthusiasts and teams that must keep data on premise. Framer is ideal when your priority is high fidelity interactive prototypes and live publishing. Affinity Designer is the right choice if you want a one time purchase and focus on illustration or print design rather than UI collaboration.

Frequently asked questions

Can I import my existing Sketch files into these alternatives?
Yes, most of them accept Sketch files. Figma and Adobe XD can import .sketch files directly, though some layer effects or symbols may need adjustments. Penpot and Framer also support import, but you may lose some interactivity. Affinity Designer does not import .sketch files, so you would need to export as SVG or PDF first.
Which alternative is best for a solo Mac designer who does not need collaboration?
For a solo designer, Affinity Designer is a strong choice because it has a one time cost and works offline. Adobe XD also works well for solo use with its Adobe integration. However, if you want to occasionally share prototypes with clients, Figma’s free tier is powerful even without a team.
