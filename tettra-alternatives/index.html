Best Tettra alternatives

Tettra is a popular wiki-style documentation platform that helps teams capture and share internal knowledge. However, some teams find its editing experience limited or its pricing structure restrictive. If you are looking for a tool with more flexibility, better search, or different integration options, exploring alternatives like Guru, Notion, and Slab can help you find the right fit. Each of these platforms offers unique strengths for team documentation.

alternative

Guru

. Guru combines a knowledge base with a browser extension that brings verified information directly into your workflow. It focuses on keeping content accurate through automatic reminders to review and update cards, making it ideal for teams where stale documentation is a problem.

alternative

Notion

. Notion is an all-in-one workspace that blends wikis, databases, and project management. Its flexible page layout and rich block-based editor let you build custom documentation structures, but it can require more setup time to organize effectively for a pure wiki use case.

alternative

Slab

. Slab is a clean, modern knowledge hub designed to replace services like Confluence and Tettra. It offers a powerful search and a simple editor with nested pages, along with tight integrations with tools like Slack and GitHub, making it easy to surface documentation when your team needs it.

How they compare

Choose Guru if your main priority is keeping knowledge up to date with minimal manual effort. Choose Notion if you want a single tool for both documentation and other workflows like task tracking or databases. Choose Slab if you need a dedicated wiki with fast search and strong Slack integration, similar to Tettra but with a simpler interface. All three have free tiers or trials, so test them based on how your team prefers to create and access content.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a free alternative to Tettra that works for small teams?
Yes. Notion offers a free plan with unlimited pages and blocks for up to 10 guests, which is suitable for small teams. Slab also has a free tier for up to 10 users with limited storage. Guru’s free trial gives you a time-limited test of its full features.
Which alternative has the best search for large documentation libraries?
Slab is known for its fast and accurate full-text search, including the ability to search inside attached files. Guru also offers strong search with its AI-powered suggestions, while Notion’s search works well but can be slower with very large databases.
