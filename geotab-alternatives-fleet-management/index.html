If you manage an electric vehicle fleet for a utility company, you likely rely on Geotab for battery monitoring and route optimization. But you may be looking for alternatives that better fit your specific needs. This article compares three leading options: Samsara, Verizon Connect, and Motive.

alternative

Samsara

. Samsara offers integrated EV battery monitoring and route optimization through its connected operations platform. It provides real-time battery health data, charging status, and intelligent routing to reduce energy consumption. The system also includes driver safety features that help utility fleets comply with regulations.

Verizon Connect

. Verizon Connect combines GPS tracking with detailed battery usage reports for electric fleets. Its route optimization tools consider battery range and charging station locations to minimize downtime. The platform also supports custom alerts for low battery or inefficient driving patterns.

alternative

Motive

(formerly KeepTruckin). Motive focuses on fleet safety and efficiency, with dashcam integration and driver coaching. For EVs, it monitors battery charge levels and provides route suggestions based on remaining range. Its simple interface makes it easy for utility dispatchers to adjust routes on the fly.

How they compare

All three alternatives offer battery monitoring and route optimization, but they differ in focus. Samsara provides a broad operations platform with strong real-time battery analytics. Verizon Connect excels in detailed reporting and integration with existing telematics. Motive prioritizes driver safety and ease of use with its dashcam and coaching tools. Your choice depends on whether you need deeper battery insights, robust reporting, or driver behavior management.

Frequently asked questions

How do these alternatives handle battery health data for utility EVs?
Samsara provides real-time battery state of health and charging status. Verizon Connect offers historical battery usage reports. Motive tracks charge levels and alerts you to low battery. All three share data through their dashboards so you can monitor degradation over time.
Which alternative is best for route optimization in utility fleets with limited charging infrastructure?
Verizon Connect is strong because it factors in charging station locations and battery range into its routing. Samsara also routes based on energy consumption and charging availability. Motive suggests routes based on remaining range but is less tailored to charging infrastructure. Review each platform’s trial to see which matches your region’s stations.
