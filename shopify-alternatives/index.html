Best Shopify alternatives

Shopify is a strong platform for selling online, but it is not the right fit for every business. Monthly subscription fees, transaction fees, and limited control over code can push some ecommerce founders to look elsewhere. This article covers five Shopify alternatives: WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Squarespace Commerce, Wix eCommerce, and Swell. Each offers different strengths in cost, customization, and scalability.

alternative

WooCommerce

. This is a free plugin for WordPress that gives you full control over your store’s design and data. You pay for hosting, domain, and extensions instead of a monthly subscription, which can lower ongoing costs. It works best if you already know WordPress or want to avoid platform lock-in.

alternative

BigCommerce

. This is a hosted platform like Shopify, but it does not charge extra transaction fees on any payment gateway you choose. It includes built-in features for multi-channel selling and abandoned cart recovery without needing paid apps. BigCommerce is a good choice for growing brands that want strong functionality without as many add-on costs.

Try BigCommerce →
alternative

Squarespace Commerce

. This all-in-one website builder includes ecommerce tools with clean, designer templates. It charges a flat monthly fee and does not take a percentage of your sales on its higher tier plans. Squarespace is best for small to medium stores that prioritize visual design and simple setup.

alternative

Wix eCommerce

. Wix offers a drag-and-drop builder that makes it easy to create a store without coding. Its ecommerce plans include features like inventory management and automated sales tax. Wix is a solid option for new sellers who want a cheap, user-friendly way to launch quickly.

alternative

Swell

. This is an open-source headless commerce platform that lets you build a fully custom storefront. You control the front end and back end separately, which gives you maximum flexibility for unique business models. Swell is ideal for developers and brands that need a tailored, scalable solution beyond standard templates.

How they compare

The main differences among these alternatives come down to control, cost, and ease of use. WooCommerce and Swell offer the most freedom but require technical skills or developer help. BigCommerce and Squarespace Commerce provide hosted simplicity with no transaction fees, while Wix eCommerce is the easiest for beginners on a tight budget. Shopify sits in the middle: it is easier than WooCommerce and Swell but charges more in fees and app costs than BigCommerce or Squarespace. Choose WooCommerce or Swell if you need full customization, BigCommerce or Squarespace if you want a balance of features and cost, and Wix if you value speed and low startup expenses.
