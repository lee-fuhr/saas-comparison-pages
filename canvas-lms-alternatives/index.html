Best Canvas LMS alternatives

Canvas LMS is a widely used platform in higher education, but some institutions look for alternatives due to cost, customization needs, or a preference for open-source tools. While Canvas is proprietary, many schools evaluate other options like Schoology, Google Classroom, and PowerSchool for different strengths. This article compares these three alternatives to help you decide which might fit your institution.

alternative

Schoology

: Schoology is a cloud-based LMS designed for K-12 and higher education with strong assessment and analytics tools. It offers a clean interface and integrates with many third-party tools, but it is not open source and requires a paid subscription for full features.

alternative

Google Classroom

: Google Classroom is a free platform that works well with Google Workspace, making it easy for institutions already using G Suite. It is simple to set up and use but lacks the advanced grading, outcomes tracking, and customization that many universities need, and it is not open source.

alternative

PowerSchool

: PowerSchool is a comprehensive student information system that also offers an LMS module. It is geared toward managing the entire student lifecycle, but its LMS component is less feature-rich than standalone platforms. PowerSchool is proprietary and often used in districts that already use its SIS.

How they compare

Canvas is a full-featured commercial LMS with strong integration and support. Schoology offers a polished user experience and robust assessment tools but lacks open-source flexibility. Google Classroom is free and simple but limited for higher education. PowerSchool is best for schools needing an all-in-one SIS and LMS solution. None of these alternatives are open source, so institutions seeking an open-source platform may need to look at Moodle, Sakai, or Open LMS.

Frequently asked questions

Are any of these alternatives free to use?
Google Classroom is free, but it has paid upgrades for some advanced features. Schoology and PowerSchool require paid licenses.
Which alternative is best for a university that wants to customize the platform?
None of the listed alternatives are open source, so customization is limited. If customization is a priority, consider true open-source options like Moodle or Sakai.
