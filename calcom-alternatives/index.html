Cal.com is a strong open-source scheduling tool that you can self-host. But you may need a different approach, for example a simpler interface or a specific integration. This article covers three alternatives to Cal.com: Calendly, Doodle, and Setmore. Each one has a different trade-off for developers who value control and customization.

alternative

Calendly

is a widely used scheduling automation platform. It offers many integrations and a polished user experience, but it is a proprietary cloud service with no self-hosting option. Developers may choose it for its mature ecosystem and reliability.

alternative

Doodle

focuses on polling and group scheduling to find meeting times across many participants. It is a hosted SaaS product, not open-source, and cannot be self-hosted. Its strength is simplicity for one-off meetings rather than recurring booking.

Try Doodle →
alternative

Setmore

provides a free scheduling platform with appointment booking, calendar sync, and team features. It is a cloud-based service only, with no open-source code or self-hosting available. It may appeal to small teams that need a low-cost, straightforward solution.

Try Setmore →

How they compare

Cal.com is the only fully open-source and self-hostable option among these four. Calendly, Doodle, and Setmore are all proprietary SaaS products. For developers who need full control over data and code, Cal.com is the best fit. The alternatives offer polished interfaces and extensive integrations that may be worth the trade-off for some teams, especially if self-hosting is not a requirement.

Frequently asked questions

Can I self-host any of these Cal.com alternatives?
No. Calendly, Doodle, and Setmore are all proprietary cloud services. They do not offer self-hosting or open-source code. If you need a self-hosted scheduling tool, Cal.com is the recommended option.
Which alternative is best for group polls and availability checks?
Doodle is designed specifically for polling to find common availability among many people. It is simpler than Cal.com for one-time group meetings, but it lacks recurring booking features and self-hosting capability.
