Best Zeroheight alternatives

Zeroheight is a popular tool for documenting design systems, but many teams outgrow its sync capabilities with Figma or Sketch. If your system relies on tight design-to-documentation workflows, you might want a tool that offers deeper integration, token management, or component-driven documentation. This article covers five strong alternatives: Supernova, Specify, Knapsack, Frontify, and UXPin.

alternative

Supernova

connects design tokens and components directly from Figma or Sketch into a living documentation site. It lets you write docs alongside your design files and automatically keeps them in sync as designs evolve. Supernova also generates developer-ready code and supports multiple output formats.

alternative

Specify

focuses on design token extraction and distribution from Figma and Sketch. Instead of a full documentation site, it gives you a central hub for tokens, assets, and metadata that you can connect to codebases or static site generators. This works best if your team wants a lightweight sync layer rather than a complete doc platform.

alternative

Knapsack

is built for teams that need a component-driven design system site with tight Figma and Sketch integration. It provides a visual editor, token management, and live component previews synced from your design files. Knapsack also supports versioning and multi-source imports for larger design operations.

alternative

Frontify

offers a broader brand management platform that includes design system documentation with Figma and Sketch plugins. You can sync styles, components, and guidelines into a single workspace. Frontify is a good fit if your team also needs brand portal features alongside your system docs.

alternative

UXPin

is a design tool with documentation and merge capabilities, not a standalone doc platform. It lets you import components from Figma or Sketch and turn them into interactive, code-backed documentation. UXPin works best for teams that want to design, prototype, and document inside one tool rather than using separate systems.

How they compare

Supernova and Knapsack are the closest direct alternatives to Zeroheight for full documentation sites with deep design sync. Specify is better for token-heavy workflows without a full doc site, while Frontify adds brand management layers. UXPin is a different approach, combining design and docs in one tool. Your choice depends on whether you need a dedicated documentation platform, a token hub, or an integrated design and documentation environment.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate my existing Zeroheight documentation to these alternatives?
Most alternatives offer import tools or APIs for content migration, but the process depends on your current structure. Supernova, Knapsack, and Frontify have documented migration guides, while Specify and UXPin may require manual restructuring of tokens and components.
Do these alternatives support both Figma and Sketch at the same time?
Yes, all five tools support both Figma and Sketch, though the depth of integration varies. Supernova and Specify offer two-way sync for tokens and components in both tools, while UXPin focuses more on Figma and Sketch imports for prototyping and documentation.
