Best AWeber alternatives

If you are a small business owner who relies on automated email sequences and wants the peace of mind that comes with talking to a real person on the phone, you have likely used or considered AWeber. AWeber has long been known for its solid autoresponder features and responsive phone support. But maybe you are looking for a different pricing model, a more modern drag-and-drop builder, or better integration with your ecommerce platform. Here are three AWeber alternatives that also offer robust autoresponder capabilities and phone support, so you can find the right fit for your business.

alternative

Mailchimp

. Mailchimp is a widely used marketing platform that now includes strong automated email sequences, especially with its newer customer journey builder. It offers phone support on higher tier plans (Standard and Premium), making it a good choice if you need help setting up complex triggers or welcome series. However, its free plan lacks phone support and limits automation features, so be prepared to upgrade.

Try Mailchimp →
alternative

Constant Contact

. Constant Contact is built for small businesses that want simplicity and reliable support. It provides powerful autoresponders, such as welcome emails and anniversary series, and includes phone support on all paid plans. The interface is easy to use, and you can get help from a dedicated support team during business hours, which is ideal if you prefer talking through problems rather than searching a help center.

Try Constant Contact →
alternative

Campaign Monitor

. Campaign Monitor focuses on visual email design and solid automation, including automated journeys triggered by subscriber behavior. It offers phone support as part of its customer success packages, but you may need to be on a higher tier to access it directly. For businesses that value polished templates and straightforward list management, this is a strong alternative, though its pricing can be higher than AWeber for smaller lists.

Try Campaign Monitor →

How they compare

All three alternatives offer robust autoresponder capabilities, but they differ in how they deliver phone support. Constant Contact provides the most accessible phone support for all paid plans, making it a natural fit if you value that direct human contact. Mailchimp limits phone support to its mid and top tier plans, which may increase your monthly cost. Campaign Monitor offers phone support mainly through higher tier packages, so it is best suited for businesses with larger budgets. In terms of automation depth, Mailchimp’s journey builder is the most flexible, while Constant Contact and Campaign Monitor keep things simpler but still effective for common sequences like welcome and follow-up emails.
