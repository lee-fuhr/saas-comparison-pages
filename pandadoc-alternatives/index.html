If your sales team uses PandaDoc for proposals and e-signatures, you might be looking for a change. Maybe the pricing no longer fits, the templates feel restrictive, or you need features like advanced analytics, custom branding, or simpler workflows. The good news is there are strong alternatives that specialize in different parts of the proposal and contract process. Below we compare five options that sales teams often consider when moving away from PandaDoc.

alternative

DocuSign

. DocuSign is the industry standard for e-signatures, but it also offers contract lifecycle and proposal generation tools. It works best if your team already uses other DocuSign products or needs deep integration with CRMs like Salesforce, though its proposal building features are less visual than PandaDoc’s.

Try DocuSign →
alternative

Proposify

. Proposify focuses on creating professional, branded proposals with drag and drop editing and a content library. It offers useful analytics like page-by-page engagement, but its e-signature capabilities are more limited than DocuSign’s, so some users pair it with a separate signing tool.

Try Proposify →
alternative

Qwilr

. Qwilr turns proposals into interactive landing pages instead of static PDFs, which can help you track viewer behavior in real time. It includes e-signatures and payment collection, making it a good choice for teams that want to close deals faster with a modern, mobile friendly look.

Try Qwilr →
alternative

Better Proposals

. Better Proposals is a simpler, more affordable tool built for small to midsize sales teams. It provides easy to use templates and basic tracking, but it lacks advanced features like multi-language support or deep CRM integrations that larger teams might need.

alternative

GetAccept

. GetAccept combines proposals, e-signatures, and video messaging in one platform. It is especially strong for sales teams that want to send personalized videos along with documents and track when prospects view and sign, though its proposal editor is less robust than PandaDoc’s.

How they compare

DocuSign leads on e-signature compliance and CRM integrations but is weaker for visual proposal design. Proposify and Qwilr offer better looking, trackable proposals, with Qwilr emphasizing interactive pages and Proposify providing detailed engagement analytics. Better Proposals is the most budget friendly option, while GetAccept stands out with its video and document tracking combination. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize design, analytics, integrations, or simplicity.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is easiest to migrate to from PandaDoc?
Proposify and Better Proposals both offer import tools and similar workflow logic, making the switch relatively smooth. DocuSign may require more setup time if you are also using its contract lifecycle features.
Can I still send documents for signature without a proposal tool?
Yes. DocuSign and GetAccept both allow you to send standalone documents for e-signature without building a full proposal. Proposify and Qwilr require you to create a proposal first, but they include signing inside the document.
