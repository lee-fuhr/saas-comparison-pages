Bear is a popular markdown note app for Mac and iOS, known for its clean design and smooth writing experience. However, you might be looking for alternatives due to Bear’s subscription pricing, limited formatting options, or desire for cross-platform support. This article covers three notable alternatives: Obsidian, Notion, and Evernote, each with different strengths for note taking.

alternative

Obsidian

is a local-first markdown app that stores your notes as plain text files. It offers powerful linking and graph views, perfect for building a personal knowledge base. Obsidian is free for personal use with optional paid sync and publish features.

alternative

Notion

is an all-in-one workspace that supports markdown shortcuts but uses its own block-based format. It excels at combining notes, databases, and project management in a single app. Notion is available on Mac, iOS, and web, with a generous free plan.

alternative

Evernote

is a long-standing note app that focuses on organization and search. While it does not use native markdown, it supports rich text and web clipping. Evernote offers robust features for power users but requires a subscription for full access across devices.

How they compare

For markdown purity and local storage, Obsidian is the closest alternative to Bear. Notion provides more flexibility with databases and collaboration but is not a pure markdown app. Evernote is less markdown-focused but offers advanced search and document scanning. Bear remains the most polished native Mac and iOS markdown app, while these alternatives fill specific needs.
