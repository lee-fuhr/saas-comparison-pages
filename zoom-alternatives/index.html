Best Zoom alternatives

Zoom is a popular video conferencing tool, but some remote teams find it too heavy on system resources or too expensive for their needs. If you want a lighter or cheaper option, several alternatives offer solid call quality with lower overhead. This article covers five tools that can replace Zoom for everyday meetings, team stand ups, and quick chats.

alternative

Google Meet

. It is included with most Google Workspace plans and works directly in your browser without extra downloads. Calls are reliable, screen sharing is simple, and the free tier supports meetings up to 60 minutes.

Try Google Meet →
alternative

Microsoft Teams

. This tool is ideal if your team already uses Microsoft 365. It offers robust chat, file sharing, and video calls with up to 300 participants on the free plan. The desktop app is heavier than some alternatives, but the web version runs lighter.

alternative

Whereby

. Whereby is a browser based tool that requires no installation at all. You create a persistent room link, and guests join with one click. The free plan allows unlimited meetings of up to 45 minutes with 4 participants.

Try Whereby →
alternative

Around

. Around is designed for remote teams who want a very lightweight and intuitive experience. It uses a floating video window that stays on top of other apps, making it easy to multitask. The free plan includes unlimited meetings with up to 12 participants.

alternative

Livekit

. Livekit is an open source, developer friendly platform that you can host yourself or use via their cloud service. It offers high performance video and audio with very low latency, ideal for teams that need custom integrations or want to control their data. Costs are usage based and often lower than Zoom for large volumes.

How they compare

Google Meet is best for teams already on Google Workspace who need a simple, browser based meeting tool. Microsoft Teams suits organizations that rely on Microsoft 365 and want chat, files, and video in one place. Whereby is the easiest option for ad hoc calls with no sign up required. Around prioritizes a lightweight, floating interface that keeps video visible while you work. Livekit targets teams that need custom video features or self hosted infrastructure, often at a lower cost than Zoom.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is the lightest on system resources?
Whereby and Around both run entirely in the browser with minimal CPU and memory use. Around’s floating window is especially efficient for older laptops or teams that keep many apps open.
Can I use any of these for free instead of Zoom?
Yes. Google Meet, Whereby, and Around offer free plans with meeting time limits (60 minutes for Meet, 45 minutes for Whereby, unlimited for Around with 12 participants). Microsoft Teams also has a free tier with 60 minute meetings and limited storage.
