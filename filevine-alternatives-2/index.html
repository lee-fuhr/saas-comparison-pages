Best Filevine alternatives

Filevine is a legal practice management platform built around case-centric task workflows, making it a strong choice for litigation firms that need to track deadlines, documents, and team responsibilities. However, some firms find its complexity, pricing, or specific feature gaps lead them to explore other options. This overview compares Filevine alternatives that also prioritize task management for litigation, focusing on Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, and Smokeball.

alternative

Clio

offers a robust task management system with customizable templates and integrations that appeal to litigation firms. Its calendar and document automation features help teams stay on top of court deadlines and case milestones. Clio also provides a client portal and payment tools, making it a good all-around option.

alternative

MyCase

includes built-in legal calendaring and task lists that sync with case files, which is useful for litigation workflows. Its dashboard shows open tasks per case, and the platform offers secure messaging and document sharing for team collaboration. MyCase is often praised for its user-friendly interface and flat-rate pricing.

alternative

PracticePanther

focuses on automating administrative tasks, such as time tracking, billing, and document generation, which can free up time for litigation work. Its task management module lets you assign deadlines and notes to specific cases, and it integrates with popular tools like Outlook and Zapier. PracticePanther also offers a mobile app for on-the-go updates.

alternative

Smokeball

is designed specifically for small to mid-sized litigation firms, with a strong emphasis on case and task automation. It automatically captures time, creates documents from templates, and tracks court rules and deadlines. Smokeball’s task workflows are closely tied to matter details, making it easy for litigation teams to see what is due next.

How they compare

All four alternatives offer case-centric task workflows, but they differ in pricing, setup complexity, and specialty features. Clio and MyCase are more general practice management tools with strong task and calendar functionality, while PracticePanther leans toward automation and integrations. Smokeball is the most litigation-focused, with built-in court rule tracking and automatic time capture. Filevine remains a leader in complex task sequences, but Smokeball and Clio often better suit firms that want simpler onboarding or lower costs.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for litigation firms that need complex task dependencies?
Smokeball and Clio both offer customizable task templates and deadline tracking. Smokeball’s automatic rule-based triggers are especially useful for firms that handle many court deadlines. Clio’s task templates and project management integrations provide similar depth for firms that prefer a broader platform.
Do any of these alternatives integrate with document management systems for litigation?
Yes, all four integrate with popular document management tools. Clio has native integrations with Dropbox, Google Drive, and NetDocuments. MyCase and PracticePanther offer similar options. Smokeball includes a built-in document generation system that saves files directly to the case matter. Check each tool’s integration marketplace for your specific software.
