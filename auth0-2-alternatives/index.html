Identity governance is the practice of managing who has access to what resources, enforcing policies, and ensuring compliance. For developers building custom authentication and authorization flows, Auth0 offers an **Identity Governance** module (part of the Okta platform) that provides policy based access reviews, automated provisioning, and risk based authentication. While Auth0 simplifies user management and integrates with custom code, you might need a dedicated governance tool if your requirements extend beyond Auth0’s core scope – for example, when you must handle complex role hierarchies, manage entitlements across dozens of on-premise systems, or pass an audit for regulations like SOX or HIPAA. The alternatives below offer more granular control for enterprise governance, but they come with higher complexity and steeper learning curves for developers.

alternative

SailPoint

. SailPoint is an enterprise grade identity governance platform that focuses on access certifications, role management, and automated provisioning for thousands of applications. It provides a policy engine that can enforce least privilege at scale, but its custom scripting (through SailPoint IdentityIQ or IdentityNow) requires dedicated IAM specialists rather than a simple SDK integration.

alternative

Saviynt

. Saviynt offers a cloud native identity governance solution with built in access analytics and risk scoring. It excels at connecting to on-premise and cloud apps via connectors, and its low code approach lets developers define custom workflows and access policies. However, you will need to manage a separate identity store or synchronize an existing one.

alternative

BeyondTrust

. BeyondTrust focuses on privileged access management (PAM) rather than broad identity governance, but its integration with developer workflows makes it relevant. It controls and audits elevated permissions (e.g., for database admins or production servers) using session management and credential vaulting. For custom auth flows, you would combine BeyondTrust with Auth0 to govern just the privileged accounts.

alternative

CyberArk

. CyberArk is the market leader in privileged access management, securing administrator credentials and monitoring sessions. It provides APIs for developers to automate vaulting and rotation of secrets, but it is not a full governance suite. You would use CyberArk alongside Auth0 if your primary governance need is protecting highly sensitive privileges rather than general user access reviews.

How they compare

For developers building custom auth flows, Auth0 Identity Governance is the easiest to embed because it uses the same API and SDKs you already use for authentication. SailPoint and Saviynt offer deeper governance (access certifications, entitlement management) but require integrating with a separate system and often demand dedicated IAM expertise. BeyondTrust and CyberArk are not direct competitors – they solve privileged access governance, not general user identity governance. Choose Auth0 when you want governance to be a side effect of your existing auth pipeline; choose SailPoint or Saviynt when compliance mandates a standalone governance engine. Choose BeyondTrust or CyberArk when you need to control and audit elevated administrative access without managing regular user identities.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Auth0 alone for identity governance, or do I need a separate tool like SailPoint?
Auth0 Identity Governance covers common needs like automated provisioning, basic access reviews, and risk based policies. For heavily regulated environments with complex role hierarchies, cross-application certification campaigns, or on-premise system integration, a dedicated tool like SailPoint or Saviynt is recommended.
How do I integrate an alternative governance tool with my Auth0 custom auth flows?
For SailPoint or Saviynt, you typically use SCIM or custom API connectors to synchronize users and groups between Auth0 and the governance tool. Then the governance tool handles access certifications and provisioning decisions, while Auth0 remains the authentication point. For privileged access tools like CyberArk, you call their APIs to vault secrets or fetch session tokens within your custom flow.
