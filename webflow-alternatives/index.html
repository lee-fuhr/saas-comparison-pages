Best Webflow alternatives

Webflow is a powerful tool for designers and agencies, but it is not the perfect fit for every client project. Some clients need simpler content management, lower costs, or specific integrations that Webflow does not offer. If you are evaluating other platforms to build and hand off client sites, here is a look at the most common Webflow alternatives and how they compare.

alternative

Framer

. Framer excels when your client needs a heavily animated, design-forward landing page or a marketing site. Its visual editor feels familiar to designers from tools like Figma, but the content management system (CMS) is less robust than Webflow’s, making it better for one-page sites than multi-page blogs or data-driven projects.

Try Framer →
alternative

WordPress

. WordPress remains the most flexible option for client sites that require custom post types, ecommerce, or complex plugins. You (or a developer) have full control over the code, but the visual editing experience is not as seamless as Webflow’s and often requires additional page builders like Elementor or Gutenberg to match a designer-friendly workflow.

alternative

Squarespace

. Squarespace is ideal for agencies that want a straightforward, all-in-one solution for clients who will manage their own content. The drag and drop editor is simpler than Webflow, but you sacrifice granular control over CSS and interactions, which can be limiting for custom design projects.

Try Squarespace →
alternative

Editor X

. Editor X is Wix’s professional layout tool, offering responsive design controls and CSS grid capabilities similar to Webflow. It works well for agencies that need a visual editor with strong breakpoint management, though its performance and plugin ecosystem are not as mature as Webflow’s.

alternative

Wix Studio

. Wix Studio (formerly Wix for agencies) provides a collaborative workspace with client billing, role management, and a design editor that supports advanced animations. It is a strong choice if you want an all-in-one platform with built-in hosting, but the design freedom is still less than what you get with Webflow, especially for complex interactions.

Choose Webflow when you need pixel-perfect design and a built-in CMS. Choose Framer for fast, animated landing pages. Choose WordPress for maximum flexibility and SEO power. Choose Squarespace for simple, managed sites. Pick Editor X or Wix Studio for a collaborative client workflow with good responsive control.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is easiest for a non-technical client to update after launch?
Squarespace and Wix Studio typically offer the most intuitive editing experience for clients. They use simple drag and drop tools and require no coding knowledge. Framer is also easy for basic text and image changes, but its animation settings can confuse some users.
Can I migrate a Webflow site to one of these alternatives without rebuilding everything?
No, a direct one-click migration is not available for any of these platforms. You will need to manually copy content and recreate the design. For WordPress, you can import content via RSS or CSV, but the layout and styles must be rebuilt. Plan for a full redesign when switching platforms.
