If your product team is re-evaluating your analytics stack, you probably already know that Amplitude is a powerful tool for tracking user behavior and running product experiments. However, your needs may have changed: maybe you are looking for lower costs, more control over data, or a different mix of features. This article covers five common Amplitude alternatives – Mixpanel, PostHog, Heap, Pendo, and Statsig – and helps you decide which one fits your product team’s actual workflows and budget.

alternative

Mixpanel

focuses on event based analytics and offers a clean, user friendly interface for building behavioral cohorts and funnel reports. It is best suited for teams that need real time insights and can pay a premium for advanced analysis features like predictive analytics.

PostHog

alternative

Heap

automatically captures every user interaction without requiring manual event tracking. This makes setup fast, but the raw data can be noisy and may require extra work to clean and define meaningful events after the fact.

alternative

Pendo

combines product analytics with in app guides, NPS surveys, and adoption tracking. It is a good choice for teams whose primary goal is driving feature adoption and onboarding, but it is less suited for deep ad hoc data exploration.

alternative

Statsig

is built for experimentation and feature management first, with product analytics as a supporting layer. Teams that run many A/B tests and need to ship features quickly will appreciate its integrated approach, but pure behavioral analysis is not its main strength.

How they compare

When comparing Amplitude to these alternatives, consider three main dimensions: pricing model, data governance, and analysis depth. Amplitude and Mixpanel charge per tracked event, which can become expensive at scale. PostHog offers a free tier and self hosting, so it is often more cost effective for high volume data. Heap uses a different model (automatic capture, usually billed on user count) which can simplify budgeting. Pendo and Statsig tie their pricing more to features like guides or experiments rather than raw event volume. For data governance, PostHog gives you the most control because you own the infrastructure. For analysis depth, Amplitude and Mixpanel are the strongest; Heap and Pendo prioritize ease of use and actionability over query flexibility. Evaluate which trade offs matter most for your team’s size and technical skill level.
