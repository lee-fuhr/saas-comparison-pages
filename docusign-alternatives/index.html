If your business sends contracts regularly, you already know DocuSign is a popular e-signature tool. But its pricing can be a challenge for small and mid-sized teams. Fortunately, several affordable DocuSign alternatives offer similar features at a lower cost. This article reviews five top options to help you find the right fit for your budget and workflow.

alternative

HelloSign

. HelloSign (now part of Dropbox) is known for its clean, simple interface and competitive pricing. It supports unlimited signature requests on its paid plans, making it a strong choice for teams that send many contracts but do not need heavy document automation.

PandaDoc

. PandaDoc goes beyond signing by including built-in document creation, content libraries, and payment collection. Its all-in-one approach suits businesses that want to propose, collaborate on, and close deals within one platform. The starter plan is affordable for small teams.

SignNow

. SignNow offers a straightforward e-signature experience with a lower entry price than DocuSign. It includes features like bulk sending, templates, and mobile signing. Advanced plans add team management and API access for those who need more control.

Frequently asked questions

Which DocuSign alternative is the most affordable for unlimited signatures?
Docsketch and SignNow both offer unlimited signature requests on their lowest paid plans, with prices starting around $8-$10 per month. HelloSign also provides unlimited signatures on its $15 per month plan.
Do these alternatives offer the same legal validity as DocuSign?
Yes. All the tools listed (HelloSign, PandaDoc, SignNow, Adobe Acrobat Sign, and Docsketch) comply with e-signature laws like the ESIGN Act and UETA in the U.S. and eIDAS in Europe. Their signatures are legally binding in most standard business contracts.
