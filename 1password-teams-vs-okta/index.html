For small-to-midsize businesses, balancing security with ease of use is key. You need a way to manage passwords and give your team one-click access to the apps they use every day. 1Password Teams is built around password management first, with single sign-on (SSO) integration included to simplify logins. This article compares 1Password Teams with Okta, a popular identity platform, to help you decide which fits your needs.

alternative

Okta

is an identity and access management platform that centers on SSO across many applications, with password management available as an add-on feature. It is designed to handle complex user directories and works well for larger organizations, but small teams may find its setup more involved. Okta is a strong choice if your priority is managing user identities across hundreds of apps rather than just passwords.

How they compare

1Password Teams focuses on password security and includes SSO as a built-in convenience, making it simple to deploy and manage. Okta provides a broader identity platform with robust SSO capabilities, but its password management features are less central and may require extra configuration. For most small-to-midsize businesses that need a practical password manager with SSO, 1Password Teams offers lower complexity and often lower cost. Okta is better suited if your business requires advanced user lifecycle management or integrates with a very large number of enterprise applications.

Frequently asked questions

Can 1Password Teams replace Okta for SSO?
Not entirely, because 1Password Teams is a password manager that includes SSO for a set of integrated apps, while Okta offers a much wider SSO network and advanced identity policies. For basic SSO needs, 1Password Teams works well, but if you require SSO for hundreds of apps or complex access rules, Okta may be a better fit.
Which tool is easier to set up for a small team?
1Password Teams is generally easier to set up because it starts with password management and adds SSO as a simple configuration step. Okta requires more upfront planning, such as directory integration and app connections, which can take longer for teams without dedicated IT support.
