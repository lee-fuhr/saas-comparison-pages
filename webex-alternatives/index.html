If your enterprise team is reassessing Webex for video conferencing, you are likely looking for a platform that better meets your needs for reliability, integrations, or user experience. Webex offers robust security and meeting features, but some organizations find its interface dated or pricing less flexible. This article covers five strong alternatives to help you compare options that may agree better with your workflows, budget, and collaboration style.

alternative

Zoom

. Zoom is widely adopted for its ease of use and reliable video quality. It offers features such as breakout rooms, virtual backgrounds, and extensive third-party integrations. For enterprise teams, Zoom provides large meeting capacity, advanced admin controls, and options like Zoom Phone and Zoom Rooms.

alternative

Microsoft Teams

. Microsoft Teams integrates deeply with Office 365 and offers a full suite of collaboration tools including chat, file sharing, and calendar. It is ideal for organizations already using Microsoft products, as it unifies communication. Teams supports large meetings with features like live captions, recording, and compliance controls.

Try Microsoft Teams →
alternative

Google Meet

. Google Meet is a straightforward video conferencing tool built into Google Workspace. It offers strong integration with Google Calendar, Gmail, and Drive, and includes live captions and screen sharing. For enterprise teams already on Google Workspace, Meet provides a seamless experience with no extra cost for video calls.

alternative

Whereby

. Whereby is a lightweight, browser-based video conferencing solution that requires no downloads. It is well suited for smaller teams or quick meetings, offering custom meeting links and screen sharing. Its simplicity makes it a good choice for organizations that want minimal setup and low overhead.

alternative

BlueJeans

. BlueJeans by Verizon focuses on high quality video and audio, with strong support for large enterprise deployments. It features Dolby voice, advanced analytics, and interoperability with room systems. BlueJeans is often chosen for its reliability and security features, especially in regulated industries.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for large enterprise meetings with over 500 participants?
Zoom and Microsoft Teams support large meetings well. Zoom offers up to 1000 participants with its large meeting add on, and Teams allows up to 1000 attendees with a public preview for 20,000 view only attendees. Both have admin controls for enterprise management.
Do these alternatives offer end to end encryption like Webex?
Zoom and Microsoft Teams provide end to end encryption for meetings, but it must be enabled and has some feature limitations (e.g., no breakout rooms in encrypted mode). BlueJeans and Google Meet use encryption in transit and at rest, but do not currently offer user controlled end to end encryption for all scenarios. Review each tool’s security documentation for your specific needs.
