Best Brandfolder alternatives

Brandfolder is a popular digital asset management (DAM) platform, but growing enterprises often find that its permission controls and scalability do not fully meet complex security or governance requirements. If you need granular access rules for hundreds of user groups, enterprise-grade encryption, or the ability to handle millions of assets without performance lags, exploring Brandfolder alternatives is a smart move. Below we review four strong contenders that prioritize secure, scalable DAM with advanced permission features.

alternative

Bynder

. Bynder offers a robust permission framework with role based access, folder level restrictions, and a dedicated “portal” feature that lets you share curated assets externally without exposing your full library. Its AI powered smart tagging and built in brand guidelines help maintain consistency across large teams, and the platform scales easily for global deployments.

alternative

Canto

. Canto provides a more straightforward permission model with user groups, customizable access tiers, and a “share link” system that supports password protection and expiration dates. While its interface is simpler than some competitors, Canto excels at bridging the gap between marketing and sales teams, and its unlimited user pricing can be cost effective for mid size to large enterprises.

alternative

Cloudinary DAM

. Cloudinary DAM delivers enterprise grade security features such as signed URLs, strict access tokens, and fine grained role based permissions at the asset level. As a headless DAM, it integrates deeply with existing tech stacks and automates image / video optimization, making it a strong choice for organizations that need a programmable, API first solution with advanced access controls.

alternative

Widen Collective

. Widen Collective offers a mature permission system that supports user groups, custom roles, and “security policies” tied to asset categories or metadata. Its built in workflow tools and detailed audit logs give enterprise administrators full visibility into who accesses what, and the platform handles high volume usage without degradation.

How they compare

All four alternatives provide better granularity for enterprise permissions than Brandfolder in specific areas. Bynder leads in external sharing controls and brand governance. Canto offers a simpler, cost effective model for unlimited users. Cloudinary DAM is best for developers needing API driven, headless security. Widen Collective combines strong permissions with workflow and auditing for regulated industries. Your choice depends on which balance of flexibility, integration depth, and budget matches your organization’s security and scalability needs.

Frequently asked questions

Can these alternatives handle millions of assets without slowing down loading or search?
Yes. Bynder, Canto, Cloudinary DAM, and Widen Collective are all built for enterprise scale and use cloud native infrastructure to support millions of assets with fast search, preview, and download performance.
Do these platforms offer role based permissions that restrict access at the folder or asset level?
Yes. All four offer role based access controls that can limit who sees, edits, or downloads specific folders, subfolders, or even individual assets. Cloudinary DAM and Widen Collective provide particularly deep asset level permission settings.
