If your fast-growing company wants HR, payroll, and IT in one platform, Rippling is a strong choice. It automates employee onboarding, device management, and global payroll. But it might not fit every budget or workflow. Here are practical alternatives to consider.

alternative

Gusto

. Gusto excels at payroll and benefits for small to midsize teams. It offers built-in time tracking, hiring tools, and a simple employee self-service portal. However, its IT management features are more limited than Rippling’s dedicated device and app controls.

Try Gusto →
alternative

BambooHR

. BambooHR focuses on core HR tasks like employee records, time off, and performance reviews. It integrates with many payroll and IT providers but does not include native IT device management or global payroll. It works best for companies that already have separate payroll and IT vendors.

Try BambooHR →
alternative

Deel

. Deel is built for hiring and paying remote workers around the world. It handles contractor and employee compliance in over 150 countries. While Deel offers some HR features, it lacks the deep IT automation Rippling provides for device provisioning and security.

Try Deel →
alternative

Workday

. Workday is an enterprise-level HR and finance platform for large organizations. It covers workforce planning, payroll, and analytics, but its complexity and cost can be too much for fast-growing startups. IT device management is not a core part of the platform.

alternative

TriNet

. TriNet is a professional employer organization (PEO) that bundles payroll, benefits, and HR compliance. It takes on employer responsibilities, which can reduce legal risks. But you give up some control over policies, and its IT capabilities are minimal compared to Rippling.

How they compare

Rippling offers the tightest integration of HR, payroll, and IT in one system, making it ideal for companies that want to automate device management, app provisioning, and onboarding together. Gusto and BambooHR are simpler and more affordable for basic HR and payroll, but require separate IT tools. Deel is best for global teams, Workday for large enterprises, and TriNet for those wanting a PEO. Choose based on whether you need strong IT automation, global reach, or a lightweight HR solution.
