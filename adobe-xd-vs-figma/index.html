Adobe XD vs Figma: which should you pick?

Choosing between Adobe XD and Figma is a common decision for designers. Both tools let you create wireframes, prototypes, and design systems. But they serve different priorities. Adobe XD is part of the broader Creative Cloud ecosystem. Figma is built from the ground up for real time collaboration. This article compares Adobe XD vs Figma to help you decide which fits your workflow.

Where Adobe XD wins

Adobe XD integrates tightly with other Adobe tools like Photoshop and Illustrator. If your team already uses those apps for assets, you can import layers and maintain vectors without leaving XD. The component workflow is solid for designers who work alone or in small teams and want a native desktop experience.

XD also offers better performance on large, complex files because it runs locally on your machine. It handles high density screens and responsive resize features smoothly. The auto animate feature is polished for transitions between artboards. And for teams that need offline access, XD works without an internet connection.

Another advantage is the developer handoff. XD lets developers inspect layers, copy CSS, and export assets directly from the app. This works well if your team uses other Adobe products or prefers a local solution.

Where Figma wins

Figma is the leader in collaboration. Multiple designers can work on the same file at the same time, see each others cursors, and chat in the browser. This is a big advantage for remote teams or anyone who needs instant feedback. The cloud based approach means no files to send or version conflicts.

Figma supports a vast library of plugins and community resources. You can find templates, icon sets, and design kits easily. The component system with variants and auto layout is more advanced than XD. Auto layout in Figma allows you to create responsive designs that adapt to content changes without manual adjustments.

Figma also works on any operating system including Windows, Mac, and Linux. You can share a public link with stakeholders or clients and they can view or comment without creating an account. The commenting and developer handoff tools are intuitive. And because everything lives in the browser, you always have the latest version.

Verdict

Pick Adobe XD versus Figma based on your team size and workflow. If you rely on the Adobe ecosystem, need offline stability, or work on very large files alone, Adobe XD is a strong choice. If collaboration, real time feedback, and cross platform access matter more, Figma is the better option. For most teams today, Figma wins because of its seamless sharing and community support. But if your design process is deeply tied to Photoshop and Illustrator, Adobe XD still holds its ground.
