If you run a SaaS business selling digital services across multiple EU countries, you need to handle VAT compliance for each customer’s location. The rules differ by country, rates change frequently, and the annual threshold for distance selling has been replaced by the One Stop Shop (OSS) scheme. Automated tools like Taxamo are built to handle this complexity. Taxamo specializes in EU digital VAT, offering real-time tax calculation, invoicing, and reporting. But it’s not the only option. This article compares Taxamo with Avalara and three other alternatives, so you can pick the right fit for your SaaS.

alternative

Avalara

. Avalara is a broad platform that covers VAT, sales tax, and other tax types across many countries. It provides a comprehensive suite with AvaTax for calculation, compliance reporting, and exemption management. For EU VAT, it works well but may include extra features you don’t need, and its pricing can be higher for smaller SaaS operations.

alternative

TaxJar

. TaxJar is widely known for US sales tax but also supports EU VAT through its API and integration partners. It offers calculation and reporting, with a simpler interface than some competitors. However, its EU capabilities are less mature than dedicated EU-first tools, and you may need additional tools for full OSS filing.

alternative

Vertex

. Vertex is an enterprise-grade tax automation platform that handles VAT, GST, and other global taxes. It provides robust rules engines and deep integration options, but it often requires more configuration and longer implementation. It is best suited for larger SaaS companies with complex billing scenarios and dedicated tax teams.

alternative

Sovos

. Sovos focuses on tax compliance and regulatory reporting, including VAT, e-invoicing, and real-time transaction reporting. It offers strong support for EU VAT and the OSS scheme, with automated filing and audit defense. Like Vertex, it is typically used by mid-market to enterprise businesses due to its higher cost and complexity.

How they compare

When choosing between Taxamo and Avalara, consider your specific needs. Taxamo is the leanest, most focused choice for EU digital VAT: it handles OSS, local thresholds, and MOSS without extra overhead. Avalara offers more global coverage and a wider feature set, which can be useful if you also need US sales tax or other compliance, but it may cost more and require more setup. TaxJar is a cost-effective alternative if you already use it for the US, but its EU support is secondary. Vertex and Sovos are powerful for large enterprises with complex compliance, but they are overkill for most SaaS startups and small teams. For a pure EU VAT solution, Taxamo often wins on simplicity and pricing, while Avalara wins on breadth. Test the APIs and review your annual transaction volume before deciding.

Frequently asked questions

Can Taxamo handle the EU One Stop Shop (OSS) scheme for me?
Yes. Taxamo supports the OSS scheme (formerly MOSS) for digital services. It calculates the correct VAT rate based on your customer’s location, generates invoices that meet local requirements, and prepares the OSS return for you to submit. You still need to file the return in your chosen member state, but Taxamo provides the data.
Does Avalara offer any advantage over Taxamo for SaaS companies only selling in the EU?
Not necessarily. Both tools calculate EU VAT accurately. Avalara’s advantage is its broader global coverage and additional features like exemption certificate management. For a SaaS that only sells digital services in the EU, Taxamo is often simpler to integrate and cheaper, while Avalara may be a better fit if you plan to expand to other tax types or regions later.
