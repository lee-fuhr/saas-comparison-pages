If your growth or product team is running experiments, you likely know Optimizely as a leading experimentation platform. However, rising costs, complexity, or a need for more specialized features might have you looking for Optimizely alternatives. Whether you want better pricing, simpler setup, or deeper integration with your data stack, several tools offer strong capabilities for A/B testing, feature flagging, and statistical analysis. Below we compare five notable alternatives: VWO, AB Tasty, Convert, Statsig, and Eppo.

alternative

VWO

is a full suite for A/B testing, visual editor, and personalization with a focus on ease of use for marketers and product managers. It offers robust reporting and a built-in heatmap tool, making it a good choice if you want an all-in-one optimization platform without deep engineering involvement.

Try VWO →
alternative

AB Tasty

combines experimentation with personalization and feature management, emphasizing visual editing and cross-channel campaigns. It is well suited for teams that need to run tests across web, mobile, and email, and it provides solid support for multi-armed bandit algorithms.

Try AB Tasty →
alternative

Convert

(formerly Convert Experiences) is a privacy-first experimentation platform that offers server-side and client-side testing along with feature flags. It appeals to teams in regulated industries because of its strong data governance controls and transparent pricing without traffic-based tiers.

Try Convert →
alternative

Statsig

is a modern platform that merges feature management, experimentation, and product analytics in one stack. It targets engineering-heavy teams that want to run experiments quickly using a simple SDK and get real-time statistical results with no manual analysis.

alternative

Eppo

is a cloud-based experimentation platform designed for data teams, offering flexible Bayesian and frequentist statistics. It connects directly to your data warehouse, so you can run experiments on any event or metric without duplicating data, making it ideal for teams with existing data infrastructure.
