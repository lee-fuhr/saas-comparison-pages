If you are a small business owner looking for digital signage, you likely want a solution that is both affordable and easy to manage. Two popular options are Yodeck and ScreenCloud. Yodeck is built around low cost Raspberry Pi hardware, making it a strong choice for tight budgets. ScreenCloud offers a more full featured cloud managed platform that works with various media players. This comparison helps you decide which fits your needs and budget.

alternative

ScreenCloud

is a cloud managed digital signage platform that works with players including Amazon Fire TV, Chromebox, and Windows PCs. Its subscription covers both the cloud service and the required player software. ScreenCloud offers more advanced design tools and integrations but typically costs more per screen than Yodeck. Small businesses with limited screens may find the higher price less attractive.

How they compare

Yodeck and ScreenCloud both deliver remote cloud management for digital signage, but they differ in cost and hardware requirements. Yodeck uses low cost Raspberry Pi devices with a free player, keeping upfront and monthly expenses low. ScreenCloud supports more player options but charges a higher subscription fee per screen. For a small business with one to five screens, Yodeck provides a simple, budget friendly solution. ScreenCloud may be a better fit if you need advanced design features or already own supported hardware like a Fire TV. Overall, Yodeck is more cost effective for most small businesses using Raspberry Pi, while ScreenCloud offers broader hardware flexibility at a higher price.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use my own Raspberry Pi with Yodeck?
Yes, Yodeck provides a free operating system image that you install on a standard Raspberry Pi (model 3 or 4 is recommended). You do not need to buy branded hardware from Yodeck, which keeps your costs low.
Which platform is easier to set up for a single screen?
Both Yodeck and ScreenCloud offer simple cloud based setup. Yodeck requires you to flash a microSD card for the Raspberry Pi, while ScreenCloud uses an app on a supported device. Yodeck may take a few extra minutes for the initial card setup, but ongoing management is similar for both.
