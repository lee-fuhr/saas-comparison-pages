Best Claap alternatives

If you run a small business and need quick, simple ways to record and share feedback on videos, you might have heard of Claap. Claap blends async video messaging with basic project tools, but it might not fit every budget or workflow. Fortunately, several other tools offer similar features at different price points. This article focuses on five Claap alternatives: Loom, Vidyard, Tella, Jumpshare, and CloudApp. Each one is designed to help you capture screen recordings, add comments, and collaborate with your team without a steep learning curve.

alternative

Loom

is a popular choice because it works directly in your browser or via a desktop app. You can record your screen, your webcam, or both, and the video link is ready to share instantly. Loom’s free plan offers up to 25 videos of 5 minutes each, which is enough for many small teams to start giving video feedback.

alternative

Vidyard

focuses on video messaging for sales and customer communication, but its free forever plan also works well for internal feedback. You can trim videos, add calls to action, and see who watched your recording. Vidyard integrates with many CRM and email tools, making it a good fit if you already use those programs.

alternative

Tella

positions itself as a clean, simple screen recorder built for creators and small teams. It lets you record and edit your video in one go, with no timeline or complicated editing software. Tella’s starter plan is affordable and allows unlimited recording length, which is useful when you need to walk through a long process.

alternative

Jumpshare

is more than a screen recorder; it also lets you share files, take screenshots, and get feedback on any kind of media. Its video feedback features include time-stamped comments and annotations. Jumpshare’s free plan gives you 2 GB of storage and 30-day link expiration, which should cover occasional recording needs.

alternative

CloudApp

combines screen recording with annotation tools like arrows and text boxes that appear right in your video. You can create quick tutorial videos or bug reports and share them via a shortened link. CloudApp’s free plan limits you to 25 videos per month with a 5-minute length cap, similar to Loom.
