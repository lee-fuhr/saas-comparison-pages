If you are an HR director evaluating Rippling alternatives, you likely value the promise of unified HR and IT management but need a solution that fits your company’s specific size, budget, or compliance requirements. Rippling offers a strong all in one platform for employee management, device control, and app provisioning, but it may not be the right fit for every organization. Some HR teams find Rippling’s pricing opaque or its IT features too deep for their needs, while others prefer a simpler HR core or deeper payroll integration. The three main alternatives to consider are Gusto, ADP, and BambooHR, each with distinct strengths in unified HR and IT.

alternative

Gusto

. Gusto is best suited for small to midsize businesses that want an intuitive, all in one HR and payroll platform with basic IT features like employee onboarding and offboarding workflows. It excels in automated payroll, benefits administration, and compliance support, though its IT device management and app provisioning are less advanced than Rippling’s. For HR directors who prioritize a user friendly experience and strong payroll capabilities over deep IT controls, Gusto is a compelling alternative.

alternative

ADP

. ADP is a heavyweight in payroll and HR compliance, offering enterprise grade tools like ADP Workforce Now that can unify HR, benefits, time tracking, and basic IT access management. Its IT integration is not as seamless as Rippling’s, but ADP provides unmatched scalability, global payroll support, and regulatory expertise for large or multinational organizations. HR directors at companies with complex compliance needs or a large distributed workforce often choose ADP over Rippling for its proven reliability.

alternative

BambooHR

. BambooHR focuses on core HR functions such as employee records, time off tracking, performance management, and reporting, with optional integrations for payroll and IT tools. It lacks native IT device management like Rippling, but it offers a clean, intuitive interface and strong applicant tracking features. For HR directors who want a straightforward HRIS that scales with a growing team and can integrate with third party IT tools (like Okta or JumpCloud), BambooHR is a solid, less complex alternative.

How they compare

Rippling provides the most unified HR and IT experience with native device management and app provisioning, but at a higher cost and complexity. Gusto is simpler and better for payroll heavy small businesses, while ADP offers enterprise scale and compliance depth. BambooHR excels at core HR with a clean interface but requires separate IT tools. Choose based on your organization’s size, IT maturity, and whether you need deep device control or just basic onboarding and offboarding automation.

Frequently asked questions

Can any of these alternatives replace Rippling’s IT automation features?
Not fully. Gusto and BambooHR focus more on HR and payroll, so you would need a separate IT tool for device management and app provisioning. ADP offers some IT access controls but not at the same level as Rippling. If IT automation is your top priority, Rippling remains the strongest choice among these options.
Which alternative is best for a company with 200 employees and no dedicated IT team?
Gusto or BambooHR would be better. Both are user friendly and require minimal IT setup. Gusto handles payroll and benefits well, and BambooHR offers strong employee data management. You can then add a lightweight IT tool like a directory service for basic app provisioning. Rippling might be overkill for a company without an IT team, and ADP could be too complex for that size.
