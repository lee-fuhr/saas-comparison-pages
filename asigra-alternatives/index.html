Choosing the right backup and disaster recovery platform is a critical decision for managed service providers. You need a solution that handles multiple clients efficiently, scales without complexity, and keeps your operational costs under control. This article compares Asigra with Veeam and other popular alternatives, focusing on the specific needs of MSPs who require multi-tenant backup and DR. Asigra is built from the ground up for service providers, offering a single unified platform with agentless protection, cloud integration, and a true multi-tenant architecture that separates each client’s data and settings.

alternative

Zerto

. Zerto specializes in continuous data protection and disaster recovery for virtualized and cloud environments. It provides near-zero recovery point objectives through journal-based replication, which can be attractive for MSPs managing high-availability workloads. However, Zerto is primarily designed for IT departments rather than service providers, and its multi-tenant capabilities are less mature than purpose-built MSP platforms.

alternative

Veeam

. Veeam is a widely used backup and replication tool that offers strong protection for VMware and Hyper-V environments. It has a large ecosystem and many third-party integrations. For MSPs, Veeam provides a multi-tenant portal via Veeam Service Provider Console, but it often requires additional licensing and setup to isolate client data properly, and its per-VM licensing can become expensive at scale.

alternative

Druva

. Druva is a SaaS-based data protection platform that eliminates on-premises infrastructure by storing backups in the cloud. Its multi-tenant architecture is designed for distributed environments and includes features like global deduplication and policy automation. However, Druva’s recovery speeds can be slower than local options, and its pricing model based on protected capacity may surprise MSPs with rapid data growth.

alternative

Acronis

. Acronis offers a complete cyber protection platform that combines backup, disaster recovery, and anti-malware capabilities. Its Cyber Cloud for service providers includes a multi-tenant console and direct-to-cloud backup. While Acronis is easy to deploy, its feature set can feel fragmented compared to specialized backup platforms, and some MSPs find its licensing structure complex to manage across many clients.

How they compare

When comparing Asigra and Veeam for MSP multi-tenant backup and DR, the core difference lies in architecture and business model. Asigra is a cloud-first, agentless platform specifically engineered for service providers, with native multi-tenancy, per-GB pricing that aligns with revenue, and built-in global deduplication that reduces storage costs across all clients. Veeam, while powerful and well known, is designed primarily for on-premises enterprise use; its service provider console is an add-on, and licensing per workload or per VM can make it less predictable for MSPs. Both tools can protect major hypervisors and cloud environments, but Asigra’s single pane of glass management, automatic client isolation, and embedded DR testing give MSPs a lower total cost of ownership. For service providers who want a platform that grows with their business without unexpected licensing costs or complex workarounds, Asigra offers a more streamlined, MSP-centric experience.
