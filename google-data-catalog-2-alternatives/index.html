Best Google Data Catalog alternatives

If your team operates on Google Cloud Platform and needs a managed metadata service with auto-tagging, Google Data Catalog is a strong native choice. It integrates directly with BigQuery, Pub/Sub, and other GCP services, letting you automatically discover and tag assets without manual setup. Data Catalog also supports business metadata like descriptions and labels, and its policy tag system helps enforce access controls. However, you may find that its search capabilities and governance features are less mature than those of dedicated third party platforms.

alternative

Alation

focuses on data intelligence with a strong query log analyzer that surfaces popular and underused datasets. It offers automated tagging based on usage patterns and natural language descriptions, but can require significant upfront configuration and licensing cost.

alternative

Collibra

provides a robust data governance framework with workflow driven stewardship and a business glossary. Its auto-tagging uses machine learning and rule based policies, though the platform may feel heavy for teams that only need basic metadata management.

alternative

Atlan

is a modern metadata platform built for collaboration, offering a Google like search experience and automated profiling. It supports auto-tagging through custom classifiers and connectors, but you may need to spend time on initial setup to map your specific data sources.

alternative

DataHub

is an open source metadata platform originally developed by LinkedIn, now widely adopted for its flexible ingestion and graph based lineage. It can auto-tag via ingestion transformers and supports real time updates, but self hosting requires operational effort unless you use the managed DataHub Cloud.

alternative

Secoda

is a lightweight data catalog that prioritizes ease of use with a Notion style interface and automated metadata extraction. Its auto tagging works by scanning schema and column names, but it may lack the advanced governance and lineage depth of larger platforms.
