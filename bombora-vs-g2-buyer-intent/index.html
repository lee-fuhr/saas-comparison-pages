Bombora is one of the most recognized intent data platforms for B2B marketers. It provides firm-level intent signals by analyzing content consumption from a large publisher network. However, Bombora is not the only option. G2 Buyer Intent, TechTarget Priority Engine, and Demandbase ABM each offer different approaches to capturing purchase intent. Understanding how these tools compare will help you choose the one that aligns with your lead scoring, ABM, and account selection strategies. This article focuses on Bombora as the primary solution and reviews the key alternatives.

alternative

G2 Buyer Intent

uses review and browsing behavior from G2’s marketplace to identify companies actively evaluating software. It captures intent based on category and product page visits, comparison downloads, and review submissions. This data is especially useful for marketers targeting buyers in the late consideration stage.

alternative

TechTarget Priority Engine

draws intent from TechTarget’s network of technology-specific editorial sites. It tracks content downloads, topic engagement, and research activity across thousands of IT and business topics. This tool is strong for reaching technical buyers who are in deep research mode.

alternative

Demandbase ABM

combines multiple intent sources, including Bombora, with its own first-party data from website visits and form fills. It uses AI to score accounts and prioritize outreach. Demandbase is a full ABM platform, so it is ideal for teams that want intent data integrated with advertising and sales orchestration.

How they compare

All four tools help identify accounts showing purchase signals, but they differ in data sources and use cases. Bombora collects intent from a large network of B2B publishers, giving broad coverage across many industries. G2 Buyer Intent is narrower, focused on software evaluation behaviors on its own marketplace. TechTarget Priority Engine serves technology buyers with deep topic-level signals from editorial content. Demandbase ABM is a complete platform that can ingest Bombora data along with its own. Bombora is best for early-stage prospecting at scale. G2 and TechTarget offer more granular, product-level intent. Demandbase is best for teams that want an all-in-one ABM system with integrated intent.

Frequently asked questions

How does Bombora’s data quality compare with G2 Buyer Intent?
Bombora relies on a broad publisher co-op which gives wide coverage but can include noise from general research. G2 Buyer Intent is more specific to software buying decisions because it tracks actions like viewing competitor pages and downloading comparison reports. Your choice depends on whether you want broader firm-level signals or more targeted product evaluation data.
Can Bombora and Demandbase ABM be used together?
Yes, many marketers use Bombora as a data source within Demandbase ABM. Demandbase offers an integrated intent module but also supports third party intent data. This combination can give you both Bombora’s broad publisher signals and Demandbase’s first party web analytics and AI scoring.
