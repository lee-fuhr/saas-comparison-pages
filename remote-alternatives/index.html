If you are hiring international employees, an employer of record (EOR) simplifies compliance, payroll, and benefits. Remote is a popular EOR, but you may be exploring remote alternatives to compare pricing, features, or global coverage. This article helps you evaluate five major alternatives: Deel, Oyster, Rippling, Papaya Global, and Multiplier. Each offers a unique approach, so understanding the differences will guide your choice.

alternative

Deel

is known for its broad global footprint and fast onboarding. It supports contractors and employees in over 150 countries and offers a self‑service platform with built‑in compliance checks. Deel also provides equity management and a wide range of benefit options.

alternative

Oyster

focuses on simplicity and an all‑inclusive pricing model. It handles hiring, payroll, and benefits in one flat monthly fee per employee, which can make budgeting easier. Oyster emphasizes a positive employee experience with self‑onboarding and local benefits.

alternative

Rippling

combines EOR services with workforce management and IT tools. It integrates payroll, device management, and app provisioning into a single system. Rippling is a strong choice if you need to manage employee hardware and software alongside global hiring.

alternative

Papaya Global

specializes in global payroll and payments with a heavy compliance focus. It offers a unified payroll platform that covers both employees and contractors in over 160 countries. Papaya Global is often preferred by companies that need robust reporting and multi‑currency payroll.

alternative

Multiplier

is a newer EOR that prioritizes speed and a simple user interface. It supports hiring in more than 150 countries and offers pre‑built employment contracts tailored to local laws. Multiplier also provides free basic compliance audits during onboarding.

How they compare

All five alternatives provide core EOR services: employment contracts, payroll, benefits, and compliance support. Deel offers the widest set of integrated features, including equity and contractor management. Oyster uses a flat‑fee model that appeals to budget‑conscious teams. Rippling stands out for its IT and device management integration. Papaya Global is a strong choice for companies with complex, multi‑country payroll needs. Multiplier aims for simplicity and fast setup. There is no single best option; your choice depends on your priority, whether that is cost, feature depth, integration, or ease of use.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main difference between Remote and Deel?
Both cover a similar number of countries, but Deel generally offers more built‑in contractor management and equity features. Remote is known for its straightforward pricing and strong focus on employee experience. The best choice depends on whether you need those extra Deel features or prefer Remote’s simpler approach.
How do I choose between Oyster and Rippling as a remote alternative?
Start by asking whether you need IT management alongside global hiring. If you want to handle device provisioning and app access in one place, choose Rippling. If you prefer an all‑inclusive monthly price and a platform built purely for global employment, Oyster is a better fit.
