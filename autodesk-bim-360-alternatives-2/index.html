Best Autodesk BIM 360 alternatives

For large construction projects that depend on cloud-based BIM coordination, Autodesk BIM 360 is often the first name that comes to mind. Its strength lies in deep integration with Autodesk design tools and robust model management capabilities. However, every project has unique workflows and budget constraints, so it is worth exploring alternatives that may offer a better fit for your team’s size, collaboration style, or document management needs. This review covers four leading options – Procore, Buildertrend, PlanGrid, and CoConstruct – with a focus on how they handle large scale BIM coordination in the cloud.

alternative

Procore

Procore is a comprehensive construction management platform that excels at unifying project teams through a single source of truth. For large projects, its document control, RFI, and submittal workflows are mature, but its BIM coordination features are less integrated than BIM 360 and often require third party plugins for model viewing.

alternative

Buildertrend

Buildertrend is built primarily for mid sized commercial and residential builders, not the largest infrastructure or mega projects. It offers solid scheduling and field management tools, but its cloud based 3D model coordination capabilities are limited compared to dedicated BIM tools.

alternative

PlanGrid

PlanGrid is now part of the Autodesk family (Autodesk Build) and remains a strong choice for field teams who need fast, reliable 2D plan viewing and markups. While it can handle some model based workflows via Autodesk’s ecosystem, it is not a standalone BIM coordination platform for large projects with complex federated models.

alternative

CoConstruct

CoConstruct focuses on custom home builders and light commercial projects, with tools for estimating, scheduling, and client communication. It lacks the advanced model clash detection and multidiscipline coordination features that large scale BIM projects require.

How they compare

Autodesk BIM 360 leads in deep BIM functionality, clash detection, and model versioning for large projects, but comes with a higher price and steeper learning curve. Procore offers broader project management but weaker native BIM tools. Buildertrend and CoConstruct are better suited for smaller builders, while PlanGrid excels at field plan access but lacks the model coordination depth of BIM 360. For large scale cloud based BIM coordination, BIM 360 remains the most capable option, though Procore can work when paired with a dedicated model viewer.
