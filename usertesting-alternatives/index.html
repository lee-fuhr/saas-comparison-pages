UserTesting is a widely used platform for remote usability testing, but it may not suit every budget, workflow, or team size. UX and product teams often look for alternatives that offer different pricing models, specialized features, or simpler setups. The following tools provide strong options for moderated and unmoderated tests, analytics, and collaboration, each with its own strengths.

alternative

Maze

focuses on unmoderated rapid testing with design prototypes from Figma, Sketch, or Adobe XD. It gives you quantitative metrics like success rate and time on task, plus video playback of user sessions. Maze is ideal for teams that want quick iterative feedback without scheduling live sessions.

alternative

Lookback

specializes in live moderated testing and also supports unmoderated studies with automatic recording. It offers a simple interface for running remote sessions and lets observers tag moments in real time. Lookback is a good choice if you need strong live conversation tools and a straightforward setup.

Try Lookback →
alternative

UserZoom

is a full suite for UX research that covers moderated and unmoderated tests, surveys, and analytics. It includes tools for creating task-based tests and gathering quantitative data such as NPS and SUS scores. UserZoom works best for larger organizations that want an all-in-one research platform.

alternative

Optimal Workshop

is geared toward information architecture and early-stage testing, with tools like tree testing and card sorting. It also includes a simple usability testing feature called OptimalSort for unmoderated tasks. This alternative is valuable when your main focus is on navigation, labels, and structure rather than visual design.

alternative

Useberry

is a lightweight testing tool that works inside your prototype tool, such as Figma or Marvel. It lets you create tasks, track clicks, and watch session replays without leaving your design software. Useberry suits teams that want in-context testing with minimal overhead.

How they compare

All five alternatives can replace or supplement UserTesting, but they differ in focus. Maze and Useberry are best for fast unmoderated prototype testing, Lookback excels at live moderated sessions, UserZoom covers the widest range of research methods, and Optimal Workshop targets information architecture. Consider your team’s primary need, budget, and whether you require quantitative data or qualitative video feedback.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a free or low cost alternative to UserTesting for small teams?
Yes, Maze and Useberry offer free tiers with limited tests, and Lookback has a free plan for one seat. These let you run basic unmoderated tests without paying for a full subscription. For more advanced features, paid plans start at lower price points than UserTesting.
Which alternative works best for testing live websites rather than prototypes?
Lookback and UserZoom both support live website testing through their browser extensions or by embedding scripts. Maze and Useberry are designed primarily for prototypes, so they are less suitable for live sites. Optimal Workshop focuses on structure, not live pages, so Lookback or UserZoom are better options.
