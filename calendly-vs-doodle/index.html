Calendly vs Doodle: which should you pick?

If your team regularly schedules meetings that involve several people, you have likely come across Calendly and Doodle. Both tools aim to eliminate the back-and-forth of finding a time that works for everyone, but they take different approaches. Calendly focuses on sharing your personal availability so others can book directly, while Doodle excels at polling a group to find the best slot. This article compares Calendly vs Doodle for teams that need to coordinate multiple attendees.

Where Calendly wins

Calendly is stronger when you control your own calendar and need to let external guests book time with you or your team. It syncs with your Google, Outlook, or iCloud calendar and automatically blocks off busy slots. For meetings with multiple internal attendees, Calendly’s round-robin and collective availability features let you set up group events where several team members can be booked at once. The tool also sends automated reminders and supports integrations with Zoom, Google Meet, and other video conferencing apps. If your team often schedules one-on-one or small group meetings with clients or colleagues who already know their own schedules, Calendly reduces friction by removing manual coordination.

Where Doodle wins

Doodle is the better choice when you need to find a time that works for a large group of people who are not on a shared calendar system. Instead of exposing individual calendar availability, you create a poll with proposed time slots, and each attendee marks their preferences. This is ideal for internal team meetings, cross-departmental syncs, or external groups where participants have different calendar platforms. Doodle also offers a “group” feature that lets you whittle down options based on availability and avoids double-booking. For meetings with many invitees who need to vote on times, Doodle’s polling model is more democratic and less reliant on everyone’s calendar being synced. It also works well for one-off meetings where you do not want to give people access to your real-time schedule.

Verdict

Choose Calendly vs Doodle based on how your team schedules meetings. If you frequently host meetings with people who can see your availability and book their own slot, and you need automatic calendar syncing and reminders, Calendly is the stronger tool. If instead you need to coordinate a large group where participants have different calendars or are not using the same scheduling system, Doodle’s polling approach will save more time. For many teams, the best answer is to use both: Doodle for group polls and Calendly for direct booking.
