Best Notion Database alternatives

You love the way Notion blends writing with structured data, but maybe you need more muscle for relational tables or a lighter interface. The good news is that several tools offer a note-centric feel while giving you true database power. Below we look at four strong alternatives: Airtable, Coda, NocoDB, and Baserow. Each one keeps the familiar wiki-like experience but handles linked records, formulas, and views in its own way.

alternative

Airtable

starts with a spreadsheet look but lets you attach notes, rich text, and long descriptions right inside each record. Its linked record field creates real relational tables, and the grid, calendar, gallery, and kanban views feel natural for knowledge work. Airtable is best when you need a polished, collaborative database that still feels like a doc.

alternative

Coda

treats everything as a document, but you can drop in tables, rollups, and formulas just like you would in Notion. Its “packs” connect to external tools, and the ability to nest pages inside rows gives you a true note-centric workflow. Coda excels when you want a single workspace that blends writing, data, and automation without switching tools.

alternative

NocoDB

is an open source platform that turns any SQL database into a smart spreadsheet with a Notion-like interface. You can self-host it, add rich text notes to rows, and link tables using lookup columns. NocoDB is ideal if you want full control over your data and don’t mind a bit of setup to get a familiar relational experience.

alternative

Baserow

offers an open source, no-code database that feels like a cleaner version of Airtable. It supports linked tables, formula fields, and multiple views, and you can add text-based notes directly in each row. Baserow is a good pick for teams that need a self-hosted or cloud-hosted relational database without the complexity of traditional tools.
