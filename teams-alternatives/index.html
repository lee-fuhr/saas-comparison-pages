Best Microsoft Teams alternatives

Many enterprises choose Microsoft Teams because it integrates deeply with Office 365, offering chat, video calls, and file sharing in one place. But Teams is not the only option. Some organizations find its complexity or cost too high, while others need different collaboration styles or better third-party compatibility. If you rely on Office 365 for email, calendars, and documents but want a change in your chat plus video collaboration tool, three main alternatives are worth evaluating: Zoom, Google Meet, and Slack. Each brings distinct strengths and trade offs, especially regarding integration depth and user experience.

alternative

Zoom

. Zoom focuses on video conferencing first, with a reliable, high quality experience for large meetings and webinars. It offers persistent chat channels, but its chat feature is less mature than dedicated team chat tools. For enterprises that prioritize video meeting stability and have moderate chat needs, Zoom can work well, especially if you already use Zoom Phone or Zoom Rooms.

alternative

Google Meet

. Google Meet is built into Google Workspace, which means it works seamlessly with Gmail, Google Calendar, and Google Drive. It lacks a persistent team chat feature on its own, but you can pair it with Google Chat or other tools. For organizations that already use Google Workspace alongside Office 365, Meet provides a simple, intuitive video experience without the overhead of a full platform like Teams.

Try Google Meet →
alternative

Slack

. Slack is the leader in team chat, with an intuitive interface, powerful search, and thousands of integrations. Its video calling feature is functional but not as feature rich as Zoom or Meet. Slack’s strength is making chat flexible and organized, and it can integrate with Office 365 apps like Outlook and OneDrive. However, you will need a separate video solution for reliable large meetings.

Try Slack →

How they compare

Microsoft Teams offers the deepest integration with Office 365, combining chat, video, and file collaboration in one app. Zoom excels at video quality and scale but is weaker in persistent chat and document co-authoring. Google Meet is simple and works natively with Google Workspace, but lacks robust chat and deep Office 365 hooks. Slack has the best chat experience and massive app integrations, but its video is basic, and it does not replace Office 365 for document editing or email. For enterprises that depend on Outlook, SharePoint, and OneDrive, Teams remains the most cohesive choice, but each alternative can fill specific gaps if you are willing to mix tools.
