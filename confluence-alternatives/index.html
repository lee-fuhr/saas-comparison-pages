Best Confluence alternatives

If your team relies on Confluence for documentation but finds it too complex, slow, or expensive, you are not alone. Many engineering and product teams outgrow Confluence when they need a more lightweight tool, better search, or lower per-user costs. This guide covers five alternatives that offer simpler setups, faster performance, and often a lower total cost of ownership. Whether you need a knowledge base, a wiki, or a shared workspace, these options can replace Confluence without sacrificing the features your team uses.

alternative

Notion

. Notion combines docs, wikis, and databases in one flexible workspace. It is known for its clean design, easy linking between pages, and powerful templates for product specs, roadmaps, and engineering docs. Many teams switch to Notion because it feels more modern than Confluence and has a generous free plan for smaller groups.

Try Notion →
alternative

Guru

. Guru is a knowledge management tool focused on keeping information current and trusted. It uses a browser extension and integrations with Slack and other tools to put answers where you already work. Teams that need a single source of truth with verification workflows often prefer Guru over Confluence for its speed and simplicity.

Try Guru →
alternative

Tettra

. Tettra is a simple knowledge base built for teams that want to capture and organize internal knowledge without the overhead. It integrates with Slack and other apps so you can answer questions without leaving chat. Engineering teams appreciate Tettra for its minimal interface and automated requests for outdated pages.

alternative

Document360

. Document360 is a purpose-built knowledge base platform for product and technical documentation. It offers version control, a powerful search engine, and analytics to see what people are looking for. Teams with public facing docs or complex user guides often find Document360 more scalable than Confluence for that specific use case.

alternative

Slab

. Slab is a knowledge base and wiki that emphasizes speed and ease of use. It supports markdown, rich embeds, and integrates with tools like GitHub and Asana. Many teams choose Slab because it feels faster than Confluence and provides a simple way to organize knowledge without heavy customization.

Frequently asked questions

Which Confluence alternative is best for a small engineering team on a tight budget?
Notion and Tettra both offer free tiers that support small teams well. Notion gives you a full workspace with databases and wikis, while Tettra keeps things simple with Slack integration. Both avoid the per user costs that add up quickly with Confluence.
I need a tool that keeps documentation accurate and up to date. Which alternative should I consider?
Guru is built specifically for maintaining trusted knowledge with verification workflows and content expiry alerts. Document360 also offers version history and audit trails. Both are stronger than Confluence for ensuring information stays current without manual policing.
