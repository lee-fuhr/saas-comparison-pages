If you use Weebly but feel limited by its design options or want more e‑commerce features, you are not alone. Many small business owners start with Weebly because it is easy and affordable. However, as your business grows, you may need a website builder that gives you more flexibility, better selling tools, or lower ongoing costs. Below we look at three strong Weebly alternatives: Wix, Squarespace, and WordPress.com. Each one can help you create a professional site with e‑commerce add‑ons, without requiring technical skills.

alternative

Wix

. Wix offers hundreds of modern templates and a drag‑and‑drop editor that is even more flexible than Weebly. Its e‑commerce plans let you sell physical and digital products, accept subscriptions, and manage inventory. Wix also includes built‑in marketing tools like email campaigns and social media integrations.

Squarespace

. Squarespace is known for its beautiful, designer‑built templates that work well for stores and portfolios. Its e‑commerce features cover everything from product variants and gift cards to abandoned cart recovery. Squarespace includes hosting and security, so you do not need separate services.

WordPress.com

. WordPress.com (the hosted version) gives you the power of the world’s most popular content management system without managing your own server. For e‑commerce you can add the free WooCommerce plugin, which allows you to sell unlimited products and manage payments. It is a good choice if you want to scale or need custom features later.

How they compare

All three alternatives are easy to use and cost‑effective for small businesses. Wix is best if you want maximum design freedom with a visual editor. Squarespace is ideal if style and polished presentation matter most. WordPress.com offers the most long‑term flexibility and e‑commerce power through WooCommerce, but it has a slightly steeper learning curve. Weebly remains a solid entry‑level option, but these alternatives give you better e‑commerce add‑ons and more room to grow.

Frequently asked questions

Can I switch from Weebly to one of these alternatives without losing my content?
Yes, but the process is not automatic. You will need to export your content from Weebly (text, images, and product info) and then import it into the new platform. Most builders offer import guides or support articles. Some providers, like Wix and Squarespace, also have migration services or apps to help you move data.
Which alternative has the lowest cost for a basic e‑commerce store?
All three have plans that start at similar price points, roughly 10 to 16 dollars per month when paid annually. WordPress.com’s Business plan (which includes WooCommerce) starts around 25 dollars per month. However, you can often find promotional discounts. For the lowest entry price, check the basic e‑commerce tiers of Wix and Squarespace, which include transaction fees that vary by plan.
