Best Salesforce CPQ alternatives

Salesforce CPQ (Configure, Price, Quote) is a popular choice for enterprises that already use Salesforce CRM. However, some organizations find its pricing model, complexity, or dependency on the Salesforce ecosystem limiting. If your enterprise needs a standalone solution, deeper product configuration, or more flexible subscription and usage-based pricing, exploring alternatives is a smart move. The tools below offer robust quoting and pricing automation with different strengths.

alternative

Apttus

(now part of Conga) provides a full quote-to-cash platform that integrates with Salesforce but also works independently. It excels at complex product configuration and contract lifecycle management, making it suitable for manufacturing and high-tech companies with intricate product catalogs.

alternative

Conga CPQ

(formerly known as Apttus CPQ after the merger) focuses on ease of use and rapid deployment. It offers strong document generation and approval workflows, and can be deployed on Salesforce or as a standalone solution for enterprises that want a cleaner user experience.

alternative

Oracle CPQ

is a mature, highly configurable platform built for large-scale deployments. It handles complex pricing logic, multi-tier selling, and global localization well, but requires significant upfront investment in implementation and training.

alternative

DealHub

(formerly DealHub CPQ) is a modern, cloud-native CPQ that emphasizes sales rep productivity and seamless integrations with CRM systems like Salesforce and HubSpot. It offers an intuitive guided selling interface and real-time pricing updates, making it a good fit for mid-market and enterprise teams that want a quick rollout.

alternative

Zuora CPQ

is built specifically for subscription and usage-based revenue models. It combines quoting with billing and revenue recognition, making it ideal for SaaS companies or any business that sells recurring services, metered usage, or hybrid offerings.

How they compare

All five alternatives offer configurable quoting and pricing automation, but their strengths differ. Apttus/Conga CPQ is best for complex product configs and document-centric workflows. Oracle CPQ handles enterprise-scale, multi-region deployments effectively but with higher complexity. DealHub offers speed of implementation and a modern UI. Zuora CPQ specializes in subscription and usage pricing while integrating billing. Salesforce CPQ remains strong for deep Salesforce-native environments, but these alternatives provide flexibility for enterprises that need independence from the CRM, simpler licensing, or specialized billing models.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a company with complex product families and many configurable options?
Apttus (Conga CPQ) and Oracle CPQ both handle complex product configuration well. Apttus is particularly strong for rule-based options and guided selling, while Oracle CPQ offers advanced constraint logic and multi-level BOM (bill of materials) support for manufacturing use cases.
Can these alternatives integrate with our existing CRM if we are not using Salesforce?
Yes. Most alternatives, including DealHub, Zuora CPQ, and Oracle CPQ, offer native integrations with Microsoft Dynamics 365, HubSpot, and other major CRMs. Conga CPQ also supports standalone deployment with API-based connections to any CRM, though some features may be tighter with Salesforce.
