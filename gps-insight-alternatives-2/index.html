Best GPS Insight alternatives

GPS Insight is a well known fleet tracking platform, but small fleets that want to avoid hardwiring often look for zero-install alternatives. Zero-install GPS devices plug into the vehicle’s OBD-II port or use a battery powered magnetic mount, so you can start tracking in minutes without a professional installer. This guide compares GPS Insight with three alternatives (Samsara, Verizon Connect, Geotab) and focuses on options that keep setup simple for small operations.

alternative

Samsara

offers a zero-install option with its OBD-II plug and play device that works for most light duty vehicles. The device reports location, engine data, and fault codes as soon as you plug it in, and Samsara’s mobile app lets you manage everything from a phone. It is a strong fit for small fleets that want a modern interface and automatic driver workflow tools.

alternative

Verizon Connect

provides the Reveal line of trackers including an OBD-II plug and play model. Their zero-install device sends real time location and basic diagnostics without any wiring, and the platform includes route replay and alerts. Verizon Connect can be more complex for very small fleets, but the plug and play hardware keeps initial setup fast.

alternative

Geotab

offers a GO device with an OBD-II version that requires no installation beyond plugging it in. The device captures detailed engine data and integrates with many add on apps, but some small fleets find the platform’s depth overwhelming. Geotab’s strength is its data richness, though the zero-install option works best for vehicles that support OBD-II connectivity.

How they compare

All three alternatives offer a zero-install OBD-II device for small fleets, but they differ in ease of use and cost. Samsara has the simplest mobile first experience and a flat fee per vehicle that includes all features. Verizon Connect’s plug and play device is reliable but its pricing often includes a long term contract and per feature add ons. Geotab’s hardware is affordable, but its comprehensive platform may require more time to learn for a small fleet that just wants basic tracking. GPS Insight itself also offers an OBD-II device, but its interface is older and less intuitive for new users.

Frequently asked questions

What does “zero-install” mean for a GPS tracker?
Zero-install means the GPS device does not need to be hardwired into the vehicle’s electrical system. It typically plugs into the OBD-II port under the dashboard or uses a magnetic mount with internal battery power. You can set it up in minutes without tools or a professional installer.
Will a zero-install OBD-II device work in any vehicle for a small fleet?
OBD-II ports are standard on most gasoline vehicles made after 1996 and diesel vehicles after 2008, but some older or heavy duty trucks may not have a compatible port. Check your vehicle’s model year and consult the device provider’s compatibility list before purchasing. Battery powered magnetic devices are an alternative for vehicles without OBD-II ports.
