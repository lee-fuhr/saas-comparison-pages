If you are a SaaS company looking for a better way to edit contracts collaboratively, you have likely evaluated Juro. Juro offers a modern interface with in-browser editing and automated workflows. But it may not fit every team’s budget, feature set, or scaling needs. Below we explore three strong alternatives that also prioritize real time collaboration for contract creation and negotiation.

alternative

Ironclad

. Ironclad is built for legal teams that need to manage high volumes of contracts with granular approval workflows. Its collaborative editing works through a wysiwyg editor and allows multiple stakeholders to comment and suggest changes within a centralized repository. It is a good fit if you already have complex approval chains and require deep integration with Salesforce or Slack.

alternative

Conga

. Conga offers robust document generation and redlining capabilities, making it easier for SaaS teams to produce and negotiate contracts from templates. Its collaborative editing features let you track changes and comments side by side, and it integrates well with CRM and ERP systems. Choose Conga if your main pain point is automating contract creation from your existing data sources.

alternative

Contractbook

. Contractbook combines a simple drag and drop template builder with real time collaboration for editing. It is designed for smaller teams that want a straightforward interface without heavy customization. The platform supports inline comments and version history, and it is a good alternative if you need to manage the entire contract lifecycle without a steep learning curve.

How they compare

All three tools support collaborative contract editing, but they differ in complexity and integration depth. Ironclad is the most feature rich for enterprise workflows and compliance, while Contractbook is the easiest to adopt for smaller teams. Conga sits between them, excelling at document generation from existing data. Juro itself is strongest for in browser editing and automation, but these alternatives offer similar collaboration with different trade offs in pricing and deployment.

Frequently asked questions

Can I migrate existing contracts from Juro to these alternatives?
Yes, most alternatives support importing contracts via CSV, API, or manual upload. However, metadata and custom fields may need to be remapped. Check each tool’s migration guide or ask their support team for a data transfer plan.
Do these alternatives offer offline editing for contracts?
Not natively. All three tools are cloud based and require an internet connection for real time collaboration. Some allow you to download documents as Word or PDF files for offline review, but changes must be re uploaded to be tracked.
