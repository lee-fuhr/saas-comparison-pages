HR leaders often face a choice between a comprehensive OKR platform and a lightweight engagement suite. Betterworks focuses on strategic goal alignment and performance management at scale. 15Five emphasizes continuous feedback, pulse surveys, and manager coaching. This comparison helps you decide which approach matches your organization’s culture and operational needs.

alternative

15Five

is a performance management and employee engagement solution centered on weekly check-ins, recognition, and culture surveys. It prioritizes ease of use and manager development over complex goal cascading. This works well for teams that want to improve communication and morale without a heavy administrative lift.

How they compare

Betterworks is designed for deep OKR management with features like multi-level goal alignment, executive dashboards, and analytics for strategic planning. 15Five offers simpler goal support but excels at lightweight check-ins, engagement surveys, and high-frequency feedback. If your priority is rigorous goal execution across a large organization, choose Betterworks. If you want to boost manager-employee conversations and culture with minimal setup, choose 15Five. Both tools can support performance reviews, but Betterworks is more structured for process-driven environments.

Frequently asked questions

Which platform is better for a company that wants to implement OKRs for the first time?
Betterworks is purpose-built for OKR management and provides templates, alignment views, and progress tracking to guide new users. 15Five can support OKRs but is less focused on goal cascading; it works best when you already have a simple goal practice and want to add continuous feedback.
Can 15Five replace an OKR platform if engagement is our main focus?
Yes, if your primary goal is improving culture, communication, and manager coaching, 15Five is a strong choice. However, if you need rigorous OKR reporting, cross-team alignment, and integration with performance reviews, Betterworks is more suitable for those strategic needs.
