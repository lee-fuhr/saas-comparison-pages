FullStory is a powerful session recording and analytics tool, but many UX and product teams start looking for alternatives when they need a different price point, simpler setup, or specific features like heatmaps or feedback widgets. Whether you are on a tight budget or want a tool that integrates more closely with your existing stack, the market offers several strong options. Below we review five common FullStory alternatives to help you compare them on key criteria.

alternative

Hotjar

. Hotjar combines session recordings with heatmaps, surveys, and inbound feedback widgets in one platform. It is often chosen for its ease of use and all-in-one approach, making it a good fit for smaller teams that want both qualitative and quantitative insights without a steep learning curve.

Try Hotjar →
alternative

Mouseflow

. Mouseflow offers session replay, heatmaps, form analytics, and funnel analysis with a focus on ecommerce and landing page optimization. It provides robust filtering and tagging options, and its pricing is typically lower than FullStory for similar recording volumes, which appeals to budget-conscious teams.

Try Mouseflow →
alternative

LogRocket

. LogRocket is built for frontend debugging and performance monitoring alongside session replay. It captures console logs, network errors, and JavaScript exceptions, making it a strong choice for development teams who need to reproduce bugs from real user sessions. UX teams may find its technical focus less intuitive than FullStory.

Try LogRocket →
alternative

Clarity

. Clarity is a free session recording and heatmap tool from Microsoft. It offers unlimited recordings with basic filters and anonymization features. While it lacks advanced analytics and integrations, it is an excellent entry-level option for teams that want to get started with session replay at no cost.

alternative

SessionCam

. SessionCam emphasizes behavioral analytics with session replay, heatmaps, and form analytics, and it includes features like customer journey mapping and fraud detection. It suits larger enterprises that need compliance-friendly recording (e.g., PCI DSS) and deeper analysis of user flows, though the interface can feel dated compared to FullStory.

How they compare

All five alternatives cover core session replay and heatmaps, but they differ in focus and complexity. Hotjar and Mouseflow are the most direct all-in-one replacements for FullStory, with similar ease of use and lower entry prices. LogRocket stands out for technical debugging, Clarity for zero cost, and SessionCam for enterprise compliance and journey mapping. FullStory’s advantage is its advanced analytics (e.g., rage clicks, dead clicks) and integration ecosystem, so teams that rely heavily on those features may find the alternatives lacking in those specific areas.

Frequently asked questions

When should I choose a free alternative like Clarity over a paid tool?
If your team is just starting with session recording and has basic needs (heatmaps, seeing where users click or scroll), Clarity is a solid no-risk option. However, it lacks advanced filtering, integrations, and support, so growing teams often outgrow it quickly.
Which alternative is best if I need to reproduce frontend bugs from session replays?
LogRocket is the strongest choice for debugging because it captures console logs, network requests, and stack traces alongside the screen recording. This makes it easier for developers to pinpoint errors without relying on separate tools.
