Best Maze alternatives

Maze is a popular tool for rapid prototype testing, but it may not fit every team’s budget, workflow, or feature needs. As a product designer, you need alternatives that let you run unmoderated tests, gather quick feedback, and iterate fast. Below we compare five strong Maze alternatives: UserTesting, Lookback, Optimal Workshop, Useberry, and Ballpark. Each has different strengths for prototype testing, so you can pick the one that matches your team’s process.

alternative

UserTesting

offers a large panel of testers and advanced targeting options. It works well for both moderated and unmoderated prototype tests, giving you video recordings and detailed analytics. However, it is more expensive than Maze and may be overkill for quick internal validation.

alternative

Lookback

focuses on live moderated sessions and recorded interviews, making it great for deep qualitative insights. You can run prototype tests with your own participants and get rich video feedback. It is less suited for automated unmoderated tests at scale compared to Maze.

Try Lookback →
alternative

Optimal Workshop

specializes in information architecture and task-based testing with tools like Treejack and Chalkmark. It is ideal for testing navigation and findability in prototypes. The platform is less focused on visual prototype interactions than Maze and may require more setup.

alternative

Useberry

is a direct competitor to Maze with features for prototype testing, user recordings, and heatmaps. It supports Figma and Sketch integration and offers clear dashboards for rapid analysis. Useberry can be a cost effective alternative for teams that want Maze like functionality with a different pricing model.

alternative

Ballpark

is a lightweight tool for testing prototypes without sign up barriers. It provides simple feedback loops with recorded sessions and annotations. Ballpark is best for quick, informal tests with internal stakeholders or small user groups, but lacks the advanced segmentation and analytics of Maze.

How they compare

If you need a large participant panel and detailed video analysis, choose UserTesting. For deep qualitative research with live sessions, Lookback is a strong choice. Optimal Workshop works best for information architecture and task flow validation. Useberry offers a feature set closest to Maze with a similar focus on unmoderated prototype testing. Ballpark is the simplest option for fast internal feedback. All alternatives vary in cost, participant sourcing, and moderation style, so match your choice to the type of rapid test you run most often.
