UX teams often choose between Hotjar and FullStory when they need session recording and heatmaps. Both tools reveal how users behave on a website, but they take different approaches to data capture, privacy, and analysis. This article compares Hotjar versus FullStory so you can decide which fits your team’s workflow and budget.

Where Hotjar wins

Hotjar is easier to set up and more accessible for smaller teams or individual UX researchers. Its dashboard is straightforward: you add a snippet of code, and within minutes you see recordings and heatmaps. Hotjar’s pricing model is also more forgiving for smaller sites. It offers a free tier (with limited recordings) and lower-cost paid plans, which makes it a natural choice when you want to start quickly without a big commitment.

For privacy compliance, Hotjar gives you built-in controls to mask sensitive fields or entire pages. This is helpful when you need to comply with GDPR or other regulations but do not want to write custom code. Hotjar also includes feedback widgets (surveys and polls) alongside recordings and heatmaps, so you can gather both behavioral and attitudinal data in one tool. If your team values simplicity and immediate insights, Hotjar wins on ease of use.

Where FullStory wins

FullStory shines when your team needs deep, searchable session data at scale. Instead of browsing random recordings, you can query every session using a search language (e.g., find all sessions where a user clicked a button but did not complete a form). This turns session replay into a diagnostic tool for complex UX issues. Hotjar offers basic filtering, but FullStory’s search is far more powerful.

FullStory also excels in data quality and accuracy. Its “rage clicks” and “dead clicks” detection helps you spot frustrated users automatically. The heatmaps are rendered from every session, not a sample, so you see a complete picture of where users click, scroll, and hover. For enterprise teams handling millions of sessions, FullStory’s infrastructure handles large volumes without slowing down. If your UX team needs to answer specific “why” questions by looking at the exact moment a user struggled, FullStory’s recall and precision give it the edge.

Verdict

Choose Hotjar versus FullStory based on your team size and analytics needs. Hotjar is the friendlier option for small to mid-sized teams that want quick heatmaps, recordings, and feedback tools without a steep learning curve. FullStory is the better fit for larger UX teams that need to search across thousands of sessions, surface subtle user friction, and integrate deeply with product analytics. There is no single winner for every team. Hotjar wins on simplicity and lower cost. FullStory wins on depth, searchability, and scale. Test both with a free trial to see which workflow matches your team’s everyday research.
