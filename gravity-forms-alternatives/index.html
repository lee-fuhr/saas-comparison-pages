Best Gravity Forms alternatives

Gravity Forms is a well known form builder for WordPress used by many product teams. However, as your product scales or your team’s requirements shift, you might need a tool that offers better collaboration features, simpler design options, or lower ongoing costs. This article reviews four popular alternatives – Typeform, JotForm, Ninja Forms, and WPForms – through the lens of a product manager evaluating long term fit.

alternative

Typeform

. Typeform focuses on creating conversational, one question at a time forms that feel like a natural dialogue. It is ideal for product managers who need polished user surveys, feedback forms, or lead generation flows where completion rate matters. The tool offers strong integrations with analytics and CRM platforms but can become expensive for high volume submissions.

alternative

JotForm

. JotForm provides a drag and drop builder with thousands of templates and a large library of widgets and integrations. It works well for product teams that need to build complex forms quickly without coding, and it supports features like conditional logic, eSignatures, and payment collection. The free plan includes generous limits, but advanced features and higher submission caps require paid plans.

alternative

Ninja Forms

. Ninja Forms is a lightweight WordPress plugin that gives you control over every form element through add ons. It suits product managers who want a simple, modular tool that integrates natively with WordPress and supports custom development. The basic plugin is free, but many useful features like file uploads and multi step forms require separate paid add ons.

alternative

WPForms

. WPForms is a beginner friendly WordPress form plugin that emphasizes ease of use with a visual builder and pre built templates. It is a good fit for product teams that value speed of setup and need reliable spam protection, entry management, and basic payment processing. The premium plans unlock more advanced features like geolocation and user journey tracking.

How they compare

When comparing these alternatives, product managers should weigh factors like platform dependency, pricing model, and user experience. Typeform shines in design and engagement but is costlier per submission. JotForm offers the widest feature set outside of WordPress. Ninja Forms gives granular control for WordPress only sites but requires add on management. WPForms is the simplest to deploy inside WordPress, though its advanced capabilities are locked behind higher tier plans. None of these tools replicate Gravity Forms’ exact balance of power and flexibility, so your choice depends on whether your team prioritizes ease of use, scalability, or integration depth.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a product team that needs to collect data from hundreds of users each month?
JotForm or WPForms are strong choices. JotForm offers a free plan with 100 monthly submissions and many integrations, while WPForms’ Pro plan supports unlimited submissions within WordPress at a lower annual cost than Typeform. Evaluate the submission limits on the free or entry level paid plans to match your expected volume.
Can I migrate existing Gravity Forms entries to any of these alternatives?
Yes, most of these tools offer import features. Typeform and JotForm accept CSV or JSON files, and WPForms has a dedicated Gravity Forms importer in its premium plugin. Ninja Forms provides a similar import option with an add on. Plan for a cleanup phase because field mapping may not be one to one across different form builders.
