If you run a small business and want to build a marketplace where others can sell their products or services, Kreezalid is a popular choice. But it’s not the only option, and its pricing or features might not fit every budget or skill level. This article covers seven affordable alternatives so you can compare costs, ease of use, and key capabilities before you commit.

alternative

Arcadier

. Arcadier is a cloud based marketplace builder that lets you launch a multi vendor store quickly with drag and drop tools. Its pricing starts low and includes built in payment processing, making it a good fit for budget conscious small businesses that need a simple, ready to go platform.

alternative

Sharetribe

. Sharetribe offers both a no code marketplace builder and a more flexible developer version. The no code plan is ideal if you want to test a peer to peer rental or service marketplace without hiring a developer, and it includes transaction fees that can fit a small budget.

alternative

Near Me

. Near Me specializes in location based marketplaces for local services, rentals, and events. It provides a customizable template with Google Maps integration, which helps small businesses that rely on geographic proximity to connect buyers and sellers in their area.

alternative

Marketplacer

. Marketplacer is a platform aimed at retailers and brands that want to create a marketplace alongside their existing e-commerce store. It handles product data, vendor onboarding, and commission management, making it a strong choice if you already run an online shop and want to expand.

alternative

Mirakl

. Mirakl is an enterprise grade marketplace solution, but it also offers a smaller “Mirakl Connect” plan for businesses with fewer products. It comes with robust catalog management and seller analytics, though it may be more complex and pricier than other options here.

alternative

Yo!Kart

. Yo!Kart is a self hosted marketplace software that gives you full control over your store’s code and data. You pay a one time license fee instead of monthly subscriptions, which can be more affordable over time if you have some technical know how or can hire a developer to set it up.

alternative

SpurtCommerce

. SpurtCommerce is an open source marketplace platform built on Node.js. It is highly customizable and free to use (you pay only for hosting and support), making it a great fit for small businesses with basic coding skills who want to avoid recurring software fees.

How they compare

All seven alternatives let you start a multi vendor marketplace, but they differ in pricing models and technical requirements. Arcadier and Sharetribe are the easiest for non technical users, with low monthly fees and minimal setup. Near Me is best for location based services. Marketplacer and Mirakl work well if you already have an online store and need deeper integration. Yo!Kart and SpurtCommerce require more technical effort but offer lower long term costs and full ownership of your data.
