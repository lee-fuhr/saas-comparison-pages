If your organization prefers an open source metadata platform with an extensible schema, DataHub is likely your top choice. It gives you full control over your data catalog, letting you define custom entity types and relationships without proprietary limits. Alation, on the other hand, is a commercial tool that offers a polished user experience but locks you into its own schema and licensing model. This article compares DataHub and Alation for teams that value flexibility and openness.

alternative

Alation

. Alation is a closed source metadata platform known for its strong data governance features and intuitive search. It uses a proprietary schema that you cannot extend freely, making it less suitable for teams that need custom metadata models.

alternative

Collibra

. Collibra is an enterprise data intelligence platform with robust governance workflows and a data marketplace. Its schema is extensible to some degree, but the platform is commercial and can be expensive, and it does not offer the same level of open source flexibility as DataHub.

alternative

Atlan

. Atlan is a modern data catalog built on a cloud native architecture with a rich user interface and built in collaboration tools. While it supports custom attributes, its schema extensibility is limited compared to DataHub, and it is not open source.

alternative

DataHub

. DataHub is an open source metadata platform originally developed at LinkedIn and now maintained by Acryl Data. It allows you to define custom entity types, relationships, and aspects through a JSON schema, making it highly extensible for any data environment.

alternative

Secoda

. Secoda is a lightweight data catalog that focuses on simplicity and ease of use, with automated metadata extraction from common tools. It offers some schema customization but is not open source and has a narrower range of extensions than DataHub.

How they compare

DataHub and Alation serve similar core purposes, but they differ in philosophy and cost. DataHub is open source, so you pay only for infrastructure and optional support, while Alation requires a paid license. For extensibility, DataHub wins: you can model any metadata structure using its aspect oriented schema, whereas Alation forces you to work within its predefined categories and fields. DataHub also integrates with modern data stacks like Airflow, Kafka, and dbt through a community driven ecosystem. Alation offers stronger out of the box governance workflows and a more polished UI, but at the expense of flexibility. If your priority is an open, extensible schema with no vendor lock in, DataHub is the clear choice. If you prefer a hands on, ready to use governance tool with less customization, Alation may be worth the cost.
