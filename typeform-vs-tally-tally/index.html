As a product manager, you often need to collect feedback, run surveys, or manage sign ups. Two popular form builders are Tally and Typeform. This article compares them so you can choose the right tool for your team. We focus on Tally as the primary option, while Typeform is a well known alternative. Both tools let you create forms without code, but they differ in pricing, design flexibility, and integrations.

alternative

Typeform

. Typeform is known for its conversational, one question at a time layout. It provides beautiful branding options and advanced logic for branching. However, its free plan is limited to 100 responses per month and fewer features. Typeform is a strong choice if your priority is a polished, interactive user experience.

How they compare

- **Pricing**: Tally is more affordable, with a robust free tier and lower paid plans. Typeform’s paid plans start higher and cap responses on free accounts. - **Form style**: Tally uses a traditional scrollable form. Typeform uses a slide by slide, conversational format. - **Customization**: Both allow custom CSS and branding. Tally gives more control over layout in its editor. Typeform’s design is more focused on its signature look. - **Logic and calculations**: Tally supports conditional logic, calculations, and hidden fields. Typeform also has logic but lacks built in calculations. - **Integrations**: Tally integrates natively with spreadsheets and databases. Typeform connects with many apps via Zapier, but native integrations are fewer on lower tiers. - **Data export**: Both offer CSV and PDF exports. Tally also provides a live response view and webhook options. - **Best fit**: Tally suits product teams that need unlimited data collection on a budget. Typeform fits teams that want a highly engaging, brand forward survey tool.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Tally and Typeform for free without limits?
Tally’s free plan includes unlimited forms, responses, and logic rules. Typeform’s free plan limits you to 10 forms and 100 responses per month, and it includes Typeform branding.
Which tool is better for embedding forms inside a web app?
Tally is easier to embed because its forms load quickly and work well within iframes. Typeform also supports embedding, but its conversational layout can feel less integrated in a continuous page.
