Creately is a popular diagramming tool, but small teams on a budget may find its pricing or feature set doesn’t quite fit. If you need an affordable way to create flowcharts, wireframes, or mind maps, there are several solid alternatives. This article covers four tools that offer strong diagramming capabilities without breaking the bank.

alternative

Miro

. Miro is a versatile online whiteboard that handles diagrams, brainstorming, and project planning. It offers a generous free plan with unlimited team members and several diagramming templates, though advanced features like private boards require a paid subscription.

alternative

Lucidchart

. Lucidchart is a dedicated diagramming tool with deep integration options for Google Workspace, Confluence, and Jira. Its single-user pro plan is reasonably priced, but team plans can add up. The free version limits you to three active documents.

alternative

Draw.io

. Draw.io (now diagrams.net) is completely free and open source, with no user limits or document caps. It works offline as a desktop app or directly in your browser, and it integrates with Google Drive, OneDrive, and GitHub for easy file storage.

alternative

Cacoo

. Cacoo is a cloud-based diagramming tool from Nulab that offers a flat monthly rate for unlimited diagrams. Its free plan includes six sheets and basic templates, while paid plans start with unlimited sheets and real-time collaboration.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is the most affordable for a team of five?
Draw.io is completely free with no restrictions, so it costs nothing. Cacoo’s paid plan starts around $5 per user per month, which is also budget friendly for a small team.
Can I import my existing diagrams from Creately into these tools?
Most tools support common file formats like .vsdx, .png, or .svg. Creately exports to images and PDFs, so you may need to re create complex diagrams manually. Check each tool’s import options, as Draw.io and Lucidchart have broader file support.
