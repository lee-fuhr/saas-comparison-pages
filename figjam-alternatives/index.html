Best FigJam alternatives

FigJam is Figma’s collaborative whiteboard for brainstorming, diagramming and team workshops. While it works well inside the Figma ecosystem, you might look for alternatives if you need more advanced facilitation features, better integrations with non‑Figma tools or different pricing. Here we compare three strong options: Miro, Mural and Whimsical.

alternative

Miro

. Miro is a full‑featured digital whiteboard designed for agile teams, workshops and product development. It offers hundreds of templates, sticky notes, diagrams and voting tools, plus deep integrations with Jira, Confluence and Slack. The free plan is generous with unlimited boards but limited private boards.

alternative

Mural

. Mural focuses on structured facilitation with built‑in frameworks for design sprints, retrospectives and brainstorming sessions. It includes timers, voting and private mode for asynchronous work, and integrates with Microsoft Teams, Slack and Figma itself. Mural’s pricing is per member and often preferred by teams running highly guided workshops.

alternative

Whimsical

. Whimsical is a lightweight tool for quick diagrams, flowcharts and wireframes. It is simpler than Miro or Mural, with a clean interface and real‑time collaboration. Best for teams that need fast visual communication rather than full workshop facilitation, Whimsical also offers a generous free tier for small projects.

How they compare

Miro is the most versatile alternative with the broadest feature set and integrations. Mural excels at structured facilitation and guided workshops. Whimsical is the simplest option for quick diagrams and early‑stage brainstorming. Your choice depends on whether you need depth of facilitation features, extensive integrations or just a fast, no‑fuss whiteboard.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for a team that already uses Figma heavily?
Mural has a direct Figma integration and is built for design workflows, while Whimsical’s simplicity may appeal for quick diagrams. Miro also connects to Figma but is a broader platform. Try Mural if you want workshop facilitation, or Whimsical for speed.
Are there free alternatives to FigJam?
Yes. Miro offers a free plan with unlimited boards but limited private boards. Mural’s free plan includes five murals and basic features. Whimsical provides a generous free tier with up to 100 boards and limited shapes. All three have free trials for paid features.
