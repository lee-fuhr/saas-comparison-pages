Training users on complex software often requires more than static manuals or videos. WalkMe is a popular choice for interactive guidance and in-app onboarding, but it may not suit every organization’s budget, integration needs, or feature preferences. This article explores four alternatives: Whatfix, Spekit, Guru, and UserGuiding. Each offers unique strengths for helping employees learn and adopt software directly inside the application.

alternative

Whatfix

provides interactive walkthroughs, product tours, and on-demand self-help widgets. It also offers detailed analytics to track user behavior and identify where training gaps exist. This makes it a strong choice for enterprises that need deep customization and scalability.

alternative

Spekit

combines in-app guidance with a centralized knowledge base that surfaces answers contextually within the software. It works particularly well for Salesforce, Slack, and other cloud tools, letting teams create and update training content without developer help. Its focus on just-in-time learning reduces time spent in formal training sessions.

alternative

Guru

integrates a knowledge management platform with browser extensions and in-app cards. Users can access verified company information, policies, and process steps without leaving their workflow. It is ideal for organizations that prioritize keeping training content current and aligned with real team expertise.

alternative

UserGuiding

offers interactive onboarding flows, checklists, and tooltips without requiring coding. It is designed for SaaS companies that want to reduce user drop-off and guide new users through key features quickly. Its simple setup and affordable pricing make it popular among small to mid-sized teams.

How they compare

Whatfix is best for large enterprises that need advanced analytics and extensive customization. Spekit excels in Salesforce-heavy environments with its contextual knowledge cards. Guru focuses on combining training with a living knowledge base, making it suitable for dynamic teams. UserGuiding is the most lightweight option, ideal for product-led growth teams that want quick in-app onboarding without complexity. Each tool offers a free trial or demo, so testing two or three against your specific software and training goals is recommended.
