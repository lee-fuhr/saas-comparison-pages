DocuSign vs HelloSign: which should you pick?

DocuSign and HelloSign are two of the most popular electronic signature platforms for businesses. DocuSign is the older, more established player, while HelloSign (owned by Dropbox) focuses on simplicity and speed. Both let you send, sign, and manage documents online, but they serve different types of users. This article breaks down DocuSign versus HelloSign so you can decide which one fits your company’s exact needs.

Where DocuSign wins

DocuSign wins when you need enterprise-level features and compliance. It offers a wider range of signature workflows, such as bulk sending, in-person signing, and advanced routing. Its dashboard supports complex approval chains and conditional logic for forms. DocuSign also integrates with hundreds of business tools like Salesforce, Microsoft 365, and Oracle, often with deeper API access than HelloSign. For regulated industries like healthcare, finance, or government, DocuSign provides stronger compliance certifications, including HIPAA, FedRAMP, and eIDAS. The platform includes more granular audit trails and identity verification options, like phone or knowledge-based authentication. If your business handles high volumes of contracts or needs custom branding at scale, DocuSign’s enterprise tier delivers more control.

Where HelloSign wins

HelloSign wins on simplicity and affordability. Its interface is cleaner and easier to learn, making it ideal for small teams or non-technical users. You can upload a document, drag signature fields, and send it in under a minute. HelloSign also offers a free plan with up to three signatures per month, while DocuSign’s free tier is more limited. Pricing for paid plans is lower than DocuSign’s, especially for smaller teams. HelloSign integrates seamlessly with Dropbox and Google Workspace, and its mobile app is praised for being fast and intuitive. For businesses that just need a straightforward way to collect signatures without complex setup, HelloSign versus DocuSign comes down to a simpler, cheaper experience. HelloSign also includes reusable templates and an API for developers, though the API is less complex than DocuSign’s.

Verdict

Choose DocuSign if your business is large, handles sensitive data, or needs advanced workflow automation and compliance. It is the safer bet for regulated industries and companies that already rely on a deep tech stack. Choose HelloSign if you want a lower cost, less friction, and a tool that your team can start using today without training. For most small to midsize businesses, HelloSign provides everything needed at a fraction of the price. But if you anticipate scaling to complex contract management, DocuSign will grow with you. Ultimately, the choice between DocuSign and HelloSign comes down to balancing power versus simplicity.
