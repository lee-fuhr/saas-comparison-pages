Best Miro alternatives

If your remote team relies on Miro for whiteboarding and brainstorming, you already know its strengths: endless canvases, sticky notes, and templates. But maybe you are looking for a lighter tool, a more affordable option, or something that integrates better with specific software. Here are five Miro alternatives that can handle real-time collaboration, from simple sketching to complex facilitation.

alternative

FigJam

. FigJam is Figma’s whiteboarding tool, built for teams that already use Figma for design. It keeps the same real-time collaboration and familiar pen-and-sticky-note feel, with widgets like timers and voting to keep brainstorming on track. Its tight integration with Figma makes prototyping and handoff seamless for product teams.

Try FigJam →
alternative

Mural

. Mural focuses on structured facilitation and workshop planning, with built-in frameworks like journey maps, retrospectives, and design sprints. It offers guided sessions with timers and private modes, which helps remote teams stay focused during synchronous meetings. Mural also has strong security and compliance features for enterprise users.

Try Mural →
alternative

Lucidspark

. Lucidspark works well for teams that want a clean, infinite canvas with smart diagramming features and easy import of Lucidchart diagrams. It includes built-in voting, sticky note clustering, and breakout features for larger team sessions. Its integration with Google Workspace and Slack makes it a natural fit for teams already using those tools.

alternative

Whimsical

. Whimsical emphasizes speed and simplicity, letting you quickly create flowcharts, wireframes, and mind maps with drag-and-drop controls. It has a minimalist interface that feels less overwhelming than larger whiteboard tools, which is great for quick brainstorming without extra layers. Real-time collaboration is smooth, and it offers a generous free tier for small teams.

Try Whimsical →
alternative

Excalidraw

. Excalidraw is a free, open source whiteboard tool that focuses on hand-drawn style diagrams and sticky notes. It runs entirely in the browser with no signup required, making it ideal for spontaneous sketching and quick collaboration. While it lacks advanced features like templates and integrations, its simplicity and privacy (end-to-end encryption available) appeal to teams who want no-frills brainstorming.

How they compare

- **Best for design teams**: FigJam (if you already use Figma) or Whimsical (for fast, simple diagrams). - **Best for structured workshops**: Mural (guided facilitation) or Lucidspark (diagramming + brainstorming). - **Best for quick, ad hoc sessions**: Excalidraw (free, no login) or Whimsical (lightweight). - **Pricing**: Excalidraw is free. Whimsical and FigJam have free tiers with limits. Lucidspark and Mural are subscription based with free trials. - **Key trade off**: More features (Mural, Lucidspark) often mean more complexity, while simpler tools (Excalidraw, Whimsical) sacrifice advanced functionality for speed.

Frequently asked questions

Can I import my Miro boards into these alternatives?
Most alternatives support importing from Miro, but the process varies. FigJam and Mural offer direct import options for sticky notes and shapes. Lucidspark can import via CSV or image export. Excalidraw and Whimsical do not have native Miro import, so you would need to copy content manually or use screenshots.
Which alternative works best for very large teams with many concurrent users?
Mural and Lucidspark are built for enterprise scale and handle dozens of simultaneous editors well, with moderation tools like locking and private modes. FigJam also supports large sessions but may slow down on very complex boards. Excalidraw and Whimsical work best with small to medium teams (under 20 people) due to performance limits.
