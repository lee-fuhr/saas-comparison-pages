If you are a creative looking for a drag and drop builder with an extensive template library, you have likely used Wix. Wix is a solid choice for many, but it might not fit every project or personal preference. Perhaps you want stronger design control, cleaner templates, or different pricing. Here are three strong Wix alternatives that cater to creatives: Squarespace, Webflow, and Weebly.

alternative

Squarespace

is known for its award winning, modern templates that look polished out of the box. Its drag and drop editor is more structured than Wix, which helps maintain consistent design across pages. If you value a refined, minimalist aesthetic for portfolios or online stores, Squarespace is a top pick.

Try Squarespace →
alternative

Webflow

offers true design freedom with a visual drag and drop interface that outputs clean, semantic code. Unlike Wix, Webflow gives you pixel level control over every element, making it ideal for creatives who want custom animations and responsive layouts. However, it has a steeper learning curve than Wix.

Try Webflow →
alternative

Weebly

provides an easy drag and drop experience with a strong focus on simplicity and ecommerce. Its template library is smaller than Wix’s, but the builder is very intuitive for beginners or quick projects. Weebly works well if you need a straightforward site without sacrificing core design options.

Try Weebly →

How they compare

Squarespace excels in template aesthetics and cohesive design but offers less drag and drop freedom than Wix. Webflow gives creatives maximum customization and code control but requires more time to learn. Weebly is the simplest to use and great for ecommerce, yet it has fewer templates and design features. Wix balances ease of use with a vast template library, while these alternatives each specialize in a different creative need.

Frequently asked questions

Can I move my existing Wix site to Squarespace or Webflow?
Yes, but the process is not automatic. You will need to manually copy your content and rebuild the layout because Wix does not allow full site export. For Webflow you can import some content via CSV, but design work must be redone.
Which alternative is best for a beginner who wants to design a modern portfolio?
Squarespace is the best choice for beginners who want a beautiful portfolio with minimal effort. Its templates are very professional and the editor is guided, so you won’t get overwhelmed like you might with Webflow.
