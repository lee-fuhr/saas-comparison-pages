If you run a mid-sized HVAC or plumbing company with 50 to 200 employees, you have likely outgrown Jobber. Jobber is a great starting point for smaller teams, but as you add more dispatchers, technicians, and service lines, its limits become clear. You may struggle with advanced dispatch, inventory across multiple trucks, or deep reporting. Moving to a more powerful field service software can centralize scheduling, integrate accounting, and give you the control you need to scale without adding administrative chaos. This article compares three proven alternatives to Jobber: ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, and FieldEdge.

alternative

ServiceTitan

is the most comprehensive platform for large field service operations, built specifically for companies with complex workflows and multiple revenue streams. It handles advanced dispatch, real-time inventory for truck stock, integrated marketing, and deep financial reporting. The learning curve and price are higher, but it removes the manual work and software sprawl that slows down a growing business.

alternative

Housecall Pro

offers a more user-friendly middle ground between Jobber and enterprise-level tools, making it a solid step up for companies that want more power without a complete overhaul. It adds features like two-way customer messaging, branded customer portals, and basic inventory tracking, but its dispatch and reporting capabilities may still feel limited for a 100-plus technician operation.

alternative

FieldEdge

(now part of the WorkWave suite) is a mature system that shines with robust dispatch boards, GPS tracking, and detailed job costing. It works best for companies that need strong operational control and do not want a purely mobile-first app. The interface can feel dated, and the transition from Jobber will require dedicated training, but the reporting and backend stability are well suited for mid-sized fleets.

How they compare

ServiceTitan is the most powerful option for companies with 100-plus employees and complex needs like multi-location dispatch, inventory, and integrated marketing. Housecall Pro is the easiest to adopt from Jobber but may need add-ons for real scale. FieldEdge provides strong operational controls and reporting for managers who prefer a desktop-heavy workflow. All three offer integrations with QuickBooks and common payment processors. Your choice depends on whether you value ease of transition, depth of features, or control over daily dispatch.

Frequently asked questions

How painful is the data migration from Jobber to a new system?
Migration is rarely seamless, but all three alternatives offer paid onboarding and data transfer services. You can expect a few weeks of parallel running, especially for historical job history, customer records, and inventory. Plan for your team to train on the new platform while still using Jobber for a short overlap period.
Which alternative is best for a company with 80 technicians and 12 dispatchers?
ServiceTitan is the strongest fit for complex dispatch routing and real-time technician tracking at that scale. Housecall Pro can work if your dispatch needs are simple, but you will hit limits with multiple overlapping zones. FieldEdge is a good choice if your managers want detailed scheduling boards and job costing on desktop screens.
