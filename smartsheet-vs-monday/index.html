If you are a project manager choosing between a spreadsheet-style platform and a visual board tool, Smartsheet and Monday.com are two of the most popular options. Smartsheet gives you a familiar grid interface that feels like a spreadsheet but adds collaboration, automation, and reporting. Monday.com, on the other hand, centers on visual boards, timelines, and colorful cards. Your decision often comes down to how your team works: do you need structured data management and formulas, or do you prefer a drag-and-drop visual workflow? This article compares Smartsheet as the primary choice and Monday.com as the leading alternative.

alternative

Monday.com

. Monday.com offers a highly visual, color-coded board system that makes project status, deadlines, and dependencies easy to see at a glance. It is designed for teams that want quick setup, intuitive drag-and-drop task management, and flexible views like Gantt, calendar, and timeline. While it lacks the deep spreadsheet functionality of Smartsheet, it excels in team adoption and real-time collaboration for less data-intensive projects.

How they compare

Smartsheet is the stronger choice for project managers who need robust data handling, detailed formulas, and a spreadsheet-like structure for complex project plans, budgets, or resource tracking. Its grid view mirrors Excel, and features like cell linking, cross-sheet references, and conditional formatting give you powerful control over your data. Monday.com is better suited for teams that prioritize visual clarity, speed of onboarding, and lightweight task management. Its boards, columns, and automations are easier to set up without training, but it becomes less effective when you need to manage detailed row-level data or advanced calculations. Both tools offer integrations, automation, and reporting, but Smartsheet leans toward data depth while Monday.com leans toward visual simplicity.

Frequently asked questions

Which tool is better for managing detailed project budgets and resource allocation?
Smartsheet is better for budgets and resource allocation because it supports formulas, cell references, and multiple sheet relationships. You can build a living budget that updates automatically when data changes across sheets.
Can Monday.com handle the same level of project complexity as Smartsheet?
Monday.com can handle moderate project complexity with dependencies, timelines, and automations. However, it does not offer the same depth of data manipulation, nested formulas, or cross-sheet calculations that Smartsheet provides for advanced scenarios.
