Best Skillshare alternatives

Skillshare is a popular platform for short-form video lessons, especially in creative fields like design, photography, and writing. But it might not suit everyone’s budget, learning style, or subject needs. If you are a learner looking for subscription-based video courses or an educator wanting to host your own content, several alternatives offer similar short, practical lessons. Below we explore four solid options for both learners and educators.

alternative

Udemy

. Udemy sells individual courses rather than a single subscription, but you can often find short video lessons on almost any topic. It is great for learners who want a one time purchase and permanent access, though you will need to buy each course separately. Educators can create and sell their own courses here with no approval process, making it a flexible platform.

alternative

Coursera

. Coursera focuses on academic and professional development with short video modules tied to certificates and degrees. It offers a subscription model (Coursera Plus) for unlimited access to many courses, though some programs require separate payment. Educators from accredited institutions use Coursera to teach structured, skill based content that is longer than typical Skillshare lessons.

alternative

MasterClass

. MasterClass provides high production, short video lessons taught by famous experts in fields like cooking, writing, and music. It uses a monthly or annual subscription and each “class” is a series of 10 to 20 minute segments. For learners who want inspiration and insight from top figures rather than hands on projects, MasterClass is a premium alternative. Educators cannot host classes on MasterClass as it uses invited instructors only.

alternative

Teachable

. Teachable is a platform for educators to create and sell their own short form video courses under their own brand. Unlike Skillshare, you keep most of the revenue and control pricing, and you can offer a subscription option for your students. Learners can find many independent teachers on Teachable, but they must pay per course or per subscription set by the instructor, so it is not a single marketplace.

How they compare

All four alternatives serve different needs. Udemy and Teachable give educators more control over pricing and content, while Coursera and MasterClass are better for learners seeking structured, expert led lessons. For subscription based short video learning, Coursera Plus and MasterClass monthly plans are closest to Skillshare’s model. Udemy and Teachable require per course or per creator payments. Educators should choose Teachable for flexibility or Udemy for passive income, while learners should pick based on subject focus and budget.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for budget conscious learners who want a single subscription like Skillshare?
Coursera Plus offers a monthly subscription that gives access to thousands of short video courses across many fields. MasterClass also has a subscription but focuses on lifestyle and creative topics from celebrity instructors.
Can I host my own short form video courses on these platforms as an educator?
Yes, Udemy and Teachable allow educators to create and sell courses. Udemy is a large marketplace where you can publish immediately, while Teachable lets you build a branded school with your own pricing and subscription options.
