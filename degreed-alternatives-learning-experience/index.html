As a Chief Learning Officer overseeing L&D for a 10,000+ employee organization, you know that a learning experience platform (LXP) is a key driver of workforce capability. Degreed is a well-known leader in this space, providing strong skill tracking and content aggregation that helps connect learning to business outcomes. However, no single platform fits every large enterprise perfectly. Your organization’s unique blend of existing HR systems, content library investments, and skill taxonomy requirements may make a Degreed alternative a smarter fit for your long-term strategy.

alternative

EdCast

. EdCast positions itself as a knowledge management and AI-driven content curation platform, making it a strong alternative when you prioritize delivering the right learning at the point of need. Its AI engine excels at surfacing content from both internal knowledge repositories and external sources, reducing search friction for busy employees. For a large enterprise focused on real-time learning embedded in workflow, EdCast offers a more content-first approach than Degreed.

alternative

Percipio

. Percipio, from Skillsoft, is best suited for organizations that want a tightly integrated content library and platform experience. If your enterprise already invests heavily in Skillsoft content or desires a seamless path from content consumption to credentialing, Percipio provides that out-of-the-box collaboration. It also features strong offline learning capabilities and a design focused on learner engagement, though its reliance on Skillsoft content can be a limitation if you prefer a more open content ecosystem.

alternative

Cornerstone

. Cornerstone offers a unified talent management suite that integrates traditional learning management with LXP features. This makes it a strong alternative for enterprises that want to converge performance management, succession planning, and learning into a single platform. Its robust compliance tracking and reporting capabilities are particularly valuable for highly regulated industries, though its LXP features may feel less innovative than Degreed for informal learning.

How they compare

When comparing Degreed against these alternatives for a large enterprise, consider your primary L&D goal. Degreed leads on skill data and personalized learning pathways, making it strong for strategic workforce planning. EdCast excels at content discovery and knowledge management in real time. Percipio is ideal for depth of integrated, vendor-managed content. Cornerstone offers the broadest talent suite for convergence. Your choice depends on whether you value skill data and openness (Degreed), AI curation and workflow integration (EdCast), content simplicity (Percipio), or system unification (Cornerstone). Evaluate each platform against your current HRIS, your content budget, and how informal versus formal learning is weighted in your culture.

Frequently asked questions

Will migrating from Degreed to an alternative disrupt our current skill taxonomy and learning data?
Migration disruption largely depends on the maturity of your data integration. Most alternatives offer APIs and data import tools to map your existing skill definitions and completion records, but you should budget for a data cleanup phase. Plan for a 3-6 month transition window to agree taxonomies and ensure reporting consistency across systems.
How do these alternatives compare in total cost of ownership for an enterprise with 10,000+ users?
Total cost of ownership varies significantly by platform and your content model. Degreed and EdCast often have higher licensing fees due to their AI and skill analytics features. Percipio’s cost is closely tied to your Skillsoft content subscriptions, which can scale quickly. Cornerstone typically offers volume discounts for its full talent suite, making it potentially more cost-effective if you consolidate multiple vendors, but integrations with non-Cornerstone systems can add hidden costs. Always request a detailed total cost proposal that includes implementation, migration, and ongoing support.
