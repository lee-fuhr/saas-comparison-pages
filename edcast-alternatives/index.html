EdCast is a popular learning experience platform known for its AI-powered content aggregation and knowledge management features. However, some knowledge-centric organizations find that EdCast’s complexity, cost, or integration gaps don’t fully match their workflows. If you need a tool that pulls in content from multiple sources while supporting internal expertise sharing, these five alternatives can offer different strengths in aggregation, curation, and learner engagement.

alternative

Docebo

. Docebo combines a learning management system with a content hub that can aggregate external courses, user‑uploaded materials, and third‑party libraries. Its AI engine recommends relevant content automatically, making it easier for knowledge teams to surface resources without manual tagging. Docebo also supports social learning features, letting users share curated content and insights across the organization.

alternative

LinkedIn Learning

. LinkedIn Learning provides a massive library of expert‑created video courses, but it also allows organizations to upload and host their own content. For aggregation, you can map external resources (articles, PDFs, internal wikis) into curated learning paths using the platform’s tagging and assignment tools. Its integration with LinkedIn profiles helps surface relevant content based on users’ roles and past learning activity.

alternative

Degreed

. Degreed is built specifically for content aggregation and skill development. It pulls in learning materials from thousands of sources, including internal documents, videos, articles, and other platforms, and organizes them into skill‑based pathways. The platform uses skill‑gap analysis to recommend content, and its open API lets you connect almost any external system to centralize knowledge. **360Learning**. 360Learning focuses on collaborative learning and employee‑generated content. It aggregates content by allowing subject‑matter experts to easily create and share micro‑lessons, videos, and documents within the platform. For external content, it offers integrations with major libraries and a content marketplace, making it a flexible option for organizations that want both user‑created and curated materials.

Skillsoft

. Skillsoft offers a vast library of courses, videos, books, and leadership content, and its Percipio platform aggregates these resources with a strong recommendation engine. You can also upload your own proprietary content and combine it with Skillsoft’s assets into customized learning plans. Its mobile‑first design and offline access make it convenient for distributed teams to consume aggregated knowledge on the go.

How they compare

All five alternatives help knowledge‑centric organizations aggregate content, but they differ in emphasis. Docebo and Degreed are strongest at pulling in content from many external sources. LinkedIn Learning and Skillsoft provide massive pre‑built libraries with limited internal content creation. 360Learning shines when you want to prioritize employee‑generated knowledge alongside external resources. EdCast itself excels in AI‑driven content discovery, so choose an alternative that matches your need for either deep external sourcing, internal collaboration, or a balance of both.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is best for aggregating content from many different external sources?
Degreed is the strongest choice for broad external aggregation. It connects to thousands of content providers, internal repositories, and third‑party tools, and it uses skill data to recommend the most relevant materials from those sources.
Can I still use my own internal documents and videos with these alternatives?
Yes, all five platforms allow you to upload and host your own content. Docebo and 360Learning offer flexible upload options, while Degreed and Skillsoft let you tag or map internal files into learning paths. LinkedIn Learning supports custom content through its “my content” feature.
