Best Google Sheets alternatives

If your team relies on cloud based spreadsheets for collaboration, Google Sheets is often the default choice. It offers real time editing, easy sharing, and integration with Google Workspace. But as your projects grow more complex or your workflows become more specialized, you might need a tool that handles databases, automations, or advanced formatting differently. This article covers four strong alternatives to Google Sheets, each with its own strengths for teams that need collaborative spreadsheet functionality with cloud storage.

alternative

Airtable

combines a spreadsheet’s grid view with a database’s relational structure, letting you link records across tables and switch between grid, calendar, and kanban views. It supports rich field types like attachments, checkboxes, and linked records, making it ideal for project tracking and content planning. Teams appreciate its automation features and built‑in templates, though the learning curve is steeper than a traditional spreadsheet.

alternative

Smartsheet

is built for project management and workflow automation, offering Gantt charts, dependency links, and automated approval processes on top of a familiar grid interface. It integrates with popular apps like Slack, Microsoft Teams, and Salesforce, and its dashboard and reporting tools give leaders a high‑level view of team progress. Smartsheet scales well for enterprise use but carries a higher price point than most spreadsheet tools.

alternative

Excel Online

brings the full power of Microsoft Excel to the browser, including advanced formulas, pivot tables, and conditional formatting that many power users rely on. It offers real time co‑authoring and automatic cloud storage through OneDrive or SharePoint, while maintaining compatibility with desktop Excel files. For teams already invested in the Microsoft ecosystem, Excel Online is a natural choice, though some features work better in the desktop app.

alternative

Zoho Sheet

is a cloud based spreadsheet that supports real time collaboration, version history, and a wide range of formulas and chart types. It integrates tightly with other Zoho apps like CRM and Projects, making it a strong option for teams using Zoho’s suite. Zoho Sheet also provides automation via macros and scripts, plus data import from many sources, all at a competitive price point.

How they compare

Choose Airtable when you need to manage relational data and switch between visual layouts like kanban and calendar. Choose Smartsheet when your team focuses on project scheduling, task dependencies, and automated workflows. Choose Excel Online if your team requires advanced formulas, pivot tables, and seamless compatibility with desktop Excel files. Choose Zoho Sheet when you want a cost effective spreadsheet that integrates with other Zoho tools and still offers strong real time collaboration.

Frequently asked questions

Can I import my existing Google Sheets files into these alternatives?
Yes, each alternative supports importing from common formats such as CSV, XLSX, and ODS. Airtable and Smartsheet also offer direct import from Google Sheets using built‑in connectors. You may need to adjust formulas or formatting after the import, but basic data will transfer cleanly.
Do these alternatives work offline?
Excel Online and Zoho Sheet offer basic offline modes when you use their desktop companion apps or browser extensions. Airtable and Smartsheet are primarily cloud based and require an internet connection for full functionality, though they can cache data temporarily in some mobile apps.
