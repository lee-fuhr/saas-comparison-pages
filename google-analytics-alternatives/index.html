Best Google Analytics alternatives

Google Analytics 4 (GA4) has become more complex and less transparent about data collection. It also relies on Google servers, which can raise privacy concerns for site owners who want to avoid sharing visitor data with third parties. Fortunately, several lightweight, privacy-focused alternatives now offer similar insights without the tracking overhead. These tools work with or without cookies, respect visitor consent, and often cost less than GA4’s free tier in terms of hidden compliance work.

alternative

Plausible

. Plausible is a simple, open source analytics tool that does not use cookies and is fully compliant with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy laws. It provides a clean dashboard with essential metrics like page views, unique visitors, bounce rate, and referral sources, making it ideal for content sites and small businesses.

Try Plausible →
alternative

Fathom

. Fathom offers a privacy-first approach with a straightforward interface that rivals Plausible in simplicity. It is also cookie-free and built to be self-hosted or used as a cloud service, with a focus on speed and minimal data collection while still delivering key traffic insights.

Try Fathom →
alternative

Mixpanel

. Mixpanel is a product analytics platform designed for user behavior analysis, such as tracking events, funnels, and retention. It prioritizes first-party data and offers advanced segmentation, but it is more feature-rich (and pricier) than the simpler alternatives, making it a better fit for product teams who need deep behavioral insights.

Try Mixpanel →
alternative

PostHog

. PostHog is an open source product analytics suite that includes session recording, feature flags, and heatmaps alongside traditional analytics. It can be self-hosted on your own infrastructure, giving you full control over data, and is well suited for engineering teams that want to experiment and iterate without third party exposure.

Try PostHog →
alternative

Umami

. Umami is a lightweight, open source analytics tool that can be self-hosted in minutes. It collects only necessary data (no personal information), respects visitor privacy by default, and provides a clean, customizable dashboard with real time statistics and detailed page analytics.

Frequently asked questions

Do these alternatives work without cookies and still track traffic accurately?
Yes. Plausible, Fathom, Umami, and self hosted PostHog use cookieless tracking methods (like aggregate page view hits) and do not rely on fingerprinting or personal identifiers. They provide accurate visitor counts, page views, and referrers while staying compliant with privacy regulations.
Can I migrate my existing Google Analytics data to one of these tools?
Most privacy focused alternatives do not support direct data import from Google Analytics because their data models are different (e.g., they don’t store user level data). You will need to place their tracking snippet on your site and start fresh. Some tools like PostHog allow importing historical event data via API, but it requires custom work. For simple metrics, it is often easier to let the new tool begin collecting data forward.
