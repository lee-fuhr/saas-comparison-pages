UXPressia is a purpose built tool for customer journey mapping, persona development, and experience diagramming. However, some design teams find its pricing, collaboration features, or learning curve don’t match their workflow. Exploring alternatives can help you find a platform that better fits your team’s size, budget, and preferred way of working.

alternative

Miro

is a digital whiteboard that excels in real time collaboration and brainstorming. It offers flexible templates for journey mapping and persona creation, making it easy for teams to co-create. With voting, sticky notes, and diagramming tools, Miro supports the full UX design process from ideation to presentation.

alternative

Smaply

is purpose built for customer experience mapping, similar to UXPressia. It provides dedicated templates for journey maps, personas, and stakeholder maps with a focus on clarity and detail. Smaply also includes features for exporting and sharing maps with stakeholders who do not use the tool.

alternative

Lucidchart

is a diagramming application that includes journey mapping and flowchart capabilities. It integrates well with other tools like Google Workspace and Atlassian, which benefits teams already using those platforms. Lucidchart’s drag and drop interface makes it simple to build complex process diagrams and customer journeys.

alternative

FlowMapp

is a UX focused tool for sitemaps, user flows, and journey mapping. It helps designers plan information architecture and visualize user paths before prototyping. FlowMapp offers a clean interface and a freemium tier that is useful for smaller projects.

How they compare

To choose the right alternative, consider your team’s primary needs. Miro is best for collaborative workshops and versatile whiteboarding. Smaply matches UXPressia’s focus on customer experience mapping with dedicated templates. Lucidchart suits teams that need strong diagramming plus integrations with productivity suites. FlowMapp is ideal for early stage UX planning and information architecture work. All four tools offer free trials or freemium options, so you can test them before committing.

Frequently asked questions

Can I import my UXPressia maps into these alternatives?
Direct imports are not typically supported. You may need to export your maps as images or PDFs and then recreate them in the new tool. Some tools like Smaply and Miro have template overlays that can speed up the process.
Which alternative is best for a large design team on a tight budget?
Miro and FlowMapp both offer free tiers with generous limits for small teams. Miro’s free plan includes up to three boards, while FlowMapp’s free plan allows one project. For larger teams, Lucidchart and Smaply have affordable team pricing starting around nine dollars per user per month.
