Best Shopify POS alternatives

If you run an online store on Shopify and are now opening a physical location, Shopify POS might be your first choice. It syncs inventory and orders seamlessly with your website. But as your retail operation grows, you may hit limits like higher transaction fees, limited hardware options, or a need for advanced inventory management across multiple locations. That is why many merchants look for Shopify POS alternatives that offer lower costs, richer retail features, or more flexible payment processing.

alternative

Square POS

is a popular all-in-one system with no monthly fee for basic features, making it ideal for small startups. It syncs well with Square’s own online store, but it can also integrate with Shopify through third-party apps. Square’s hardware is affordable and widely available, and its payment processing is simple with flat-rate pricing.

alternative

Lightspeed Retail

offers a robust platform for merchants with multiple locations and complex inventory needs, such as clothing boutiques or bike shops. It provides advanced reporting, purchase order management, and customer profiles that go beyond Shopify’s basic POS features. Lightspeed charges a monthly subscription plus lower transaction rates if you use their payments, but it does not natively sync with Shopify without a separate integration tool.

alternative

Vend

is a cloud based POS built for brick-and-mortar retail, with strong inventory management and barcode scanning capabilities. It works well for stores that need offline mode and granular control over stock across different outlets. While Vend integrates with Shopify via Zapier or a dedicated connector, the sync is not as seamless as Shopify’s own POS, so you may need to manage inventory in two systems.

alternative

NCR

(formerly NCR Silver) is a enterprise grade POS designed for larger retailers and restaurants that need heavy customization and robust hardware. It offers features like loyalty programs, employee management, and detailed analytics that suit high volume environments. NCR is less common for small shops and typically requires a long term contract and higher upfront costs, but it provides stability and support that growing merchants may value.

How they compare

Square POS is the easiest to start with due to low cost and wide hardware availability, but it lacks deep inventory tools for multi-store operations. Lightspeed offers the best all-in-one retail management for businesses with several locations, though it comes with a monthly fee and separate integration cost to work with Shopify. Vend is a strong middle ground for independent retailers who want offline capability and detailed stock control, but it requires manual syncing with your web store. NCR is best for large scale operations that need a dedicated, customizable system, but it is overkill for most small merchants and does not integrate easily with Shopify. Choose based on your number of locations, budget, and how tightly you need your online and in-store systems to work together.

Frequently asked questions

Can I still use my Shopify online store while using a different POS system?
Yes, but you will need to manually manage inventory between the two systems or use a third party integration tool like Zapier. No alternative POS natively syncs inventory, orders, and customers with Shopify as smoothly as Shopify POS does. Be prepared for some data duplication or additional monthly costs.
Which alternative is best for a small retailer with one physical location and a Shopify website?
Square POS is often the best fit because it has no monthly fee, affordable hardware, and simple pricing. It also offers a free online store, but you can keep your existing Shopify site and use Square only at the register. Just note that you will need to update stock manually or use a bridge app.
