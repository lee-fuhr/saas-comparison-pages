As a large contractor managing complex projects, you need enterprise-grade visibility across every phase. Procore is a leading platform for this, but it may not fit every budget, workflow, or integration need. This guide reviews Procore and three alternatives: Buildertrend, CoConstruct, and Autodesk BIM 360. We focus on their suitability for large contractors who require robust project visibility and control.

alternative

Buildertrend

. Buildertrend is popular among residential and commercial contractors but is generally better suited for small to mid-size firms. For large contractors, its project visibility capabilities may lack the depth needed for complex, multi-year jobs. It does offer financial tools, scheduling, and client communication, but enterprise-grade reporting and scalability can be limited compared to Procore.

alternative

CoConstruct

. CoConstruct is designed for custom home builders and remodelers, not typically for large commercial contractors. Its strength lies in detailed client management and change order tracking, but it does not provide the level of field management, document control, or integrated financials that large contractors require. It is a niche alternative rather than an enterprise replacement.

alternative

Autodesk BIM 360

. Autodesk BIM 360 (now part of Autodesk Construction Cloud) is a strong enterprise-grade solution focused on construction project delivery and field management. It offers robust model coordination, quality and safety management, and document control. Large contractors already using Autodesk design tools will find deep integration, but its financial and project management features are less comprehensive than Procore’s.

How they compare

For enterprise-grade project visibility, Procore remains the most well-rounded choice for large contractors, offering extensive modules for financials, field management, and reporting. Autodesk BIM 360 excels in design and field coordination but lacks built-in accounting. Buildertrend and CoConstruct are better suited for smaller firms or niche residential work. Evaluate your specific needs: if you require deep financial integration and advanced project controls, Procore leads; if your focus is on model-based collaboration, BIM 360 is a strong alternative.

Frequently asked questions

Can Buildertrend or CoConstruct handle large commercial projects with hundreds of subcontractors?
Not effectively. Both tools are designed for residential or small commercial work. Their reporting, permissions, and document management lack the scale and complexity needed for large enterprise projects. Procore or Autodesk BIM 360 are better suited for that scale.
Does Autodesk BIM 360 include financial management and accounting features like Procore?
No. Autodesk BIM 360 focuses on project delivery, field management, and design coordination. It does not have built-in accounting, budget management, or change order financial tracking. You would need to integrate with separate ERP or accounting software for full financial visibility.
