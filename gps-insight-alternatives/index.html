If you manage a small to medium fleet, you already know that GPS tracking can save time, fuel, and headaches. GPS Insight is a solid platform, but it might not be the perfect fit for every operation. Some teams find its setup process more involved than expected, or they want a streamlined interface with less clicking around. That is why we are looking at three strong alternatives: Samsara, Verizon Connect, and Geotab. Each one is built to get you up and running quickly without a steep learning curve, so your drivers and office staff can start seeing benefits within days.

alternative

Samsara

. Samsara pairs plug-and-play hardware with an intuitive dashboard that focuses on real-time location, driver behavior, and vehicle health. Installation takes minutes because the devices connect automatically to your cellular network and cloud account. Many small fleets appreciate that Samsara requires almost no IT support to get started, and the mobile app works for both managers and drivers right out of the box.

alternative

Verizon Connect

. Verizon Connect offers a range of GPS tracking plans that can be tailored to fleets of any size, with a strong emphasis on ease of use. Their hardware often comes pre-activated, and the web-based software lets you view routes, idle time, and alerts without complicated configuration. For fleets already using Verizon wireless, integration can be seamless and support is readily available.

alternative

Geotab

. Geotab is known for its open platform and wide compatibility with third-party add-ons, but it also provides a straightforward starter package for smaller fleets. The GO device plugs directly into a vehicle’s diagnostic port and starts transmitting data immediately. While Geotab offers deep customization later, the initial setup is simple, and their MyGeotab dashboard is clean enough for a manager to learn in an afternoon.

How they compare

All three, Samsara, Verizon Connect, and Geotab, provide reliable GPS tracking with minimal setup, which is the top need for small to medium fleets. Samsara wins on hardware simplicity and modern interface, Verizon Connect excels for fleets that want bundled wireless and tracking in one bill, and Geotab stands out for those who may need to expand features later. Pricing varies by vehicle count and features, but each offers a free trial or demo so you can test before committing. Think about whether your team values a closed but polished system (Samsara), a telecom-backed suite (Verizon Connect), or a flexible platform with many integrations (Geotab).

Frequently asked questions

Can I install these GPS trackers myself without a professional installer?
Yes. All three alternatives use plug-and-play devices that connect to the OBD-II port under your dashboard or to the vehicle’s battery with simple wiring. Samsara and Geotab include clear instructions, and Verizon Connect often ships pre-configured units. Most small fleet owners can complete installation in under 10 minutes per vehicle.
How long does it take to see live tracking data after installation?
You should see your first vehicle location within a few minutes to an hour after plugging in the device and creating an account. Samsara and Geotab typically show data almost immediately, while Verizon Connect may take a bit longer if the device needs to activate on the cellular network. All three providers offer real-time updates every few seconds once the tracker is online.
