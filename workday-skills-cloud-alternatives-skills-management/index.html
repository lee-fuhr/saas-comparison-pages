As Head of Talent Mobility at a large enterprise, you know that a skills cloud is central to internal mobility, workforce planning, and reskilling at scale. Workday Skills Cloud is a natural starting point if you are already on Workday HCM, offering deep integration and a unified data model. However, some organizations find that Workday’s skills ontology and matching capabilities do not fully address their need for more granular, real-time skills intelligence, AI-driven career pathing, or a marketplace that engages employees directly. Evaluating alternatives such as Gloat, Eightfold, and Fuel50 can help you decide whether to supplement or replace Workday Skills Cloud for specific use cases like talent marketplace, skills inference, or development planning.

alternative

Gloat

Gloat focuses on creating an internal talent marketplace powered by AI that matches employees to projects, gigs, and full-time roles based on skills and aspirations. Its strength lies in driving real-time mobility and project-based work, which is especially useful for large enterprises that want to move beyond traditional job postings. Gloat also offers a skills ontology that can pull data from Workday, but it operates as a standalone system requiring integration.

alternative

Eightfold

Eightfold uses deep learning and a large dataset of professional profiles to infer skills from resumes, job titles, and career histories, even when those skills are not explicitly listed. This makes it powerful for identifying hidden talent and predicting career paths across very large and complex organizations. Eightfold can coexist with Workday by ingesting HR data, but its proprietary skill graph is separate from Workday’s native model, which may require ongoing data synchronization.

alternative

Fuel50

Fuel50 emphasizes employee-driven career development through an AI coach that helps individuals explore career paths, identify skill gaps, and find mentors or learning resources. For talent mobility leaders focused on retention and growth, Fuel50 provides a transparent progression framework and a skills-based career architecture. It integrates with Workday for core data but adds a motivational layer that many enterprises find more engaging than Workday’s built-in career tools.

How they compare

Workday Skills Cloud offers the tightest integration with your existing HRIS and payroll data, making it a low-friction option for organizations that already run Workday HCM. However, its skills inference and marketplace features are less advanced than those of dedicated vendors. Gloat excels at project-based mobility and internal gig matching. Eightfold provides the most sophisticated AI for inferring skills from unstructured data, useful for very large and diverse workforces. Fuel50 prioritizes individual career coaching and transparent progression, which supports long-term retention and development. The right choice depends on whether your priority is seamless integration (Workday), real-time project allocation (Gloat), deep skill inference (Eightfold), or employee engagement in career planning (Fuel50). Many large enterprises use Workday for foundational HR data and layer one of these alternatives on top for specialized talent mobility functionality.

Frequently asked questions

What are the main limitations of Workday Skills Cloud for talent mobility at a 5000+ employee company?
Workday Skills Cloud is a solid foundation, but its skills ontology is primarily built from job descriptions and past data, which can lag behind emerging skills. Its matching logic tends to favor keyword-based matches rather than deeper inference from work history or learning behavior. For large enterprises, the internal marketplace functionality is often less engaging for employees and less flexible for managers seeking project-based staffing compared to dedicated platforms like Gloat or Fuel50.
Can we run Workday Skills Cloud alongside an alternative like Eightfold or Gloat without causing data conflicts?
Yes, many large enterprises operate a dual system. Workday remains the system of record for core employee data, job history, and organizational structure. The alternative tool ingests that data via API or SFTP and then builds its own skill profiles, career paths, or marketplace. The key is to define clear data ownership (Workday for source truth, the alternative for derived skill intelligence) and ensure regular synchronization to avoid discrepancies. Some vendors also offer prebuilt Workday connectors to ease this integration.
