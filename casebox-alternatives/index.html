If you are an open source enthusiast who wants full control over your eDiscovery tools, Casebox is a strong choice. It is a self hosted platform that gives you ownership of your data and the ability to customize workflows. Casetext, by contrast, is a commercial cloud based service that relies on artificial intelligence to speed up document review. This article compares Casebox with Casetext and other popular alternatives to help you decide which fits your self hosted, open source philosophy best.

alternative

Relativity

. Relativity is a leading commercial eDiscovery platform that runs in the cloud or on premises. It offers advanced analytics and scalable processing but requires a paid license and often external hosting. For open source enthusiasts, its proprietary model and high cost can be a barrier compared to self hosted tools.

alternative

Everlaw

. Everlaw is a cloud only eDiscovery tool known for its intuitive interface and collaborative features. It uses AI to predict relevance and privilege, but it is a fully managed SaaS product with no self hosted option. This makes it less attractive if you want to keep your data on your own servers.

alternative

Logikcull

. Logikcull is a cloud based eDiscovery service that emphasizes simplicity and fixed pricing. It handles data processing and review in its own environment, so you cannot host it yourself. While easy to use, it lacks the flexibility of an open source platform like Casebox.

alternative

Disco

. Disco is another commercial eDiscovery provider that offers a cloud platform with AI assisted review and analytics. It is designed for law firms and corporations and does not provide a self hosted installation. If you prioritize software freedom, Disco’s proprietary license may not appeal to you.

alternative

Casetext

. Casetext is a legal research and eDiscovery tool built on artificial intelligence, called CARA and CoCounsel. It is a cloud only service that automates document analysis and summarization. While powerful, it is proprietary and requires a subscription, making it unsuitable for anyone who wants to self host their eDiscovery infrastructure.

How they compare

Casebox and Casetext serve different needs. Casebox is open source and self hosted, which means you install it on your own servers, control all data, and can modify the code. Casetext is a proprietary cloud service where you pay per user or per case and rely on the vendor’s infrastructure. Casebox has no license fee but may involve costs for server setup, maintenance, and support. Casetext offers advanced AI features like instant document analysis, but you cannot access the underlying algorithms or store data offline. For open source enthusiasts, Casebox provides transparency and data sovereignty, while Casetext trades those for convenience and AI sophistication. Other alternatives like Relativity or Everlaw also lack self hosting, making Casebox the only true open source option on this list.

Frequently asked questions

Is Casebox completely free to use?
Casebox is open source software with no license cost. You do need to provide your own server hardware and handle installation, maintenance, and any potential support. Some hosting providers offer managed Casebox services for a fee, but the core software itself is free.
Can I use Casetext without paying anything?
Casetext offers a limited free trial for new users, but it is a paid subscription service for ongoing use. There is no self hosted or free tier available. You must agree to their cloud terms and pay per user or per matter to access features like CoCounsel AI.
