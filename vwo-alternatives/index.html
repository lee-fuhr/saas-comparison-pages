Best VWO alternatives

If you are a CRO team currently using VWO for A/B testing, you might be looking for alternatives. Maybe your team needs a lower pricing tier, or more advanced analytics depth, or simply a different interface. VWO is a solid platform, but it isn’t the only option. This article covers five alternatives: Optimizely, AB Tasty, Convert, Statsig, and Crazy Egg. Each one suits different priorities, from cost savings to statistical rigor. We’ll compare them so you can decide which fits your workflow and budget.

alternative

Optimizely

. Optimizely is a full-featured experimentation platform known for its enterprise-grade analytics and personalization capabilities. It offers deep statistical controls, server-side testing, and feature flagging, but its pricing is typically higher than VWO’s, making it best for teams with larger budgets and complex needs.

Try Optimizely →
alternative

AB Tasty

. AB Tasty combines A/B testing with built-in personalization and a visual editor that many teams find intuitive. Its pricing is often more transparent than VWO’s, and it provides solid reporting dashboards, though analytics depth may not match Optimizely or Statsig.

Try AB Tasty →
alternative

Convert

. Convert focuses on simplicity and affordability, with a clear per-visitor pricing model that can be cheaper than VWO for lower-traffic sites. It offers essentials like split testing, heatmaps, and a visual editor, but lacks some advanced statistical features such as sequential testing or multi-armed bandits.

alternative

Statsig

. Statsig is a modern platform built for product-led teams, offering feature gates, dynamic config, and deep experimentation analytics with real-time statistical methods. Its pricing starts free for smaller usage, making it attractive for cost-conscious teams, but it requires more technical setup than VWO for front-end testing.

alternative

Crazy Egg

. Crazy Egg specializes in visual insights like heatmaps, scroll maps, and session recordings, with basic A/B testing capabilities. It is much cheaper than VWO but should be considered a lightweight alternative for teams that prioritize user behavior analytics over rigorous statistical experimentation.

Frequently asked questions

Which VWO alternative is best if I want the same level of statistical rigor at a lower price?
Statsig offers robust statistical methods (like sequential testing) and starts with a free tier, so it can be more affordable for small to medium traffic. Convert also provides solid testing at a lower per-visitor cost, but its analytics depth is less advanced than Statsig.
Can I run both visual editor experiments and server-side feature tests with one of these tools?
Yes, Optimizely and Statsig both support visual editor experiments (for client-side) and server-side feature flagging. AB Tasty also has a visual editor and some server-side capabilities, though it is less comprehensive than Optimizely.
