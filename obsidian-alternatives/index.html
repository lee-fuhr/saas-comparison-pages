Best Obsidian alternatives

If you love Obsidian for its local-first markdown notes and powerful graph view, you might be curious about other tools that offer similar features. Notion, Roam Research, and Logseq are popular alternatives, each with their own strengths and trade-offs. This article compares them to help you decide which one fits your workflow best.

alternative

Notion

is a cloud-based platform with a block-based editor and graph view, but it does not store notes as local markdown files. It requires an internet connection for full functionality, so it may not suit users who want local-first ownership of their data.

Try Notion →
alternative

Roam Research

pioneered the block-based outliner and bidirectional links, with a graph view that visualizes connections. It is cloud-only and proprietary, so your notes are not available as plain markdown files on your device.

Try Roam Research →
alternative

Logseq

is an open-source app that uses local markdown or Org-mode files, giving you full control over your data. It offers a graph view and bidirectional links similar to Obsidian, making it the closest alternative for local-first markdown note-takers.

Try Logseq →

How they compare

- Local-first: Logseq and Obsidian both store plain markdown files locally; Notion and Roam Research are cloud-only. - Graph view: All four tools have a graph view, but Logseq and Obsidian’s graphs are more similar in detail and customization. - Markdown: Logseq and Obsidian use standard markdown; Roam Research uses its own block format; Notion uses a proprietary block system. - Pricing: Obsidian is free for personal use; Logseq is free and open-source; Notion and Roam Research charge for advanced features.
