Best Square POS alternatives

Square POS and Clover are two of the most popular all-in-one point of sale systems for small retailers. Both offer hardware, payment processing, and software in one package, but they differ in cost, contract terms, and feature depth. This article compares Square POS with Clover and four other alternatives to help you choose the right fit for your business.

alternative

Clover

offers a wide range of hardware options from countertop terminals to mobile readers. It requires a long term contract and monthly service fees, which can add up, but it provides built in inventory management and employee tools. Clover works best for retailers who want a dedicated device with offline mode and don’t mind a fixed monthly cost.

alternative

Lightspeed Retail

is a cloud based POS designed for growing businesses with complex inventory needs, like multi location stores or apparel shops. It charges a higher monthly subscription but includes advanced reporting, purchase orders, and supplier integrations. Lightspeed is a good choice if you need detailed stock control and are willing to pay for it.

alternative

Shopify POS

integrates seamlessly with the Shopify ecommerce platform, making it ideal for retailers who sell online and in person. Its hardware is simple and affordable, but advanced inventory features require a higher tier plan. Shopify POS is best for businesses that already run a Shopify online store or plan to.

alternative

Vend

is a simple, cloud based POS that focuses on retail basics like sales, inventory, and customer management. It offers a free trial and affordable monthly plans with no long term contracts, but hardware is sold separately. Vend works well for small to mid size retailers who want a straightforward system without hardware lock in.

alternative

NCR

(formerly NCR Silver) provides a robust POS for retailers with moderate to high transaction volumes. It includes offline capability, advanced reporting, and strong inventory features, but it often requires a contract and dedicated hardware. NCR is a solid option for retailers who need reliability and phone support.

How they compare

Square POS and Clover are both good all-in-one systems, but they cater to different business styles. Square has no monthly fees, simple pricing, and a free card reader, while Clover requires a contract and monthly subscription for its hardware and software. Square’s inventory management is basic but sufficient for many small shops, whereas Clover offers more built in features like employee clock in and offline mode. For most small retailers who want low upfront costs and flexibility, Square POS is the better choice. However, if you need offline mode, a dedicated countertop terminal, and are okay with a fixed monthly cost, Clover may be a better fit.
