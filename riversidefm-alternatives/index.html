Best Riverside.fm alternatives

Riverside.fm is a popular platform for recording remote interviews with high-quality local audio and video. But it may not fit every budget, workflow, or feature set you need. Whether you’re looking for a lower price, better post-production tools, or simpler guest onboarding, several alternatives can deliver the same local recording quality. Below we compare four top choices for podcasters and remote interviewers.

alternative

SquadCast

. SquadCast focuses on reliability and lossless local recordings. It offers separate audio and video tracks per guest, plus automatic backup uploads if the connection drops. The platform also includes a simple post-production studio for trimming and combining your final file.

alternative

Zencastr

. Zencastr records each participant’s audio and video locally in high quality and automatically syncs them in the cloud after the session. It provides a soundboard for adding intros and outros live, and it integrates directly with editing tools like Audacity and Descript. The free tier is generous for short recordings, but advanced features require a paid plan.

alternative

Castos

. Castos is best known as an all-in-one podcast hosting and analytics platform, but it also offers a remote recording tool called Castos Recordings. It captures separate WAV files per participant on your own server, giving you full data control. The recording feature is included with Castos hosting plans, making it a cost-effective choice if you need hosting anyway.

alternative

Zoom Webinars

. Zoom is not a dedicated podcasting tool, but its webinar mode can record local audio and video for up to 100 panelists. You can enable “Record on each participant’s computer” to get separate local files (though this requires manual coordination). Zoom is widely familiar, low cost, and reliable, but it lacks automatic syncing and may not offer the same audio fidelity as purpose-built tools.

How they compare

Riverside.fm leads in ease of use and built-in features like live editing and mediaboard. SquadCast excels in reliability and lossless tracks. Zencastr offers a strong free option and integration with editing platforms. Castos is ideal if you already host with them or want full ownership of your recordings. Zoom Webinars is the most accessible and budget-friendly but requires extra setup for separate local files and may not match others in audio quality.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative provides the simplest guest experience for non-technical participants?
Zencastr and Riverside.fm both use a browser-based interface that requires no downloads. Zencastr’s guest link is especially straightforward, while Riverside.fm offers a slightly more polished mediaboard for sharing content during the call.
Do any of these alternatives offer built-in transcription or post-production features?
Riverside.fm includes automatic transcription and a basic editor. Zencastr provides integration with Descript for transcription and editing. SquadCast has a simple trim and combine tool. Castos and Zoom Webinars do not offer built-in transcription, but you can export audio files and use third-party services.
