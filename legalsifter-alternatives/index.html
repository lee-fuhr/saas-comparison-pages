As in-house counsel, you need to review contracts quickly and confidently. AI tools that suggest clauses can save you time and reduce risk. LegalSifter offers a straightforward approach by flagging risky language and suggesting alternative phrasing in real time. But you may also be evaluating Lexion and other similar tools. This article compares LegalSifter with key alternatives so you can choose the right fit for your legal team.

alternative

Kira Systems

. Kira excels at extracting and analyzing clauses from large volumes of contracts, especially for due diligence and M&A work. It uses machine learning to identify patterns, but it requires more upfront setup and is less focused on real-time drafting suggestions.

alternative

Luminance

. Luminance uses a unique unsupervised learning model to spot unusual clauses and deviations from your playbook. It is strong for post-execution contract analysis, though its clause suggestion features are less direct than LegalSifter’s inline editing.

alternative

Lexion

. Lexion combines contract management with AI clause review. It helps you organize your contract repository and then surfaces recommendations during redlining. Its strength is integration with your existing workflows, but the clause suggestion engine is more passive than LegalSifter’s interactive prompts.

alternative

Malbek

. Malbek focuses on the entire contract lifecycle, including authoring and negotiation. Its AI suggests clauses based on your playbook and past approvals, making it a good fit for teams that want a full CLM platform with built-in intelligence.

How they compare

LegalSifter and Lexion both target in-house teams needing AI clause suggestions, but their approaches differ. LegalSifter provides immediate, inline feedback as you draft or redline a contract in Microsoft Word or Google Docs, acting like a smart reviewer. Lexion, on the other hand, sits alongside your contract repository and offers suggestions after you upload or edit a document, which can be less disruptive but also less integrated. If you need real time suggestions without switching tools, LegalSifter is more direct. If you prefer a centralized contract hub that also manages approvals and obligations, Lexion may work better.
