Hotjar is a popular tool for session recording and heatmaps, but some users look for alternatives due to pricing, data sampling limits, or feature gaps. This article covers five strong options for UX designers and conversion optimisers who need reliable session replay and visual analytics.

alternative

Microsoft Clarity

is a free tool that offers unlimited session recordings and heatmaps with no traffic caps. It integrates with Google Analytics and provides useful filters for rage clicks and dead clicks, making it a strong entry level choice.

alternative

FullStory

focuses on advanced session replay with powerful search and analysis features, including automatic event tracking and frustration signals. Its heatmaps are detailed, but the tool is more expensive and complex for small teams.

alternative

LogRocket

combines session recording with frontend performance monitoring and error logging, making it ideal for debugging UX issues. It offers heatmaps too, though its strength is in technical troubleshooting rather than pure conversion optimisation.

Try LogRocket →
alternative

Crazy Egg

is a straightforward alternative that specializes in heatmaps, scroll maps, and A/B testing. Its session recordings are reliable, and the tool is easy to set up for marketers and designers who want a simple workflow.

alternative

PostHog

is an open source product analytics platform that includes session recording and heatmaps as part of its suite. It gives full data control and self hosting options, but requires more technical setup than Hotjar.

How they compare

Consider your budget, data volume, and technical needs. Microsoft Clarity is best for free unlimited data. FullStory excels in deep analysis but costs more. LogRocket suits teams that need performance insights. Crazy Egg is simple and focused on visual analytics. PostHog offers self hosted flexibility. None match Hotjar’s exact feature set, but each covers session recording and heatmaps well.

Frequently asked questions

Is Microsoft Clarity really free with no limits?
Yes, Microsoft Clarity is completely free to use. It offers unlimited session recordings and heatmaps without any traffic or data retention caps, though some advanced filtering options are less robust than paid tools.
Can I use PostHog on my own server?
Yes, PostHog is open source and can be self hosted. This gives you full control over your data and no per usage fees, but you need to manage the infrastructure yourself.
