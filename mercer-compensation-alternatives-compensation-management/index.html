As a Head of Rewards at a consulting firm, you know that reliable compensation data and precise job matching are the backbone of competitive pay strategies. Mercer is a long-standing primary source, but it is not the only one. Exploring alternatives can provide broader geographic coverage, different job matching methodologies, or lower costs. This article reviews three strong options – Payfactors, Radford (Aon), and CompAnalyst – to help you decide which tools best support your global benchmarking needs.

alternative

Payfactors

. Payfactors offers a large, self-reported survey database focused on North America with growing international coverage. Its strength lies in real-time data updates and flexible job matching that lets users map their roles to multiple survey sources at once.

alternative

Radford (Aon)

. Radford, part of Aon, specializes in technology, life sciences, and financial services with deep global data for these sectors. Its job matching relies on detailed role descriptions and a consistent global grading system, making it a strong choice for consulting firms with tech or pharma clients.

alternative

CompAnalyst

. CompAnalyst provides a user-friendly platform that aggregates data from many third-party surveys, not just its own. It excels at simplifying complex job matching across multiple vendors and geographies, offering high customization for global consulting firms.

How they compare

Each alternative brings distinct advantages. Payfactors focuses on real-time, self-reported data with strong North American roots. Radford offers unmatched depth in select industries and global consistency. CompAnalyst acts as an aggregator, reducing the burden of managing multiple survey sources. For a consulting firm needing broad global compensation data and flexible job matching, CompAnalyst may offer the most versatility, while Radford is ideal for industry-specific benchmarks. Payfactors suits firms prioritizing speed and cost efficiency in North America.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative has the best global compensation data for consulting firms?
It depends on your client industries. Radford (Aon) provides excellent global data for technology, life sciences, and financial services. CompAnalyst aggregates many global surveys, so it offers broad geographic coverage but relies on the depth of its third-party sources. Payfactors is strongest in North America.
How does job matching differ among these tools?
Payfactors uses a flexible, multi-source matching approach that lets you agree your roles to several surveys. Radford uses a consistent global job grading system with detailed role descriptions. CompAnalyst automates cross-source matching, making it easier to compare roles across different vendors and geographies.
