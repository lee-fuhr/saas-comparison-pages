As a Director of Compensation at an S&P 500 company, you likely rely on Radford (Aon) for executive compensation benchmarking. However, shifting data needs, budget constraints, or a desire for more flexible tools may lead you to evaluate alternatives. The right platform must deliver robust executive level data, customizable peer groups, and clear scenario modeling. Below is a practical look at three strong alternatives to Radford and how they compare for complex, top tier compensation analysis.

alternative

Payfactors

. Payfactors offers a broad survey library with strong analytics for executive compensation benchmarking. Its platform allows you to build custom peer groups in real time and apply detailed filters, making it easier to isolate specific industry or revenue cohorts. The self service model can also reduce reliance on consulting hours for standard queries.

alternative

Mercer

. Mercer provides a large global dataset including their proprietary executive compensation surveys. Their module includes detailed percentile reporting and robust tools for long term incentive design and pay mix modeling. Mercer also offers custom survey options and deep peer group analytics, which can be especially valuable for validating pay strategies across multiple markets.

alternative

Compoze

. Compoze is a modern, cloud based platform focused on fast scenario modeling and pay equity analysis. It is designed to replace spreadsheet driven processes with an integrated workflow that includes dynamic what if modeling and visual reporting. For S&P 500 firms with complex equity structures, its speed and user experience can reduce analysis time significantly.

How they compare

Radford remains a market leader for technology and life sciences sectors with deep executive pay data. Payfactors excels in self service analytics and flexible peer group creation, but may have a smaller executive level sample size. Mercer offers unmatched global reach and a long established database, making it ideal for international comparisons. Compoze provides a newer, agile interface with strong visualization and equity modeling, though its data pool is smaller than the incumbents. Your choice will depend on how much you value data breadth versus platform speed and customization.

Frequently asked questions

How does the quality of executive data from Payfactors compare to Radford?
Payfactors relies on participant submitted survey data, similar to Radfords model, but its executive level sample size can be narrower for very senior roles. That said, its real time filtering and peer group creation let you adjust comparability more precisely, which can offset a smaller base.
Can Compoze handle the complexity of long term incentive plans at an S&P 500 company?
Yes, Compoze is built to model complex equity vehicles such as performance shares, stock options, and restricted stock units. Its strength lies in dynamic scenario testing and rapid what if analysis, though for the broadest dataset you may still want to supplement with a survey based platform.
