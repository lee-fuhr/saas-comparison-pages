Best WordPress alternatives

You may be thinking about moving away from WordPress if you run a blog, publish content, or own a small or medium business. WordPress is powerful but can feel heavy: it often requires regular updates, security patches, and plugin management. Many publishers, bloggers, and SMBs want a simpler, faster, or more design focused platform. Here we look at five strong alternatives that remove some of that upkeep while still giving you control over your site.

alternative

Webflow

. Webflow lets you design visually without writing code, and it handles hosting and security automatically. It is a good fit if you want pixel perfect control over layout but still need a CMS for blog posts or product pages. The learning curve is steeper than drag and drop builders, but the result is more polished and responsive.

Try Webflow →
alternative

Ghost

. Ghost is an open source publishing platform built specifically for creators, newsletters, and blogs. It offers clean typography, built in membership features, and fast loading times. If your main goal is to write and grow an audience (with subscriptions or donations), Ghost keeps things focused and lightweight.

Try Ghost →
alternative

Squarespace

. Squarespace provides all in one website building with beautiful, modern templates and a simple editor. It includes hosting, domain options, and ecommerce tools in one monthly price. For SMBs and bloggers who want a polished site without technical work, Squarespace is a reliable choice.

Try Squarespace →
alternative

Wix

. Wix is a user friendly drag and drop builder with hundreds of templates and a large app market. It works well for beginners who want to launch quickly and add features like booking or online stores. The trade off is less flexibility for complex customizations and slower performance on large sites.

alternative

Framer

. Framer is a newer visual tool that started as a prototyping app and now offers full website publishing. It excels at creating animated, interactive pages with a strong design focus. For publishers and SMBs who prioritize visual storytelling and modern effects, Framer can be a more creative alternative to WordPress.

How they compare

Each alternative handles content management, design control, and ease of use differently. Webflow and Framer give the most design freedom but require some learning. Ghost is best for pure publishing and membership sites. Squarespace and Wix offer the easiest setup for beginners, though they limit advanced customization. None of these platforms require plugin management or regular manual updates like WordPress does. Your choice depends on whether you value speed (Ghost), design flexibility (Webflow or Framer), or simple all in one convenience (Squarespace or Wix).
