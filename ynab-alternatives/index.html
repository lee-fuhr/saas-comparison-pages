If you love the zero‑based budgeting method where every dollar has a job, you likely know YNAB as the go‑to app. But you might be looking for a different price, a simpler design, or extra features like investment tracking. The good news is that several other tools can help you follow the same principle of giving every dollar a purpose. This article covers three popular YNAB alternatives: Mint, Personal Capital, and PocketGuard. Each one approaches zero‑based budgeting a little differently, so you can pick the one that fits your style.

alternative

Mint

. Mint is a free budgeting app that automatically tracks your spending and categorizes transactions. It does not enforce a strict zero‑based budget like YNAB, but you can create a monthly budget and see how your actual spending compares. Mint shines for people who want a hands‑off overview of their finances without manually assigning every dollar.

Try Mint →
alternative

Personal Capital

. Personal Capital focuses more on net worth and investment tracking than on day‑to‑day budgeting. It has a free financial dashboard and a paid advisory service for portfolio management. While you can set a budget within Personal Capital, it does not guide you through a zero‑based approach as thoroughly as YNAB does.

Try Personal Capital →
alternative

PocketGuard

. PocketGuard uses a “In My Pocket” feature that shows how much spending money you have left after bills and savings are covered. This aligns well with zero‑based thinking because it forces you to account for your fixed expenses first. It is simpler than YNAB and good for people who want a quick, automated way to stay on track without manual transaction entry.
