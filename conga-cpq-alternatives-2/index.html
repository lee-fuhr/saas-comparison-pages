Best Conga CPQ alternatives

Conga CPQ is a popular tool for managing quotes, pricing, and product configurations. However, for mid-market firms, its implementation can be complex and time-consuming, often requiring dedicated IT resources and lengthy consulting engagements. If you are looking for an easier path to get up and running faster, the alternatives below offer simpler setups, faster time to value, and lower total cost of ownership.

alternative

Salesforce CPQ

. This tool is tightly integrated with the Salesforce platform, which means you must already use or plan to adopt Salesforce. It offers robust features but requires significant configuration and ongoing admin work, making it a heavier lift for mid-market teams without dedicated Salesforce expertise.

alternative

DealHub

. DealHub is a no-code CPQ solution that works with popular CRMs like Salesforce, HubSpot, and Microsoft Dynamics. Its drag-and-drop interface and prebuilt workflows allow mid-market firms to go live in weeks rather than months, with less reliance on technical consultants.

alternative

Pricefx

. Pricefx is a strong option if your business prioritizes advanced pricing logic, analytics, and deal optimization. Its CPQ module can be customized, but the initial setup may still need some technical support to configure price rules and approval flows.

alternative

Zilliant

. Zilliant focuses on price optimization and guidance rather than a full quote-to-cash process. It is best used as a supplement to an existing quoting system, not a direct replacement for Conga’s end-to-end CPQ functionality.

alternative

ConfigureOne

. ConfigureOne is built for complex product configurations, especially in manufacturing and industrial sectors. It offers a more streamlined implementation than Conga for rule-based products, but it still requires careful mapping of configuration logic and may need specialized knowledge.

How they compare

Each alternative trades off between features and ease of implementation. DealHub generally offers the fastest, simplest path for mid-market firms with standard quoting needs. Salesforce CPQ and Pricefx are more powerful but demand more setup time and admin effort. Zilliant is a niche add-on, not a full CPQ. ConfigureOne fits specific complex configuration scenarios but can still be easier to deploy than Conga for those use cases. Your choice should match the complexity of your products and the size of your implementation team.

Frequently asked questions

Which alternative is easiest to implement for a mid-market company with no dedicated IT team?
DealHub is typically the simplest because it is no-code and integrates with your existing CRM. Most teams can configure it themselves using guided templates, and the support team provides quick onboarding assistance.
Can I replace Conga CPQ with these tools without losing functionality for complex quoting?
It depends on your specific needs. DealHub and Pricefx handle standard and moderate complexity well. For very complex product rules or heavy customization, ConfigureOne or Salesforce CPQ may be better, but they still require more setup time. Evaluate your top configuration scenarios before deciding.
