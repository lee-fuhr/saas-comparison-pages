Best NCR alternatives

Large retailers and enterprises that rely on NCR for point of sale hardware and software know the platform offers deep integration with barcode scanners, receipt printers, cash drawers, and payment terminals. However, shifting business needs, pricing changes, or limited flexibility can push companies to evaluate other options. This article covers four credible NCR alternatives that support robust hardware ecosystems and are designed for high volume retail environments. We focus on real world capabilities without overstating features.

alternative

Square POS

. Square POS offers a hardware suite that includes a dedicated retail terminal, barcode scanners, and receipt printers, all managed through a centralized dashboard. For larger retailers, Square’s Retail Plus plan provides advanced inventory and employee management, though its payment processing rates may be higher than enterprise negotiated deals. Square’s hardware tends to be more plug and play, which can simplify deployment across multiple locations.

alternative

Lightspeed Retail

. Lightspeed Retail supports a wide range of peripherals through its open API and certified hardware list, including RF scanners, label printers, and chip card readers. It is especially strong for omnichannel retailers who need real time sync between physical stores and ecommerce. Lightspeed’s higher tier plans include dedicated account management and custom integrations for specialized hardware like scales or age verification scanners.

alternative

Shopify POS

. Shopify POS Pro works with a curated list of certified hardware from brands like Star Micronics, Zebra, and Ingenico, covering scanners, printers, and payment terminals. Large enterprises benefit from Shopify’s scalable infrastructure, where hardware configurations can be standardized across hundreds of stores via the Shopify device management app. However, retailers should note that some legacy NCR peripherals may require adapter cables or replacement due to compatibility constraints.

alternative

Vend

. Vend integrates with a broad ecosystem of hardware vendors, including Epson printers, Honeywell scanners, and Moneris payment terminals, though the setup relies on the retailer’s own networking and driver configuration. It offers offline mode for processing sales during internet outages, which is critical for high volume brick and mortar operations. Vend’s cloud based approach means hardware firmware updates are handled separately, so retailers need a technical contact for ongoing maintenance.

Frequently asked questions

Can I keep my existing NCR hardware when switching to one of these alternatives?
It depends on the alternative and the specific hardware model. Some older NCR peripherals use proprietary drivers or serial connections that newer cloud based POS systems may not support. Vend and Lightspeed have broader compatibility via APIs, but you should check the alternative’s certified hardware list or request a compatibility test before committing.
Which alternative offers the best multi store management and centralized hardware control?
Shopify POS Pro provides a dedicated device management dashboard that lets you assign, update, and monitor hardware across all locations from one screen. Lightspeed Retail also offers centralized hardware settings in its back office, but the level of control is more inventory focused. Square POS has multi location support but its hardware management features are less granular for large enterprise deployments.
