If your finance team still spends hours chasing down receipts, correcting manual entries, and reminding employees to submit expense reports, you know the pain. Traditional tools like Expensify have been the default for years, but modern alternatives now offer smarter ways to manage spending. They combine corporate cards, automated receipt capture, real-time policy checks, and direct integrations with your accounting software. Instead of reacting to past expenses, you can set budgets, approve transactions before they happen, and close the books faster. Here are five strong alternatives worth evaluating.

alternative

Ramp

. Ramp combines corporate cards with an automated expense management platform that flags policy violations in real time. It pairs with your accounting software to sync transactions automatically, eliminating manual data entry. Ramp also offers insights on spending patterns to help you negotiate better vendor contracts.

Brex

Its platform uses AI to categorize transactions and enforce spending policies, reducing the need for manual approvals. Brex also supports bill pay and vendor management, making it a good all-in-one option for growing companies.

Concur

. Concur by SAP is a longtime enterprise-grade solution for travel and expense management. It offers robust policy controls, travel booking integration, and audit trails suitable for large organizations with complex compliance needs. Concur’s mobile app simplifies receipt capture and approval workflows for managers on the go.

alternative

Zoho Expense

. Zoho Expense is a cost-effective alternative that integrates tightly with the Zoho ecosystem and other popular accounting tools. It automates mileage tracking, receipt scanning, and expense report creation with customizable approval workflows. For teams already using Zoho products, this is a natural fit with no extra integration costs.

alternative

Spendesk

. Spendesk focuses on giving finance teams control over company spending with virtual and physical corporate cards. It lets you set per-employee or per-project budgets and approve expenses before they happen. Spendesk also automates receipt collection and provides a clear audit trail for each transaction.

How they compare

All five alternatives automate receipt capture and integrate with accounting software, but they differ in focus and pricing. Ramp and Brex offer free card and expense management software with no subscription fees (they earn from interchange). Concur is a premium solution best for large, global enterprises; its pricing is typically per-user per-month. Zoho Expense is a low-cost subscription suitable for small to midsize businesses, especially those already using Zoho apps. Spendesk targets European and mid-market companies with its pre-approval budgeting features. If you need advanced travel booking and compliance, Concur is the strongest choice. If you want a modern, corporate-card-first approach with real-time controls, Ramp, Brex, or Spendesk are worth exploring.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best Expensify alternative for a small startup with a tight budget?
Ramp or Brex are strong options because they let you use corporate cards and expense management for free, with no monthly fees. Both automate receipt matching and policy enforcement, which reduces manual work. Zoho Expense is also affordable if you prefer a subscription model and already use Zoho tools.
Can these alternatives integrate with my existing accounting software?
Yes, all five alternatives integrate with major accounting platforms like QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite, and Sage. Each tool provides direct sync or CSV export options. Check the specific integrations on their website to confirm compatibility with your current stack.
